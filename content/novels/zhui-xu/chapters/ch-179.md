The moon shone bright with few stars as Kang Xian's voice drifted out from the room. Zhou Pei crouched beneath the window, listening intently—lately, her curiosity about this young teacher of hers had only grown stronger. She assumed they were discussing some new development in the assassination case from a few days ago, but as she listened on, she realized it was something else entirely.

"Apparently it concerns a case from last year, with some connection to the assassination of Song Xian—and it seems to involve another official's disappearance and a family massacre..."

The one answering from inside the room was Lu Agui, Kang Xian's trusted attendant. Hearing him speak, Zhou Pei blinked in astonishment outside, momentarily stunned. Inside the room, Kang Xian had probably already furrowed his brows.

"How did it become so serious?"

"The matter isn't confirmed, and there's no solid evidence. But this Chen Feng fellow—I've known him in the past, and he's quite capable when it comes to solving cases. What he's uncovered so far isn't much, but hearing him speak reminded me of some other things."

"Oh?"

"Does the Master still remember that Young Master Ning showed great interest in martial arts around that time last year?"

"Hah, of course I remember." Inside, Kang Xian chuckled. "Though his understanding of these matters was rather fanciful—who knows what adventure tales he'd been reading. I mentioned to Master Qin at the time that for all his maturity in most affairs, he was actually quite spirited about this one. But given his resourcefulness, it wouldn't be strange if he later found some way to actually study a bit. Looking at things now, he really has learned something—but having heard the details of that assassination attempt a few days ago, what truly impressed me about Liheng was his decisive temperament."

"Indeed. However, does the Master also recall that he once asked about Song Xian and the assassin back then...?"

"If you're suggesting he's connected to that matter, I don't believe it."

"I find it hard to believe as well. But this association only came to me after listening to Chen Feng today. Chen Feng himself doesn't know about it—what he was investigating at the time wasn't the assassination of Song Xian, but the disappearance of an official named Gu Yanzhen."

Kang Xian thought for a moment. "Gu Yanzhen... I've met him a few times. Quite talented. Did he pass his examinations?"

"He received an actual posting last year. He returned to Jiangning to visit friends around March or April, with plans to take up the position of County Magistrate in Leping, Raozhou Prefecture by July. But after leaving Jiangning in June, he vanished without a trace. Later, his body and his servant's were discovered outside the city. There was also a family surnamed Yang who were all killed—and around the same time, several servants in Gu Yanzhen's own household were murdered, apparently silenced for knowing something. The killer was that female assassin who had slain Song Xian, so at the time they believed Gu Yanzhen's death was also connected to her. Chen Feng found some suspicious elements back then, but with the assassin still at large, they could only hastily close the case—and without many leads, the investigation stalled."

"How does this involve Liheng?"

"The family who died alongside Gu Yanzhen was no gentle sort. These Yang brothers—Yang Yi and Yang Heng—were notorious ruffians in Jiangning, famously vicious. Even ordinary mid-sized gangs didn't dare cross them lightly. Their entire household consisted of five people: the two brothers, Yang Yi's two grown sons who were said to be quite skilled themselves, and a wife. They didn't bother with petty crimes, but reportedly took on kidnapping and extortion jobs from time to time—the authorities never managed to convict them, though they likely had considerable blood on their hands. The most probable theory at the time was that they'd accepted a job from Gu Yanzhen to abduct someone within the city..."

At this point, Kang Xian suddenly snorted from inside the room. "A court-appointed official, consorting with such scum!"

"...As for whom they actually kidnapped, it's now impossible to determine. But the most likely candidate is someone the Master also knows—Miss Nie Yunzhu from Zhu's."

Silence fell in the room as Kang Xian presumably digested this information while connecting the threads to Ning Yi. After a moment, Lu Agui continued.

"They say Gu Yanzhen had some history with Miss Nie back when she was still at Golden Wind Tower. After passing his examinations and returning to Jiangning, he still couldn't forget her—except by then, Miss Nie had already left that life behind..."

"Hmph. I've heard of Gu Hong's reputation as a literary talent. Given how many courtesans he was deeply acquainted with, there must have been quite a few. But his eye for Yunzhu wasn't bad—though she came from the pleasure quarters, that woman's character and conduct are truly admirable."

"Indeed. According to what I know, by that time Miss Nie's relationship with Young Master Ning was already quite close. Yet even so, Gu Yanzhen apparently couldn't let her go—there was even talk of him proposing marriage in the street and getting slapped for it. Chen Feng combined this with Gu Yanzhen's past behavior patterns and speculated that whoever the Yang brothers had kidnapped for him might well have been Miss Nie. But when he investigated, Miss Nie hadn't actually gone missing during that period, so he also looked into the men connected to her—though nothing came of it at the time."

"...The Yang brothers kidnapped Liheng?" Without needing to hear more, Kang Xian had already grasped the implication. "And then what—you said the Yang family was completely wiped out?"

"All five members—not one survived."

"...When did this happen last year?"

"June."

"That's impossible." Kang Xian shook his head. "In May, Liheng was still asking me about martial arts—he was clearly just a frail scholar back then. The assassination of Song Xian also happened in May. Even if he'd somehow found a martial arts master to study under, or even taken that female assassin as his teacher, his skills couldn't have improved that much—who's ever heard of mastering martial arts in a single month? Ah, but if that female assassin went and massacred the family, then later killed Gu Yanzhen, that would be somewhat possible..."

Kang Xian's conjecture was reasonable enough, but Lu Agui contradicted it. "The strange thing is, the deaths of the Yang family and Gu Yanzhen most likely weren't the work of any martial arts expert. According to Chen Feng's investigation at the time, it was probably someone at a disadvantage who killed the entire Yang household—and also killed Gu Yanzhen and one of his guards that same night... seven people in total."

Outside the window, Zhou Pei's eyes had already gone wide—she'd hidden here precisely to hear this sort of thing, never imagining her teacher could be caught up in such affairs. Sure enough, after a short while, the two men inside seemed to have reached the same conclusion.

"Liheng did it?"

"I believe it's possible, though the exact sequence of events is now difficult to reconstruct..."

"What's Chen Feng's thinking?"

"Someone was kidnapped by the Yang brothers that day. This family was notorious for their brutality—four men and one woman—and they were all killed by someone who was at a clear disadvantage. If the one kidnapped was Young Master Ning, he didn't even know martial arts at the time. The reason Chen Feng made this conjecture is that after killing the Yang household, this person was already severely wounded—yet instead of leaving, he made a choice. He stayed near the Yang residence, set up some traps, and spent who knows how long waiting for the mastermind, Gu Yanzhen, to arrive..."

Kang Xian nodded slowly. "...Ruthless and thorough—eliminating every last threat."

"There were traces left at the scene showing the person had stayed in the nearby woods, vomiting twice in the same spot and chewing large quantities of bitter leaves. This indicates he was severely injured, possibly in continuous pain that caused the vomiting, while chewing leaves to stay conscious so he could wait for the one who had ordered the kidnapping to arrive... This isn't something an ordinary person could do. I ask myself whether I could manage it under those circumstances, and I don't think I could. Most importantly, having already killed the Yang household while seriously wounded, the normal choice would be to leave first..."

As Lu Agui spoke these words, Zhou Pei outside had already opened her mouth slightly, utterly overwhelmed by the implications: kidnapped by desperate criminals, he'd turned the tables and killed the entire family; severely wounded, he'd sat in the forest chewing bitter leaves to dull the pain. Trying to reconcile this with the image of her young teacher, she couldn't quite process the feeling—a shock she couldn't name. Had her teacher really done such a thing? Yet thinking about it now, she felt it was entirely possible—that teacher of hers might really be capable of it.

"That feeling of having someone on your trail is unbearable—this is the foundation of someone meant for great things..." Kang Xian said, then a note of realization entered his voice. "Ah, that's right... his hand got burned and he broke a bone too. He just didn't say much about it at the time..."

Lu Agui probably nodded. "Young Master Ning disappeared inexplicably for several days around that time, returning to say he'd been injured helping a friend, and his hand was wrapped in bandages until before the new year. Chen Feng doesn't know about this—it only came back to me after hearing him speak—but that would make everything fit together."

"Chen Feng certainly must not learn of it." Kang Xian spoke, then paused briefly. "This matter... there's no evidence?"

"Actually, I'm just speculating blindly—perhaps it's merely coincidence. Chen Feng only discovered Young Master Ning's connection to Miss Nie these past few days, which stirred his interest to investigate again. But if he tried to formally raise it, it wouldn't be easy."

"Easy or not, whether it's true or not, this cannot be brought up. A court-appointed official hiring criminals for kidnapping—that Gu Yanzhen deserved to die. And that Yang family certainly deserved their deaths as well. But..." Kang Xian trailed off, then resumed. "Hah, I've been puzzling over these past few days—during the assassination attempt, while Liheng's actions were mostly clever, his skills had actually become quite formidable too, living up to that 'Bloody Butcher' name. Truly impressive... beautifully done. Ah Gui, go handle this matter. That Chen Feng... can he be reasoned with?"

"He's been pursuing this out of a constable's instincts, but he's not an inflexible man. Besides, if you simply explain to him Young Master Ning's contribution to the disaster relief strategy, he'll know what to do."

"That's good then. Hearing your account of his deductions, this fellow is quite capable. Since you're acquainted with him, find out what kind of ambitions he has as an official. If he's a capable man, then try to find him a better position within the Jiangning prefectural government—see about moving him up. Most of those currently in power are seat-warmers anyway, so let's not waste real talent."

Hearing that Chen Feng was reasonable and could be persuaded, Kang Xian spoke with good humor. If Lu Agui had said the man was too rigid and would pursue justice at all costs, this old gentleman's next course of action would probably not have been so benign. Lu Agui answered with a "Yes" and nodded in acknowledgment, and then came the sound of a door opening.

Having heard all this, Zhou Pei's mind was still reeling from the shock. She hurriedly scrambled up to run away, but she'd been crouching too long—the moment she moved, she plopped right back down onto the ground. Scrabbling on hands and knees toward one side, she then heard Lu Agui's voice call out: "Little Commandery Princess."

The little girl's head drooped—she knew there was no point in running now. Then Kang Xian's voice came from the room, not particularly surprised, simply saying: "Xiaopei, come inside."

"Ugh..." The little girl rubbed her ear sheepishly and shuffled in. "I won't be a traitor, Imperial Son-in-law Grandfather—please don't silence me..."
