"Early in the morning, entering her inner rooms¹—who is this scholar? Doesn't look like an ordinary acquaintance."

"When we investigated last year, we didn't have this kind of information, did we?"

"After Nie Yunzhu retired, she rarely associated with unfamiliar men and decisively cut ties even with those she knew from before. We definitely never found any trace of this scholar."

In the morning light, two constables watched the scholar's retreating back, quietly exchanging their doubts. In fact, they'd had dealings with Nie Yunzhu once before last year. Their covert investigation hadn't turned up much meaningful information, and later, due to pressure from above to close the case and various other issues, the operation regarding that case had temporarily stopped. At this point, the middle-aged Deputy Squad Leader Xu smiled.

"Two leading courtesans retire and only maintain close contact with this one scholar. If word got out, plenty of self-proclaimed romantics would probably die of frustration—especially Gu Yanzhen..."

"Old Xu, you know what I'm thinking right now?"

"...Gu Yanzhen?"

"Wasn't there a theory like that back then..."

"Fair enough... I'll follow him and investigate."

Back when investigating Gu Yanzhen's cause of death, Chen and Xu had several theories, but truthfully none had particularly concrete factual basis—they were all rather vague, which was why the investigation had eventually stalled. Hearing this now, Deputy Squad Leader Xu nodded and followed after the scholar's distant figure. Squad Leader Chen remained behind, thinking about this case that had been dead for over half a year and long since closed by the higher-ups. He felt the chances of finding any leads this time were slim. After a while, Deputy Squad Leader Xu returned.

"How did it go?"

"Nearly got spotted, couldn't keep following. That scholar... has very high vigilance."

"Hm?" Squad Leader Chen paused. "Wouldn't have guessed that."

"Remember our theory back then?"

"Which one?"

"The person who killed the Yang brothers and later Gu Yanzhen was a real ruthless character. He might not have matched the Yangs in martial skill, but his temperament was that of a true desperado—only someone like that could have pulled it off. This person also has connections to the female assassin from Lüliang Mountains. Back then we couldn't find him with a casual investigation, so there wasn't much to say. Now that so much time has passed, if we really did find this guy... Squad Leader Chen, have you really thought this through?"

In truth, though constables like them constantly dealt with various criminals and had their temperaments trained to not be disturbed by ordinary crimes, when facing an opponent truly insane to a certain degree, if they could avoid contact, most people would still choose to stay away. The Yang brothers had been like that, for instance. After roughly deducing the profile of whoever had wiped out the entire Yang family, it was clearly someone not to be trifled with. If they investigated this and their guess was wrong, that would be one thing. But if they really found the person, they would inevitably have to confront them.

Squad Leader Chen thought for a moment, then stuck a blade of grass between his teeth and shook his head. "Those were just casual theories back then. In this vast sea of humanity, how could we so easily run into them... Women like Nie Yunzhu and Yuan Jin'er—after retiring from courtesan life, if we said there truly weren't any men involved with them, that would be the real joke. It's just that this matter must be kept secret, so that scholar is vigilant—it probably comes from that. Not so easy to really match up with our theory. But even if it really does match up..."

He smiled. "A desperado? When have I, Chen Feng, ever been afraid of anyone..."

Ning Yi hadn't actually noticed anyone following him. He just felt something at a certain street corner, observed for a while without finding anything, and assumed he was being overly suspicious, not pursuing it further.

That morning he naturally still went to the academy to teach. Zhou Pei, who had witnessed yesterday's entire fight, saw Ning Yi arrive with a shocked expression. During a break between classes, she found a moment to ask, "Te-Teacher, you were injured yesterday—are you all right?" Only after Ning Yi answered that he was fine did she relax.

Yesterday's events had shocked her beyond measure: the decisive shooting, the astounding fight, and his unyielding stance against such fierce opponents. Zhou Pei had never known a scholar capable of such things on short notice. While she had heard of scholars who faced death without fear, that was merely the passive courage of accepting execution². She had never imagined someone who could teach the sages' words one moment and fight with such brutal efficiency the next.

The scholar's refinement and confidence, combined with a warrior's ferocity—what shocked Zhou Pei most was the fire lance Ning Yi threw later. At the most critical moment, when the most vicious of the large men caught the fire lance in his hand, for an instant her heart nearly leapt to her throat. But in the next moment when the fire lance fired, it truly left most people's minds blank. At the time, Zhou Pei couldn't process it at all. Only later, after her emotions settled and she listened to the discussion at Grandfather Qin's, did she begin to understand the incredible contest of wits and courage that had unfolded.

She finally saw the full picture: her teacher's initial composure, his decisive actions, the pre-arranged exploding fire lance, and even Grandfather Qin's timely shout from the doorway. The sheer calm and quick-wittedness displayed were qualities that would leave anyone dumbstruck. The little girl had always considered herself smart and destined for great things, but yesterday was the first time she had ever witnessed true capability.

Imperial Son-in-law Grandfather must have known these things, which is why he had her and Junwu take teacher as our master. Perhaps Imperial Son-in-law Grandfather, who she'd spent so many years with, was also this capable a person—he just never showed it in front of children like us.

Thinking I'd already grown up, I really am still just a child...

Holding this mood, seeing her teacher this morning felt somewhat strange—she herself couldn't quite figure it out. Actually, she was still bothered about losing face yesterday morning and being seen crying afterward, but feeling that "this barbarian teacher really is quite capable," it bothered her a little less, to the point where it became bearable.

As for Zhang Rui and Li Tong, the two tutors who had originally planned to debate with her teacher—after seeing what happened and later witnessing Grandfather Qin's attitude toward her teacher, they just exchanged polite greetings and quickly left.

The little girl, impacted by yesterday's events, felt somewhat different. Regarding the troubles arising from having to select a commandery prince consort, she also relaxed a bit. Feeling that with people like teacher, Grandfather Qin, and Imperial Son-in-law Grandfather in this world, she shouldn't be bothered by such trivial matters. As long as she became capable herself, she could handle anything. If Ning Yi could know the little commandery princess's thoughts today—that she'd placed this mood upon marriage—he'd probably have to silently mourn for her future commandery prince consort.

The incident had only happened yesterday, so by afternoon Ning Yi made his way to the Qin manor to check on the situation. Just reaching the corner of the street where the Qin residence was located, he saw carriages, horses, and sedan chairs stopped all along the road. Qin Siyuan had originally settled in Jiangning in obscurity, but by this year quite a few people had come to visit. Yesterday's incident involving Liao people meant that today, figures from all walks of life had surged over together. Ning Yi looked for a few moments, turned to leave, deciding to come back after the commotion died down. But just as he turned around, someone blocked his path.

"If Liheng left just like this, I'm afraid the master and mistress would both blame this concubine."

Appearing before him was Qin Siyuan's concubine, Yun'niang. The learned and refined woman, veiled and followed by a maid, curtsied slightly. Ning Yi hastily returned the courtesy. "Ah, Lady Yun, returning from outside?"

"This concubine came specifically to wait for young master." Yun'niang smiled. "The mistress knew young master would come today. Earlier at home she said that later she absolutely must properly thank young master for his life-saving grace toward the master. At that time Old Kang was also nearby and said with a laugh that if young master saw the scene at the gate, he would definitely turn and leave, only coming after many days. Elder sister then instructed this concubine to come wait at the street corner. Young master's reaction truly is just as Old Kang predicted, *heh*."

After saying all this, Yun'niang slightly withdrew her smile and became somewhat serious. "Yesterday, young master saved the master's life—a great kindness to the entire Qin household. Please allow this concubine to pay respects."

She and the maid behind her bowed deeply this time, extremely solemnly performing the courtesy. Ning Yi could only return it solemnly as well.

Having said this much, there was no way to extract himself and leave for the moment. Following Yun'niang into the Qin manor, sure enough the Qin household had gathered quite a few people at this time—officials or great Confucian scholars. Only a few were people Ning Yi recognized, probably former Go partners of Qin Siyuan's. Seeing Ning Yi arrive, they all inquired whether he was injured. The others asked each other who this young man was, and after getting a rough outline, lavishly praised him as a young hero. Some who knew of his live-in son-in-law status couldn't help lamenting the situation. However, his nickname "Ten Steps, One Scheme" didn't concern anyone—they just assumed he had some small business tricks. That this young hero was actually a merchant was truly a regrettable thing.

Afterward, everyone engaged in casual conversation in the reception hall. Inevitably they chatted about Liao assassins, about the Liao state, and then turned to Qin Siyuan himself. At this time it was still too early to discuss his past "achievements," after all the relationship between Jin and Liao was still unclear, and Qin Siyuan was unwilling to discuss such things now. As they spoke, people's attention unavoidably turned toward Ning Yi. Though Ning Yi was no longer enthusiastic about social engagements these days, his cultivation in this area was extraordinarily deep—he chatted with easy grace, occasionally even guiding the atmosphere, handling it with practiced ease.

At this moment the room was full of officials and great scholars with status and position. When ordinary young people handled such occasions well, they could at most be called appropriately courteous—neither servile nor arrogant³. But even if neither servile nor arrogant, there was still always a gap in status and position. Ning Yi's performance was somewhat different. Ordinarily he already interacted with Qin Siyuan and others as peers, and at this time he didn't act ostentatious because of it, only answering a couple of sentences when others spoke to him. His bearing inherently carried the natural attitude of a superior. Sometimes when he said a few interesting things, everyone would laugh, yet no one felt he was speaking out of turn in front of his elders, being too arrogant or ostentatious. Combined with the importance Qin Siyuan and Kang Xian showed in their attitude toward him, it increased Ning Yi's sense of belonging.

While his behavior felt natural at the time, it was only upon later reflection that some guests realized just how exceptional the young man was. His bearing was beyond anything they had seen from Jiangning's younger generation, and upon learning of his prior association with both Qin Siyuan and Kang Xian, they speculated he must be a disciple cultivated by Qin Siyuan over the past few years, with the Imperial Son-in-law clearly involved in his training.

They reasoned that if the formidable old Qin Siyuan truly had such great merit, he might one day return to office, and perhaps only a man of his caliber could cultivate such an outstanding disciple in just a few years.

It was just that this disciple was both a live-in son-in-law and a merchant, which truly was... rather strange.

FOOTNOTES:

¹ *登堂入室 (literally "entering the hall and inner rooms"): An idiom with suggestive connotations in this context, implying intimate access to a household's private quarters, particularly the women's areas. In Song Dynasty spatial hierarchy, outer halls were public male spaces while inner rooms were private female quarters. The constables are speculating about the nature of Ning Yi's relationship with the women.*

² *引颈就戮 ("stretching the neck for execution"): The traditional form of scholar courage—passive acceptance of martyrdom for one's principles. Zhou Pei is shocked because Confucian scholars were culturally expected to face death bravely but passively, not to actively fight with lethal martial skill. Ning Yi's combination of scholarly teaching and brutal combat capability violates the fundamental Song Dynasty cultural separation between civil (文) and martial (武) paths.*

³ *不卑不亢 ("neither servile nor arrogant"): The ideal social bearing in Confucian culture, especially for younger people in the presence of elders. In Song Dynasty society, age-based hierarchy created clear behavioral expectations—even achieving this balanced bearing was exceptional for youth. Ning Yi's ability to interact as a natural peer with elders, possessing what observers describe as the "natural bearing of a superior," was almost unprecedented and prompted speculation about secret elite training.*
