"Liheng, are you... alright?"

"Hm?"

By the time the city gates closed, the afternoon lunch rush had long passed, leaving only a scattering of customers in Zhu's main restaurant. On the second floor, Nie Yunzhu's question made Ning Yi pause in surprise. She sat down across from him, smoothing the hair at her temples with a faint smile. "I heard about what happened with the Su family. They said... the family master was attacked. I also heard that the young miss and the son-in-law were there, so I've been worried these past few days... wondering if you were alright."

Jiangning had many wealthy merchants, with well-developed businesses in every trade. Though the Su family held a prominent position in the cloth industry, the atmosphere had been tense these days. News of the attack hadn't spread widely—after all, it wasn't everyone's concern. Even in the information-rich world a thousand years later, ordinary people wouldn't immediately hear about a merchant being robbed. Nie Yunzhu had been particularly attentive to the Su family, which was why she'd heard about it through conversations. Unable to see Ning Yi these past few days, she'd been anxious, fearing he'd been caught up in it somehow.

After listening to her explanation, Ning Yi nodded. "I'm fine. But... things have been quite tense at home, so I've been pulled into it these past few days. Been busy."

Hearing his confirmation that he was unharmed, Nie Yunzhu's worried expression finally relaxed. She smiled again. "Is the Su family master... alright?"

"Just out of mortal danger. He's paralyzed—won't be able to walk anymore. If he's to truly recover, it'll take months." Ning Yi took a bite of food and shook his head, though his tone remained casual. "We're keeping it secret for now, haven't dared announce it publicly."

"Oh? Why's that?"

"The attacker was caught and is at the yamen now. He insists the Su family ruined his family first... Someone's pulling strings behind the scenes. Whether the victim lives or dies affects the judgment, so we're stalling for now. How long we can keep it up is hard to say..."

"Someone... wants to frame the Su family?" Nie Yunzhu's eyes widened.

Ning Yi smiled and casually explained the situation. His tone held some regret about his father-in-law's injuries, but the rest of his description was matter-of-fact. His conversations with Nie Yunzhu usually had this atmosphere. Though Nie Yunzhu grew worried again, she then reminded herself that such matters wouldn't affect a live-in son-in-law. After a moment, she asked casually, "Then... that Sister Tan'er of yours in your household, she can handle this situation, right?"

"She happened to catch a cold and fell ill." Ning Yi sighed. "So recently I've been helping to hold down the fort."

"Uh..." Nie Yunzhu had asked offhandedly but now froze, unsure what expression to show. Finally, she repeated, "Is she alright?" —though whether she meant Su Tan'er's condition or Ning Yi's situation was unclear.

"She's fine. She'll be better after some rest. Stress was a big factor. Anyway, she'll recover with some time."

After exchanging a few more pleasantries, Nie Yunzhu smiled. "So recently, it's been Liheng managing the Su family's business?"

She'd always felt Ning Yi had talent that just lacked opportunity to display itself. Zhu's had only succeeded because of his guidance. Now that he finally had such a chance, as a friend, she naturally wanted to be happy for him.

Ning Yi laughed and shook his head. "Not really managing, just that the Su family needs someone in this position right now. I don't understand much about specific operations. These past few days I've just been bumbling around for show. Oh, speaking of which, two days ago something happened—I mistook the aromatic herbs and camphor used for storing cloth for dye materials. Told them not to waste it, to collect it all. Haha, I was nearly laughed to death..."

As Ning Yi ate and shared his embarrassing story, Nie Yunzhu listened halfway through, then didn't know what expression to make. She laughed helplessly. "Liheng did it on purpose, right?"

"Tch, of course! I'm so amazing, how could I make such a minor mistake, right?" Ning Yi winked. "I was deliberately teasing them and they didn't even know. But don't spread this around..."

"Now that you've said that, I don't believe you anymore." Nie Yunzhu frowned with a smile. "But since Liheng has stepped forward, things will definitely work out fine, right?"

"Haha, maybe. Anyway, I'll probably be wandering around outside these next few days. Mornings I go check on shops... I'm looking after several shops now, you know. They're much bigger than your shop. Might come here for lunch or afternoon meals. Not much serious business—those managers are all shrewd veterans who don't need me teaching them anything. So in a couple days, I'll find time to accompany you to see Old Master Qin... I'd already promised, but didn't have time these past few days. I actually came today to apologize."

Nie Yunzhu looked at him, then pursed her lips. "If Liheng is very busy right now, there's no need to rush making time for this..."

"Not busy, nothing much going on." Ning Yi resumed his serious expression, shaking his head as he ate. "The Su family's problems aren't major and won't be troublesome to resolve. Someone has half a foot in a pit already. I'm just responsible for digging the pit a bit bigger, waiting for them to fall in so we can see who's in the pit."

His mood was relaxed, no different from their usual runs and chats. He then smiled and talked about various small incidents he'd witnessed in the cloth shops these past few days. Yunzhu rested her chin in her hand across the table, listening. When Ning Yi asked questions, she'd share what had happened during the lockdown. Though the atmosphere was tense and there'd been several incidents large and small in the city, overall they hadn't encountered any safety issues.

Ning Yi spoke lightly about the Su family matters without going into much detail, so Yunzhu naturally didn't pry. But after Ning Yi left, she couldn't help thinking about Ning Yi's style and performance now that he was formally managing Su family business. To her, once Ning Yi made his move, he'd surely impress everyone. Regardless, as a friend, she liked hearing such things, just as she'd feel proud when Old Master Qin praised Ning Yi. In the evening, when Yuan Jin'er came over after teaching dance at the Swallow Jade Tower, she heard Ning Yi had visited and teased with a grin.

"Well, look who finally showed up. Now you don't have to pace around worrying anymore, Yunzhu."

When she heard Nie Yunzhu mention the Su family crisis, Jin'er's eyes widened. "Wait, even his formidable wife fell ill? How is someone like Ning Yi supposed to manage...?"

"I don't know. He didn't say much. But since Liheng has stepped forward, there definitely won't be problems. Besides, he said the issues aren't major and he'll resolve them."

"Empty comfort." Jin'er rolled her eyes dismissively. "Each field has its specialists, Yunzhu Sister, even I know that. That Ning Yi... he's very capable, I'll grant that, but he can't possibly understand everything. When it comes to business, I'd bet he definitely can't match his wife!"

Jin'er made sense. Yunzhu thought about it and showed a slightly hesitant expression. Yuan Jin'er looked at her and sat down nearby. "It's not our problem anyway. We can't help this time. The Su family has so many people—it's not like they're making him handle everything alone. They just need someone who can step forward and take charge. Don't worry... Actually, how about this—tomorrow I'll ask some sisters who know the Su family and find out exactly what's going on..."

Though Yuan Jin'er had bought her freedom, she hadn't cut those connections yet, still wanting to leverage them for business. The next afternoon, Ning Yi came to Zhu's for another meal, chatting with Yunzhu for a while before leaving. That evening, Jin'er lay in bed sharing the intelligence she'd gathered with Yunzhu.

"So the Su family wants to become Imperial Merchants! It's been huge news lately. Now the Xue family, Wu family, and some Chen and Lü families all see them as rivals. Oh, and the Su family is even fighting internally. But if they really succeed, all these problems would be resolved naturally... Your Ning Liheng, though—he's completely going about this the bookworm's way..."

"What do you mean 'my Ning Liheng'..."

"Fine, someone else's Ning Liheng then, happy? He's been trying to meet Lord He Fang these past two days, but isn't really using connections—though connections wouldn't help much anyway. But he just goes directly to request meetings. Yesterday and today, he annoyed the gatekeeper for over an hour before leaving. He's truly persistent. I heard he's going again tomorrow. In just two days, everyone in the cloth business is talking about it... He probably plans to annoy them for an hour every day until Lord He agrees to see him. How foolish—doesn't he have that Prince Consort connection? If he just asked..."

"Using others' connections carelessly for things you can accomplish yourself will make people look down on you later."

"Tch, anyway... such a foolish person. You couldn't tell he was this foolish when arguing with him..."

The two lay in bed wearing undergarments. Nie Yunzhu laughed. "That's called having principles, not foolishness. Lord He will have to meet him eventually. You're always thinking about using connections..."

"Hmph, even if he doesn't want to be Ning Liheng, you'd also say he was foolish, Yunzhu Sister! And even if he gets a meeting, if the other party's unhappy, you can't possibly convince them. In the end, connections are still what matters. Want to bet?"

"I won't bet on that. I'll bet Liheng will definitely resolve this matter."

"Fine, then I'll bet he can't pull it off!" Jin'er thought for a moment, a mischievous grin spreading across her face. "And I know just the stakes. If I win, you, my dear Yunzhu, have to go and confess to that Ning Yi of yours that you like him!"

"I-I don't... that..." In the dim light, Yunzhu's face suddenly burned hot. She turned to look at Yuan Jin'er, who tilted her chin up defiantly, batting her eyes in challenge. They stared at each other for a moment before Yunzhu frowned in embarrassment—she actually didn't like Jin'er constantly teasing about this. Taking a deep breath, she asked, "What about you? If Liheng resolves the matter?"

"If he's that amazing, I'll like him along with Yunzhu Sister and won't say bad things about him anymore." This was clearly Jin'er's mischievous side emerging. She maintained her slightly proud pose, chin up as she looked at Yunzhu beside her. Yunzhu's eyes widened, then she turned away to stare at the bed curtains' canopy. After a long moment, she said deliberately: "Deal!"

"Tch..." Jin'er's bravado deflated. The bed fell silent for a moment before she scooted closer to Yunzhu. Yunzhu shifted away. She scooted closer again, and Yunzhu finally laughed, though she also sighed. "It's impossible anyway. Go away! You're so annoying..." She pushed at the persistent Jin'er. "Always causing trouble!"

"I was just joking with you. How could you really agree to bet, Yunzhu Sister..." Jin'er curled up, grumbling. "Win or lose, doesn't it just let that Ning Yi take advantage? I don't want to sacrifice myself..."

"Win or lose, how could I possibly say anything? How would we interact afterward... What kind of person do you take Liheng for?"

"All men would like it... Besides, I think Yunzhu Sister really does like him. Watching you sometimes makes me anxious too..."

"I..." Yunzhu trailed off, her gaze lost in the canopy of the bed curtains. She let out a soft, resigned sigh.

"What is it?" Jin'er whispered, sensing the shift.

A long silence stretched between them.

"...I do like him."

The confession hung in the air, thick with complex emotions. After a moment, Jin'er held up her hands to form an 'X'. "Okay, joke's over. Bet's off. Cancelled."

Yunzhu mirrored the gesture, then gently flicked Jin'er's nose. Jin'er laughed and burrowed back under the covers, and the room fell quiet. After a while, rustling sounds started again as Jin'er shamelessly scooted back over, hugging one of Yunzhu's arms. "Yunzhu Sister, I was letting you win earlier..."

"Hm?"

"Though I don't think it's very likely, I do hope Ning Yi really can succeed, since he's someone we know. But I know Yunzhu Sister wants to support him, so I had to take the opposing side. Mm, Jin'er was letting you win..."

"I know."

"But I still think he can't do it..."

"..."

The atmosphere grew heavy as the bedroom fell into soft silence...

While Nie Yunzhu and Yuan Jin'er watched Ning Yi's movements, many others were also observing the situation. Ning Yi wasn't the real key player—no one believed he'd truly be useful. But with Su Tan'er hidden behind events, any clues about the Su family's decisions had to be gleaned from Ning Yi's actions.

His blunders in the cloth shops didn't matter, just adding some laughs. But behind these laughs, those managers were meticulously building momentum for the Imperial Merchant position—this was clearly Su Tan'er's true intention. And when Ning Yi suddenly visited Lord He Fang of the Imperial Textile Bureau—though it seemed absurd and he'd been turned away—people like Xue Yan and Xue Jin raised their guard, watching whether he'd make any progress or looking for the Su family's subsequent moves to find Su Tan'er's true meaning.

Not just rivals like the Xue family—even within the Su family system, managers like Xi Junyu couldn't read the current situation clearly. They didn't understand whether Ning Yi's sudden focus on the He Fang connection was scholarly impetuousness—truly believing he could convince someone just by meeting them—or if Su Tan'er's will operated behind it with deeper intentions.

"What on earth is going on here..." The evening Ning Yi was turned away at the He residence for the second time, Xi Junyu drank with a fellow manager in his courtyard, discussing these matters. Regarding Ning Yi's actions, they could only shake their heads and laugh. But as for Su Tan'er's intentions, with Ning Yi muddying the waters this time, he truly couldn't figure it out.

"The Young Master's actions could just be a smokescreen. Mistaking herbs for dye is one thing—a harmless blunder. But going to Lord He's residence... that was reckless. It's one thing if he makes no progress, but if he actually offends Lord He, we'll have a real problem on our hands."

"Sigh, let's just assume Second Miss has given instructions and has her ideas behind this. As for you and me... for now we'll just focus on the Imperial Merchant matter. Anyway... with his scholarly temperament, once he's made a decision, you and I can't really interfere..."

Meanwhile, in another part of Jiangning, some people were purely observing Ning Yi's personal actions, with no regard for Su Tan'er or Su Yu and their ilk.

"Turned away for two days now. Says he's going again tomorrow?" In the pavilion at the Imperial Son-in-Law's Mansion, Kang Xian laughed as he listened to Lu Agui's report. "What's this boy trying to do? Does he really think meeting someone means he can convince that He Fang?"

Nearby, a young lady and gentleman who'd accidentally overheard these matters and grown interested exchanged glances before running off to whisper together...
