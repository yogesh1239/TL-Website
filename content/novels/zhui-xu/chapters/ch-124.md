On this evening when many watched Ning Yi or Su Tan'er's movements, the Su family courtyard remained peaceful and warm.

In Su Tan'er's room, the soft clicking of chess pieces mixed with cheerful chatter. Chan'er and Xing'er were playing gomoku by the bedside while Su Tan'er watched from bed, occasionally offering advice. When Xing'er rarely won a game against Chan'er, she'd often protest. If the voices grew too loud, Ning Yi, who was discussing matters with Juan'er, would scold them: "Can't you see there's a patient in the room? How can she rest with all this racket?"

"But Young Miss is the one talking loudest," Xing'er pointed out, and Chan'er nodded in agreement. "That's right, that's right." Su Tan'er laughed. "I like it lively. You won't let me out of bed, now you won't even let me talk?"

"Not a hint of proper patient behavior. You're completely incorrigible."

"I'm feeling much better now."

"Likely story..."

"Oh? And what story is that?"

"The one where I have to deal with a dozen of your chattering sisters if you don't rest up. Let's see... there's Little Three, Little Four, Little Five..." Su Tan'er giggled, starting to count on her fingers. She'd been idle and silly lately. Ning Yi just laughed helplessly and tossed a few notebooks onto the nearby table.

"Chan'er, Xing'er, Juan'er—the three of you work together and draft a plan for upgrading the looms in each region. Take your time with it. Figure out the fund allocation, which department handles what, who the managers and supervisors should be... you know the drill. Why are you all looking at me? Yes, I'm asking you. Just think it through and give me a rough outline."

As Ning Yi spoke, Su Tan'er started to protest from the bed, "Husband, that's..."

"You have a different job." Ning Yi picked up a stack of letters and tossed them onto the bed. "Since you have so much energy, you can help me sort through the reports that have come in these past few days."

Su Tan'er quickly gathered the letters, a smile touching her lips as she glanced at the three maids. "But husband, the loom upgrades... that's such a major project."

"You'll have to approve it in the end anyway. What does it matter if they do it? They've been following you all along—maybe they'll have better insights."

Su Tan'er thought about it and finally nodded, then looked at the letters in her hands: "These are..."

"A compilation of opinions from all the shopkeepers in Jiangning over the past few days, including their submitted letters. I've read through them several times and asked Chan'er and Juan'er about them, but you understand best. I want you to tell me your impressions—these people's thoughts, who's unhappy with me, who wants to test me, who doesn't care, who's clueless, their personalities and why they think this way. Well, you can't sit still anyway, right?"

Su Tan'er smiled, then opened the documents with a serious expression. After a moment, she began thinking and analyzing.

The recent evenings in the room had mostly been like this. Su Tan'er's fever had broken. Apart from being emotionally low the day they confirmed Su Boyong would be crippled, she'd rallied afterwards. Ning Yi had restrictions for her, and she was cooperating as much as possible. Still physically weak, she'd need to recuperate for quite some time. Usually Chan'er or Juan'er stayed to keep her company. After Ning Yi left, she'd get out of bed to walk or sit in the courtyard. Though she inevitably thought about many business matters, the truly intense mental work had been reduced.

Of course, Ning Yi was often unreliable too. For instance, casually tossing evening work to the three maids, then having Su Tan'er review it afterward. But they'd actually done well. Still, having the three of them handle the loom upgrade plans made her worry. But since Ning Yi clearly knew what he was doing, so be it—she'd review everything in the end anyway.

Time passed in this atmosphere. Chan'er, Juan'er, and Xing'er discussed their ideas while occasionally Ning Yi and Su Tan'er would interject. Su Tan'er mostly pondered the shopkeepers' intentions, discussing and analyzing with Ning Yi. The work wouldn't last long, and afterward, there would be time for idle chat. Once Ning Yi returned to his own room, the household would begin preparing for bed. Under the warm glow of the eaves lamp, Xiao Chan's laughter mixed with the others' as she carried a basin of water toward his quarters, before the courtyard finally settled into a peaceful quiet.

At dawn, Jiangning City awakened to roosters crowing.

After washing up and eating breakfast, Ning Yi and Chan'er boarded a carriage heading to the Su family's main shop in Jiangning. The routine was simple: a morning meeting, then Ning Yi and Chan'er would visit shops one by one. Usually adorable, Xiao Chan took her role as Ning Yi's assistant quite seriously. She'd introduce things they saw along the way, explaining the cloth business bit by bit to Ning Yi. Apparently his blunder mistaking aromatic herbs for dye had embarrassed her deeply—she'd given him resentful looks all that afternoon.

Resentment aside, she'd still helped him deflect most issues. During morning meetings with the shopkeepers, she'd sit beside him with a small notebook, impeccably professional, occasionally speaking up about certain ideas, saying "This differs from what Young Miss said," while Ning Yi just nodded along saying "Mm, mm, mm."

After the morning meeting, Ning Yi and Xiao Chan would tour Jiangning in their carriage. Mornings were usually uneventful, just casual rounds. Jiangning still appeared prosperous, but soldiers and officials came and went, the atmosphere relatively tense. Occasionally they'd see fights breaking out. Xiao Chan would lift the curtain to watch from beside Ning Yi, then lower her head in silence. When Ning Yi put his arm around her shoulders, she'd turn and rub her cheek against his hand like a small cat.

"Young Master, are you going alone to see Lord He again today?"

"Mm."

"But it's not safe."

"It's fine."

"But I'm worried..." She mumbled softly. At times like this, she made Ning Yi think of her as a kitten even more. He couldn't help patting her head. "The situation outside isn't that bad. Don't worry so much. No being unhappy now, be good." Xiao Chan shook her head vigorously, then after checking Ning Yi's expression, she smiled and spoke softly.

"I know I'm just a maid. In another household, I'd probably be beaten for speaking out of turn..." She trailed off, then continued softly, "And I know Young Master has important things to do, and that the situation outside probably isn't as bad as I imagine. I know all that. But... but when it's about you and Young Miss, I can't stop my heart from worrying. I just... I can't help it. I just wanted you to know."

She made a little fist, earnestly thumping her chest, then blushed and lowered her head. Ning Yi didn't know how to respond. When the carriage reached the last shop, Xiao Chan had returned to normal, bouncing out of the carriage, always maintaining her serious, professional demeanor before the shop clerks.

At noon, after arranging for Xiao Chan and the others to return to the Su residence, Ning Yi headed toward He Fang's residence. This was the scheduled third day of visits. On the way, he happened to run into Wu Qihao from the Wu family on the street. They chatted briefly, with Wu Qihao politely inquiring about Su Tan'er's illness before leaving with a smile. Compared to the Xue family, the Wu brothers were better at social graces, probably because there'd been less friction between them. As he neared the He residence, Ning Yi was suddenly stopped.

Two short men in blue clothes and small caps, looking like household servants from any wealthy family, blocked Ning Yi's path and pulled him into a side alley. One of them bowed: "Teacher." This was young Prince Zhou Junwu. The other stood more reservedly—despite the amusing blue clothes and cap, it was naturally his sister Zhou Pei.

After exchanging greetings, Ning Yi looked puzzled. "What are you two doing? Did you sneak out?"

"No, no! Uncle Mu and the others are right nearby." Zhou Junwu quickly explained. Ning Yi looked out to see several similarly disguised men watching this way, clearly guards from the Prince's mansion.

"Sister and I heard you were going to meet that official, He Fang. Father said you have a plan to convince him in a single meeting, and we want to see how you do it! So... we came out." He gestured to their outfits. "What do you think of our disguises? We can pretend to be your attendants delivering gifts. We promise we won't talk or cause any trouble! Absolutely not!"

He pointed to a bundle they had. "We even brought the gifts for you. It would look strange if you suddenly had two more servants but no more packages, right? We got him really valuable things—a lingzhi mushroom, tribute fruits, pearl-white velvet... We checked, and he's sure to like them. We just really, really want to see how you're going to do it..."

Little Junwu spoke excitedly, waving his hands to emphasize they absolutely wouldn't cause trouble. Zhou Pei stood silently beside him, not speaking. She had issues with Ning Yi but still wanted to watch. Their little adventure was mostly because their father, Kang Xian, had analyzed the situation for them last night. He'd concluded it was impossible for Ning Yi to win He Fang over with words alone. If not with words, then how? The siblings had excitedly discussed the possibilities and concluded it had to involve conspiracy, blackmail, or threats.

Think about it—meeting a court official, grabbing leverage and weaknesses, making threats, even completing the task while ensuring the other party couldn't speak of it—surely this was Ning Yi's plan, since he wasn't stupid. Such dark and evil matters seemed thrilling just to imagine. So today the siblings had disguised themselves and waited here, ready to negotiate with Ning Yi and witness the spectacle.

Ning Yi's eye twitched. After listening to Zhou Junwu finish all his words with hopeful anticipation, he shook his head.

"You'll cause trouble. You're not allowed to follow..."
