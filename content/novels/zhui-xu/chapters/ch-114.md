"Good predictions never come true, but the bad ones always do..."

The sounds of chaos hadn't spread for long before they could confirm the problem came from the eastern gate. Everyone on the street looked in that direction, including some refugees who, not understanding what was happening, began to panic. They frantically speculated about the events unfolding there. Lu Agui glanced around.

"Princess, Young Prince, get in the carriage and prepare to return. The city gates may close... I need to go check on this. Liheng, the carriage will pass by the Su residence. You should return home as well. Once something like this happens, there's bound to be chaos."

Ning Yi nodded. Lu Agui rushed toward the city gate while Ning Yi boarded the carriage with Zhou Pei and Zhou Junwu to head back. Ning Yi sat beside the driver's seat as Zhou Pei and Zhou Junwu lifted the curtain to watch the situation outside. The city had been tense for days, and now the refugees were beginning to panic. The roads filled with arguments, curses, and children's cries. Soldiers and constables maintained order. Though chaotic, no major incidents had erupted yet.

They made it home safely. The Su household had already gone on alert, the main gate firmly shut. Some people had set up ladders against the walls to peer outside at the commotion. Everyone was somewhat bewildered. Juan'er was waiting near the main gate, and only then did he learn that Chan'er was waiting at the side gate—Su Tan'er and the three maids had returned home, and when the disturbance began outside, she'd sent them to wait for him. If he hadn't returned after a while longer, they would have organized the household guards to search for him.

Then they heard voices rising in the streets outside: "The city gates are closed—" The cry passed from person to person, gradually building into a somewhat panicked and confused roar. The sunlight in the sky seemed to grow pale...

At noon on the thirteenth day of the seventh month, amid waves of turmoil, Jiangning closed all four gates.

The cause traced back to the approaching Ghost Festival. Though the fifteenth was the true center of observance, the ghost gates opened on the first day of the seventh month, providing various reasons for memorial rites thereafter. Jiangning's streets filled with paper money and spirit candles, while outside the city walls gathered refugees who had lost family members. No wonder Su Tan'er and Lu Agui had both said the gates would certainly close before the fifteenth.

However, some among the refugees outside could see this coming too. Those who had entered Jiangning naturally fared better, but without proper travel documents and identity papers, entry was forbidden. Once the gates closed, their situation would become even more desperate. So on the thirteenth, someone at the eastern gate incited the refugees to rush inward. As the chaos grew worse, the officials there quickly chose to close the gates—a decision they had already prepared to make anyway.

After the eastern gate closed, the other three gates followed suit.

The city's blind panic didn't last long. Order was maintained, though that night Jiangning fell eerily quiet. People silently burned paper money in their courtyards and streets. The occasional passing of carriages and pedestrians seemed cold and desolate. Those living near the city walls could hear various sounds coming from beyond the gates.

By the next morning, aside from no one entering or leaving through the gates, everything seemed to return to normal. The Su household bustled with its usual harmony—the routine of rising, washing, eating, reading, practicing calligraphy, and chatting. When Xiao Chan entered in the morning to tidy the bedding and clean the room, she also asked about Miss Nie. Ning Yi answered casually with a few words but didn't elaborate. The matter had become rather unimportant now.

With more people staying home and fewer going out, the atmosphere grew even livelier. Children ran about everywhere, and both familiar and unfamiliar faces visited to chat. The third and fourth days continued this way. People gradually adapted to the reality of the closed gates. After the Ghost Festival, business at the brothels and entertainment quarters grew even more bustling. The nightlife flourished. Those going out often gathered in groups of friends, spending lavishly and enjoying life even more enthusiastically than usual.

On the other hand, grain prices in the city had risen to absurd levels. The government's grain sales had daily limits. Wealthy households hoarded grain to sell through black market channels. Jiangning had many rich merchants. As long as no major incidents occurred, the government couldn't truly enforce strict measures. They could only use appropriate means to pressure these wealthy families to contribute something to help maintain city order.

The gates had closed. The impact over these first few days was inevitable. Su Tan'er seemed to grow even busier, and with the Ghost Festival ancestor worship, trivial matters abounded. She still went to bed late at night. One evening she fell asleep midway through work again. Ning Yi went over to blow out the lamp, but she woke, looked at him, sniffed, then smiled: "I'll sleep soon..." This time she didn't wait long. Shortly after, she truly extinguished the lamp and went to bed.

On the evening of the seventeenth, the two chatted on the second-floor corridor. Su Tan'er ate the pastries Ning Yi had brought her: "Mm, the next couple of days should be quieter. We're going out to give charity porridge and grain to help the refugees. Will you come, Liheng?"

"You mean setting out food and having refugees line up to receive it one by one?"

"Yes, we're preparing porridge and mantou¹. They line up, and each gets a small bowl of porridge and one mantou. That's enough for one meal. Children get a portion too. A few years ago when the city was sealed, I went to distribute food. Placing things in their hands and hearing their thanks made me quite happy. There were many people then, but it's still early days now, so there shouldn't be as many. Though fewer people is actually a good thing."

"Oh, you don't love the country, but you're actually quite sentimental..."

"I'm a woman. I can only handle the kindness of helping one person at a time. A whole country has so many people—who knows them all?" Su Tan'er tilted her head back with a smile, answering casually. "But will my husband come tomorrow or not?"

"Mm, I'll come."

"Good."

The gates had only been closed for four days. Nothing had settled yet. Many things remained unfamiliar, and many things hadn't yet begun. Ning Yi had visited Nie Yunzhu, who was fine for now, though these past few days weren't suitable for visiting Old Qin to decline the adoptive daughter arrangement. Kang Xian was surely busy too. Ning Yi had only gone out once and naturally hadn't encountered him, though Lu Agui had mentioned Master Kang wanted to debate with him. That obviously wasn't possible now.

These were all the matters on his schedule. Perhaps after things stabilized and the children had played enough, Ning Yi would call them over to give lessons in the courtyard. Going out tomorrow to do charity work was a simple matter for both him and Su Tan'er. At this time, no one knew that the next day would bring such an event—without warning, yet as if a long-lurking conspiracy suddenly emerged...

******************

After four days of closure, the city's tension hadn't increased much. Not many people had completely run out of food yet. But hearing that the Su family was giving charity relief today, many refugees and beggars still gathered at the small square near the Su compound.

The disaster relief format wasn't much different from what Ning Yi had seen on television—simply forming several lines, ladling out thin porridge, giving small steamed buns. Though difficulties were just beginning, many refugees already showed wan complexions and desolate expressions. Some remained silent, others offered profuse thanks. Some discussed among themselves, saying that one was the Su family's Second Miss, that one was the Su family's son-in-law... Such charity naturally aimed to build reputation for merchants. This was normal.

Su Tan'er naturally understood the purpose of gaining reputation, though she was also genuinely happy to do good deeds—probably the kind side of her nature. But for Ning Yi, it was more complex. Speaking of bad things, he'd seen the deepest darkness, the most unfair circumstances, the most twisted human nature. But speaking of good things, he'd also seen much fairer discourse and atmosphere. So any sense of superiority from such actions had become meaningless to him. He simply treated it as something that needed doing.

The small square buzzed with voices. Halfway through the food distribution, Su Tan'er came over: "Father's here too."

"Hmm?" Ning Yi turned to look. A carriage pushed through the crowd from one side of the square. It was Su Boyong, who had gone out this morning and was now returning home. The carriage stopped beside the row of tables where they were distributing porridge. Su Boyong came over to greet Ning Yi and Su Tan'er.

Though they were father and daughter, the interactions between Su Boyong and Su Tan'er weren't as warm as typical fathers and daughters. Su Tan'er never acted coquettishly around her father like daughters from ordinary families. Su Boyong seemed perpetually uncertain around Su Tan'er, not knowing whether to show his kind side, his stern side, or his professional merchant side.

After exchanging greetings and joking briefly with Ning Yi, Su Boyong looked at Su Tan'er and gave simple instructions: "You haven't looked well these past few days. Rest when you can. Don't overwork yourself." Su Tan'er nodded: "I know."

Then Su Boyong went to a nearby long table to personally distribute mantou. Ning Yi stayed with Su Tan'er, one ladling porridge, one giving out mantou, working together. There was no need to elaborate on Su Tan'er's relationship with her father. The two chatted casually. At one moment, something caught the corner of Ning Yi's eye—a small disturbance in one of the lines.

It was the line in front of Su Boyong. Someone pushed forward, apparently trying to cut in line, creating a perfectly normal small commotion. The Su family guards maintaining order didn't react in time. The person had already closed the distance. Su Boyong looked up, holding a mantou in his hand. Ning Yi noticed this for less than a second before the two figures collided. Su Tan'er was also looking in that direction.

Blood sprayed forth. The person held a knife and stabbed Su Boyong once. Su Boyong staggered backward and turned. The person stabbed him again in the back, then turned and ran.

"Ahh—" Screams erupted from the crowd as chaos spread. Ning Yi flipped over the table and ran toward them. Su Tan'er started moving almost simultaneously, without crying out or panicking, her face and eyes almost expressionless. Ning Yi rushed over to prevent the chaotic crowd from surging forward. Su Tan'er threw herself down beside her father. She glanced once at the fleeing attacker, then spoke simply and curtly to the surrounding guards: "Catch him." Then she just lowered her head to press on her father's wounds, ignoring everything else. Actually, quite a few guards had already surrounded the attacker.

Ning Yi watched the surroundings, confirming that even if there was a second attacker, they couldn't rush forward now. Only then did he turn back to help Su Tan'er press the wounds. Tears now glimmered in Su Tan'er's eyes. She pressed her lips tightly together without speaking, probably somewhat confused for the moment. So Ning Yi gave orders to those around: "Find the nearest doctor! Bring those clean cloths! Quickly, quickly, quickly! Do what you can do..."

Both knife wounds were quite deep. Though not immediately fatal, the outcome was uncertain. Su Boyong remained conscious, grasping Su Tan'er's hand and saying something. Ning Yi frowned, looking around for any clues he might spot.

It might be premeditated, or it might not. But among the Su family's three branches, the First Branch was the weakest. Though everyone said Su Tan'er was the most capable potential successor of the third generation who might lead the Su family in the future, she was still in the testing phase. The Su family's First Branch was always controlled by Su Boyong. He was their backbone.

With these two knife wounds, what the Su family would become tomorrow was now uncertain...
