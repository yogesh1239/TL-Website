Su Boyong had fallen, but the frantic mobilization that followed wasn't limited to just the main branch. The moment word arrived from the yamen, everyone understood—someone was making their move against the Su family. From that afternoon onward, the entire Su family's forces within the city burst into feverish activity. Shopkeepers, managers, and various advisors began streaming toward the Su compound, from the second branch, the third branch... while the main branch's affairs grew even more overwhelming.

In the past, though Su Tan'er managed the main branch's business—nominally half of it—Su Boyong had always been there, a steady presence backing her from behind. With Su Boyong down, the full weight of controlling the main branch crashed squarely onto Su Tan'er's shoulders. The Old Master might consciously share some of the burden, but the elderly patriarch had even more pressing matters to attend to. He was old, after all—he couldn't step forward to carry the entire Su family on his back again.

After the Old Master departed that afternoon, Su Zhongkan and Su Yunfang also left in anxious haste. Su Tan'er began summoning everyone she could reach to the residence, this time including even those shopkeepers who'd previously reported to Su Boyong. Actually, even following established routines, these shopkeepers could maintain operations for quite some time. But if someone was truly pulling strings from the shadows, the Su family's nationwide business would become dangerously vulnerable. Worse still, with the city gates sealed, transmitting information in or out would be countless times slower than usual.

Upon hearing of Su Boyong's injuries, Su Tan'er's mother had fainted dead away. Afterward, Su Tan'er remained by her side in the room while Ning Yi directed Chan'er, Juan'er and the others to handle various minor matters. As the lamps were first being lit, he left the courtyard briefly. When he returned, lamps glowed within the room as well. Su Tan'er's mother had regained consciousness. Approaching the chamber, he could see the silhouettes inside and hear voices—Su Tan'er's mother and the two concubines were weeping, words spilling between their tears. Su Tan'er sat in utter silence. After listening for a few moments, Ning Yi quickly grasped the situation.

The three women were crying while simultaneously berating Su Tan'er—berating her ambition, berating her... for this matter of the imperial merchant appointment.

"We told you before... why must a girl be so ambitious..."

"This incident, who knows if Second Uncle and Third Uncle aren't involved..."

"They know Tan'er was trying for the imperial merchant position..."

"They've been discussing it for days..."

"Perhaps it frightened them... if she truly succeeds, all their efforts would come to nothing..."

"Even we womenfolk understand this principle..."

Su Boyong had managed the main branch's various affairs quite competently, but his wife—Su Tan'er's mother—tended toward weakness. This was mainly because she'd only given Su Boyong a daughter, leaving her with little authority to speak in such a household. Later, she'd helped Su Boyong take two concubines, yet the main branch remained without male heir. Only then did people begin to suspect the problem might lie with Su Boyong, though by that point, everyone's position and character had essentially been established.

In earlier years, because Su Tan'er was a girl, her mother hadn't been particularly affectionate, desperately wanting to produce a son. This likely established their pattern of interaction—similar to that between father and daughter, lacking everyday warmth. When Su Tan'er had wanted to involve herself in the family business, her mother had objected, but later fell silent. Now, even if they yearned for a more normal mother-daughter relationship, neither knew how to bridge that gap.

The two concubines typically had little say before Su Tan'er. Now they only dared weep and hint obliquely through their tears.

Not many in the household knew of Su Tan'er's pursuit of the imperial merchant position; fewer still could see through to the truth. But no matter how well-concealed, the day would come when it must be laid bare. After all, those responsible for imperial merchant affairs were officials of various ranks. When matters concerning the Jiangning Weaving Bureau reached the critical stage, exposure was inevitable. A few days before the city gates closed—likely after her discussion with Xi Junyu—Su Tan'er had formally met with the relevant officials, laying out all the connections she'd cultivated. If the imperial merchant appointment was to be secured this year, it would happen within the next month or two.

Once exposed, everyone could see—especially the Su family members. They'd originally planned to trip up Su Tan'er, waiting for her gender to disqualify her from succession. Who could have imagined this woman would strike such a devastating blow from the shadows? The imperial merchant matter—if handled beautifully and profitably—would end all disputes forever.

In the days since the gates closed, this matter had still been circulating in hushed discussions when Su Boyong's incident occurred. Su Tan'er's mother and the concubines primarily interacted with household members, their perceptions of goodwill and malice naturally focused on these family members. Now they suspected Su Zhongkan and Su Yunfang—perhaps some among them had taken desperate action. Even if it meant betraying the family, at least Su Tan'er wouldn't claim everything... taking less was still better than nothing at all... Women's thoughts often circled such matters.

At this moment, Su Tan'er's mother wept and wailed, the two concubines also crying, their fragmentary words mixing complaint with insinuation... In the shadows cast on the window, Su Tan'er sat throughout in silence, head bowed, saying nothing, offering no defense. Ning Yi knocked and opened the door to find Su Tan'er still wearing the bloodstained clothes, seated on a small stool by the bed, fists clenched on her lap, gaze fixed obliquely on some point on the floor, coldly unchanging.

Ning Yi greeted the two concubines and his bedridden mother-in-law. His mother-in-law continued weeping, ignoring him, her gaze holding some resentment but more grief. Resenting Su Tan'er's excessive ambition, likely resenting this son-in-law as well, plus the family members, the two brothers-in-law scheming for inheritance, the second and third branches... Common enough in wealthy households.

"...Several shopkeepers have arrived, so I came to check. Shopkeeper Liao has some matters for Tan'er to review..."

After these greetings, Ning Yi delivered his message. Tan'er nodded, wiped her eyes, and softly apologized to her mother and the concubines. Her words felt perfunctory, almost formulaic, but there was no helping it now. After leaving the room and courtyard, Su Tan'er walked with Ning Yi along the path. Starlight filtered down as she gazed indifferently at the surrounding scenery. Walking in silence to their residential courtyard's entrance, Xiao Chan was already waiting, hurrying over.

"Master, hot water is prepared." She glanced at her mistress beside him.

Su Tan'er frowned. "Shopkeeper Liao..."

"I made that up. You've been sitting all day. If you'll be busy tonight, go bathe first," Ning Yi said.

Su Tan'er froze, turning to look at Ning Yi. After a moment, she nodded silently. "Thank you, husband..." With that, she strode into the courtyard. Only then did he see her raise a hand to wipe her eyes, though her steps never faltered. Ning Yi signaled Xiao Chan to follow.

The stars hung high in the night sky, the moon waning from full as if something had taken a bite. Moonlight, starlight, and lamplight converged throughout the Su compound amid various clamorous voices and footsteps. Ning Yi stood there thinking, then sighed softly. This night would likely be sleepless...

*********************

For half the night, lamps burned ceaselessly in the neighboring courtyard. Su Tan'er met continuously with the main branch's shopkeepers, predicting potential developments, discussing countermeasures, estimating their hidden enemies, identifying possible sources of support. No clear leads yet emerged, but everything requiring preparation had to be set in motion.

The three maids Chan'er, Juan'er, and Xing'er bustled about with their own tasks. By comparison, this courtyard seemed rather desolate. Ning Yi had little to do—in most people's eyes, this wasn't his affair, nor something he had the ability to participate in or change. He took half a bowl of peanuts to the pavilion in the courtyard's center, slowly shelling them while sensing the atmosphere pervading the compound, pondering the incident's possible causes and eventual direction.

Of course, with so few clues to grasp, achieving any real insight was impossible. When Xiao Chan hurried past the corridor and saw no one around, she came over to embrace him briefly. After releasing him, she pressed her lips together softly. "Master, are you worried?" She spoke quietly, apparently intending to comfort Ning Yi. Ning Yi smiled, placing several peanuts in her hand. "I'm fine. Go on with your work, keep an eye on Tan'er."

The young maid nodded, tucking the peanuts into her bosom. After thinking briefly, she turned to leave. "Master should sleep early..."

Perhaps an hour later, Juan'er glanced over while passing under the eaves, then came to sit down quietly. Ning Yi was idly arranging peanut shells on the table to represent various parties involved in this affair. Juan'er surely couldn't understand. She sat quietly for a while, her gaze moving between the bowl of peanuts and Ning Yi. Ning Yi glanced at her and pushed the bowl over. "What is it?"

Juan'er smiled. "Just now when I passed by, Xiao Chan took a peanut from her bosom to eat. After eating just one, she went back to work. I asked her about it, and she smiled telling me Master gave her several peanuts—this way she could eat them until dawn..."

"Oh, so impressive..."

"So I came to eat one too." Juan'er took a peanut from the bowl, shelled and ate it, then rose to leave. As she departed, she added, "Master should sleep early..."

Ning Yi watched her retreating figure, shaking his head with a smile. This night, whether truly busy was hard to say. Su Tan'er and the numerous shopkeepers were busy—mainly with anxiety and discussion, but for now, leads were scarce. The subordinates were mostly carried along by this emotional atmosphere, waiting. If not busy, they'd likely be criticized as neglecting duty. Currently, the only real breakthrough remained that Chen Er in the yamen. If they couldn't identify the puppet master, they would have to wait for their opponent's next move.

After thinking through what he could, Ning Yi collected the peanut bowl and went to bed. He slept perhaps just over an hour before rising again. By then it was the darkest, quietest period before dawn, yet the unease and agitation throughout the Su compound remained palpably felt. He took a cup of tea outside. The neighboring courtyard had darkened—presumably the meeting shopkeepers had departed. Chan'er, Juan'er, and Xing'er should have gone for brief rest. Su Tan'er's room still glowed with lamplight. When Ning Yi approached, she held a brush, staring blankly at the oil lamp's flame. A letter lay half-written on the table—correspondence meant to be sent outside the city.

Ning Yi walked to the window and set the teacup on the table. Only then did Su Tan'er react. She looked up sharply at Ning Yi, then her gaze calmed. She stared vacantly at the proffered teacup before reaching out with one hand to take it, lowering her head.

"Almost dawn," Ning Yi said.

Su Tan'er nodded but didn't respond. She sat in prolonged silence before finally raising her head with a faint smile—both sorrowful and resolute. "Mother... and the concubines think perhaps the imperial merchant matter being exposed led someone to desperate measures. Some shopkeepers... think so too. People from the second and third branches might be involved..."

"This world has no shortage of idiots," Ning Yi nodded. "But idiots don't accomplish great things."

"Heh..." Su Tan'er smiled. "Even if there are, they couldn't be the masterminds. Besides, the second and third branches have only known about the imperial merchant matter for days. They lack such resolve—they couldn't decide so quickly to betray the family. Even if they decided, they lack the capability. Those behind this have surely planned for a long time. But even so... I can't say it's definitely unrelated to me."

Su Tan'er acted with conviction and determination. Even with these events, she'd still calmly begun handling everything tonight, actively responding, supporting the overall situation. With her father fallen, she absolutely couldn't fall—this temperament exceeded even typical men, the true attitude for accomplishing things. Yet speaking of this now, tears still glimmered in her eyes. The woman raised her head, drawing those tears back.

"But regardless, once decided, action must follow. There will certainly be resistance—every kind of resistance possible. If you try to avoid everything, nothing gets done. Husband... I'll see this through... Once finished, everything will be clear."

Su Tan'er gazed at him, showing a smile before sniffling slightly. These words seemed directed less at Ning Yi than at herself. Ning Yi nodded and turned to leave, then looked back.

"The tea's freshly brewed... Finish soon, sleep soon."

"Thank you, husband..."

They were the same type of people. Ning Yi understood that crisis was crisis—this sudden devastating blow might be overwhelming, but Su Tan'er needed no excess sympathy. For the entire Su family, this was merely another crisis management process. Everything that should be done, could be done—she saw it all with perfect clarity. Watching her proceed was enough.

However, in the following days, perhaps due to certain unexpected developments, the entire situation still deteriorated sharply...

******************

During these days, Ning Yi didn't leave the compound.

The Su family's situation was chaotic. Ning Yi could only watch, naturally unable to intervene. During this time, Old Master Su Yu, Su Zhongkan, and Su Yunfang frequently went out visiting various people, but the yamen still had no new progress regarding Chen Er. The main branch's shopkeepers repeatedly visited officials at the Weaving Bureau. Under these circumstances, they still displayed their determination to secure the imperial merchant appointment—Su Tan'er employed precisely the correct response strategy. She'd been cultivating these matters for over a year. Once revealed, it showed an ambition that left others speechless.

Su Boyong's condition still hovered between life and death; the final outcome remained uncertain. Everyone waited in silence. Su Tan'er visited once daily, unwavering in her work.

Ning Yi occasionally watched those shopkeepers coming and going from the second floor, occasionally listening to updates on latest developments, but mostly reading and writing, mentally organizing these developments.

He couldn't pinpoint when the situation quietly shifted. Four days after Su Boyong's collapse—around the morning of the twenty-second day of the seventh month—Ning Yi noticed Su Tan'er's spirit seemed altered. She appeared to have caught cold, but this change seemed not merely physical but a difference in her spiritual energy from when they'd spoken previous nights.

After dusk that day, she again summoned numerous shopkeepers for discussion. While Chan'er, Juan'er, and Xing'er busied themselves with reception, Su Tan'er had fallen asleep at her desk. Several letters blew out in the wind. After Ning Yi retrieved them and brought them inside, he placed the letters on the table beside Su Tan'er, securing them with a paperweight. Su Tan'er startled awake, standing up and colliding with Ning Yi before stepping back. Seeing it was Ning Yi, she smiled weakly. "Ah, husband."

Ning Yi studied her briefly. "Do you have a fever?"

"Hmm?" Su Tan'er paused, touching her forehead. After a moment she smiled, shaking her head. "No, just tired these past days, as husband knows... Once things are finished, I'll be fine."

After speaking, she turned to organize the letters on the table. Then Juan'er came to say the shopkeepers had arrived. Su Tan'er smiled apologetically at Ning Yi, exchanged a few words, then left with Juan'er.

That night, Ning Yi stood at the second-floor window observing the neighboring courtyard. Several of the main branch's servants and maids stood guard outside while the meeting proceeded within, everyone discussing various chaotic matters. Su Tan'er's mental state still seemed adequate; he saw her speak several times. After watching for a while, Ning Yi sighed, turned, descended the stairs, and headed toward that courtyard.

Despite the ongoing meeting, the supervising maids were Chan'er, Juan'er, and Xing'er. Seeing Ning Yi's grave expression, they naturally didn't stop him, though Xing'er followed. "What's wrong, Master?"

"What exactly happened? How many days has your mistress been ill?"

"Mistress..." Xing'er froze, then nearly burst into tears. "We... we discovered today too, but, but..."

Ning Yi strode into the room. Su Tan'er stood with her back to the door, left hand braced against the table edge, head lowered as she pointed at something on the table with her right hand, discussing some matter. Seeing Ning Yi enter, all the shopkeepers turned to look. Ning Yi approached and patted Su Tan'er's shoulder. Su Tan'er unconsciously waved him off. Ning Yi patted again. Only then did she turn, slightly puzzled but still showing a trace of a smile. "Husband, you..."

The moment her left hand left the table, her body began swaying. Ning Yi placed his palm on her forehead—faintly burning. Su Tan'er lowered her head, clutching Ning Yi's hand with both of hers.

"I'm fine, fine..."

Murmuring these words, her body went limp. Xi Junyu moved from the side to help, but Ning Yi had already lifted Su Tan'er into his arms.

"Mistress!" Chan'er, Juan'er, and Xing'er all rushed in. The shopkeepers stared wide-eyed, rising to their feet amid confused chatter. But moments later, one voice cut through calmly—not loud, but everyone heard clearly.

"Continue your discussion. Shopkeeper Liao, please take charge. Juan'er, go fetch Doctor Sun. Chan'er, come with me. Xing'er, stay here to manage things. Everything proceeds as normal."

With these simple words, Ning Yi frowned, carrying Su Tan'er as he turned to leave.

The night sky loomed deep and dark as heavy storm clouds gathered on the horizon, rolling in over the city. The night wind carried a chill, but the woman's body in Ning Yi's arms burned scorching hot. As he laid Su Tan'er on the bedroom bed, the woman's lips parted slightly, her face flushed entirely red from the fever, still unconsciously shaking her head...
