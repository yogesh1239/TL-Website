Shouts and wails erupted throughout the restaurant, voices overlapping in a cacophony that made the place feel like an exploding pot. Caught in the middle of it all, no one could grasp the full picture of what was happening.

When Song Qian pulled Qin Siyuan and burst through the main door, the burly giant behind them had already forced his way through the crowd in direct pursuit, the distance between them shrinking with every second. Ahead at the street entrance, dozens of men surged forward to block their path, creating what felt like a perfectly laid ambush. Though the imperial family's actual authority had diminished in recent years, Song Qian hadn't earned his position as a Prince Kang mansion guard by being easy prey. Seeing enemies closing in from both directions, he gritted his teeth and drew his sword. The chaos around the doorway intensified—they weren't the only ones rushing out, as other customers who'd been near the entrance also burst through. When this wave of people spilled onto the street and saw the dozens of men blocking their way, they froze for just a moment. The leader of that blocking force seemed equally surprised, throwing up his hand sharply to halt his group and creating a brief standoff.

In that split second when no one could distinguish friend from foe, gunfire echoed through the main hall with deafening force. The mighty warrior who'd been cutting through the crowd charged straight for Qin Siyuan. Song Qian's steel blade cleared its sheath as he prepared to fight to the death, though he couldn't decide which group to engage first. A voice rose through the chaos with steady authority: "It's them! Take them down!" Though the words were quickly swallowed by the surrounding confusion, enough people heard them clearly—and the speaker was none other than Qin Siyuan himself, the man Song Qian had just pulled outside.

Looking back later, though the crisis had erupted so suddenly and Song Qian had dragged him out without explanation, this man past sixty showed not a trace of panic when it mattered most, responding first with perfect clarity. Others naturally heard his command as well. The burly giant behind them roared "Ah—!" and hurled a potted plant. Song Qian swung his arm to deflect it, but the massive impact forced him back several steps as shattered ceramic and dirt flew through the air. The main body of the pot veered slightly off course and sailed toward the dozens blocking the street entrance. Their leader had just raised his hand to stop his gang when the projectile came flying at him. He hastily smashed it away with both hands, and though he avoided serious injury, the splattered mud left him covered in filth.

"Damn it, they were ready for us too..." The leader wiped wet dirt from his face, then suddenly yanked a club from his side. "Brothers, smash them!" The dozens of men roared "Ah—!" and charged forward.

The chaos at the entrance coalesced into a surging tide as the two groups collided. Inside the main hall, Yuan Jin'er crouched behind a counter, peeking out without any understanding of why everything inside had descended into madness. All she could register were shouts, gunfire, spreading smoke and splashing blood. The fire lances¹ of this era used gunpowder that produced enormous smoke clouds, and their report was staggering—when one erupted with a sudden bloom of sound, the spectacle was truly terrifying beyond words. On the other side of that smoke cloud, flesh and blood flew outward with a spray, splattering across the faces and bodies of several people behind.

At that moment on the second-floor corridor balcony, several figures hurried out to witness the scene—Zhou Pei, Zhang Rui, and Li Tong, who'd come with Ning Yi. They'd seen him take the fire lance and leave, and after a moment's hesitation had followed, but before they'd even emerged from the corridor, someone had already jumped down from the balcony platform and chaos had exploded below, throwing the entire hall into disorder. Zhou Pei was startled and crouched by the railing to look down, and before she could make sense of what was happening, that tremendous boom erupted, smoke rising on one side of her vision mixed with shocking amounts of blood.

Then Zhou Pei and the two tutors saw the scholar who'd been discussing literature with them just moments ago, all traces of his refined scholarly bearing gone. As chaos consumed the main hall and the entrance simultaneously descended into melee, that suddenly appearing gunshot had been crisp and decisive without a trace of hesitation. The gun barrel folded closed, and the scholar pulled the white-clothed woman beside him close as he tried to retreat through the crowd.

The three people on the balcony above couldn't adapt to the sudden eruption of blood and death. Even Zhou Pei, who'd seen more of the world than most young ladies, and Zhang Rui and Li Tong who understood military strategy—when they suddenly witnessed a body collapse on the floor with blood spraying everywhere, their minds went completely blank for that instant, unable to connect it to any concept they knew. Then they saw a scarred man below stop in his tracks and suddenly charge toward the long-robed scholar.

"Yah—ahh—"

"Kill Old Dog Qin first!"

"Fire lance! Don't let him light it again—"

These shouts came from several mouths, but Zhou Pei, watching from above in her dazed state, couldn't even distinguish who was speaking through the chaos. All she could see was that scarred man charging toward Ning Yi with a furious roar. The northern warrior stood a full head taller than Ning Yi, his build powerfully muscular, and in the blink of an eye the two collided within the smoke that hadn't yet dispersed.

The young girl's heart almost leaped into her throat. In her impression, Teacher Ning had always been a frail scholar, and even when she occasionally heard him boast about being some "Bloody Butcher," she could only attribute that to his taste for lowbrow jianghu tales and legends. But this was no time for jokes—the size difference between them wasn't even in the same category. She feared Teacher Ning would be sent flying with blood spraying from his mouth at the slightest collision. Yet in the next moment, she saw that within the smoke cloud, the two men had already engaged in combat.

From the first instant it was the most direct collision imaginable—a brutal fight with none of the aesthetic grace of martial arts. Tables and chairs flew as they crashed through them. The fire lance's barrel was bamboo with only a metal endcap, and Ning Yi swung it at the scarred man's head. The man blocked and the entire bamboo tube shattered, the flexible bamboo strips turning the fire lance into something like a whip with the steel end scraping past the back of the man's head before Ning Yi was sent flying backward from the impact.

He'd been practicing that internal energy technique for some time now, his physical condition far surpassing ordinary people, and when he explosively released that force it was truly remarkable—otherwise that single swing couldn't have turned the fire lance into a whip. But this kind of strength only mattered in that explosive instant; his body still couldn't match this scarred giant's. In the blink of an eye he'd already taken a punch, though he landed one in return. He swung a stool that the man casually smashed to pieces, and these brief exchanges had already proven the imbalance of power between them. In the next moment, the scarred giant reversed his grip and swung up a steel mace, bringing it down hard enough to shatter a table into splinters.

That steel mace was pitch black throughout, its momentum fierce and heavy—if it connected even once, bones would shatter and flesh would pulp. After dodging the first swing, the mace swept horizontally without pause, and as Ning Yi lunged backward his outer robe tore with a ripping sound, his stomach burning with what might have been psychological or might have been actual injury. He didn't dare wait for a third swing. His legs explosively pushed off and he lunged forward, throwing everything he had into locking his arms around the man's neck and right side.

When Ning Yi's internal energy erupted in bursts, the force was remarkable. Lu Hongti had once told him this kind of explosive power harmed the body—not having trained from childhood, he could never fully adapt to this method of force generation—but in critical moments it was truly domineering. The man seemed unprepared for a scholar to possess such audacity and strength. For that instant, his neck and right arm felt like they'd been clamped in iron rings. He ground his teeth but couldn't break free, naturally unable to bring down the steel mace in his right hand as the two crashed to the ground in a tumbling heap.

This entire sequence unfolded in mere moments—when that scarred man charged, the two fiercely engaged, and then they crashed rolling to the floor, the surrounding smoke still swirling and now churned into chaos by their struggle. The moment the scarred man hit the ground he rolled hard, his left hand clawing toward Ning Yi, but Ning Yi had already released his grip and rolled with the motion, desperately grabbing the scarred giant's mace-wielding right arm while his legs scissored toward the man's shoulder.

After arriving here, Ning Yi had been interested in martial arts, and once he had internal energy he'd learned a few sets of forms. Lu Hongti had taught him the practical applications of these techniques back then. But in this half-year and more he'd never experienced actual combat, had never formed the conditioned reflexes for these routines, and without those reflexes the martial arts were useless. Now facing the enemy, his mind was almost completely blank. From the first exchange he'd known he couldn't beat these people in orthodox martial arts, so even risking his life, he'd fallen back on his old methods.

When your opponent had a weapon, running meant almost certain death—you could only charge in.

This man's strength was fierce and powerful; on a battlefield he might make a fine general, but he certainly wasn't a martial arts master like Lu Hongti. Against Lu Hongti, Ning Yi would never get a chance to close in and engage, and even if he managed to grab her, she'd simply use technique to slip free. But this warrior relied purely on courage and raw strength. Though his fighting experience was rich, in past brawls his power had always let him fling or beat down opponents with casual ease. In these few moments as they rolled across the ground multiple times, the notion of a scholar clinging to his body and constantly attempting lethal joint locks was something he'd never encountered before.

In modern combat systems, with scientific methods supporting their development, understanding of the body's vital points had reached an extreme level. Close-range submission grappling could be lethal at any moment, and various joint manipulation methods—whether traditional Chinese grappling arts or other combat systems—all targeted these vulnerabilities. Though Ning Yi was far from reaching the level of a grappling master, in this life-or-death struggle his mind stayed clear. He knew that the moment he let go meant certain death, so combined with the force his internal energy could explosively generate, through several position changes—grabbing the man's neck at one moment, his limbs the next—he applied joint lock techniques, rolling right then suddenly reversing left, trying to snap the man's arms or legs.

Perhaps only those who'd truly practiced these joint techniques could understand how fragile the human body really was. But the giant's grasp of life-and-death situations was equally acute. Though he was thrown into embarrassing disarray for the moment, his powerful counterattacks still managed to force Ning Yi away at the crucial instant—only for Ning Yi to lunge back onto him the moment he created distance. They rolled across the ground for barely ten seconds, but every instant balanced on the razor edge of life and death. The furniture around them suffered, smashed to fragments by the scarred man's wild swings. Ning Yi's leg took a punch from the man's left hand, but at every moment Ning Yi also risked his life trying to wrench the man's limbs to breaking. Now locking the man's right arm again and trying to snap it with maximum force, the giant's left hand clawed toward Ning Yi—when suddenly a white figure rushed in.

It was Yunzhu, whom Ning Yi had pushed aside at the start of the fight. Though her body had trembled as she crouched nearby watching for those few moments, she'd gritted her teeth, pulled the hairpin from her head and lunged forward, stabbing it hard into the man's lower abdomen.

The giant roared "Ah!" and kicked Yunzhu away. The kick caught her shoulder at an awkward angle and though he hadn't used full force, Yunzhu still rolled away from the impact. Ning Yi had no idea where she was injured, but clenching his teeth he explosively wrenched the man's forearm with a crack, snapping it as the steel mace clattered to the floor.

The searing pain of a broken arm and stabbed abdomen made the giant's eyes bulge. His body rolled again, but this time the scholar didn't follow. As the man scrambled to his feet and raised his head, Ning Yi had already gripped that dozens-of-pounds steel mace and charged straight at him, the weapon swung back to its furthest position.

"Go die—"

With a bang, the steel mace's ridges smashed into the scarred man's left temple. Half his head exploded. He'd reached the wall by the riverside, and Ning Yi's full-powered strike nearly lifted his body off the ground, sending him crashing through the main riverside window, his upper body dangling outside before falling back in.

On the balcony platform, Zhou Pei watched Ning Yi deliver that swing and the opponent's head explode. Her mind was a chaotic blank. She couldn't think, couldn't understand. All she could do was tremble—her lips, her body, everything.

Ning Yi turned to check on Yunzhu, seeing her clutching her shoulder as she sat up and forced a smile for him. Across the hall, Jin'er screamed "Ah—!" and ran toward them.

At the main door, no one could tell how many people had crashed together in a chaotic brawl. The most powerfully built giant was cutting through the melee, constantly pressing closer to Song Qian and the protected Qin Siyuan amid the confusion. This man was clearly the most formidable of the five, and though the dozens who'd blocked the street entrance all carried weapons, they could barely slow him down—especially with Song Qian pulled into the fight. Though Song Qian's martial skills were decent, he was protecting Qin Siyuan while being blocked by others, unable to break through for the moment.

The relatively tall, lean man had also killed his way into the confused melee, his dual blades flashing as he cut down several of the street thugs in moments. Glancing back this way, he saw his companion's skull crushed and Ning Yi gripping that steel mace. For an instant he seemed ready to charge back, but as Ning Yi met his gaze with equally fierce eyes, he hesitated. In that moment, Ning Yi suddenly swung the mace again, bringing it down once more on the scarred man's head. The wall was wooden and though fairly sturdy, having been struck once already, this second impact actually cracked it open as the scarred man's head was beaten beyond recognition.

"Come on!"

Ning Yi threw down the mace and shouted toward that direction, simultaneously pulling out the remaining fire lance with one hand while his other hand produced his tinder box. At the same moment, a blade's wind swept in as the noble young master at the very back turned and charged at him.

"Kill Old Dog Qin—"
