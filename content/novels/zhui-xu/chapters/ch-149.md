The wheels turned rhythmically as the carriage rolled silently through street after street, sometimes passing areas where voices and lamplight spilled from nearby buildings, other times traversing dark alleys where silence enveloped everything around them. Manager Xi sat in the carriage, occasionally frowning as he glanced at the silent Guard Geng sitting across from him.

"At this hour... what exactly are we going to do?"

He had already asked similar questions several times in roundabout ways, but each response had been essentially the same.

"Manager Xi will understand when the time comes."

Originally, he had been contemplating what methods Su Tan'er might have to turn defeat into victory on this night, but gradually he began to feel that such a thing was unlikely. The imperial merchant affair had already shown signs of trouble four months ago, with everything interconnected leading up to now. Tonight's clan assembly would inevitably see the second and third branches erupting against Su Tan'er—this was already a foregone conclusion. If this matter couldn't be resolved, Su Tan'er would be stripped of her authority afterward, and all talk of the future would become mere bubbles. What could possibly be done at this point?

He despised this kind of situation where he couldn't see the overall picture clearly, especially since Su Tan'er and the others were essentially students who had emerged from under his guidance, yet in circumstances like these, he found himself completely unable to fathom her intentions. However, regarding the degree to which he was trusted, he ultimately still had confidence—he would simply wait and see what she planned to do...

While sitting in the carriage, he calculated their current position based on the vehicle's movement, occasionally peering through the curtain to observe distinctive features outside. The carriage seemed to be heading toward the outskirts of the city, and this vehicle was somewhat strange—it wasn't one of the Su family's carriages. Along the way, the carriage had taken several detours, perhaps out of concern about being followed. Manager Xi's mind grew increasingly puzzled: he was crystal clear about all the adversaries the Su family faced this time, so who exactly was involved, and what matter required such precautions?

The carriage left Jiangning City and finally stopped in front of a courtyard on the outskirts, where Manager Xi surveyed the surrounding environment. This area was relatively secluded, but not far away was a small place that was usually quite busy during the day and relatively chaotic¹, called Ten Steps Ridge². There were several shops and fish stalls there, where people from nearby villages would come to buy things, and occasionally there would be brawls, murders, and territorial disputes.

Manager Xi walked through the courtyard gate.

The next moment, he stood there as certain thoughts that were difficult to believe surged up in his mind, and he began to understand some things.

A sharp blade pressed against his waist as figures began to emerge from beside the door.

"Brother Geng, what exactly... is happening?"

"Let's go inside first, Manager Xi. We'll wait here for now. Someone will come to explain what you want to know. If this turns out to be a mistake, I'll apologize to you properly then."

*****************

At Moon Fragrance Tower³, the qin music was clear and elegant, the singing voices soft and beautiful. Luo Miaomiao plucked the strings, singing leisurely under everyone's gaze. Xue Yan, Xue Jin and the others joined in the singing, immersed in the performance. After the piece ended, they smiled and raised their wine cups to offer praise.

They had been waiting here for the Su family's results today for quite a long time already, drinking and making merry during the wait. With Luo Miaomiao's company, it hadn't been boring at all. After a while, Xue Jin glanced toward the Su family's direction: "Speaking of which, the Su family should have reached a conclusion by now."

"It's a pity we couldn't go to the Su family in person to watch. I imagine those three branches of the Su family scheming against each other in private must be quite a spectacle," someone nearby said with a laugh.

"Today we have Miaomiao's company here, so we're just waiting for the results. You actually want to go watch those scheming affairs—that's truly like boiling a crane and burning a qin⁴, vulgar and intolerable. Where does that leave Miss Miaomiao? Penalty drink!"

Everyone laughed and joked around, inevitably lamenting how truly disunited the Su family's situation was, feeling fortunate that their Xue family didn't have this kind of struggle between branches over inheritance. During their conversation, someone lifted the curtain and entered—this person was a member of the Lu family who had been there from the beginning but had left earlier to handle some matters, only now returning. Xue Yan laughed: "Brother Lu, everyone has been waiting for you for so long. You've finally returned, but you don't know—you missed Miss Miaomiao's performance when you left earlier. What a regrettable loss..."

The Lu surname young man apologized with a few laughs, and after sitting down, he smiled and said: "I took a turn outside earlier and heard about some quite lively events. Oh, right, has there been any result from the Su family's side?"

"Nothing has come through yet. Is Brother Lu getting anxious? Haha, just as I said earlier, the Lu family has put in great effort this time. You were even a bit cold toward Miss Miaomiao just now—this deserves punishment."

"Heh, Brother Xue is joking. Who doesn't know that Brother Xue's family has prepared most thoroughly for this affair? Once the Su family starts having problems, it's Brother Xue's family business that will benefit most. Our Lu family is merely following behind to pick up some scraps and fish in troubled waters⁵. Brother Xue saying this is absolutely slander. Miss Miaomiao, don't believe him—he must be the one whose heart is focused on the Su family's results, so he's teasing others to deflect attention."

Luo Miaomiao glanced at them: "You people—not a single word you say is trustworthy. Miaomiao really doesn't know who to believe. I'm afraid I'd be sold by you and still help you count the money, and wouldn't even fetch a good price..." The woman laughed: "That Su family is truly pitiable, having become your opponents."

Several people laughed heartily, and Xue Yan shook his head: "Let's not discuss this, let's not discuss this. The Su family matter was already determined long ago—why worry about it? Today pleasure comes first, everything else is secondary. But Brother Lu mentioned earlier that there were some lively events—what exactly happened?"

"Oh, over at Changyun Pavilion, things got quite heated. I heard that Liu Qingdi engaged in a poetry battle against many scholars, heh heh, it almost came to physical blows."

Today Puyang Yi was hosting a banquet at Changyun Pavilion, with Liu Qingdi, Cao Guan and others all in attendance—it was considered one of the more important gatherings in Jiangning City that day. When those poets and lyricists got together, naturally Xue Yan and the others couldn't participate. Even if Xue Jin and others had some literary skills, they could only manage not to write doggerel poetry. During the earlier banquet, everyone had also discussed the poetry gathering over there, so hearing that the situation had become intense, Luo Miaomiao asked with concern: "Sister Qilan is all right, isn't she?"

"Heh heh, naturally nothing happened. It's just a figure of speech. With Puyang Yi there, it couldn't actually come to blows—both sides just got heated up. But..." He paused, glancing at Xue Yan and Xue Jin, "this matter involves that Ning Yi from the Su family."

Xue Jin was startled: "Impossible. How could Ning Yi be at Changyun Pavilion at this time?"

"He wasn't there in person, heh heh, but someone at Changyun Pavilion brought out a new poem by Ning Yi. This matter is quite interesting to tell. It started when Liu Qingdi got into a dispute with someone, then he used numerous poems to overwhelm the scholars there, and afterward Scholar Kongshan got angry..."

As he spoke about the situation at Changyun Pavilion, describing everything from Liu Qingdi's conflict with others to his use of various poems to suppress the other scholars, and finally to Scholar Kongshan's outburst, he also pulled out two sheets of paper from his chest, on which were copied some of the excellent works from this Changyun Pavilion poetry competition.

"...The last one is the new poem written by Ning Yi. I heard he's currently teaching at the Yushan Academy at home, and this was casually composed a few days ago while explaining poetry to a nine-year-old child. He didn't make any public announcement about it—it was just seen by Su Chonghua, who later told that Chen Lu, Chen Kongshan. This poem is actually called 'Settling Wind Waves'—truly an excellent piece. I'm afraid Ning Yi's literary reputation will rise another level after today... Though thinking about the Su family's current situation, it's rather ironic..."

Amidst the laughter and conversation, everyone passed around those poems. Today's competition at Changyun Pavilion was high-level, with every piece being excellent, but when they looked at the final poem, everyone's expressions became somewhat complex. After Luo Miaomiao received them, she read through each one slowly, her eyes quite bright with interest, but when she reached the last piece, she still hesitated for a long moment before reciting the verses aloud.

"Settling Wind Waves... Pay no heed to the sound of leaves rustling in the forest, why not chant and whistle as you walk leisurely? A bamboo staff and straw sandals are lighter than horses—who's afraid? A misty rain, a lifetime's journey... The chilly spring wind sobers me from wine, slightly cold, but the slanting sunlight from the mountaintop welcomes me still. Looking back at the desolate places I've passed through, returning... there is neither wind nor rain, nor clear skies..."

Among those present, quite a few had already read this piece. After Luo Miaomiao finished reciting it, the atmosphere became somewhat awkward for a moment. Xue Yan glanced around, then burst into laughter.

"Settling Wind Waves, Settling Wind Waves... Haha, this Ning Yi's talent in poetry is truly unquestionable, but with all his recent troubles, still writing about 'settling wind waves'—could it be that his heart is melancholy and he wants to console himself somehow?"

As he said this, the others also joined in laughing: "No wonder he only showed it to a nine-year-old child. He probably felt it was too much like self-deception, so he could only write it for a nine-year-old to see for comfort."

"I think it's not as good as that poem he wrote for Wu Chenghui that night in sorrowful indignation, 'Drinking Wine with Pei Di.' At least that one, even if it was plagiarized, wouldn't make people laugh, hahahaha..."

"We're all rough people who can't really judge the quality of poetry, but Miss Miaomiao's learning far exceeds ours. What does Miss Miaomiao think of this poem?"

Luo Miaomiao looked at everyone's expressions, then at the poems in her hand, and said with a soft laugh: "The composition is quite good." Her giving the poem a "quite good" evaluation made everyone laugh even more heartily. Luo Miaomiao casually glanced at those verses several more times before smiling and passing them to someone else, silently reciting them in her heart.

Afterward, there was another round of conversation and laughter, repeatedly discussing the Su family's efforts from two months ago and their ultimate spectacular failure, Ning Yi's pathetic composition of "Drinking Wine with Pei Di" in front of the Wu family members, and various events that followed. However, this atmosphere was undergoing imperceptible changes—sometimes when people discussed a few excellent pieces written by Liu Qingdi, holding those manuscripts to look at them, they inevitably let their gaze linger on "Settling Wind Waves" for a moment before turning away.

This suddenly appearing "Settling Wind Waves" was like a small thorn, invisibly lodged in this space.

However, no one voiced this observation, as it wasn't really a significant matter anyway. Once the results from the Su family's side arrived, this seemingly present yet absent small thorn would also dissipate into nothing. Xue Yan occasionally glanced downstairs casually, and at a certain moment, he finally smiled.

"The results are here."

A servant ran up from downstairs, and everyone had already begun laughing. Xue Yan's position by the window was right next to the door, so he opened the room door and walked out while everyone watched his expectant figure from the corners of their eyes.

"Come, drink, drink." Xue Jin pretended not to care much, calling out to everyone, and they responded with laughter, waiting for Xue Yan to come in and announce the news.

The Su family matter had long been a certainty—announcing it would only take a sentence or two. Even if there were some complications, there probably wasn't much to say about them. Everyone waited for Xue Yan to turn around with a smile and relay the results to them, but that servant spoke mysteriously in Xue Yan's ear for a very long time, and they waited like this for quite a while.

"What did you say..."

"How... is that possible..."

"You said who?"

After a long while, vague, fragmentary sounds drifted in, not very clear, but some people sitting relatively close to the door still heard Xue Yan questioning and repeating things out there. The people who had been laughing and toasting just moments before finally quieted down, exchanging puzzled looks, not knowing what unexpected situation or complication had occurred. However, it might have been some unexpected development with the Xue family, so participants like the Chen and Lu families weren't too worried yet. Finally, Xue Jin stood up, thought for a moment, then headed toward the door.

He intended to ask: "Brother, what happened?" However, these words didn't make it out of his mouth, as Xue Yan had already turned around. His expression was complex, his mind seemingly no longer present, and he just glanced at his younger brother before stepping inside. He looked at everyone in the entire room, opened his mouth as if to say something, but didn't speak. Under everyone's gaze, he walked all the way back to his seat, shook his head, and simply felt that some things were incomprehensible.

"Brother Xue, what's wrong?" the Lu family member asked.

"Heh." Xue Yan chuckled, and after a moment, said quietly, "The Su family's results are out."

"How did it go?"

"How did it go..." Xue Yan repeated, blinking his eyes. After a moment, he pressed his forehead hard and closed his eyes tightly. The Xue family had made the most arrangements regarding the Su family matter, so at this point, everyone somewhat realized that the results probably weren't as desired—or rather, very much not as desired. Xue Yan opened his eyes and forcefully swept away the dishes and chopsticks in front of him with one hand, then noticed the two poetry manuscripts beside him. He extended two fingers to tap them, then with a flourish pulled out the bottom sheet and held it before his eyes. After a while, he recited it aloud, as if reading it for everyone to hear.

"Heh... Pay no heed to the sound of leaves rustling in the forest... why not chant and whistle as you walk leisurely? A bamboo staff and straw sandals are lighter than horses—who's afraid... A misty rain, a lifetime's journey..." He recited the entire "Settling Wind Waves" at this point, and listening to it now, everyone heard it with a completely different attitude. Then he looked at everyone again. "Looking back at the desolate places I've passed through, ah... If I told you that we all guessed wrong, that everyone was calculated against, calculated completely and thoroughly, what would you say?"

No one answered.

"Four months..." Xue Yan gazed out the window, murmuring, "Heh, the Wu family was probably calculated against most miserably, and those incompetent second and third branches of the Su family too..."

"Brother Xue... specifically, what exactly happened?"

"Just like this." Xue Yan slapped the poetry manuscript on the table. "He's laughing at us. The result... is the best possible outcome for Su Tan'er... internal troubles and external threats all cleared up at once. That fabric... that fabric actually..." His emotions seemed somewhat out of control as he rubbed his forehead with his hand. "Now that I think about it... it's simply... calculating every ten steps..."

"...Ning Liheng."

This sigh, ending with that name, rang out in the hall, and everyone was stunned. But regarding the entire affair, they still weren't clear about the details. Xue Yan took a deep breath, finally raised his head, and smiled.

"My apologies, everyone. Four months of planning... no, more than two months of planning, all completely ruined. I lost my composure a bit—please bear with me. The Su family's results are already out. I'll tell you all about it, and then you'll understand..."

Time returned to not long before this moment, at night, in the Su family's ancestral council chamber.

A debate had finally reached its conclusion...

FOOTNOTES:
¹ *龙蛇混杂 (lóng shé hùn zá): Traditional Chinese idiom meaning "dragons and snakes mixed together"—referring to places where both respectable and disreputable people gather*

² *Ten Steps Ridge: A dangerous trading settlement outside the city walls where merchants, villagers, and unsavory characters conduct business. Violence over territory and goods is common in such suburban trading posts*

³ *Moon Fragrance Tower: An upscale entertainment venue offering music, poetry, dining, and courtesan services. Such establishments were important social hubs for wealthy merchants and scholars*

⁴ *煮鹤焚琴 (zhǔ hè fén qín): Classical idiom meaning "boiling a crane and burning a qin (musical instrument)"—destroying beautiful and refined things; being crude and philistine*

⁵ *浑水摸鱼 (hún shuǐ mō yú): Traditional idiom meaning "fishing in muddy waters"—taking advantage of chaotic situations for personal gain*
