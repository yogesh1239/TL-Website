As wavering lamplight cast shadows of amber and gold, time seemed to congeal atop Green Ripples Tower, where the collective weight of gazes and whispers pressed the air toward a predetermined outcome. Cutting through the mounting pressure, He Fang's voice finally rang out: "For our finale, let us have the manager of Su's Cloth Store tell everyone about the past year's business, and additionally..."

After his somewhat trivial remarks concluded, nearly everyone turned their attention toward the Su family's table. Su Zhongkan and Su Yunfang sat in quiet stillness with slightly furrowed brows, while Manager Liao beside them lowered his head in silence for a moment before revealing a smile and rising to his feet. He clasped his hands toward the surrounding crowd, preparing to step forward. Behind them, the maid called Xiao Chan hesitantly reached for the brocade box beneath her young master's right hand and pulled with some force.

But it didn't budge.

Ning Yi sat with his head slightly tilted, as if lost in thought. His gaze appeared calm and tranquil, though at this moment it carried a certain cold desolation, occasionally glancing toward the Wu family from the corner of his eye. His right hand remained absolutely motionless atop that brocade box.

Sensing Ning Yi's attitude, Manager Liao, who had been about to step forward, hesitated. He turned back awkwardly and reached for the box himself: "There's still a chance..." he said softly. Ning Yi smiled, then spoke coldly: "Let go."

"Young Master, there's still a chance..."

Their side fell quiet. Though the crowd couldn't hear the hushed exchange, they watched with sighs and cold smiles until Ning Yi's voice cut faintly through the hall.

"We... withdraw."

The declaration was the spark everyone had been waiting for, igniting a flurry of whispers and pointed fingers as the tense atmosphere finally broke. Manager Liao frowned and glanced around before suppressing his voice: "There's still a chance, Young Master, don't act rashly..."

He had been working under immense pressure for over a month on this matter, doing everything that should be done. These past few days, believing all human effort had been exhausted with little reason to fail, he had allowed himself the slightest optimism—making the shock all the greater when the Wu family produced that imperial yellow brocade.

Tonight's situation was complex, but as someone at the center of it all, he could already sketch out a dark outline: the timing of the Wu family's reveal, Lord Dong's arrangements and attitude—everything pressed down like a nightmare come to life. In truth, Manager Liao, who actually controlled the Su family's First Branch affairs tonight, perhaps felt the greatest pressure of all. Yet even under such circumstances, he had just managed to suppress all his emotions and pull Ning Yi back, still trying to make one final effort to at least complete what needed to be done. Acting impulsively with stubborn scholarly pride would change nothing now—when circumstances overwhelmed human capability, bullheaded resistance accomplished nothing except making others think the Su family lacked composure.

However, at this point, Ning Yi still shook his head and repeated: "We withdraw."

Manager Liao suppressed his anger and was about to speak again when He Fang at the front had already stood up with a frown: "Nephew Ning¹, today we've merely invited your Su family to participate in this gathering, to speak of your Su family's achievements and exchange views with the assembled gentlemen. The Jiangning Imperial Textile Bureau² conducts itself with complete propriety—we've never made anyone participate in any unseemly circles. When you speak here now of withdrawing repeatedly, dare I ask what exactly you're withdrawing from? Young man, you should think carefully before you speak."

As whispers of agreement and soft laughter at his loss of composure rippled through the crowd, Ning Yi slowly rose to his feet, his gaze locked on Wu Chenghou and his son, who smiled back at him. Manager Liao grew anxious, but before he could intervene, Ning Yi's voice cut through the hall, not with a hysterical scream, but with genuine anger: "You cannot do this... Shameless!"

"Ning Liheng, show some restraint!"

"Ning Liheng, show some restraint!" He Fang shot to his feet, but Dong Decheng, who had been observing with a slight smile, calmly patted his hand.

"No matter," Dong Decheng said smoothly. "Nephew Ning is young and hot-blooded. If anyone has an opinion about today's banquet or our Bureau, they should speak freely. This official never prevents anyone from speaking."

At the same moment, since Ning Yi had directed his words at Wu Chenghou, some merchants close to the Wu family had also stood up, prepared to help continue oppressing the Su family. Yet Wu Chenghou extended his hand: "Is Nephew Ning perhaps speaking of my Wu family?"

On this side, just as Dong Decheng finished speaking, Su Zhongkan, Su Yunfang, and Manager Liao's expressions had all shifted slightly, fearing Ning Yi might truly be foolish enough to drag the Imperial Textile Bureau into this. They were about to speak when they saw Ning Yi glance at Dong Decheng, then nod, take a deep breath, and smile. Throughout the entire evening, apart from his brief loss of composure when the Wu family produced that brocade and his angry outburst just now, he had maintained a quiet, somewhat dignified bearing even when others could see his distress. Now it seemed he had finally suppressed his anger as he looked toward the Wu family's side.

"Heh, very well... Didn't Uncle say you wanted this nephew to help compose a poem? Given today's events, this nephew suddenly thought of a poem most fitting. I'll write it out... Would Uncle like to see it?"

"Haha, excellent indeed." Wu Chenghou laughed, immediately responding. He glanced around the room. "My Wu family has been merchants for generations, truly somewhat crude and lacking in literary refinement. Nephew Ning is Jiangning's premier talent, known to all. That you're willing to write poetry for today is truly a grand occasion. Everyone, that we gather here atop Green Ripples Tower today and receive a poem from Jiangning's foremost talent is truly a magnificent event. Come, come, quickly present paper and brush to our worthy nephew..."

Some people stood up laughing while others harbored sighs in their hearts—at this moment, no matter what he wrote, it would only invite mockery. Though Ning Yi was a great talent, what use was that in such circumstances? Even if his poetry was exquisite now, when others spoke of it in the future, they would only say Ning Yi had lost face in business dealings. And even if the poem cursed the Wu family viciously, others would merely think merchant families were naturally thus, only building the Wu family's reputation while losing his own dignity.

With the words already spoken, there was no taking them back. As two servants presented paper and brush, Ning Yi simply stood gazing at the Wu family, ignoring the offering. A crowd of merchants gathered, the room growing quiet save for the whispers from outside and the faint fragrance wafting up from the tavern below. After a long moment, with the Wu family watching him with confident smiles, Ning Yi finally turned, picked up the brush, and held it suspended over the paper before the tip finally descended.

Someone leaned in, looking carefully, then read out the first line with slight confusion.

"Pouring wine for Pei Di..."

The words spread outward as people glanced at each other.

"Is there someone named Pei Di here today?"

"Old Mo Si, you're truly uncultured..."

"What?"

"This is an ancient poem..."

Voices grew chaotic as some became puzzled—though all present were merchants, many still had some learning. "Pouring Wine for Pei Di"³ was clearly a poem by Tang dynasty poet Wang Wei. Was Ning Yi now merely going to copy it? However, given Ning Yi's usual eccentric style, it was hard to say he wouldn't deliberately use this title but write something new. But the next line dispelled this speculation.

"Pouring wine for you, that you may ease your mind..."

Ning Yi wrote quite quickly at this point, his calligraphy neither particularly good nor bad but somewhat hasty—perhaps demonstrating the indignation in his heart. When the poem was finished, the rice paper bore only cursive script that couldn't be called a masterpiece:

Pouring wine for you, that you may ease your mind—  
Human feelings overturn like waves, I find.  
White-haired acquaintances still grasp their swords;  
Those first through vermillion gates mock promoted lords.  
The grass is soaked through by the gentle rain;  
Flower branches would sway, but spring winds bring cold pain.  
Worldly affairs are but floating clouds—why inquire?  
Better to lie at ease and dine as you desire.³

Not moving once, not changing a word, Ning Yi finished writing and looked down with brush in hand: "Wang Mojie's pearls precede me, so I won't write randomly. This poem shall be gifted to Uncle Wu—how about it?"

Wu Chenghou looked at the poem, then at Ning Yi. His smile remained utterly unchanged as he spoke with calm indifference: "This poem is quite good. Though it speaks words that common people might find unpleasant to hear, it perfectly suits the way of commerce. Nephew, I have no interest in pursuing the cause of your anger today, but this poem—I accept it. Hereafter I shall preserve it well."

Ning Yi also smiled, exhaled, and set down the brush. Then he turned around and spoke in a low voice: "Let's go." Grabbing the brocade box from the table, he casually flung it out the window. Though he seemed to use little force, the box flew straight through the window as the lid burst open in midair with a 'pop,' and a flash of imperial yellow⁴ swept past the corners of everyone's eyes, falling toward the floor below.

Xiao Chan let out a soft "Ah" and hurried downstairs. Ning Yi hadn't yet reached the stairway when Wu Qilong walked over smiling, patting his shoulder and speaking in a low voice: "Brother Ning, you have extraordinary talent. Why involve yourself in the unfamiliar world of commerce? Wouldn't you be better off at home, writing poems and teaching? Heh."

Ning Yi glanced at him with a smile but didn't respond, continuing downstairs.

The voices of discussion behind him began to grow louder...

Such an interlude had disrupted the gathering's proceedings. Though the several lords hadn't intervened, the established program still had to continue. The Su family might disregard Imperial Merchant status, but what needed to be said still had to be said. Everyone returned to their seats as discussions continued unabated. During this time, two children dressed as maids and servants also angrily stamped their feet and ran downstairs, though such matters drew no attention. Wu Chenghou had someone properly collect the "Pouring Wine for Pei Di" that Ning Yi had written, exchanging polite conversation with some people around him.

The Wu family's actions had always been unhurried, but this time the matter displayed a sharp decisiveness that struck like thunder from clear skies. From the yellow cloth Ning Yi had thrown down, most people roughly guessed what had happened, but under such circumstances, even the Su family couldn't prove what to write and thus couldn't speak—others would only think the Wu family truly formidable. This affair was indeed too formidable: the Su family had laid groundwork for years and now painstakingly prepared for a month, only for the Wu family to overturn everything with a casual move.

From today onward, the Su family would gradually withdraw from Jiangning's three-way textile industry balance, with the Wu family truly growing strong while the Xue family could no longer compete, relegated permanently to second place. People discussed this turning point with excitement while beginning to reconsider the Su family's position and their relationships with them. As for Ning Yi, he was merely a pitiful figure—simply stuffed into the middle, powerless from the start.

When someone looked down from above, they saw the scholar's blue-robed figure standing below, looking back up at this building—probably trying to remember it and utter some pitiful harsh words. All of this was merely the desolate afterimage of the defeated, with only the maid Xiao Chan by his side. The people above glanced a few times before turning back to chat and laugh with others...

Next, they would need to adapt to a new structure—for those in the cloth trade, it was more like adapting to a new era. As for the losers, they would only exist in after-dinner conversation; during serious times, even a single glance was wasteful.

Thus, the atmosphere upstairs continued to heat up.

"The crab here today seemed quite good, didn't get to eat any... what a pity."

Downstairs, Ning Yi stood by the roadside gazing at the Green Ripples Tower's sign, sighing with some regret.

"Then..." Xiao Chan frowned, looking troubled. "Should Xiao Chan go ask for some to take home?"

"Your head needs packing..." Ning Yi laughed, then patted Xiao Chan's shoulder. "Let's go, time to head back. Busy for over a month, now I'm free as a bird..."

The night breeze stirred as master and servant walked toward where the carriage was approaching. Behind them, Zhou Pei and Zhou Junwu followed.

A rare cool, relaxed evening...

**Footnotes:**

¹ **Uncle/Nephew (世伯/贤侄)**: In Chinese business culture, these terms establish a hierarchical relationship between merchant families. The elder generation addresses juniors as "worthy nephew" (贤侄) while juniors address elders as "generational uncle" (世伯), regardless of actual blood relations. This creates a familial framework for business relationships.

² **Jiangning Imperial Textile Bureau (江宁织造局)**: The imperial textile manufacturing office in Jiangning. During the Qing Dynasty, this institution held enormous economic and political power, controlling silk production for the imperial court. In this story, it oversees the merchant guilds and can grant Imperial Merchant status.

³ **Wang Wei's "Pouring Wine for Pei Di"**: This Tang dynasty poem speaks of betrayal by friends and the fickleness of human relationships. The key lines "White-haired acquaintances still grasp their swords / Those first through vermillion gates mock promoted lords" describe how even old friends remain wary of each other (keeping hands on sword hilts) and how those who achieve success first mock those still struggling. Ning Yi uses this poem to condemn the Wu family's betrayal.

⁴ **Imperial Yellow (明黄)**: The color reserved exclusively for the emperor and imperial family. For a merchant to possess imperial yellow fabric implies they have connections to produce textiles for the palace—a tremendous commercial and political advantage that essentially guarantees Imperial Merchant status.
