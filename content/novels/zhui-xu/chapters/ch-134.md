Above the flowing riverbed wrapped in autumn's embrace, their kiss was soft and quiet—as their lips simply touched, Ning Yi froze momentarily while the woman before him trembled, her eyelashes fluttering like butterfly wings. A heartbeat later, she stepped back, clutching her blanket with a crimson flush on her lowered face, though she quickly raised her gaze to meet his.

"Yunzhu... Yunzhu has nothing else to offer," she said, smiling earnestly before lowering her head once more. "I can only play a few tunes and sing some songs. Aside from that... there's only this."

"These past few days, hearing about what happened to you, Liheng, I've been so anxious not knowing what to do, yet you never came by. Today, seeing you're alright, I'm truly happy... but I also know that even with your open-minded nature, encountering something like this must surely leave you somewhat unhappy. If... um..."

"What you're doing is very dangerous..."

Ning Yi sighed softly and reached up to touch her left cheek, causing Nie Yunzhu to instinctively shrink back, her eyes darting about uncertainly before she tilted her head slightly with timid hesitation, leaning into his palm to feel the gentle caress. Ning Yi also tilted his head slightly, and after a moment, laughed with some complexity.

"Heh, these past few days at home have indeed been quite annoying," he mused, a complex laugh escaping him. "Everyone chattering and making noise, the Su family's resentment about to boil over... Mm, heh, seems I'm quite pitiful after all. Ruined the business, made a huge fool of myself, got played by someone, and now everyone thinks I'm an idiot..." He trailed off, shaking his head with an amused "Heh..."

Nie Yunzhu stood there holding her blanket, her cheek pressed against his palm, feeling its warmth. Though she hadn't dared to look up at first, she sensed something odd and glanced upward just as his figure drew closer—in the blink of an eye, her lips were sealed again.

"Mmm..." Her body retreated a step until her back pressed directly against the wooden wall as Ning Yi's form pressed close in the sunlight, nearly molding against her despite the thin blanket between them—though she didn't find it unpleasant as one hand slid along her back to encircle her waist. Through her vision she could see leaves rustling in the breeze, golden sunlight dancing between them, making her feel dizzy and intoxicated.

When she regained some clarity, she found herself nearly lying on the terrace floor with her back against the wall preventing her from falling completely. Ning Yi crouched beside her, holding her close, having just pulled their joined lips slightly apart to gaze at her with that same strange smile tinged with acceptance. Though Nie Yunzhu naturally couldn't analyze these nuances at the moment, not with their bodies pressed so intimately together. Her lips moved as she tried to regain some composure, but naturally failed.

When she'd impulsively kissed him earlier, she'd tried to consider the consequences, just not expecting certain things would progress so quickly. She'd never experienced such things before, but if he liked this, then...

"Yunzhu's body has never been touched by another man, but... if Liheng wants it, I would like that..."¹

Her face burned crimson as her words came out soft as a mosquito's buzz, though at such close proximity, Ning Yi naturally heard every word clearly. He simply gazed at Nie Yunzhu's expression, that smile unchanged on his face, when a soft sound came from the terrace entrance. Both Ning Yi and Nie Yunzhu turned their heads.

Standing at the terrace doorway was Yuan Jin'er in her green dress, having apparently just returned home and rushed over excitedly upon hearing voices. She froze just two steps past the threshold, her right index finger still lightly between her teeth, a smile frozen on her face. As the three of them stared at each other, Yuan Jin'er's eyes rolled frantically, her face cycling between red and white before she suddenly spun to flee.

After running in the wrong direction, she whirled back around only to trip over the threshold, falling through the doorway with a tremendous crash so painful that even Ning Yi's eye twitched—especially since she'd been biting her finger. Ignoring the embroidered slipper that had flown off her foot, she scrambled away on all fours, leaving her feet dangling on the other side of the threshold.

On this side, Ning Yi and Nie Yunzhu had naturally lost their previous atmosphere. Nie Yunzhu's gaze wandered everywhere before meeting Ning Yi's eyes and immediately dropping to the floor, then shifting left to empty space. When Ning Yi released her, she still clutched her blanket with her back against the wall and legs drawn up.

"I-I... I'll go check on Jin'er..."

She whispered this and glanced at Ning Yi before scrambling up to chase after the other woman.

"Heh..."

Ning Yi continued laughing as he sat back against the wall in his previous position, tilting his head up to gaze at the dappled sunlight filtering through the rustling leaves. Near the guqin not far away, the smile on his face deepened further—it was the smile of someone who'd sensed something, a genuinely happy expression...

He naturally understood the reason for Nie Yunzhu's emotional shift today, and had been happy about it. In this world, there were always people who genuinely cared about you, and regardless of whether you needed it, this was always cause for joy. He didn't need to hide everything from Nie Yunzhu, but since they hadn't discussed it earlier, there was no need to explain everything that had happened these past days. He hadn't expected she would go to such lengths.

This made things both simpler... and more complicated.

From the main hall came the sounds of arguing as Yuan Jin'er, apparently quite upset, cried and wailed—or at least made noises to that effect.

"Sister Yunzhu, how could you do this... in broad daylight, on the terrace, wanting to... wanting to... And even if you were going to do that on the terrace where no one could see, you shouldn't... you shouldn't use the blanket I sleep with!"

"Ning Liheng is a huge pervert!"

Yuan Jin'er's shout was followed by a hard kick to the wall, which she'd gauged perfectly to transmit the vibration like a sharp blow to Ning Yi's back. Shifting away from the wall, Ning Yi's laughter grew uncontrollably until he was pounding the terrace floor with his fist in mirth.

Yuan Jin'er was full of righteous fury while Ning Yi was completely shameless. Between them, perhaps only Nie Yunzhu suffered the most embarrassment—caught in the middle and transformed in moments from an ethereal, qin-playing celestial beauty to a timid young wife who couldn't cook and was being scolded by her mother-in-law. Ning Yi watched her approach with a smile and patted the spot beside him. Nie Yunzhu walked over, somewhat bashfully bending her legs to sit down while tugging her skirt hem to cover her ankles and socks.

"Um, about what we just discussed, does it still count?"

Ning Yi took her hand and asked with a smile. With Yuan Jin'er's interruption, the atmosphere had naturally dissipated, but certain things that needed to be clarified couldn't be avoided any longer, and relationships that needed defining had to be addressed. Of course, starting with such words made Nie Yunzhu flush with renewed embarrassment: "Jin'er... Jin'er is home..."

Ning Yi laughed again as golden powder drifted through the air while two figures sat talking on the terrace. Nie Yunzhu alternated between shyness, seriousness, and surprise, but ultimately, their clasped hands never separated...

***********************

By the time he left the small building and set out on his return journey, it was already afternoon. Thinking about what had transpired—the confessions and all that followed—Ning Yi sighed softly: "This damned old society..."² If this had happened over a year ago when he'd first developed such a relationship with Nie Yunzhu, he might have chosen to find another place to live with her, but now at the Su household, there wasn't just Su Tan'er but also Xiao Chan. On Nie Yunzhu's side, she'd never thought to make things difficult for him, which perhaps made it all the more troubling.

Of course, thinking about it this way seemed like a man taking advantage then playing the victim with his complaints...

Passing by Old Qin's residence, he prepared to drop in for a visit when he saw Lu Agui standing outside and learned that Kang Xian was also present today.

Entering the house, he found the Zhou siblings there as well. Seeing Ning Yi, little Junwu ran over to lodge his complaints: "Teacher, my sister and I went looking for you this morning, but where did you go?"

"Uh, I had some matters this morning..." Ning Yi patted his head while Kang Xian, having just finished a game with Old Qin, exchanged pleasantries and invited him to play. Zhou Junwu dragged over a small stool to sit nearby while Zhou Pei followed somewhat silently, occasionally glancing at Ning Yi's expression. As Ning Yi chatted casually with Old Qin and Kang Xian, he frowned while making his moves until after a while, Kang Xian spoke: "The city gates will open in the next few days, so we should handle the ceremony for these two children's formal apprenticeship.³ What do you think?"

Ning Yi looked at Zhou Junwu, then at Zhou Pei, and smiled: "You still want me to teach after all this? Won't you be very disappointed in me?"

"Victory and defeat are common in military affairs—the Imperial Son-in-law Grandfather⁴ said this isn't even what Teacher excels at, so losing just means they were too despicable. I still really like things like the telescope..."

Zhou Pei remained silent for a moment: "I'm learning accounting and calculation methods from you, not business..."

"What do you think?" Kang Xian laughed while Old Qin pulled little Junwu's hand beside them: "Two good children."

"Since that's the case, of course I'll teach them, though let's hold off on the formal ceremony for now—it's a bit too ostentatious."

Kang Xian considered this and placed his stone while they chatted about other matters before asking: "Something troubling you lately?"

"Mm." Ning Yi held a game piece and nodded.

"Actually, I've been waiting these past days for you to come ask for help, but you never came..."

Ning Yi glanced at him: "Heh, Lord Kang's righteousness..." He hadn't thought of this and laughed. Kang Xian, however, remained serious.

"Those who achieve great things need not excel at everything. I know your temperament—you don't like owing favors⁵ easily, so I didn't interfere before. But at this point, it's just a matter that could be resolved with minimal effort. What's so difficult about asking? Does our friendship make you feel you can't even owe me this small favor?"

As he spoke these words, Ning Yi looked around the room and also grew more serious before finally nodding: "Alright then..."

In the grand scope of Jiangning city, this was perhaps just a small corner where idle people gathered—a pebble thrown into a pond creating tiny ripples before settling back into calm waters amid wind and rain. Not long after, the city gates opened and Li Pin left Jiangning for the Eastern Capital to seek office, even taking time to console Ning Yi about the Wu family matter before departing. Yushan Academy resumed classes with some students abandoning Ning Yi's courses while Su Zhongkan seemed intent on stirring up minor troubles, hoping to have other teachers criticize and ostracize him, though given Ning Yi's consistently self-contained style, this hadn't yet achieved much effect.

Everything developed according to expectations: the Wu family secured their imperial merchant status and busied themselves with preparations, while Su Tan'er's attempts to stabilize her family's situation seemed like an inevitable slide downward. She poured massive funds into markets originally targeted by the Wu family, a move most viewed as a woman's hysterical preparation to dump goods at low prices and disrupt the market—though since she hadn't implemented it yet, few were ready to strike her down.

Externally, the situation showed only minor decline thanks to Su Tan'er's efforts, with others likely waiting for her to truly step down or for everything to settle before abandoning the Su family, while internally, mounting pressure left even Su Boyong barely able to contain the crisis despite still being alive. As cousins originally close to the First Branch began gravitating toward the Second and Third Branches, the outside world gleefully discussed the Wu family's ruthless tactics, Ning Liheng's humiliation, and the future landscape of the cloth trade.

With another month passing without movement, Jiangning's cloth trade situation fermented and transformed while Ning Liheng was all but forgotten—no one understood or felt any need to understand his recent activities until certain things finally began revealing their nightmare-like portents.

It was late September, exactly one month after the Mid-Autumn Festival's annual cloth trade gathering, when everything that had seemed so clear suddenly turned bizarre without warning. Viewed from a thousand years in the future, it resembled a stock that, after steadily climbing to its natural peak where everyone believed it would remain, plummeted into collapse without explanation—only when people finally grasped what had happened could they see the darkness that had lurked beneath those past events all along, and the shadow that had loomed over everyone from the very beginning...

**Cultural Footnotes:**

¹ **Virginity and Female Virtue**: In traditional Chinese society, a woman's virginity before marriage was considered paramount to her virtue and value. Nie Yunzhu's confession here represents an enormous emotional commitment and sacrifice within the cultural context, as she's essentially offering her entire future and reputation.

² **"万恶的旧社会" (This Damned Old Society)**: A modern Chinese phrase typically used to criticize feudal practices like polygamy, arranged marriages, and patriarchal oppression. Ning Yi's use of this anachronistic phrase reveals his modern perspective on the historical period he now inhabits, where taking multiple wives/concubines was socially acceptable for men of means.

³ **拜师礼 (Formal Student-Teacher Ceremony)**: A traditional ceremony establishing the master-disciple relationship, involving ritual bowing, tea serving, and sometimes gifts. This formal recognition created lifelong bonds and obligations between teacher and student in Confucian society.

⁴ **驸马爷爷 (Imperial Son-in-law Grandfather)**: A respectful title for Kang Xian, who married into the imperial family. The title indicates both his special status and the children's formal relationship to him.

⁵ **人情 (Favor/Human Feelings)**: A complex concept in Chinese culture encompassing personal relationships, mutual obligations, and social debts. Accepting help creates an obligation to reciprocate, making many prefer to avoid owing such favors.
