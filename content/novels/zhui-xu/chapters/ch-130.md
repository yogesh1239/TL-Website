Amidst the glow of lanterns, boisterous voices merged into a vibrant cacophony as crowds gathered at Green Ripples Tower¹, though the evening's banquet still had some time before its official start. People came and went in steady streams as carriages arrived and discharged various figures connected to the weaving trade, while on the second floor, Ning Yi and the Su family contingent had already been present for some time, settling into their seats while handling an endless succession of merchants who came to pay their respects—those who favored the Su family, those who didn't, those with existing partnerships, those without—none of whom could afford to ignore the Su family's presence.

However, compared to Ning Yi, Su Zhongkan, and Su Yunfang as the Su family's representatives, it was perhaps Manager Liao and his associates who received the most attention tonight. This wasn't surprising given the circumstances—outsiders had originally speculated that either Su Tan'er would make an appearance or that the Su family's Old Master Su Yu himself might come. Had either of these two attended, they would naturally have been the ones with authority to make decisions regarding the Imperial Merchant position, yet neither the granddaughter nor grandfather had shown up, resulting in those genuinely concerned with the Imperial Merchant matter—the practical businesspeople who handled real affairs—focusing their attention on Manager Liao and the others who currently steered the operations of the Su family's main branch at a practical level.

As for Su Zhongkan and Su Yunfang, these two certainly couldn't interfere with Imperial Merchant matters, though if the Su family failed to secure the Imperial Merchant position tonight, their status would become entirely different. Many people were still speculating about these possibilities, and since they had long been considered magnates in Jiangning's weaving industry, the attention they received was naturally considerable. Meanwhile, Ning Yi, though he now held decision-making authority for the Su family's main branch, served merely as a symbol that the Su family's main house hadn't been sidelined by Manager Liao and the others, making any actual decision-making naturally impossible for him.

Therefore, those who approached Manager Liao and his associates for greetings and conversation were mostly the real power holders from various households—those concerned with the Imperial Merchant position or managers operating cloth merchants would come for pleasantries, and occasionally some Manufacturing Bureau officials whose relationships had already been secured would come by with smiles to hint that there would be no problems tonight.

Around Su Zhongkan and Su Yunfang gathered mainly the major figures from merchant families, people of similar status such as heads of medium-sized cloth merchants or even the current guild leader of cloth merchants. When Wu Chenghou, head of the Wu family, arrived, he first exchanged greetings with them and chatted about matters in the cloth trade.

As for Ning Yi, he continued exchanging pleasantries with all manner of people, and once everyone confirmed Su Tan'er wouldn't be attending, their attitudes toward Ning Yi became quite enthusiastic, though naturally the topics of conversation ranged far and wide with little connection to the cloth trade. Regardless, he stood at the center of this stage tonight, and families with less power than the Su family wouldn't be foolish enough to completely disregard Ning Yi. Among families of similar standing, some had learned not to care about such things. The Xue family and Su family were among the early arrivals, with their divisions relatively clear. Xue Sheng only exchanged simple greetings with Su Zhongkan without acknowledging Ning Yi, though Xue Yan came over with plenty of smiles and conversation, mentioning their recent encounter with Li Pin and insisting that when the city gates reopened and Li Pin headed to the capital, they must definitely invite him to the farewell banquet.

Xue Yan wasn't particularly familiar with Li Pin, having only had contact through incidents like the one at Yancui Tower, but after Ning Yi began managing the main branch, Xue Yan had invited Ning Yi to dinner twice, with quite pleasant atmospheres each time. When Xue Yan wanted to make gestures, he could certainly deliver them properly, so now he treated Li Pin as an old friend too. Before long the Wu family arrived, and Wu Chenghou along with his two sons each came to exchange words with Ning Yi, with Wu Qilong even teasing him about arriving late that morning: "I heard Manager Luo mention it today—it seems that though Liheng is usually so composed, when faced with today's matter, you're inevitably a bit nervous after all, haha..."

The Wu family, as guild leaders of the cloth trade, had always maintained fairly good relationships with all the various households. When tensions arose between the Xue and Su families, they often mediated and eased things. During this period, the two brothers Wu Qilong and Wu Qihao had met with Ning Yi quite often. At least in terms of attitude, calling them familiar friends would be no exaggeration. Ning Yi shook his head with a wry smile, somewhat helplessly: "I originally wanted to persist in doing these things properly until the very last day, but who knew I'd oversleep this morning... Um, you all know about this already... It hasn't spread everywhere again, has it..."

"Hehe." Wu Qilong lowered his voice, suppressing his laughter. "I'm afraid everyone already knows..."

"Tsk..." Ning Yi froze for a moment, then rolled his eyes, after which Wu Qihao also burst out laughing beside them.

"Brother Ning's affairs aren't afraid of being known. Now everyone in Jiangning knows Brother Ning may be muddled about small matters, but when it comes to big matters, you don't hesitate. Tonight's Imperial Merchant position... Ahem, to be honest, since we're competitors, I won't wish you luck or anything, haha..." Wu Qilong laughed magnanimously. "However, though Brother Ning's side is formidable, our Wu family also has our trump card, you know. When the time comes, regardless of success or failure, we must all be convinced of the outcome."

The two brothers' open-minded manner earned them considerable goodwill from those nearby. After exchanging a few more pleasantries, the brothers took their leave as Ning Yi watched their departing backs with a smile before turning to handle other "acquaintances."

As the appointed time approached, everyone gradually took their seats. The second floor of Green Ripples Tower was spacious, and merchants qualified to attend had basically all been assigned specific seating arrangements. The Su family contingent occupied one large round table, while other merchant families were each allocated their own round tables. Some merchants had brought fewer people but wouldn't be arranged to share tables, as this gathering would also determine the Imperial Merchant attribution—each household needed to maintain their own position.

The Su family, Xue family, and Wu family were positioned at three corners of the venue². As the hall quieted slightly with some people still arriving in succession, officials from the Manufacturing Bureau came around to exchange greetings and offer reminders about various matters.

The bidding for Imperial Merchant status actually didn't follow the typical fair tendering process for competition, mainly due to the Imperial Merchant's special nature over the years. The annual tribute cloth issue made everyone avoid it when possible, and if they held an open tender with no participants, that would obviously be quite embarrassing. Through decades of evolution, the Imperial Merchant duties had come to be determined through a form of "respectful offering"³—as if you had something good to offer the imperial household, and the imperial court would correspondingly grant you certain privileges, though of course this wouldn't be done so openly on the surface.

The cloth actually sent to the imperial palace could be quite profitable, and if the imperial family wanted it, timing really didn't matter—offerings could be made at any time. But each year at this time, the Manufacturing Bureau had to arrange and allocate the annual tribute cloth quotas, and if the few merchants who regularly undertook Imperial Merchant duties complained too much, they would often portion some out, designating families like the Su, Wu, or Xue families: "Here's a batch of duties you need to help share." No one dared refuse—refusing meant wearing small shoes later for certain—though of course the Manufacturing Bureau wouldn't go too far and always maintained some sense of proportion.

Thus the pattern of Manufacturing Bureau gatherings over the past decades had mostly been like this: each household would take turns showcasing whatever fine cloth they had, allowing everyone to appreciate and evaluate it while also counting as their achievements to offer to the imperial household. Though the burden quotas for annual tribute cloth had already been secretly determined for each household, the surface appearance remained elegant, like an achievement exchange and fine cloth appreciation event. This year maintained that surface appearance, but the underlying reality had already changed dramatically.

Everyone was actually well aware of this fact.

Others whispered among themselves, observing the situation with the Su, Xue, and Wu families, while Manager Liao and his associates were actually gathering intelligence about the prevailing winds from others' comments. As they took their seats, he smiled and said quietly to Ning Yi: "Looking at today's atmosphere, the Imperial Merchant position should be no problem—this past month's efforts haven't been in vain, as most people favor our Su family..."

He paused, then sighed: "It was ultimately Second Miss's foresight, having already begun preparations years ago. Though I vaguely sensed something in the past, I wasn't clear about how things were developing. When something happened to First Master, I truly thought the Su family would stumble badly... but small tricks ultimately can't compare to genuine accumulated strength. With that cloth in hand, we truly have confidence, and this month's maneuvering can genuinely be called an open strategy."

Ning Yi pursed his lips slightly and looked around: "Really no problems?"

"Nothing major." Manager Liao also glanced around the surroundings. "The Lü family recently has a new cloth that's good, quite good actually, but unfortunately not very suitable for imperial requirements—it's called Smoked Tea Silk, which I've already seen. The Xue family has always had their signature Purple Wash Cloth that's been beloved by wealthy households, commanding rather high prices, but they shouldn't have anything new coming out recently. Though the Wu family is number one in weaving with substantial strength, what they excel at most is weaving craftsmanship. With Master Luo's Divine Needle in their household, they always surpass others by a notch in cloth weaving techniques. However, before our Su family's Golden Dawn Brocade, even superior weaving techniques probably won't mean much..."

In preparing for the Su family's Imperial Merchant bid, Manager Liao and the others had done extensive homework. He now spoke knowledgeably before frowning slightly: "However, the true mastermind behind First Master's assassination hasn't been found yet. If this person was truly instigated by the Xue family, I fear they might have further moves..."

Manager Liao glanced toward the Xue family, then shook his head with a smile to reassure Ning Yi: "The possibility isn't high, and besides... we've done all we can. Since matters have developed to this point, just watch with peace of mind..."

Ning Yi nodded and said no more, then turned to signal Chan'er to place the brocade box she was carrying on the table before them. After another moment, an official came to speak with Manager Liao, who smiled and tilted his head toward Ning Yi: "Lord Dong and the others have arrived. Since our Su residence has the greatest momentum this time, Lord Dong wants to make this gathering impressive and has arranged for our Su family to serve as the finale⁴."

"Being the finale is good?"

"Each year the finest cloths always serve as the finale, and several are still continuously supplied to the imperial family..."

Within his words, Manager Liao was actually somewhat nervous himself, smiling as he shared this news with Ning Yi before spreading word to the several managers around them.

"The finale..." Ning Yi murmured the phrase, sitting there thinking for quite a while before shaking his head with a slight smile. "Tonight's matter is settled..." Due to his tone sounding somewhat like a question, Manager Liao beside him smiled: "Still unknown, son-in-law. This is quite hard to say."

At the same moment elsewhere in the venue, someone also glanced in this direction, toying with a jade thumb ring while whispers escaped their lips: "Tonight's matter... is settled?" The voice was too low, like a murmur, or perhaps a quiet inquiry directed at the thumb ring in hand, with a faint smile of leisurely composure at the corner of their mouth.

The formal banquet would still take some time, as celebration and revelry were only appropriate after sufficient boasting had been accomplished. What almost no one anticipated was that the events truly belonging to this night would occur completely and utterly in barely half an hour's time. The reversal would be so abrupt and dramatic, the reactions so flamboyant and intense, the darkness lurking beneath so profound along with the various twisted secrets interwoven within—when these layers were peeled back one by one afterward, this incident would become a deep scar repeatedly recounted in Jiangning's weaving industry and even the broader business world for months and years to come...

¹ **Green Ripples Tower (绿漪楼)**: A prestigious venue in Jiangning used for high-level merchant gatherings and guild meetings. The name suggests elegance with "green ripples," evoking images of refined water features common in upscale establishments.

² **Three-corner seating arrangement**: In Chinese business culture, seating arrangements carry deep significance. The three competing families positioned at three corners of the venue indicates their equal status as major contenders for the Imperial Merchant position, with none given precedence over the others.

³ **"Respectful offering" (敬献) system**: A euphemistic practice where merchants present their finest goods as "offerings" to the imperial household rather than engaging in commercial bidding. This maintains the fiction that serving the imperial family is an honor rather than a business transaction, reflecting the complex relationship between commerce and imperial dignity in traditional Chinese society.

⁴ **Finale position (压轴)**: In Chinese cultural tradition, the most important or prestigious position often comes last, opposite to Western customs where "first" typically indicates highest honor. Being given the finale position at this banquet signals the Su family's favored status and the officials' confidence in their presentation.
