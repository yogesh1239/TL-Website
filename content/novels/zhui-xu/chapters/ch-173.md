Blade light flashed forward as the young noble spun around and bellowed at his companions to go for Qin Siyuan first, even while continuing his own assault. Ning Yi gripped the fire lance in one hand and had already drawn his fire starter with the other, but he stumbled back several steps as the surging crowd prevented him from lighting the weapon. Among the people blocking the entrance, the tall lean fighter suddenly turned and charged toward Qin Siyuan, and within a few breaths the burly giant had also noticed what was happening—he snatched weapons from several thugs and carved out a path with savage swings, closing the distance to Song Qian until the two exchanged a fierce clash of blades.

The young noble had already reached the scarred fighter's corpse and glanced down at the pitiful body before looking toward Ning Yi, who couldn't break through to provide support. With a menacing snort, he raised his steel blade and prepared to charge, but just as Ning Yi was about to drop the fire lance, a woman's shout suddenly rang out beside him.

"Ahh—"

With a crash, two figures smashed through the already-broken window frame and plunged into the Qinhuai River in this barely-second-month of the lunar calendar.²

"Jin'er—"

It was Jin'er, who, after seeing Yunzhu injured, had come running over in tears to drag the young noble down with her into the freezing Qinhuai River. With the weather still bitterly cold and ice yet to melt in places, the water was something most young women could never endure. Alarmed, Ning Yi rushed to the edge to look down at the river's surface just as Yunzhu climbed to her feet.

"Jin'er will be fine, you go help Old Qin..."

In Yunzhu's heart, these men were so vicious that it would be best if Ning Yi could just avoid them entirely, but hopes aside, she could still distinguish what mattered most. She quickly got out these words and also went to the broken opening to look down, where the water's surface churned in chaos—the two figures had fallen in, but for the moment neither could be seen. Ning Yi watched for a few more seconds before finally gritting his teeth and charging toward the entrance. He'd only run a few steps when he heard Yunzhu call out "Jin'er!" but naturally he had no time to go back and check on Jin'er's situation.

From the moment this sudden violence had erupted to now, it had probably been only about a minute. The entrance was in complete chaos—amid the fighting and killing, some customers who had originally been trying to rush out were now running back inside instead. Ning Yi opened the fire starter and waved it a few times to make the flame brighter, but as he charged toward the other side, fleeing customers blocked him several times in succession, making it completely impossible to get through.

In the melee visible ahead, the burly giant had already exchanged several blows with Song Qian. The crowd made it hard to see clearly, but Song Qian, who was trying to protect Old Qin, was clearly at a disadvantage. Ning Yi shouted "Move aside! Move aside!" but it had no effect whatsoever. He raised the fire lance and aimed it toward the enemy, yelling "Make way!"

The tall lean fighter wielding twin blades heard Ning Yi's shout, and with blood painting his face into a vicious mask, he swung one blade toward Ning Yi. The crowd was too dense—Ning Yi squeezed to the side and the blade only ended up plunging into the arm of someone in front of him, triggering another wave of anguished screams.

The tall lean fighter wasn't worried about Ning Yi firing at this distance through such a crowd—the accuracy would be nowhere near sufficient, and there were far too many people in between. But he noticed that the young noble was no longer following Ning Yi and had no idea what had happened, so with eyes splitting in rage, he roared a challenge—"Come on!"—exactly mirroring Ning Yi's earlier provocation. He could see that his burly companion was about to kill Qin Siyuan, and as long as he held things here for just a little while longer, they would succeed.

Ning Yi extended his hand to light the fire lance, pointing the barrel first at the tall lean fighter, then at the burly giant, though people in the crowd kept bumping into the weapon. The fuse was still burning, and over there the tall lean fighter shifted to the side, calling out "Watch out for that bastard's fire lance!" The large man who was fighting Song Qian shot a cold glance in this direction before several more blade strikes sent Song Qian flying backward in retreat, the blade light exploding outward.

It all happened in the space of a single breath as the fuse burned shorter; a mocking smile appeared on the tall lean fighter's face as he watched Ning Yi struggle awkwardly through the crowd, but in the next moment, he saw Ning Yi hurl the fire lance with all his strength.

"Catch!"

"Look out—"

"Ahhh—"

The shouts blended together as the fire lance flew through the air above the crowd with its fuse still sparking. Leaping high, Song Qian slashed at the burly giant while simultaneously reaching to catch the weapon as it flew past the large man's back.

In the next instant, a hand caught the fire lance, and Song Qian was kicked away while still in midair.

The burly giant had suddenly spun around, kicking Song Qian away and flashing a vicious grin in Ning Yi's direction as he gripped the fire lance. With a sharp movement, he turned back and aimed it at Qin Siyuan, who now stood completely exposed only a few meters away.

In that instant, time froze.

Bang—

The gunshot rang out like a massive explosion, smoke and dust accompanied by red light, and then blood sprayed into the air as the burly giant's body swayed slightly backward.

But that wasn't from recoil.

In that moment, the fire lance exploded,³ sending bamboo fragments and the iron handle tearing across half of the burly giant's face with a tremendous backward force that took one of his eyes.

If it had been a normal firing, the explosion probably wouldn't have been this powerful, and in most cases the projectile would still have been propelled forward. But this explosion was different—the amount of gunpowder was nearly several times more than a normal shot, and the front had been stuffed and blocked as tightly as possible to direct the blast toward the rear.

Most of the fire lances Ning Yi had prepared to return to Kang Xian this time were ones that had been damaged from use. When loading them, he'd already considered that these weapons were difficult to reload, and against true experts their power was actually quite limited. If these really were bold and skilled assassins who planned to escape after striking in the city, he would probably only get one chance to fire. Like that young noble who'd charged at him—people like that wouldn't give him a chance to light the weapon a second time, so he'd only loaded one fire lance properly and simply turned another into a hand grenade just in case.

His original thinking had been that with their superior numbers, even if he took down one person with a shot, he and Song Qian would still be at a disadvantage against the remaining enemies. The fire lance on his back would be there—as long as he showed an opening to tempt them into trying to grab it, after the first shot he could disable one more person. This kind of scheme could only work once though, so there'd been no need for a third weapon. Though things had turned out somewhat differently from his plan, by deliberately acting like he was blocked and couldn't get through, he'd still achieved the desired effect.

Over there, heart-tearing screams mixed with an indescribable sense of shock and confusion. The tall lean fighter stood almost frozen in place for a moment. Too bad he didn't know Ning Yi and had never heard of his nickname "Ten Steps, One Scheme"—otherwise his feelings would certainly have been even more complex. When he looked back, he saw that Ning Yi, who'd been blocked just moments before, had now forcibly pushed aside several people and was charging this way, the figure calling out in a low voice: "You're already dead!"

The burly giant raised his hand to cover his face, and once he'd regained some clarity of mind, he seemed like he still wanted to rely on savage courage to charge toward Qin Siyuan, but the tall lean fighter grabbed him. "Go! Go!"

"Kill them! Kill them!"

The large man struggled a few times but finally realized the situation was hopeless, and together with his tall lean companion, he cut through the thugs blocking the exit and fled.

Ning Yi had never had much confidence in close combat against these kinds of experts to begin with—that scarred fighter earlier had been proof of that. If he hadn't had some foundation in joint locks and other self-defense techniques, and if he hadn't studied them together with Lu Hongti before, he'd probably already be dead. Seeing them leave now, he naturally didn't dare pursue them. There were still some thugs here who'd charged in from somewhere, and they'd been caught up in the attacks from the two large men and Song Qian, with most of them already down—but the remaining ones still seemed like they wanted to fight. At this point Song Qian had already climbed to his feet, and having roughly realized that these thugs came from a different background than those assassins, he now blocked the path in front of Qin Siyuan and pulled out a waist token from his clothing.

"The Prince Kang's Mansion is conducting business here! Who are you people, daring to act together with assassins! Do you want to rebel!"

At these words, the thugs also came to a stunned halt in their fighting.

With the situation somewhat stabilized, the surroundings revealed a scene of devastation. Looking back at the chaos throughout the main hall, fortunately what had seemed like extreme crowding earlier had mainly been due to all the tables, chairs, and furnishings taking up space—when some people had later hidden under tables, it hadn't resulted in any vicious trampling incidents. When Ning Yi spotted the white-dressed woman standing at the broken opening in the wall, he suddenly remembered that Jin'er and that young noble had fallen into the river. He quickly walked over to look, just in time to see water splashing as half of the young noble's body tried to surge up out of the river surface. The moment he emerged, he suddenly sank back down again.

The situation in the water was complete chaos. A few seconds later, the young noble's hands flailed up trying to grab onto something before sinking down again. This repeated several times, and he occasionally got his head above water wanting to call for help, but usually only got half the word out before river water poured into his mouth again. Jin'er's figure had never appeared, but after a while, fresh blood actually began welling up in the water.

Ning Yi watched for a bit before he could faintly make out a figure moving around the young noble's body like a mermaid—or rather, like a piranha. A moment later, Old Qin also walked over. The old man had seen his share of major events after all, and his composure had settled down. Standing beside Ning Yi, he watched for a few moments: "That is..."

"Should be Jin'er..."

As blood trailed downstream, the movement in the water gradually quieted until a woman suddenly emerged onto the riverside stone steps. Soaked through, her long hair hanging like water plants, it was Jin'er, dragging the young noble's barely-living body with one hand while a hairpin was clenched between her teeth. The man was still bleeding from his lower body.

While they'd been in the water, Jin'er hadn't just been pulling him under repeatedly—she'd also grabbed a hairpin and jabbed it viciously into his thighs and buttocks. With visibility so poor underwater, she might have missed her target a few times during all this—for instance, when aiming for the buttocks, she might have hit the front instead. Ning Yi had known before that Yuan Jin'er's swimming skills were exceptional, but only now did his heart run cold as he silently vowed to always stay far away from water whenever he argued with this woman in the future.

After soaking in water at this temperature for so long while completely drenched, she had to be freezing cold. Once she reached the shore, she hugged her body and sniffled, and then someone came over and draped a garment over her shoulders. She looked up to see that Ning Yi had taken off his torn robe and put it on her. For the moment she didn't refuse, and when Ning Yi put his arm around her shoulders, she even leaned into his embrace.

"Are you alright?"

"I'm fine..." She sniffled. "How's Sister Yunzhu?"

While they were talking, Yunzhu had also come out clutching her shoulder, and she took Jin'er from Ning Yi's arms. Ning Yi looked at the mark on her white clothing and asked with concern, "Are you alright?"

"It hurts a bit." Yunzhu smiled. "But I should be fine."

"I'll have a doctor come right away."

"Mm, I'll take Jin'er upstairs first, she needs to change clothes and take a bath."

Though Yunzhu's mind was in turmoil and she wanted to talk with Ning Yi, Jin'er obviously couldn't stay in this condition any longer, so she helped Jin'er leave. As for the chaos here, naturally Song Qian would handle those thugs. Ning Yi glanced at the young noble who was already half dead, then turned to Qin Siyuan and asked, "Old Qin, do you have any idea which faction these people are from?"

Old Qin was also looking at the man, and after thinking for a moment, his expression became somewhat complex as he smiled, though he also sighed. "It's been many years since I've encountered this kind of thing... These people... I'm afraid they're from Liao..."

Ning Yi nodded. Thinking about their northern-style dress, they did indeed seem like they'd come from the Liao side, though the fact that their actions today seemed to lack any formal planning or organization was rather strange.

Looking at Qin Siyuan again, Ning Yi reflected on how he'd once thought the old man led a leisurely life. Things had clearly taken a turn: not only was he being praised, criticized, and visited, but now even assassins from Liao were targeting him. This realization lent the rumors circulating among the common people a fair degree of credibility.

In recent years, the storms and conflicts between Jin and Liao—this old man who'd been living in quiet retirement in Jiangning for so many years had probably contributed his share of effort to them... Ning Yi felt a slight sense of wonder in his heart, but given Old Qin's attitude, unless something truly major happened, he probably still wouldn't speak openly about these matters. Ning Yi wasn't someone who gossiped, and though he found these events magnificent and interesting, he'd done similar things in the past after all. His immediate concerns were Yunzhu's injury and Jin'er's condition, so he turned around and instructed people to summon a doctor to examine them both.

This afternoon's events had caught everyone by chance. As for those several dozen thugs at the entrance, no one knew what they'd come to do—only after Ning Yi called for a doctor and then went to ask did he learn that they'd actually been called over to smash up Zhu's. Song Qian's position in the Prince's Mansion wasn't low to begin with, and having just fought through a life-and-death struggle, he still felt the lingering fear. But he also knew that this time he'd truly achieved great merit. He deeply admired Ning Yi for throwing over that fire lance, and seeing these thugs now, he was extremely displeased. With a cold snort he said, "Conspiring with assassins to commit violence in the streets—not only them, but including whoever's behind them, not a single one will escape this time."

He naturally knew the assassins had nothing to do with these people, but since they'd happened to run into this, they really were just unlucky.

Officers from the government bureau had already arrived by now, and before long, people from the Prince's Mansion and the Imperial Son-in-law's Mansion also rushed over, led by none other than Lu Agui. They took that barely-breathing young noble into custody, and also searched some items from his person, including trade permits.

"People from Liao." Lu Agui showed that permit to Ning Yi as well. "Never expected something like this would happen. Master Qin has already been in retirement for seven years—these people... truly going too far!"

FOOTNOTES:

¹ "White Strip in the Waves" (浪里白条, Langli Baitiao) is the legendary nickname of Zhang Shun, one of the 108 heroes from the classical novel Water Margin (水浒传), one of China's Four Great Classical Novels. In Chinese martial culture, such nicknames are iconic titles that define a warrior's heroic identity and domain of mastery—Zhang Shun's pale skin would flash white through water like a fish as he dominated aquatic combat. Yuan Jin'er earns this traditionally male warrior title for her feat of defeating an armed opponent in the freezing Qinhuai River, elevating her from entertainer to legendary martial figure and transcending gender boundaries in a culture where such heroic epithets represent the highest recognition of martial prowess.

² The second lunar month typically corresponds to late February through March in the solar calendar. In the Jiangnan region (modern-day Nanjing area), this period can still have freezing temperatures, making the river water life-threatening. This emphasizes the magnitude of Yuan Jin'er's sacrifice and her extraordinary toughness in enduring conditions that would incapacitate ordinary people.

³ A barrel explosion (炸膛) was a common danger with Song Dynasty fire lances—the weapon would explode instead of firing properly, potentially killing the operator. Ning Yi deliberately engineered this malfunction by overloading the gunpowder (several times the normal amount) and blocking the barrel front tightly to direct the explosion backward, essentially creating an improvised hand grenade. This reflects the pragmatic military strategy tradition rather than honorable combat conventions.
