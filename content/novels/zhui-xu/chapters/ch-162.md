Faint light slipped into the room from somewhere, quietly illuminating the outlines of various objects while dog barks echoed distantly in the darkness—though what hour it was remained uncertain.

The mosquito netting stirred slightly as a woman's bare arm emerged, fingers reaching toward the wooden mat at the bedside where various garments lay scattered in the darkness. After groping about for a moment, she found a piece of clothing and withdrew back behind the netting.

The movements were small and careful, clearly meant not to disturb the person beside her, producing barely any sound. A moment later, however, the hand emerged again, and the garment fell back onto the mat with what seemed like faint disappointment. In the dim light, one could make out a dudou—blue-green fabric decorated with red lotus and white lotus blossoms. As one of those intimate garments belonging to a woman's private world, it seemed to carry something of a young girl's purity and freshness, a touch of ancient grace.

But its ties were broken, knotted in the middle and impossible to wear properly. Perhaps that was why the hand had set it down again with such dejection. After another moment, the hand moved once more, found another piece of clothing, and quietly pulled it behind the netting.

Following a brief silence came the soft rustle of fabric, and then the netting parted to reveal a pair of pale, slender feet extending outward, touching the mat lightly with just their toes. As the netting opened further, one could see the woman had sat up, now draped in a loose robe held closed with just one hand while her long hair spilled down in disheveled, languid waves that she brushed aside with her fingers. She searched the mat for her embroidered shoes, and after finally locating them and slipping them on to stand, she frowned slightly, pressed a hand to her lower abdomen, and sat back down.

In the tranquil stillness, the woman pressed her lips together, then puffed out her cheeks slightly before finally standing up. Taking a deep breath, she turned and crouched down to gather the scattered garments, wearing nothing beneath the outer robe and shoes, her lower body occasionally visible as she moved. She didn't quite understand why she was collecting these clothes, but after gathering them all up, she placed them on the chair by the desk before turning to open her own wardrobe and feeling around inside for fresh undergarments and a dress.

With only the faint ambient light, finding her own clothing wasn't difficult given her familiar memory of where everything belonged, but after pulling the items out, she merely held them against her chest and looked back at the bed, apparently considering whether to actually put them on. In the end, she simply set them on the cabinet by the bedside, then turned to find the tinderbox and carefully blew it to life, lighting the oil lamp.

Shielding the light with her body as much as possible, she walked to the newly moved wardrobe on the other side and carefully selected a few garments, extinguished the lamp, and folded the masculine clothing atop her own dress and undergarments. Only after completing these tasks did she return to sit on the edge of the bed, remove her shoes, and slip back beneath the covers.

This was her embroidered bed, everything familiar, yet tonight a man had invaded her private world for the first time. But she didn't dislike it—rather, she quite liked it. She sat there looking at the dark outlines, lifted the covers to lie down again, then reconsidered and removed the robe wrapped around her body before sliding back in from one side of the bedding, still holding the robe until finally extending her arm through the netting to toss it onto the floor beside the bed.

Warmth enveloped her from the side. She wasn't wearing anything now. Then her leg brushed against her husband's body beneath the covers, and she shifted slightly closer. From childhood through the years of becoming a young woman, ever since understanding the concepts of chastity, modesty, and propriety between men and women, this was the first time she had lain completely naked beside a man, attempting to make this feel natural and proper.

It felt as though she belonged to someone now, and in his presence, all those former rules no longer applied...

She didn't really understand why she'd gotten up to do those things just now, nor why she'd removed her clothes again when returning to bed. She could have dressed, but somehow she didn't want her husband to know she'd woken in the night.

Turning on her side toward where her husband slept, she could only make out his outline in the darkness, though beneath the covers she could clearly feel the heat radiating from his body. Lowering her head, she carefully shifted closer until their bodies barely touched. In the next moment, he seemed to sense her presence, turned slightly, and drew her into his embrace.

Skin pressed against skin.

She curled up, not quite daring to move. No matter what, being naked like this still made her shy whenever her mind cleared, though perhaps he would hold her this way until morning... The clothes were right there—should she slip on the dudou later? Her mind buzzed with these thoughts while her body remained still... Perhaps she liked being held together like this... Drowsy notions drifted through her consciousness...

At last... she and her husband were truly married now, and it would be like this from now on...

With such thoughts gradually blurring, she drifted back to sleep in his arms...

When he woke, the sky had brightened slightly, and light rain pattered outside—sha sha sha sha.

The woman who was his wife slept in his arms, the usually sharp-edged young woman now docile as a child, her vibrant body soft and warm, comfortable to hold. Ning Yi had rarely experienced this feeling of holding someone and finding warmth on a psychological level—or more accurately, had never experienced it before.

In the past... in what now seemed like an incredibly distant previous life... he'd never lacked for women. In his youth he might have pursued this kind of pleasure and stimulation with something approaching madness, interested enough for a time—though that period had grown quite hazy—but even then he'd never felt this way. Perhaps he'd been too busy, and eventually it became merely about satisfying biological needs.

Some of his friends had been quite promiscuous, sleeping around with four, five, or even more women simultaneously and so forth. Very troublesome and boring. If he were to hold three or four naked women at once, he'd probably only feel how heavy they were. One was enough to satisfy biological needs; more than that was meaningless. Excessive sex only made you tired and even distracted from work. Though one of male nature's flaws was constantly boasting about prowess in this area, exhaustion was still exhaustion.

He'd hardly experienced anything like romance, except for occasionally recalling certain feelings from his youth, though the ledger was quite clear. Whether one-night stands or high-class call girls could provide everything you wanted for an evening, but genuinely pursuing a woman required enormous mental investment that would prove distracting and sometimes painful—investments that later, in business terms, involved costs measured in hundreds of millions, making it a rather poor business proposition with uncertain prospects. Eventually he'd stopped even seeking one-night stands because these still inevitably led to the other party hoping for further development. After encountering such situations a few times and witnessing those women's tears and tantrums, he'd chosen only those transactions where silver and goods were clearly exchanged.

Some said that desire for power or control often superseded sexual desire and similar emotions, because once material conditions were satisfied, the latter became too easy to fulfill. Perhaps there was truth to that—he was too lazy to think deeply about it—but he'd rarely felt there was much point in holding someone this way. Yet now, holding his wife truly did feel quite pleasant.

In this ancient, simple world, he could indeed forget much of what came before. That world existed more than a thousand years in the future, and even holding someone there, he could only feel his own warmth. Admittedly part of that was his own doing, but... was this a good beginning?

Letting such thoughts drift, he held his wife and dozed a bit longer before deciding to rise. Theoretically he should be sleeping on the outer side, but somehow he'd ended up on the inner side, so he moved as carefully and quietly as possible when climbing out, smiling slightly when he noticed the clothing laid out by the bedside.

He should go bathe in the adjacent courtyard. As for Tan'er's side, Chan'er and the others outside would handle everything properly. Given their special circumstances now, living together for the first time, his wife was rather shy about such matters, so he'd let them deal with the blood-stained white cloth from the bed and the sheets that needed changing. No need to involve himself in that.

Thinking back on last night, it had been rather exhausting. His wife had been a virgin after all, closing her eyes with an expression like awaiting execution. He'd worked hard to help her relax, and when he'd entered, she'd clearly still been in pain. Being mindful of her emotional state naturally meant he couldn't focus much on himself. Virgins were truly troublesome. Still, she'd been in pain and he'd been tired, so as a married couple they'd come out even. Having put in such effort, his wife shouldn't be left with any lingering trauma going forward.

He'd assumed that given her shyness, if he simply left now she'd either pretend to sleep or actually sleep without much else happening. However, just as he was about to leave, a soft voice came from behind: "Husband."

He turned to look. Su Tan'er had awakened and was lying there watching him, hand pulling the covers up to her chin, showing him a smile as she said softly:

"Good morning."

This was the casual greeting Ning Yi had often used with her in the past. Hearing her say it now, he couldn't help pausing for a moment before smiling and nodding.

"Good morning..."

This was the sixth day of the eleventh month in the eighth year of Jinghan, a year and a half after Ning Yi and Su Tan'er's wedding, when the feeling of home finally took proper shape between husband and wife. Winter had long since arrived, and the weather had been dropping quickly. In just a few more days, the first snow would fall and Jiangning City would truly enter the long winter period.

The charred ruins of the small building on the other side of the courtyard still stood there, not really suitable for moving just yet. In the bedroom on this side, Ning Yi's belongings had finally been mixed together with Su Tan'er's, making the room feel rather cramped for now, though at least for this winter, no one had considered changing rooms.

Come spring next year, they planned major construction work in the courtyard to create an entirely new layout including this building. Recently Ning Yi and Su Tan'er had discussed these matters, also asking Chan'er, Juan'er, and Xing'er for their ideas. In the evenings, the five of them—master and servants—would gather in the parlor around the brazier in a warm, cozy atmosphere still much like always, though now that Chan'er and the others understood the progress in the relationship between the young miss and the young master, they could naturally share more intimate jokes together when gathered.

Xiao Chan occasionally felt somewhat lonely and envious, but more than that, she was happy for the two most important people in her life. Ning Yi and Su Tan'er treated her no differently than before, and she naturally understood that since the young miss and young master had only just made progress together, it was impossible for the young master to take her as a concubine right now. When envy occasionally stirred in her heart, she would sit before the mirror at her dressing table and whisper to herself: "Xiao Chan isn't in a hurry. It's a lifetime matter, after all..." Then she'd purse her lips at her reflection as encouragement.

She already knew what kind of person the young master was—he would never abandon her.
