"...I've long heard of Liheng's reputation at the Prince's Manor, but unfortunately Liheng didn't attend the last gathering, so I never had the chance to meet you—I've deeply regretted it. Seeing you today, I realize Liheng truly lives up to his name as a talented young man. Old Zhang and I were planning to go to East Market to look at a fine Duan inkstone¹. I wonder where Liheng and the Little Commandery Princess are headed..."

"It's just that the Commandery Princess is approaching her coming-of-age ceremony². Having her walk around outside like this is somewhat improper..."

It was only early February now, and Jin'er's shop had been open just a few days, but business was already booming. Still, it was past lunchtime, so there were private rooms available on the second floor. When Ning Yi arrived with Zhou Pei, Song Qian, and the two scholars Zhang and Li, the main hall downstairs looked quite lively. He didn't know whether Yunzhu and Jin'er were around, but since he'd brought outsiders, there was no need to look for them. He got a room on the second floor, sat down, and started drinking tea and chatting.

The room was beautifully decorated. Its location was excellent—pushing open the rear window would give a full view of the Qinhuai River's scenery, though with the weather still cold, the windows had to stay closed for now. Several potted plants sat around the room, and a few ink paintings and poetry manuscripts hung on the walls, creating a scholarly atmosphere. After everyone sat down and the shop's female attendants brought tea and snacks, Zhang and Li began their pitch.

The slightly plump Li Tong wore a genial smile. Of the two, he basically played the friendly face, warmly engaging Ning Yi with an enthusiastic attitude. Zhang Rui's status and position were clear, and being older, he went straight to frowning and questioning Ning Yi. In truth, these two probably weren't without jealousy and resentment in their hearts. Though Li Tong had some reputation, during his years at the Prince's Manor he'd had little real interaction with Zhou Pei or Zhou Junwu. Zhang Rui had taught the siblings some things and held a teacher-student relationship, but no matter what, he could never have the kind of relationship Ning Yi had—taking them to classes at various academies, reportedly often bringing them here and there. That kind of connection was truly different.

Zhou Pei was an independent-minded girl. On any other day, she'd probably have gotten annoyed at Zhang Rui's remark that "the Commandery Princess is approaching her coming-of-age ceremony, and having her walk around outside like this is somewhat improper," but today was different—she'd just competed with Ning Yi this morning, and he'd even seen her crying on the side. Now these two teachers were clearly here to challenge her master, and little Zhou Pei was secretly delighted, completely unconcerned about the old scholar's comments on her lack of freedom to go outside. Her stomach was already a bit hungry, and now her appetite increased even more. She grabbed some pastries and sat to the side nibbling, playing the role of a good student who respected teachers and listened to instruction, almost wanting to nod vigorously. On the other hand, she was looking forward to seeing what twisted logic Ning Yi would use to refute the two teachers. Of course, at this point the conversation was still warming up, so Ning Yi just smiled and replied that he was making a trip to the Imperial Son-in-law's mansion to deliver some things, and was bringing Zhou Pei along on the way.

Li Tong smiled and nodded. "The Imperial Son-in-law's mansion... would that be Lord Ming's residence? I've heard Liheng is quite close with Lord Ming?"

"We're chess partners."

"Liheng's chess skills must be quite high. I also have some understanding of the game. If there's time in the future, we could arrange a match."

While Li Tong made polite conversation, Zhang Rui glanced several times toward a corner of the room. "What Liheng is taking to Lord Ming's residence—could it be those things?"

Ning Yi looked at him. "Teacher Zhang recognizes them?"

"These appear to be military fire lances, no? I wonder where Liheng obtained them?"

Zhang Rui frowned as Ning Yi gave a general explanation. He'd originally gotten these fire lances from Kang Xian for research. Fire lance technology wasn't particularly advanced, and by now he'd mostly figured them out. Having too many sitting around the courtyard served no purpose, and two of them were already broken. Though Kang Xian's hidden influence was substantial, these fire lances were still military equipment, so Ning Yi thought it better to return some and have Lu Agui report them properly. That's why he'd brought them to deliver.

By this point they'd exchanged some conversation, and the topic had suddenly shifted to guns. Zhou Pei chewed her pastry, looking left and right in confusion. The topic should have turned to matters of literary talent and poetry. The three of them should compete—first some provocation, then the two teachers would have a literary duel with Ning Liheng, doing drinking games, composing poetry, writing essays, which would then become an admired story. That was the development she'd been expecting.

At this point, she heard Zhang and Li discuss some trivial matters about Kang Xian. Then Zhang Rui said, "I've also seen fire lances a few times. Such items are truly distasteful. Placing them in the army, they're like chicken ribs³—frivolous clever tricks⁴ that do harm but no good. I've heard several times that Liheng is interested in these things. Such matters are truly improper. Liheng should focus on proper learning and devote his thoughts to beneficial pursuits. Otherwise, I'm afraid it will inevitably lead to self-harm."

With a thump, Zhou Pei's eyes widened into circles. She held the pastry in both hands, scooted her bottom backward, and sat up properly with an upright posture, her eyes darting around as she watched Ning Yi's and the others' expressions, pressing her lips together. She looked like a rabbit waiting for Ning Yi's rebuttal, but when she saw Ning Yi simply smile and cup his hands with "Teacher Zhang is right," the fire hadn't caught, and the rabbit's ears drooped.

She'd heard Kang Xian describe Ning Yi's temperament before. Her master was the type who could politely apologize for something a hundred times, nod a thousand times, but absolutely wouldn't change his actions. If Grandpa Qin or Imperial Son-in-law Grandpa were here today, he might bring out some sincerity to debate with them. But these two in front of him clearly didn't arouse his fighting spirit, so he just went with the flow and nodded his agreement.

However, though Ning Yi was willing to back down, the other side wasn't necessarily willing to let it go, and Zhang Rui shook his head. "Liheng is young and spirited, so you may not take what this old man says seriously. But what I'm referring to isn't actually the firearms themselves. In this old man's view, these firearms can only make a loud noise when fired to frighten people. Their range doesn't match bows and arrows, their accuracy is extremely poor, reloading between shots is extremely troublesome, after firing just a few rounds they might explode and injure the user, and they can't be used on rainy days. The only advantage is that because they use gunpowder propulsion, even children or old men can use them as long as they point them in the right direction... but this is also the greatest disadvantage."

Though this old man clearly had a confrontational attitude, he wasn't a fool. He was quite knowledgeable about fire lances. "What benefit is there in placing a weapon that even children can use in the military? What our Wu Dynasty's soldiers have always lacked isn't these strange odd contraptions, but military morale and fighting spirit. Consider how the Jurchen tribe could defeat the Liao army despite being vastly outnumbered. Our Wu Dynasty soldiers scatter at the mere sight of the Liao. Are people really so different from each other? I've also seen Jurchen people—they don't have three heads and six arms. Our Wu Dynasty's soldiers are greedy for life, afraid of death, only know pleasure. They can only be strictly disciplined and harshly trained to awaken their courage and fighting spirit—that's the hope for victory. Right now they're already lazy in training, can't swing swords, can't draw bows. It's not that they lack strength, but that they lack courage and spirit. If you place these things in the army, it will only make the military more useless. Even if these firearms' power doubled, it would still be harmful with no benefit!"

"That makes sense." Ning Yi nodded. This time it wasn't just a brush-off—he actually agreed with some of what the other said.

"'Heaven's movement is vigorous; the superior man never ceases to strengthen himself.'⁵ These frivolous clever tricks will only make people lazy and corrupt. What the ancient sages said is all supreme truth. I've heard Liheng is interested in these matters. Since you're young, it was originally harmless. But now Liheng is a teacher to others, and I've heard you're actually having the Little Prince study these things too. This is going a bit too far..."

Zhang Rui had a teacher-student relationship with Zhou Pei and Zhou Junwu. Though they weren't very close, he knew that recently the Little Prince had suddenly become interested in something called investigation of things. This explanation might fool ordinary people, but in these old scholars' eyes, it was genuinely just frivolous clever tricks—craftsman's learning. The old man turned the conversation, finally arriving at this matter. Li Tong didn't know about this and frowned. "With Liheng's learning, it surely wouldn't be like that, right? I wonder what Old Zhang is actually referring to..."

The atmosphere in the room finally became strange. In a certain sense, it could be said they'd finally entered the main topic. Zhou Pei held a pastry to her lips and took a bite, frowning as she looked at Ning Yi, feeling this matter was thorny. If after a while the Prince's Manor really spread stories about Old Zhang scolding Ning Liheng, she'd also feel she'd lost face. At this moment she worried about what words Ning Yi should use to refute them.

Over there Zhang Rui and Li Tong were speaking back and forth when suddenly someone knocked on the door. A moment later, a veiled woman carrying a tea tray entered. She wasn't one of the restaurant's regular staff. The woman had an extremely beautiful figure, and behind the veil her eyes were lively. She appeared to be at least a manager-level figure in the restaurant. Smiling, she said a few courteous words, added tea and pastries, then turned to leave. Li Tong seemed to remember something and stared blankly at the woman's figure, momentarily forgetting to criticize Ning Yi. Zhou Pei recognized her though. She'd seen her at Zhu's main location—this was the courtesan Yuan Jin'er. She'd already left that life and was acquainted with her master.

Sure enough, after the woman left, Ning Yi also smiled and stood up. "You two chat first. I'll step out for a moment."

After giving this greeting, Ning Yi left the room and, as expected, found Yuan Jin'er sneakily peering his way not far along the corridor. When she came here before to direct workers and such, she didn't mind people seeing her face. But now, treating herself as Jin'er Shop's owner, she'd become more reserved, covering her face with a veil to maintain an air of mystery. When Ning Yi came over, she said, "I've been so busy lately. What brings you here?"

"Just passing by. Where's Yunzhu?"

"Sister Yunzhu was upstairs earlier, but now I can't find her either. Don't know what matter called her away. Little Min told me you seemed to be here, so I came to look. Besides that little girl with you, who are the others?"

"Big talents here to argue."

"Talents?" Jin'er blinked. Ning Yi nodded. "Very famous ones."

"No wonder... I think I've seen that fat one before..."

As they spoke, noisy sounds mixed with performance music drifted up from the first floor hall. Jin'er apparently had something on her mind—she looked out the window opening onto the street several times. Ning Yi also poked his head out for a glance. Nothing but people coming and going on the street. "What are you looking at?"

"Uh... it's nothing. None of your business. You go argue with those two talents." She thought for a moment and gave a light snort. "Honestly, you stinking men aren't fun when you compete without women around. In the end it's all about showing off in front of women. How about I have someone go in to watch you trade sharp words? Two sisters from Swallow Jade Tower are here today—they're beauties. You wouldn't want Sister Yunzhu to accompany you, though she'd definitely favor you..."

Men just compete to show off in front of women. Yuan Jin'er's casual words really hit the nail on the head. Actually, there wasn't much to debate about today's matter. Zhang Rui and the others had their own logical system, forged through thousands of tests without flaws. As for discussing the influence after technology undergoes qualitative change—if it were someone like Old Qin who they'd known for a while, maybe he'd think seriously about it. But these people wouldn't.

Even if both sides were completely sincere, there was no possibility of persuading each other. This kind of thing had no meaning for sincerity. Ning Yi was planning to go back and compile some detailed data about frivolous clever tricks to fool those two anyway. Either way, he was bored with nothing better to do.

He went downstairs to use the privy, washed his hands and came back up. The data was already compiled in his mind. He'd just entered the second floor corridor when, inadvertently, something happened.

Noisy sounds mixed with music came from behind. Ning Yi was walking toward the room when footsteps rushed from behind, then pushed his shoulder.

Zhu's second shop had a spacious floor plan, and the corridor wasn't narrow, so for that person to push Ning Yi, he was clearly in a great hurry. Ning Yi didn't particularly mind—the man was a burly, tall fellow who from his appearance seemed to come from the north. He went straight into a room with an open door just ahead to the side.

"That's the one..."

The man said something like that. As Ning Yi walked past, someone was just closing the room door. A few sentences of conversation faintly came through. Ning Yi didn't pay much attention. He looked toward the corridor's turning point—Yuan Jin'er's figure was no longer there. She seemed troubled about something today, though he didn't know what exactly... Just as he was thinking this, his footsteps suddenly stopped. A moment later, his fingers tapped twice at his side.

Since learning that second-rate internal energy technique Lu Hongti taught him, his physical condition had improved considerably, and his hearing had gotten better too. As he'd walked past just now, he seemed to vaguely hear sentences like "that Qin Siyuan's side... guards..." though he couldn't be certain. Now he stepped back two paces, concentrating to listen. Faintly he caught some fragments.

"...certain you want to do this?"

"...just happens to be an opportunity..."

"...too hasty..."

"Keep watch first. After it's done, leave the city immediately..."

What's going on?

Someone in that room had a louder voice, so it was clearer, but for the moment he couldn't piece together the full picture. After the people inside exchanged a few more words, Ning Yi walked forward, and then that door opened, the person coming out glancing at him before heading toward the corridor exit. When Ning Yi looked back, he saw one man was tall and thin, wearing a coat of animal fur. The other looked like a young noble, but both men's steps were quite steady.

After thinking for a moment, Ning Yi also turned and walked that way.

Leaving the corridor, there was a platform connected to the stairs from which you could see the main hall below. The two men who'd come out of the room stood there looking down. At this moment the noise in the hall had lessened somewhat, and girls invited from Swallow Jade Tower were performing on stage. Ning Yi stood beside those two men, glanced down below, then beckoned to the side and called over a shop assistant, asking the name of the girl performing on stage. Without looking further, he turned and left.

At a table on one side of the hall, Nie Yunzhu was accompanying Old Qin, talking with a shop assistant. There was nothing on the table yet, clearly Old Qin had just arrived and sat down not long ago. A few days earlier at Old Qin's place, the old man had mentioned that after Zhu's second shop opened, when he had time he'd come take a look.

What those two men beside him had been watching was also this direction.

In the room, Zhang Rui was talking with Zhou Pei. Regarding Ning Yi's earlier departure, Zhang Rui and Li Tong's interpretation was that Ning Yi had fled in defeat. Zhou Pei knew in her heart that wasn't the case. Ning Yi leaving with Yuan Jin'er made her gossip desire burn fiercely—she wished she could chase out and secretly peek. After all, one was the number one talent, one was a former leading courtesan of the pleasure quarters. Who knew if there might be some lingering romantic love story between them. But on one hand she didn't really want Ning Yi to lose face, and on the other she was still somewhat sulking about this morning's incident, so she didn't follow them out. She just waited for Ning Yi to come back and turn things around—as for her master being able to turn things around, there was nothing to doubt about that. She just didn't know what reasoning he'd use.

Zhang Rui was happily expounding on the remarks he'd just made targeting Ning Yi, putting on an earnest teaching manner for Zhou Pei, mainly hoping this intelligent little princess would return from her wayward path and recognize who was the better teacher. Li Tong was thinking that when Ning Yi came back, he'd have nothing to say. Though this matter was also a bit strange—usually when talents competed, they fought for pride. If others said you were wrong, you absolutely couldn't admit it. But today that Ning Yi had no temper, just smiled and said a few "you're right" phrases. It really made people uncomfortable. When he came back, they'd have to make it more serious. Afterward when word spread, they had to make people recognize that this Ning Liheng had lost face. Then he remembered this shop was called Jin'er Shop, and that woman who looked very much like Yuan Jin'er. For a moment, he put his thoughts on that.

Before long, the room door finally opened. Ning Yi walked back in, but first he quietly said a few words to Song Qian, the Prince's Manor guard who'd been sitting to the side like an invisible person. Song Qian frowned, his expression serious as he went out. Ning Yi came over this way. Li Tong smiled. "Liheng, Old Zhang and I chatted for a while just now, and have some thoughts..."

Zhang Rui frowned. "It's not just thoughts anymore. Liheng, I truly feel this matter is quite serious. It concerns the Little Prince's future and can't be taken lightly. These next few days, you really must give an explanation. Otherwise, not only will this spread throughout the Prince's Manor, this old man will also submit a memorial to the Prince. This matter isn't directed at you personally, but still... still..."

The old man's words trailed off as he blinked. Li Tong also widened his eyes. Zhou Pei on the side was shrinking her head and nibbling pastries, her mouth hanging open, unable to bite down. At this moment Ning Yi hadn't responded to the two men's words. He glanced back at the room door, then went to the room corner and picked up the long bundle wrapped around five fire lances. He placed the bundle on the table, opened it for a look, grabbed one gun, opened a small packet of gunpowder, poured the powder into the gun barrel, took a long iron ramrod and tamped it down, then began loading ammunition.

At this moment Ning Yi stood there performing his task, already having lost interest in that boring debate. Across the round table Zhang Rui and Li Tong sat, not knowing what this fellow intended to do. Ning Yi loaded one gun, thought about it, then went to load another. When his gaze swept over the two men's faces, he even smiled at them kindly, though the corner of his eye glanced toward the doorway. Of course, at this moment Zhang Rui and Li Tong had no way to notice such movements.

"You... you..." Li Tong swallowed. "G-gentlemen use words, not... not hands..." Having stuttered through this sentence, he had no way to continue. What kind of debate was this—because they said his frivolous clever tricks were useless, he was going to shoot the two of them...

Ning Yi took out a fire starter from his body, opened it and waved it in his hand a few times, blew it into flame, then covered and put it away. Then he wrapped the two loaded fire lances, positioning them so they could be drawn out at any moment and slung them over his back. Then he took a deep breath.

"There's something going on outside. You all sit in here for a while... maybe I got it wrong..." He thought for a moment, then added, "I hope I got it wrong..."

FOOTNOTES:

¹ *Duan inkstone (端砚): Highly prized traditional Chinese inkstone from Duanzhou (modern Zhaoqing, Guangdong), valued by scholars and calligraphers for its fine quality and ability to grind ink smoothly.*

² *Coming-of-age ceremony (及笄): Traditional coming-of-age at 15 when girls became marriageable, marked by a ceremony where the girl begins wearing a hairpin (笄). Societal expectations placed increased restrictions on unmarried women's freedom after this age.*

³ *Chicken ribs (鸡肋): Proverbial phrase from Three Kingdoms general Cao Cao, meaning something with minimal value—"tasty but no meat," essentially useless but hard to discard.*

⁴ *Frivolous clever tricks (奇巧淫技): Confucian dismissive term for technology and practical innovation, literally "strange frivolous techniques." Traditional scholar-officials viewed technological pursuits as morally corrupting distractions from self-cultivation and classical learning.*

⁵ *"Heaven's movement is vigorous; the superior man never ceases to strengthen himself" (天行健，君子以自强不息): Classical quotation from the I Ching (Book of Changes), a foundational Confucian text. Emphasizes constant self-improvement through moral effort. Zhang Rui invokes this to argue that relying on technology weakens moral character.*
