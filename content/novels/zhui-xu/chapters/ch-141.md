"Don't overthink it. Once this matter escalates, your Wu family will definitely face family extermination and property confiscation¹—there's no escaping it..."

This voice drifted calmly from the upper floor of the teahouse, leaving Wu Qilong sitting in stunned silence, momentarily unable to speak. Having spent years tempering himself in the commercial world, he was no ordinary incompetent fool who could be easily frightened by anyone's alarmist warnings, yet this time was different. The earlier revelation about the yellow fabric's fading had already made him acutely aware of Ning Yi's intricately interconnected and shocking strategic layout, and now these words emerged with such methodical progression that Ning Yi's natural demeanor made it seem as though he were casually tossing the concept of family extermination onto the table before him.

Few could recover their composure when faced with words like "household execution and clan annihilation," especially when it concerned their own survival. While Wu Qilong had considered the severity of the fading fabric, he had imagined at most a punishment—after all, even matters qualifying as "deceiving the emperor" were rarely judged hastily, with numerous avenues for mitigation, which was precisely why Ning Yi's demand for a third of the Wu family's assets had seemed so preposterous. But now, Ning Yi's words highlighted a factor they had never considered.

War was coming...

Steward Liao's trip to the capital was specifically to establish the Wu family's guilt of deceiving the emperor...

When all these elements combined...

Ning Yi quietly sipped his tea, waiting for Wu Qilong to digest this information before continuing in the same conversational tone.

"What the Su family can accomplish, many others in Jiangning can achieve as well—when you first obtained the yellow fabric, everything here was already predetermined. The first delivery deadline passes, you request an extension, and news of the fading will definitely be released."

As he spoke of plans that were almost entirely of his own devising, he simultaneously seemed completely detached from the situation, expressing what appeared to be genuine regret toward Wu Qilong and the entire Wu family: when the time comes, we will definitely have to kill your entire family.

He continued: "Such an approach lacks finesse. Once the news is out, the situation will spiral beyond the Su family's control. Even if we don't escalate things in the capital, others will gladly exert their influence to bring you down—though naturally, we will continue our own efforts. Your family would be punished, the market would descend into chaos, and while we would move to seize our share, securing the best portion would be a troublesome struggle."

"So I still hope to resolve all these matters before the imperial merchant delivery gets delayed." Ning Yi refilled his teacup and shook his head. "Time will be somewhat tight, but this is also to ensure you hand everything over—you don't need to organize it too meticulously, just deliver whatever you can manage, and for what you can't handle, Tan'er will surely exercise proper judgment..."

"Of course, sincerity is paramount—that's the most critical part. If possible, I'll show you that plan document where I emphasized several points to Tan'er: First, absolutely no delays—we have no time for negotiations. Regardless of what you say, if matters aren't completely resolved by the time you propose an extension, even if there's the slightest unfinished detail, the Su family will release the information, and once it's released, the situation will no longer be something the Su family can control, so we'll all have to trust to fate. The second point is actually just a supplement to the first: there's only one opportunity—the Su family won't say something like 'we want something else, go prepare it again,' so it's best to get everything right the first time. You should understand why things must be handled this way."

After delivering this lengthy explanation in one breath, Ning Yi took a sip of tea while Wu Qilong placed both hands on the table and leaned back against his chair.

"You mean... my Wu family... must submit to your slaughter?"

"Different phrasing, but that's the meaning." Ning Yi pursed his lips and nodded, deeply agreeing with Wu Qilong's summary. "I believe this should be the most peaceful approach—naturally, with everything laid out before us, you certainly won't feel comfortable about it, but at this point... perhaps you should step outside the situation to take a look. It's like Chinese chess²: once you're in checkmate, you can't expect your opponent not to capture. The next move is inevitable. Do you understand?"

Wu Qilong stared at Ning Yi with the expression one might reserve for a madman, opening his mouth several times without managing to speak. Ning Yi met his gaze calmly, and after a moment, he nodded and murmured softly: "You understand—I'm truly pleased..."

Wu Qilong's voice was suppressed and low, nearly word by word: "One-third... if we give it to you, then the Brilliant Gold Brocade formula..."

"There is no formula." Ning Yi shook his head. "From beginning to end, there was never a correct formula—it was because of the fading that Tan'er fell ill at the time. You've certainly experienced this kind of psychological pressure these past few days as well. She had been researching for three years, fighting alone, and with Su Boyong having been stabbed and injured, that's why she collapsed."

"Then why should my Wu family still give you this one-third?"

"Oh, let's clarify this so you can prepare mentally: it's not just one-third. One-third is what the Su family wants. The method is this: we will help you make arrangements³ in the capital and Jiangning, temporarily suppressing the news so no one knows you have a problem. Then, you will voluntarily accept punishment by first paying to grease palms at all levels—with our cooperation—and second, by paying fines to serve as military funds. Third, and most importantly..."

Ning Yi paused: "You must voluntarily take on several times your current annual tribute quota⁴. Only this amount can ensure the higher-ups won't confiscate and exterminate the Wu family, because even during war, annual tribute is still needed—perhaps for the Jin Kingdom... This burden will be somewhat heavy, requiring market contraction where you hand over your own produced ordinary fabrics to the court, and you can also purchase more from the market. Additionally, as you know, Tan'er recently improved the looms with increased efficiency, so regarding annual tribute, the Su family also has surplus capacity to help—there will definitely be spare fabric available here, and your side would provide a sales outlet."

"So, one-third to the Su family, one-third for various governmental expenses—I don't know if one-third will be enough, but you can certainly manage it somehow. Then over the next several years, although the Wu family's external market will continue to shrink, you'll have secured the imperial merchant position, and after a few years, you'll at least maintain the scale of the former Lü family and others who specialized solely in imperial commerce."

Wu Qilong's fist slammed down on the table.

"It's difficult to accept initially—I understand." Ning Yi remained completely unmoved. "However, neither you nor I clearly understand the current situation in the court's upper echelons, so having one path is better than having none, right? As for the alternative route, indeed, it's not without chances—who knows whether the fading issue might be resolved? Anyway, the Su family spent several years on it before giving up, Su Tan'er fell ill because of it, and perhaps you'll be particularly lucky and solve the problem in a month or two. In ten days, you can gamble on this opportunity—we'll release the news according to plan, then Steward Liao will operate in the capital, that's basically the process. Perhaps there are other solutions I haven't thought of yet. Oh, does your family have any reliable imperial relatives⁵? I think you could ask such people for help..."

"You..." Wu Qilong took a deep breath, pointing at Ning Yi. "You... are now seeking to destroy my Wu family, yet here you sit with an attitude of detachment, offering me advice..."

"You still don't understand?" This time, Ning Yi looked at him and frowned. "This kind of matter... isn't some children's game. Much of the time in this world, you feel like something has many, many choices, but in the end you discover there's really only one choice—you just follow it. Seeking to destroy your Wu family? Why do you speak of it so casually, as if this matter were no different from someone hitting me with a brick before? How do you expect me to respond?"

"This affair began with your actions. Do you want to hear this? You cannot behave this way—I warned you about this. Do you want to hear this? Or perhaps 'White-haired intimates still press their swords, those who achieve success first mock the crown-seekers'⁶? Your family elder took that poem home to have it mounted and framed, saying at the time that commerce was exactly like this... do you want to hear this? It's meaningless—from beginning to end, I never intended to discuss these things with you. I simply want to tell you what the current situation actually looks like. As for why it became this way, that's something for you to reflect on afterward; discussing it now—can it solve anything?"

"No one wanted to target your Wu family specifically. It's just that someone stabbed Su Boyong—oh, don't say this has nothing to do with you, that you knew nothing about it, this is most important—then you began scheming for the imperial merchant position, the fabric faded, Su Tan'er fell ill. The Su family encountered problems, and everything afterward has simply been problem-solving, carried out to the best possible degree. No one wants deaths, no one wants to exterminate entire families—this is what problem-solving looks like."

"Before that night of the textile guild's annual meeting, no one knew it was you—it was simply that whoever jumped in would face this. Whether it was you or the Xue family, it would be the same. Now I'm telling you there are two paths: first, your entire family lives; second, your entire family dies. Do I really need to explain in detail how to choose? And on the Su family's side, as I said, since you're already in checkmate, how the next move should proceed is perfectly natural—even if I let you choose, wouldn't it be obvious at a glance?"

Having reached this point, Ning Yi's expression had actually become serious, and he subsequently shook his head with a sigh.

"Returning to the earlier point: I know this is very difficult to handle, difficult to accept initially—anyone would feel the same. Events unfolded too rapidly with too sharp reversals, leaving little time for acceptance, and naturally you harbor resentment. 'Even if my entire family dies, I won't let you gain the slightest advantage'... most people would initially think this way, which is understandable, but a single person cannot devise solutions to something this significant, with so many decisions required."

Ning Yi paused, glancing at the sky outside before turning back to regard him earnestly: "Time is getting late. While I don't mind talking longer, you should return early, because the coming period will be busy with communicating information and devising strategies. 'Ten thousand years is too long'⁷; you must seize every moment. Matters this significant require everyone gathering to find a solution, so don't waste time—fight for every minute and second."

He finished speaking with a smile, observing Wu Qilong's expression before picking up the chemistry book beside him. Across from him, Wu Qilong pushed back his chair and stood up, prompting Ning Yi to look up as if remembering something: "Oh, this pot of tea is my treat."

Wu Qilong stood for several seconds but ultimately said nothing. His expression had become impossible to read, and when he was nearly at the bottom of the teahouse stairs, he turned back for one last look. Ning Yi sat there, having already lowered his head to resume writing and sketching with his brush, occasionally frowning in thought and recollection while taking small sips of tea. In his demeanor, the entire confrontation—with its sharp killing intent and world-altering consequences—seemed to be just another "ordinary" matter, no different from the outdated Persian chemistry pamphlet he was studying.

The afternoon was still early, with sunlight casting his silhouette within the teahouse's shadow...

**Cultural Footnotes:**

¹ **Family extermination and property confiscation (抄家灭族)**: The most severe imperial punishment, involving the execution of the entire extended family and confiscation of all property. This represented complete biological and social annihilation—the erasure of a family from both the physical world and the ancestral record. In Confucian society, where honoring ancestors and continuing the family line were sacred duties, this punishment was designed to destroy not just lives but the very existence of a bloodline, making it a fate more terrifying than individual death.

² **Chinese chess (象棋)**: The traditional Chinese board game, where "checkmate" (将死) literally means "general death," referring to the king piece being trapped with no escape.

³ **Arrangements (打点)**: A euphemism for systematic bribery of officials at various levels. In traditional Chinese commerce, this was considered a necessary business expense rather than criminal activity.

⁴ **Annual tribute quota (岁布)**: Specialized textiles that regional merchants were required to provide to the imperial court annually as tribute. This was a major economic burden that could bankrupt families.

⁵ **Imperial relatives (皇亲国戚)**: Members of the imperial family and high-ranking court officials who could provide protection through their political connections.

⁶ **Classical poetry reference**: "白首相知犹按剑，朱门先达笑弹冠" - A poem about the caution required in political relationships and the mockery faced by those seeking advancement. This shows the character's classical education and sophistication.

⁷ **"Ten thousand years is too long"**: A reference emphasizing the urgency of immediate action over distant planning, used here in the historical context.
