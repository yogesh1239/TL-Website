Flames roared and crackled in the night wind, casting light that danced wildly and violently across the courtyard. When Cheng Lie casually struck Xi Junyu down and strode forward with his blade raised, most of the Hundred Blades Alliance members in the courtyard already knew—influenced by Ou Peng's earlier attitude—that tonight, this underworld boss who had dominated Jiangning for so long had truly lost his temper.

Though to be fair, whether he lost his temper or not, the outcome was probably going to be the same either way.

A group had already charged out to hunt down that fool Ou Peng, their battle cries and screams spreading throughout the forest. In the courtyard, someone roared and lunged at Ma Lin, who was already half-covered in blood, only to be hacked back by Ma Lin's blade in a shower of sparks. Su Tan'er, protected by two servants, moved toward her father and Guard Geng.

"Are you alright, Miss?"

"I'm fine." Su Tan'er shook her head. "Now that I think about it... Uncle Geng, it seems like whoever has the bigger blade is stronger, doesn't it?"

Blood spattered across the courtyard as Ma Lin, now surrounded with no way out, swung his blade horizontally to fend off his attackers, blood flying from his wounds in a terrifying display. Su Tan'er gripped the hem of her robe tightly, but since their side had the upper hand, she tried to ease her tension with a light joke. In the current courtyard scene, the earlier knife-wielder had been outmatched by Ma Lin's large saber, but Ma Lin's steel blade, though fierce, still fell short of Cheng Lie's thick, ancient-looking broadsword.

On the other side of the courtyard, Ma Lin roared "Ahhhhhhh—" as he exchanged several strikes with Cheng Lie. Guard Geng, himself a blade user, looked down at his nine-ring broadsword and chuckled.

"Generally speaking, you can't really tell with regular weapons, but once they reach a certain size, it's exactly as Miss says. That Ma Lin's blade technique is fierce and unusual—earlier inside, when he burst in, I nearly fell for his tricks. But Alliance Master Cheng's blade is so heavy, yet he wields it as if it were light as a feather. Every strike is steady and measured, never taking those risky angles. You saw his one-handed stance just now—that Ma Lin has three moves at most before he goes down..."

Su Tan'er naturally didn't understand these matters, but she listened attentively anyway, mainly because Ning Yi would occasionally discuss such things at home. Thinking of Ning Yi, she glanced over at Xi Junyu lying on the ground where he'd been struck down.

He should be killed...

The decision about how to handle Xi Junyu had only come this afternoon, so there hadn't been time to settle on specifics. When exactly to kill him tonight, how to do it—she'd never dealt with such brutal decisions before, so naturally her father would make the final call.

After all, people weren't heartless. Despite everything, she still felt something like friendship or mentorship toward Xi Junyu. She knew he would die tonight, but since someone else was making that decision, Su Tan'er didn't dwell on it. She originally hadn't wanted Xi Junyu to learn about her husband, because if he survived, he'd pose a threat to Ning Yi. Now that he knew, he needed to die soon—this was the first time tonight she'd started thinking about it.

In the center of the courtyard, flames blazed fiercely as Ma Lin was hacked back step by step through his hysterical shouts. Cheng Lie's blade strikes came steady and relentless, forcing Ma Lin backward even when he blocked directly—boom, boom, boom—as fire and blood sprayed through the air, dust rising from the ground as his retreating feet practically plowed furrows into the earth. The air was dry, dust flying everywhere.

When Cheng Lie raised his blade with a "Ha!" and brought it down, Ma Lin was finally sent flying, blade and all, crashing into the rear courtyard wall and coughing up blood. At that moment, the sounds of fighting intensified on the other side of the wall, and a figure vaulted up from there, soaring high into the air with a large spear spinning overhead. It was Ou Peng, who had been fighting along the outer wall—now he leaped directly into the courtyard.

"Old bastard!"

"Hell has no gate for you..."

BOOM! The spear came whistling down, Cheng Lie's hair and beard bristled as he roared and swept his blade up to meet it. The two clashed in midair for an instant, then pushed apart in a shower of sparks.

"You broke through!"

Cheng Lie retreated one step while Ou Peng, whose aerial strike had been deflected before his feet could touch ground, bounced off the rear wall. He kicked off and launched himself at Cheng Lie again. Cheng Lie swept his blade to knock him aside, and the moment Ou Peng landed, a blade strike that seemed to solidify into gold in the firelight came crashing down like thunder.

CRASH! Sparks flew everywhere as Ou Peng's feet slid three or four meters across the ground before stopping, dust billowing. Hundred Blades Alliance disciples surrounded the courtyard, and before Ou Peng could steady himself, he spun his spear to drive back the nearby enemies. When he turned his head, Cheng Lie's blade was already thundering toward him again.

With Cheng Lie engaging him, the Hundred Blades disciples held back from joining the attack, simply retreating to maintain formation while moving with the combat circle. Cheng Lie's blade work was heavy and fierce, and Ou Peng was himself a tall, powerfully built man—one wielding a blade, the other a spear, they fought in the courtyard center like a massive storm, the battle thrilling and terrifying.

Fighting still raged fiercely in the forest. In those brief moments, everyone in the courtyard became absorbed in the duel. The Hundred Blades disciples focused on surrounding Ou Peng and the badly wounded Ma Lin, failing to notice that their perimeter had shifted several meters in one direction. Just then, an intense rumbling sound came charging from outside one side of the small courtyard, rapidly closing in.

CRASH—

Countless chunks of earth and brick flew through the air as a large cart smashed straight through the mud-brick wall on one side of the courtyard. Two Hundred Blades disciples were nearly knocked flying on impact, dust rising in clouds. The breach opened near where Xi Junyu lay, and a blood-soaked, heavily built figure burst through the dust, slamming aside a nearby Alliance member and yanking Xi Junyu up from the ground. Two more figures rushed in behind him. Outside the breach, Hundred Blades disciples surrounded those who had pushed the cart, fierce fighting erupting—the Alliance still had the advantage there, and the breach was almost blocked, but in those few moments, this group of less than ten managed to rescue Xi Junyu.

Cheng Lie glanced back. Everyone in the courtyard turned to look as well. The towering, blood-drenched giant wiped blood from his mouth, grinned "heh heh," and slammed a heavy iron shovel into the ground with a resounding THUD.

"Come on then. Who dares!"

Two seconds later, everyone in the courtyard surged toward them.

******************

The battle raged on. At a certain point, the center of the fighting began pressing back toward the small courtyard, then extended outward toward the small market and fish stalls at Ten Steps Ridge.

On a small hill not far away, two carriages sat parked by the roadside. A man held up a long tube, peering toward Ten Steps Ridge.

"Wow, how did it get this intense..."

From what he could see, this brawl had apparently drawn several hundred people. A fight reaching this scale proved just how formidable the power backing Xi Junyu really was. Originally, everyone had assumed that with the resources the Su family could mobilize, crushing the small group behind someone like Xi Junyu would be like squashing a grasshopper. But now it seemed this particular grasshopper wasn't so easy to crush—things had definitely blown up.

The night was quiet, but roars and battle cries carried from that direction, along with occasional random shouts that made Ning Yi frown... Meanwhile, on Cheng Lie's side of the Hundred Blades Alliance, they were actually surprised, amazed by how tenacious these outsiders were.

Back in the courtyard earlier, they'd already surrounded them completely, but in the end, the group had fought their way out by sheer force. Among them, Ou Peng's skills were exceptional—he could actually match Cheng Lie blow for blow. They might also have someone skilled in tactical formations, because when the group was surrounded in the center, the fighters originally battling in the small forest began charging toward the courtyard. Eventually everything merged into chaos—the Hundred Blades outnumbered them, but ultimately part of the group still managed to fight their way through.

Still, even if they counted as formidable outsiders, after rescuing Xi Junyu, they'd suffered heavy losses. As they fought their way toward the small market at Ten Steps Ridge, they lost quite a few more. Now, in a formation of barely twenty men, Ou Peng led at the front while the giant with the iron shovel guarded the rear. Ma Lin, already seriously wounded, held the center alongside the others, fighting off the surrounding Hundred Blades members. Beside Ma Lin, Xi Junyu—who'd also taken a blade wound—was speaking with a lean-faced man.

"...Brother Jiang, this is my fault. I've dragged everyone into this..."

"We who walk this path repay kindness and settle grudges. You showed us kindness, and when we helped you, it was willingly. The pity is that the plan seemed flawless, but now we know we were outmaneuvered from the very start. That Ning Yi is formidable..."

"He was in the shadows while I was in the open. Since I know who he is now, I may have lost this time, but next time I'll settle the score..."

Mentioning Ning Yi's name made Xi Junyu grind his teeth, though even now, the whole situation still felt somewhat unreal. The man beside him, named Jiang Jing, was himself skilled at scheming and had participated in the original plot and assassination arrangements. At this moment, he merely gave a cold laugh.

"Lost? Not necessarily..."

"Hm?"

"In the end, strength can always overcome cleverness! But nothing's settled yet—let's fight our way out first, then we'll talk. Everyone, push south!"

"Kill!"

At the rear of the formation, the giant with the shovel sent someone flying through the air with a BANG. Up front, Ou Peng's spear danced like wind, driving back four or five Hundred Blades members at once. Not far away, Cheng Lie appeared in view with the Su father and daughter, charging forward with his blade. Ou Peng flicked his hand, sending a hidden weapon toward them. Cheng Lie deflected it with a clang of his blade, and Ou Peng burst into laughter.

"Su family, listen well! If we escape today, we'll return with our brothers to slaughter your entire clan! To honor the spirits of the brothers who died here today!"

"Haha." From the middle of the formation, Ma Lin blocked an attacker. "Count me in! Su family's little bitch, remember your brother Ma, haha!"

During combat, trading insults and provocations was also a tactic. This wasn't the first time they'd shouted such things—these were all desperados, and with dozens of brothers already dead or wounded, they'd thrown caution to the wind. They'd curse anyone out, hoping to provoke someone into losing their head. For a moment, curses filled the air.

"You Su family better remember..."

"As long as I don't die today..."

"If you dare..."

Jiang Jing also shouted with a laugh, "Tell that Ning Liheng when I return, he won't be so lucky next time..."

"That Su family bitch better remember—when I have my fun with you, I'll make your husband watch from the side..."

From a short distance away, Su Boyong's hands gripped his wheelchair armrests until they creaked. Guard Geng's face had long since darkened as he charged toward the group. Su Tan'er knew they were deliberately provoking them, but she still flushed red with anger. However, this was ultimately a martial world brawl, not a military operation with organized encirclement—the Hundred Blades Alliance's defensive line probably wouldn't be able to cut off their escape route in time.

Just then, from a side road at Ten Steps Ridge, a carriage came racing from the distance, recklessly crashing through the Hundred Blades disciples' loose perimeter.

A figure was shoved forward as someone shouted, "Stop! Don't anyone dare move—"

Lamplight swayed on the carriage as a man bound hand and foot was pushed out from inside the curtain, dressed in scholar's robes. Behind him, several men dressed as Su household servants pressed steel blades against his throat.

The surrounding Hundred Blades members saw the Su household servant uniforms and couldn't immediately tell which side they were on. Then the lantern on the carriage went out. Those few moments were enough for observant people to glimpse the scholar's face. Jiang Jing looked once, then suddenly burst out laughing. "Ha! Success! We did it! Brothers, charge over there!"

Xi Junyu thought his eyes were playing tricks on him—the bound man in the distance was clearly Ning Yi. "Brother Jiang, is that..."

"Ning Yi! Haha, that's the backup plan I arranged. When the clan assembly results came out, I guessed you'd been betrayed, so I ran both operations simultaneously... It was already set up beforehand anyway, but now it's paying off big—capture the hostage and force them to let us through!"

The Hundred Blades Alliance was powerful—even if they barely broke through the encirclement now, they'd face continuous pursuit afterward. How could that compare to the leverage of a hostage? The group changed direction, charging toward that end of the perimeter. The already-wounded Ma Lin laughed maniacally as he fought at the front. "That Su family bitch, today you'll learn what pain really means..."

On this side, Cheng Lie asked Su Boyong and Su Tan'er what had happened. Only Su Boyong answered briefly. Even the Hundred Blades disciples closing the encirclement were confused, unsure how to classify those Su household servants on the carriage. Seeing the group charging in, everyone maintained their surrounding formation, but for a moment, the battle cries around Ten Steps Ridge actually quieted slightly.

Su Tan'er stood there, her entire heart sinking.

According to her previous training, as a merchant, she should maintain composure. In any situation, she should stay calm and figure out how to respond. Because panicking wouldn't help—when problems arose, you had to solve them. But in this moment, even her breathing nearly stopped.

Her head went blank all at once, her body feeling as if it had plunged into an ice cellar, freezing her thoughts solid.

The carriage was blocked and stopped on the slope. Weapons still clashed as Ma Lin cut through like a blade, charging straight for the carriage to link up with the four brothers there. Finally, his heavy saber sent the last obstacle flying, and he reached the darkened carriage. He turned his head to look at Ning Yi, bound and seated on the cart shaft, then turned back to raise his blade toward those below. "Stop! Anyone else dare come closer and see what happens!"

By now Cheng Lie had learned from Su Boyong just how important Ning Yi was—far more than just a live-in son-in-law. At his signal, the disciples who'd been closing in stopped one after another. Jiang Jing spread his arms on the slope. "Haha, what can you do! I told you we'd settle the score!"

"This won't work. We'll only lose more." In the distance, Cheng Lie turned to Su Boyong. A moment later, Su Tan'er suddenly shook her head. "No! We can't!"

After a moment to catch their breath, Ou Peng dragged his spear up the slope, and the others began turning to follow. The distance was only four or five zhang, but with little light, the people above were just silhouettes—Ma Lin standing by the carriage, the hostage seated on the cart shaft.

Then they saw the hostage stand up.

The movement was casual, but it carried enough impact to stun everyone. How could a hostage just stand up like that? In the dark outline, the figure faced the crowd and raised his hand toward Ma Lin beside him. A point of red light flashed in the darkness.

BANG—

From less than half a meter away, a bloom of fire burst in the darkness like an opening flower, engulfing Ma Lin's entire head. Blood and flesh exploded outward, continuing the fire's expansion into the distance.

The figure didn't even have time to sway before falling straight over, stiff as a board.

Everyone stopped in their tracks.

The dark figure lowered his hand and looked down to pull the ropes off his body.

The massive explosion still echoed and reverberated across the night sky, lingering endlessly.

"I heard you all over here talking about me, having such a good time..." He tossed the ropes aside and spread his arms, saying with great enthusiasm, "So I came!"
