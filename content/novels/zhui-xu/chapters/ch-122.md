So it was that after the First Branch's master and the Second Miss who'd been groomed to take over the business both fell ill, the live-in son-in-law began managing the Su family's affairs.

This counted as major news within Su family circles recently.

When Su Boyong was stabbed and Su Tan'er collapsed from illness, it would be impossible to say the other two branches felt no schadenfreude whatsoever. News of Su Tan'er's illness hadn't spread outside yet, but family members had mostly heard. The gossip mainly speculated about how the traditionally weak First Branch would weather the current crisis, with some saying that for all Su Tan'er's previous fierce reputation, she was still just a woman who couldn't shoulder real responsibility. In short, while everyone watched and waited, Ning Yi was pushed forward as temporary manager, immediately sparking heated discussion.

As a mere live-in son-in-law, in any other family, mockery and derision would have descended instantly. But their family's particular son-in-law was somewhat different. Since entering the household, he'd repeatedly shattered expectations. Originally thought to be a simple bookworm, he'd turned out to possess brilliant talent. His abilities in poetry and teaching had been proven over the past year. Family members could only sigh in admiration at Old Master's judgment, believing he must have orchestrated this match knowing the bookworm was extraordinary.

Given the current situation, with him finally being pushed forward by the First Branch, family members initially maintained a wait-and-see attitude. Whether it was Wenxing and Wenji from the Second and Third Branches, or relatives originally close to the First Branch, all quietly watched to see if he was equally profound in business matters. Had Old Master really been so incredibly shrewd and partial as to find Su Tan'er a husband exceptional in both literary and commercial talents—even as a live-in son-in-law?

This quiet observation lasted about three days before dissolving into laughter.

Despite his stunning poetic talent, this Ning Yi was ultimately an amateur in business—and an amateur with scholarly pretensions at that.

He clearly wanted to do well, but his methods were frankly clumsy. For several consecutive days, he rode out each morning in a carriage to inspect various shops and warehouses in the city—apparently having inquired about Su Tan'er's daily routine and now imitating it step by step.

In fact, with the Su family's foundation, each shop had relatively trustworthy people in charge. The owner didn't need to visit daily. Su Tan'er did so to prepare for eventually taking over the entire Su family, thus holding herself to extremely high standards. When problems arose in the shops, Su Tan'er could resolve them on the spot. But after Ning Yi maintained this routine for several days—though he mostly just observed and tried not to speak—a few of his casually "casual" questions leaked out, revealing his pretense to onlookers.

The gap between professionals and amateurs was simply too vast. When it came to specialized knowledge, Ning Yi's questions might seem simple, but to insiders they appeared laughable. For instance, while Wenxing and Wenji might not manage shops well, they were familiar with the cloth business. These past two days they'd laughed themselves sick over an incident at the cloth shop: Ning Yi had mistaken the aromatic herbs used for storing fabric for dyeing materials, and had very kindly instructed a clerk to sweep the scattered "dye ingredients" back into bags to avoid waste. The clerk had been mortified. Within a day, this story had spread throughout the entire Su compound.

Then his speech to the managers that morning also leaked out. Focusing on the Imperial Merchant position was probably Su Tan'er's idea—nothing wrong there. But the words Ning Yi had spoken, though they sounded passionate and stirring with his "quality goods will always be quality goods" philosophy, were actually full of idealistic scholarly naivety. Even the First Branch's own managers would shake their heads when repeating it. They couldn't say it was completely wrong, but to claim that having good products meant having everything... that could only be described as scholarly pretension.

That this live-in son-in-law didn't understand business was now certain—though not many had originally thought he "might understand" anyway. After a few days, this merely confirmed expectations.

However, despite Ning Yi's minor blunders in his first days managing the First Branch's commercial affairs becoming after-dinner gossip, one thing was actually being accomplished. The First Branch's managers had begun vigorously promoting their years of preparation for the Imperial Merchant position. Professional and capable as these managers were, within just three or four days, news that the Su family had created new fabric and prepared for years for the Imperial Merchant role had spread explosively through Jiangning's textile industry. Combined with their previous high-profile stance, now everyone in the cloth business knew that due to Su Boyong's stabbing, the Su family—or at least this woman Su Tan'er—was preparing to reveal her full capabilities, gambling everything in a desperate bid.

If she won, leveraging years of preparation to perfectly resolve the Imperial Merchant matter, she'd secure the First Branch and even formally establish her position as next family head despite being a woman. If she lost, then she'd truly lost, because Su Boyong was already stabbed anyway—there wasn't much left to lose.

"To have such spirit, even while ill... she truly is a woman who puts men to shame. If we have to face her as an opponent in the future, the pressure will be immense." In the teahouse, Xue Yan set down his cup with a wry smile. "Ah Jin, if we'd known earlier, perhaps having you marry into the Su family would have been a good match."

Today Xue Yan had come to the teahouse with Xue Jin and several clan brothers for tea and conversation. Being in the same cloth business, they naturally discussed the Su family's recent upheaval. Xue Jin shook his head helplessly with a bitter smile: "I see clearly now—I couldn't handle her. If she married into our Xue family, I'd naturally treat her well. But if I married in there, wouldn't I never be able to hold my head up?"

Xue Jin had once been arrogant but now seemed remarkably humble. The clan brothers couldn't help laughing and teasing him before inevitably turning the conversation to Su Tan'er's current husband.

"Such a pity—even with stunning poetic talent, he's still an embroidered pillow, useless in practice. With the family in crisis, what use is poetry without official rank?"

"As for business, looking at Ning Liheng's recent performance, I fear Su Tan'er is propping him up from behind, scheming and planning."

"I bet her hand was behind his previous reputation, too. Talented bookworms aren't that rare, but it takes real maneuvering to build that kind of fame. It's clear now she's just trying to build up her husband's name..."

Hearing them speculate wildly, Xue Jin hesitated before finally shaking his head: "Don't make assumptions. That Ning Liheng isn't simple—he's no fool."

After nearly becoming a stepping stone when Ning Yi first gained fame, Xue Jin had learned humility—definite progress. But after Xue Yan nodded agreement, he still patted his shoulder: "Humility is good, but you're being overly cautious, little brother. That Ning Liheng certainly isn't stupid, but his talents are in poetry. Literati are often eccentric—that's understandable. But if he were truly a commercial genius, why would he have married into the Su family in the first place? Life isn't a storybook."

He paused slightly: "This Ning Yi, in my view, has average ability in commerce at best, perhaps with more potential. He's clever, but regardless, he's new to this field. Making some jokes is normal. However, laughing aside, one point must be clear... No matter what Ning Yi does, Su Tan'er stands behind him. That woman isn't simple. Regardless of how many jokes Ning Yi creates, if they secure the Imperial Merchant position, everything else will blow away like wind. Su Tan'er making a big show of competing for Imperial Merchant while letting her husband create laughingstocks might be part of her calculations. The Imperial Merchant position is something we want a share of too. Everyone, don't laugh so hard you miss them taking all the benefits."

After Xue Yan said this, everyone's expressions grew serious. In the Imperial Merchant matter, the Xue, Su, and Wu families had all revealed their intentions. Each had their own connections and advantages, but overall, the Su family's preparations were indeed the most thorough. Xue Jin shook his head, glancing at his brothers: "I told you so." Before he finished speaking, his gaze moved downward. "Hm?"

Xue Yan also looked down to see Ning Yi carrying a box through the crowd below. He had no servants or maids with him, just walking alone. Whether wandering on impulse or with specific purpose was unclear. They saw him ask several street vendors some questions before heading toward a nearby compound gate.

"That's..."

"Lord He Fang of the Imperial Textile Bureau's... residence."

"Is he joking? He just came alone?"

The Xue family members looked at each other in bewilderment. To compete for Imperial Merchant status required connections. From what Xue Yan and Xue Jin knew, the Su family had already opened many channels in the Imperial Textile Bureau and reportedly maintained close relationships with several officials. But for passing the Imperial Merchant matter, the three officials with the most influence in the Imperial Textile Bureau were Lord He Fang, Lord Han Chaoying, and Director Dong Decheng. Of these, the Su family had only truly connected with Han Chaoying.

Director Dong Decheng's attitude remained consistently ambiguous—he wouldn't easily take a stance. With this year's changing situation, He Fang also hadn't declared his position. He should still favor the previous medium-sized merchants continuing as Imperial Merchants according to old precedent. Now several families all wanted to court him. With the situation growing increasingly sensitive, He Fang had simply stopped receiving visitors about this matter. Two days ago the Xue family had been turned away at his door. They were still thinking of solutions, yet unexpectedly Ning Yi had just wandered over like this.

Alone, looking almost like a spontaneous decision—because he stood at the gate seeming to think for quite a while before finally knocking. Then he disappeared under the eaves. The Xue family members upstairs couldn't help discussing again, some mocking.

"Impossible."

"How could he possibly get a meeting..."

"Coming alone as a mere son-in-law, thinking to represent the Su family in discussing such business—does he really think he's been given a vital mission..."

"Probably wants to achieve something to show off."

No one believed Ning Yi could meet He Fang. But after half an incense stick's time passed without him emerging, their curiosity grew. One of them went down to investigate and returned trying to stifle a laugh.

"He's just talking with the gatekeeper! That man has the patience of a saint—he's going to annoy the poor gatekeeper to death."

Hearing this, the group understood.

"Haha, what scholarly pretension. Does he really think he can grind his way into a meeting with Lord He Fang?"

"It's not unheard of," Xue Yan frowned, then shook his head with a smile. "Though it usually only happens in stories..."

They continued to watch as the scene unfolded. Ning Yi wasn't aggressive or unreasonable; he just kept talking. The gatekeeper, seemingly defeated, eventually just ignored him and let him ramble on.

Coming for tea only to witness such an odd scene was truly interesting. Thus they talked, laughed, and discussed, watching from upstairs for over an hour. Some wondered if he might actually grind out a path, but eventually Ning Yi emerged carrying his box. He looked back at the compound gate, shook his head, and left. Someone went down to inquire, returning with an expression mixing amusement and admiration.

"Talked with the gatekeeper for over an hour, wearing him out completely... Said he'd continue visiting tomorrow..."

Xue Yan sat stunned for a long moment: "This bookworm..."

Xue Jin was silent for a while: "Seems he's determined to show some business achievements too... This foolish method..."

"If he really keeps grinding like this, Lord He sooner or later will have to meet him once..."

"What's the point of that?"

So they said, but by now, most of their mocking expressions had faded. This was the kind of "fool's method" their fathers had told them about in childhood—the idea that business wasn't about cleverness, but about grounded, solid patience. Of course, few who actually tried it ever succeeded. But seeing Ning Yi's persistence today, they couldn't help but feel a flicker of grudging respect.

Someone clearly not at home, yet waiting however long for business was one thing. But when someone clearly refused meetings, this fellow apparently hadn't thoroughly investigated everything before coming to torment the gatekeeper for an hour. The stubbornness of bookworms was truly frightening...

Whether this faint hope would succeed remained to be seen. By now it was afternoon. While they gossiped in the teahouse, Ning Yi had already walked through nearby streets, putting the matter behind him. Turning a corner, ahead lay the main Zhu's restaurant. Ning Yi entered and went upstairs. Having spent the last hour testing the gatekeeper's patience, he hadn't eaten lunch and now came to replenish his energy. After two small dishes arrived, the one bringing the final bowl of egg soup was a woman in emerald green. Ning Yi nodded to her.

Before the city gates closed, he'd said he'd accompany Nie Yunzhu to apologize to Old Qin in a few days. But due to Su Boyong's matter, this was their first meeting since then. Ning Yi was about to apologize when he realized Nie Yunzhu showed no sign of blame. From the moment she'd arrived, she'd been looking him up and down with worried eyes. Then she spoke first, asking softly: "Liheng, are you... alright?"

"Hm?"

(End of Chapter)
