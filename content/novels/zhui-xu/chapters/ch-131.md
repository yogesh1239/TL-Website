Every banquet like this invariably began with tedium...

Lord Dong summarized the various events in Jiangning over the past year and his expectations for the coming year—the same platitudes recycled annually with different packaging. Given the increasingly peculiar circumstances this year, he spoke in vague terms about many things, though in truth, most of those seated here likely understood the real situation of Jiangning's textile industry far better than this Lord Dong.

"Tonight we're having crab¹..." Wang Wenzhuo of the Wang family murmured in the flickering lamplight, both witness to and participant in this evening's events, the aroma already wafting up from below as he turned to confer with a steward beside him. "What do you think about tonight's matters?"

The Wang family steward replied in a low voice: "Naturally we still hope the Su family will emerge victorious, and from the looks of it, there shouldn't be any major problems."

"The Wu and Xue families aren't pushovers either—look over there, those two families don't seem particularly nervous, and the Su family... frankly, this Ning Liheng makes me feel somewhat discouraged..."

The Wang family was a small-to-medium merchant house in Jiangning with a history of good cooperation with the Su family, so naturally they hoped the Sus would secure the Imperial Merchant position—it would certainly benefit them as well—yet as Wang Wenzhuo glanced at Ning Yi, he felt this was perhaps the only unreliable element. The Wang steward smiled knowingly: "He's just a scholar—no need to mind him. As long as we know Second Miss Su is really managing things behind the scenes, that's enough. Tonight the Su family has come well-prepared and now they merely need to reel in their lines, so there should be no problems."

"As long as the Xue family doesn't pull any tricks, I'm relatively reassured..."

The Xue and Su families had poor relations, so the Wang family naturally held little goodwill toward the Xues. Regarding Su Boyong's assassination attempt last month, most insiders privately suspected the Xue family was behind it, though such accusations were extremely serious and naturally couldn't be voiced publicly.

Amid the private conversations, Lord Dong's speech was approaching the main topic as everyone quieted down, waiting in the aromatic atmosphere of music and delicacies for the evening's most crucial business to begin.

********************

"...Today I invite everyone to jointly appreciate the newly woven Snow Pattern Gauze from our Qi family. The silk threads used in this gauze are difficult to weave, but once completed, it is light, thin, and resilient. Please observe—this gauze is nearly transparent with natural white snow-line patterns, and we use special looms to control the thread count..."

Night had not long fallen, and everyone in the Green Ripples Tower² had taken their seats. Among the various tables, a textile merchant representative was now showcasing a bolt of gauze in the center of the venue. The round tables were already laden with fruits, pastries, and other refreshments. After he finished speaking, the various merchants and officials discussed briefly before Lord He Fang stood with a smile, announcing that anyone interested could come forward to evaluate the fabric. Representatives from each table approached the center to examine the gauze quality up close and engage in pleasant conversation with the Qi family representative.

Tonight's gathering concerned the annual tribute cloth, the Imperial Merchant position, and each textile house's future standing in Jiangning. Of course, the various exchanges wouldn't be limited to competing for the Imperial Merchant title alone—those confident in their products would showcase them, potentially attracting interest from other merchants for future cooperation or other matters. The presentations had just begun, and after the Qi family representative finished speaking, people would generally gather around with smiles to take a look, while others sat chatting at the periphery.

"This gauze is quite nice..."

"The thread-splitting method—the Wu family had it years ago..."

"But the Wu family's cloth production isn't high."

"This Qi family is worth befriending..."

Detailed discussions couldn't happen now, of course, but interested parties had already examined the quality and would have the entire evening to slowly consider and negotiate. Even those without particular interest—like the Su, Wu, and Xue families—would send representatives to evaluate and offer pleasant comments. Once this began, the atmosphere grew lively.

After the Qi family, Lord He called another household to discuss their year's business. Everyone listened attentively as some merchants would also reveal certain information they wished to share at such gatherings—potentially serving as weathervanes for the coming year. After speaking, this family didn't present any new fabrics, and then came the next...

Once this process began, everyone grew somewhat serious, temporarily setting aside their focus on the Imperial Merchant position to concentrate on the immediate matters and discuss beneficial opportunities. The Wang family had taken interest in a particular fabric, and Wang Wenzhuo conferred with his steward before deciding to explore their intentions during the banquet.

About twenty-odd merchant families were participating in this gathering. Each household would certainly have something to say, though not all would present products—it was entirely voluntary. When the gathering was about halfway through, Lord He announced: "...Please have the Lü family come forward to discuss this year's events." The whispered conversations sparked by a recent fabric display gradually subsided as everyone quietly awaited the Lü family's presentation.

The Lü family representative then emerged to give a brief summary while an attendant brought over a brocade box. He patted it with a smile: "...The Lü family's Tea-Scented Silk has received everyone's support in the past. Recently, we've applied the Tea-Scented Silk concept to create a new fabric that hasn't been named yet. We're presenting it for everyone's evaluation—please, esteemed seniors, offer your guidance..."

He opened the box and had his subordinates present a black fabric before the assembly. Gasps of admiration arose as Wang Wenzhuo's mouth fell open. After a few moments, he and his steward almost simultaneously turned their gazes aside, casually observing the reactions of various merchants—primarily those from the Su, Xue, and Wu families. The Lü family's Tea-Scented Silk was already famous in Jiangning, and their presentation at this gathering could potentially threaten the positions of these three leading families. After a moment, he withdrew his gaze and exchanged knowing smiles with his steward.

"It seems all three families have trump cards—they're not concerned about the Lü family."

"As it should be."

"Still, this black cloth is truly impressive. I'll go take a look."

Wang Wenzhuo rose to his feet. Among the seated merchants, many had been observing the expressions of the Wu, Xue, and Su families when each new item appeared. The Wu family members had maintained their gracious smiles throughout, courteously discussing each presentation before approaching with questions—unchanged even now. The Xue family also appeared supremely confident, as did the Su family. The substitute head of the First Branch, the top scholar Ning Yi, kept his right hand pressed on the lid of a brocade box on the table, his fingers tapping leisurely in a display of quiet confidence. Shopkeeper Liao wasn't speaking with him at this moment but was instead exchanging pleasant words with a nearby manager before rising to approach the display, accompanied by Su Zhongkan.

The Lü family's fabric couldn't shake the positions of these three families, but within Jiangning it was already considered quite excellent, creating a small climax in the gathering. Wu Chenghou of the Wu family had also emerged to discuss the fabric with Su Zhongkan for a while, offering high praise. After the free viewing period ended, the black cloth was placed at the front of the floor so everyone could see it throughout the remainder of the banquet.

When the next family emerged, the discussions hadn't yet ceased. These conversations gradually subsided as everyone entered the time for other merchants. The afterglow of the Lü family's black cloth lingered until several more families had presented. When He Fang announced the name of the Xue family's "Great River Cloth Shop," the atmosphere in the banquet hall was suddenly severed as if by a knife. The moment almost everyone had been anticipating this evening had finally arrived.

At the round table, Ning Yi's tapping fingers stilled. Shopkeeper Liao and the others straightened in their seats. Xue Yan smiled and approached with a wooden box, beginning to recount the Xue family's successes over the past year. The second floor's great hall fell utterly silent as everyone focused on what the Xue family would present and the Su family's attitude. When the Xue family finally unveiled a new purple fabric radiating nobility, the very air in the space seemed to freeze for a dozen breaths. People waited in absolute silence for others' reactions, most looking toward the Su family, even the Xue family members casting glances in their direction. The Su family shopkeepers examined it for a while before exchanging meaningful looks among themselves.

One second, two seconds... Finally, Shopkeeper Liao surveyed the room, adjusted his robes, and rose with a smile, preparing to approach for a closer look. As he took his first step, Ning Yi frowned slightly, his fingers dropping as he leaned back in his chair. Only then did the surrounding whispers suddenly explode into a cacophony as everyone gradually stood to move forward.

"The Su family showed no reaction?"

"What's going on?"

"The Xue family has no follow-up move?"

"The Su family has been preparing for years—it seems merely assassinating Su Boyong wasn't enough. Su Tan'er hasn't fallen..."

"This time the Su family's all-in gamble has paid off..."

"The finale position³—the Imperial Merchant will likely go to the Su family..."

"The Wu family is still hard to say, but if they had intentions, based on past precedent, the Wu family should have taken the finale position..."

"Accumulated strength finally unleashed—this is what true accumulated strength looks like..."

"Su Yu can finally rest easy entrusting the family to his granddaughter..."

Amid the various whispered discussions, everyone approached with smiles. As one of Jiangning's three great textile families, people would still give them face despite their surprise, and the scene immediately grew lively. Of course, within this liveliness, everyone harbored different feelings. Lively as it was, after the Xue family placed their purple fabric at the front, Xue Yan returned to his seat with an unreadable expression, tilting his head to speak quietly with his brother.

"I'm thinking Su Tan'er might actually secure the Imperial Merchant position tonight..."

"Just now Su Zhongkan and Su Yunfang's expressions turned rather strange, hehe..."

The banquet remained awash in massive confusion and discussion. He Fang stood and waved his hand without managing to suppress much of it, then routinely announced for the next family to come forward as Wu Chenghou rose from his seat with a smile, bringing a brocade box to the front. The crowd stirred with slight bewilderment, but Wu Chenghou had already begun speaking cheerfully.

The rear grew somewhat quieter as Wang Wenzhuo glanced at Wu Chenghou with a frown: "Why is the Wu family right after the Xue family?"

"Indeed..." The Wang family steward nodded, equally puzzled, then tilted his head to continue their earlier conversation. "Regardless, as long as the Su family secures the Imperial Merchant position, their hearts will settle. If Su Tan'er can't take over the family business, the consequences would be dire..."

"I'm already prepared for after today..."

They spoke urgently about what they'd been discussing while Wu Chenghou's speech above remained rather dry, and though most people listened somewhat, their confusion remained. Nearby, the Xue brothers frowned and whispered: "Heh, I've been thinking the Su family might have overthought Su Boyong's assassination..."

"Their reaction was intense, but it's understandable. I just hope they make some mistakes in the end..."

"I still don't quite believe they can produce fabric that surpasses everyone else, though they've certainly publicized it impressively..."

"With Su Tan'er ill, those managers can only do so much. Regardless, when it comes to the final moment, we'll... Whoa."

Xue Jin's words cut off abruptly as his gaze fixed ahead in sudden shock. Though everyone had been chatting, they'd still been half-listening to Wu Chenghou's speech when a golden brocade suddenly unfurled before everyone. Wu Chenghou's words might have been uninteresting, delivered casually, but when that golden brocade spread open, it instantly captured everyone's attention.

Xue Jin and Xue Yan both stared for a long moment while Wang Wenzhuo, who'd been speaking with his steward, involuntarily straightened his body and craned his neck. After watching for a while, Xue Yan seemed to forget his half-hearted conversation with his brother, then exclaimed with feeling: "The Wu family really is... the dog that bites doesn't bark⁴..."

"The Wu family has brought out their trump card too..."

"This fabric... no, wait..." Xue Yan suddenly realized something and looked aside sharply!

At the front, beside Wu Chenghou, the golden fabric unfurled with its matching gold-dyed silk threads presented as raw materials from the box, the color brilliantly eye-catching and magnificently luxurious. Wu Chenghou continued speaking: "This Brilliant Gold Brocade was created using a special dyeing formula discovered by our Wu family, with the weaving process handled by the Luo Divine Needle⁵ technique, therefore..."

He paused slightly, smiling as he stopped his introduction. In the hall that had fallen silent at some unknown moment, a figure in green robes had already crossed several tables—Ning Yi, who had risen without anyone noticing and had been gazing at the golden brocade beside Wu Chenghou. While everyone else remained seated, he slowly walked closer before pausing slightly as the entire assembly turned their eyes toward him. He Fang considered for a moment before standing slightly: "Nephew Ning, the Wu family hasn't finished speaking yet—it's not time to approach. Please return to your seat first?" Given their usual cordial relationship of "nephew" and "lord," He Fang's tone remained friendly.

Yet there was no response.

In the profound silence, people's gazes began shifting between Ning Yi and the Wu family members as some gradually grasped certain implications, then more understood even more...

"Something's wrong..."

"What's happening..."

"Wrong, wrong, this is all wrong..."

"The Wu family..."

"The Su family has a problem..."

Certain realizations suddenly avalanched through the crowd like a snowball gathering mass, yet for a moment it seemed merely an atmospheric shift—no one spoke, but the complexity in their exchanged glances deepened, growing ever more intricate. The atmosphere grew restless, as if voices would erupt at any moment, and Shopkeeper Liao had already approached, trying to get Ning Yi to return. Wu Chenghou gazed at Ning Yi for a long moment, maintaining his slightly confused smile before letting out a soft "heh."

"Ah, no matter. If Nephew Ning wishes to look, by all means come look. No matter at all. Speaking of which, I chatted with Nephew Ning a few days ago about something—our family also has new fabric to present, and if Nephew Ning feels poetic inspiration, I'd like to request a poem for it. It needn't be too excellent, just borrowing Nephew Ning's fame. In any case, since this fabric is already presented, I needn't introduce it further. The Luo Divine Needle's craftsmanship is certainly worth boasting about. Come, come, everyone please don't stand on ceremony, please offer your guidance. Hehe, I won't say more..."

Several managers close to the Wu family stood up, but for the moment, few people spoke. Barely audible whispers threaded through the space.

"Something's happened..."

Wang Wenzhuo frowned, then rubbed his forehead, his expression growing incomprehensibly complex.

"Is it... the Wu family?" Xue Yan leaned back in his chair somewhat incredulously, then laughed with similar complexity. "Heh."

Ning Yi and Shopkeeper Liao stood at the center of everyone's attention. Shopkeeper Liao said something, but Ning Yi merely frowned without speaking, simply looking at Wu Chenghou and the approaching Wu Qilong and Wu Qihao brothers with their smiles, his gaze sometimes seeming lost, not knowing where to look. Only once did he seem to grit his teeth and attempt to continue forward, but Shopkeeper Liao caught his shoulder.

In the moments that followed, the entire hall seemed cleaved into two parts by some invisible force—one pale, one clamorous. Ning Yi finally retreated to sit at his table, gazing only at the center of the venue with an expression so complex he seemed lost in thought. Of course, anyone in business had likely seen such a look before—the eyes of someone who'd poured their lifeblood into commerce only to be suddenly swallowed by its darkness, complex beyond words, disbelieving yet speechless. Many had already guessed at certain things, and even those uncertain of their suspicions had at least sensed the atmosphere.

Buzzing, buzzing, buzzing...

The Wu family members returned to their seats with the golden brocade placed at the front, yet that sense of division didn't depart, and whether in the pale or clamorous scenes, the fast-forward sensation never ceased. Everyone awaited the final conclusion of certain matters, watching the still-smiling Wu family, watching Ning Yi sitting silently at the Su family table, the several shopkeepers maintaining composure while exchanging whispers. The Xue family had also guessed much, and Xue Sheng frowned and sighed to his two sons.

"The dog that truly bites doesn't bark⁶—this is the Wu family's fearsome quality. Silent and still, watching everyone else create commotion while they quietly turn clouds with one hand and summon rain with the other... Heh, the Su family made such tremendous noise during this period, all for nothing. This move is truly too ruthless. The three-legged balance⁷ of Jiangning's textile industry will cease to exist. After the Su family falls, we must take this as a warning... Sigh..."

Though the Xue and Su families had poor relations in the past, his sigh now held no trace of schadenfreude. It was as if witnessing the twilight of an era—the burning clouds reddening the horizon, the fading crimson about to shatter, tinged with faint regret and melancholy.

Not because the Su family had failed, but because behind it all, the Wu family had proven truly too formidable. Xue Yan looked at Ning Yi sitting there—over the past month, this scholar had likely played the smallest role, feeling like a sheep standing among wolves. He'd originally had no real means of resistance, and Xue Yan had previously found the situation laughable, but now that silhouette appeared particularly lonely—he still couldn't do anything, hadn't done anything, yet somehow seemed pitiable now.

Finally, at a certain moment, He Fang's voice rang out, pushing everything toward its conclusion...

**Footnotes:**

¹ **Eating Crab (吃蟹)**: A Chinese metaphor meaning to handle a dangerous or delicate situation. Just as eating crab requires skill to avoid injury from its claws and shell, dealing with complex business or political matters requires careful navigation. Wang Wenzhuo uses this metaphor to indicate the evening will involve risky maneuvering.

² **Green Ripples Tower (绿漪楼)**: A prestigious venue for merchant gatherings. In Chinese culture, conducting business in elegant settings like towers and pavilions demonstrated sophistication and face, making the venue choice significant for this high-stakes meeting.

³ **Finale Position (压轴)**: In Chinese performances and presentations, the finale position is reserved for the most important act. Traditionally, appearing last indicates supreme confidence and superiority. The discussion about who should "rightfully" have the finale position reflects the merchants' understanding of the established hierarchy.

⁴ **"A dog that bites doesn't bark" (咬人的狗不叫)**: A Chinese proverb warning that the most dangerous threats are often silent. Unlike aggressive competitors who make noise, the truly formidable ones strike without warning. The Wu family's quiet maneuvering exemplifies this principle.

⁵ **Luo Divine Needle (骆神针)**: Likely referring to a master weaver or weaving technique. In Chinese textile tradition, exceptional weavers were often given poetic titles combining their surname with terms suggesting divine skill.

⁶ **Same proverb repeated**: This repetition by Xue Sheng emphasizes the Wu family's calculated strategy of remaining quiet while others fought openly.

⁷ **Three-legged balance (三足鼎立)**: Literally "three-legged tripod," a classical Chinese metaphor for a stable balance of power among three parties. The phrase originates from ancient bronze tripod vessels and implies that removing one leg would cause the entire structure to collapse.
