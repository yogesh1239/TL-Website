"I hear you made quite a scene last night..."

He'd thought last night's affairs had been handled discreetly, but when he returned from his morning run, everyone in the household already knew. After all, his actions might have fooled others, but naturally couldn't escape the notice of the nearby Su family members and Li Pin. They'd treated it as an amusing story. Apparently Su Wending and the others had come by this morning to share the tale, and now Su Tan'er brought it up.

"Five hundred taels in one go—quite the generous hand, Young Master." Xiao Chan said with a grin as she brought over a bowl of rice porridge. Beside her, Juan'er turned to whisper to Xing'er: "Spendthrift." Having grown familiar with Ning Yi, this was just teasing. Her voice was perfectly audible to everyone. When Ning Yi raised his spoon threateningly, she laughed and ran away.

"All right, all right. Anyway, my husband rarely goes to places like that. Let's stop teasing him."

Though five hundred taels was indeed a substantial sum, Su Tan'er found last night's events merely amusing and showed no sign of minding. Once everyone had settled down to eat, she asked casually: "So my husband knows that Yuan Jin'er?"

Ning Yi thought for a moment: "Not very well, but I do know the other one."

Xiao Chan's eyes lit up: "The one in white who sang Water Melody Prelude? Young Master Wending said she sang beautifully this morning, using a new singing style. I thought it might be the style you taught, Young Master, but when I tried singing it, Young Master Wending said it wasn't the same." She laughed as she spoke, humming a few bars with obvious self-satisfaction: "Is it as nice as what you taught me?"

"She's quite accomplished." Ning Yi picked up a pickled green bean and shook his head with a smile. "You're an amateur, Xiao Chan. Can't compare."

"Oh." Xiao Chan pursed her lips and lowered her head to eat her porridge. Xing'er asked from the side: "So who is she?"

"Must be some young lady who admires my husband's talent." Su Tan'er smiled.

"Her name is Nie Yunzhu. Very talented. I saved her once before." Ning Yi answered, then continued eating his porridge while recounting that morning when Nie Yunzhu had chased a chicken into the river—from her clumsy pursuit of the chicken to how he'd been caught in the crossfire and slapped. Everyone in the room wore odd expressions.

"Is this the same... Nie Yunzhu who sells preserved eggs and had some entanglement with Gu Yanzhen?"

"Gu Yanzhen... tsk..." Ning Yi shrugged noncommittally.

By now breakfast was finishing. After discussing a few more trivial matters about Nie Yunzhu, Su Tan'er occasionally glanced at Ning Yi, then smiled lightly: "My husband speaks so highly of her. If there's an opportunity, I'd quite like to meet this Miss Yunzhu..."

"Not many people recognized her last night. Best not to spread it around."

"I understand."

There was much more that could have been said, but it was time for Su Tan'er to head out for business. She could only suppress some thoughts for now, looking at Ning Yi who seemed unchanged as always. These past few days had grown increasingly busy. She left in the morning with Chan'er, Juan'er, and Xing'er. Ning Yi planned to go organize his small laboratory in the courtyard next to the academy. Near noon, as he left the courtyard and circled toward the academy, he saw two carriages stopped at the now-closed academy gates. They were from Prince Kang's mansion. The siblings Zhou Pei and Zhou Junwu stood with several guards, having just discovered no one was there after knocking. Among the guards was Lu Agui, who called out a surprised greeting as they approached.

"We just arrived and found the academy closed. We were about to head to the Su residence, but what a coincidence meeting you here."

"Heh, these days are tense. They might close the city gates at any time, so the academy held a meeting yesterday and temporarily closed."

After exchanging pleasantries, Ning Yi looked at Zhou Pei and Zhou Junwu before asking with a smile: "What brings Brother Lu here? Heh... not another challenge match, I hope?" He glanced teasingly at the siblings.

"Wouldn't dare." Lu Agui quickly shook his head. "We're here to..."

"My sister and I are here to take you as our master!"¹ Zhou Junwu interrupted before Lu Agui could finish, putting on an extremely sincere expression. Zhou Pei beside him froze, looking slightly embarrassed. She glanced at her brother, then at Ning Yi: "I... I still have questions to ask..."

Ning Yi looked at her and couldn't help laughing. Lu Agui coughed awkwardly beside them, knowing Ning Yi's temperament and trying to smooth things over. Ning Yi thought for a moment and looked at Zhou Pei: "I heard you're very good at arithmetic?"

Zhou Pei looked at him, blinking as she thought for a while before letting out a soft "Mm."

"I'll ask you a few simple questions. If you answer correctly, you can ask me questions. How about it?"

"...All right." Zhou Pei hesitated before nodding, then turned. "I'll get paper and brush."

"No need. These are really simple." Ning Yi smiled. When Zhou Pei turned back doubtfully, he held up one finger. "Tell me, what's this?"

The young girl looked at the finger, then at Ning Yi, then back at the finger and Ning Yi again. Her gaze shifted twice as she furrowed her brow, probably thinking about how Ning Yi might try to trick or deceive her. After quite a while, she finally spoke cautiously: "Master Chen once said that one is one, two is two, right is right, wrong is wrong. Anyone who tries to confuse these basic things is just playing sophistry..."

She spoke slowly with a righteous air, watching Ning Yi's reaction. Ning Yi's finger wavered slightly in the air: "Uh, did someone say that? Who's Master Chen?"

"Chen Qiulan, Master Chen, is a guest scholar² at Prince Kang's mansion and a great Confucian scholar of our time. He often associates with my master." Lu Agui explained from the side.

"Oh." Ning Yi nodded, finger still extended. "Makes sense. But after all that, what number is this exactly?"

"...One." After a pause, her answer was short and forceful.

"Oh." Ning Yi nodded and held up two fingers. "What's this?"

"Two." This time without hesitation, the girl lifted her chin as if to say 'let's see what tricks you can pull.'

Then three fingers: "What's one plus one?"

"Three!" The answer was still crisp and clear.

Ning Yi withdrew his hand and smiled. In front, Zhou Pei, beside them Zhou Junwu and Lu Agui were still unconsciously waiting for Ning Yi's fourth question. Seeing Ning Yi's expression, Zhou Junwu went "Ah" as he realized. Zhou Pei blinked: "What, aren't you going to continue... uh... gah?"

Zhou Junwu and Lu Agui both started laughing. Only then did the girl realize, her face flushing bright red: "You, you, you... you cheated... how could you..."

"Heh, you were overthinking it... Be polite now. Or... are you going to welch on the bet?"

"I... I don't welch on bets. What do you want?"

"Nothing really. Just joking around. But now I don't have to answer your questions, right?" Ning Yi shrugged at Lu Agui. "They must be very hard. Good thing I don't have to answer." Lu Agui laughed along. Zhou Junwu raised his hand, his eyes practically glowing: "Me, me, me! I don't need to ask questions. Master Ning, can I take you as my master?"

"The academy's right there. Anyone who wants to can enter. Though it's closed now, if you find it interesting, just pay the tuition and attend classes when it reopens."

Ning Yi spoke casually. Lu Agui said quietly: "Actually, if possible, Prince Kang hopes Liheng could teach at the mansion, preferably with a guest scholar position. I know Liheng doesn't like official positions, but this guest scholar role has no obligations—just collecting a monthly stipend. What do you think?"

"How does Prince Kang know about me?"

"It's a long story. Actually, Prince Kang has only heard of Liheng's reputation. This request comes from my master. If possible, he hopes you'll take on the two young royals and teach them something useful. Of course, the guest scholar position is entirely up to you."

Ning Yi thought about it: "Then... I'll have to decline the kind offer. I don't know that much, and having two more students is fine—they can come to classes and I'll teach what I can. But going to the prince's mansion... no thanks. I have an odd temperament. Telling stories to a crowd is fine, but teaching individually? I really wouldn't know what to teach."

Zhou Junwu tugged at Lu Agui's sleeve, then declared happily: "I think the academy's better too! And sister... sister?"

He turned to look at his sister, who was still sulking silently after losing the bet. But Zhou Junwu remained cheerful, turning back: "It'll be more fun when my sister and I come to the academy." Apparently he usually found studying at home or attending various scholars' private schools boring and couldn't wait to try somewhere new. Lu Agui considered: "Since Liheng says so, I'll report back accordingly. It shouldn't be a big problem. Though there will probably be one or two attendants, but of course they won't disturb your teaching."

"I understand." Ning Yi nodded. They walked along with the carriages, the guards following behind. After a while, Ning Yi asked: "What did Brother Lu mean by 'it's a long story'?"

Lu Agui thought before saying quietly: "Actually... the young prince and princess happened to overhear what you discussed with Li Pin, Li Dexin, in the classroom a few days ago. I don't know exactly what you said, but..."

He explained the whole story, and Ning Yi finally understood what had happened.

"My master has been pondering your words these past few days. I can see he values them highly. Sometimes he says you're heterodox and outrageous, but overall, you seem to have hit the mark. If he didn't have matters today, he would have accompanied the young royals personally. Heh, knowing my master's temperament, he'll definitely want to debate with you. But having the young royals take you as their master was his personal instruction. Today we're just seeking your opinion. My master said given your temperament, the young royals should come personally to show proper respect. When it comes to the formal ceremony, it won't be this simple—Prince Kang will have to appear too. That's proper protocol, so you should be prepared, Liheng..."

Lu Agui spoke with a smile, then brought up another matter.

"Oh, about closing the city gates that Liheng mentioned—it'll be these next day or two. Today's the thirteenth, tomorrow the fourteenth, and the fifteenth is the Ghost Festival. Every household will worship their ancestors. Many outside the city have lost family members and might cause trouble..."

Before he could finish, urgent bells and gongs sounded from the east side of Jiangning. The carriage stopped briefly as everyone turned to look in that direction. Layers of buildings blocked their view, but in that moment the entire city seemed to grow quieter. A sense of oppression rolled in from the east. Then faint sounds of shouting and chaos began to grow louder.

"Something's happened..."

Near noon on the street, Ning Yi heard Lu Agui murmur...

**FOOTNOTES:**

¹ *Taking a master (拜师): A formal ceremony establishing a master-disciple relationship with lifelong obligations of respect and loyalty, much more serious than simply attending classes*

² *Guest scholar (客卿): A prestigious position where learned men served noble households as advisors and teachers while maintaining relative independence*
