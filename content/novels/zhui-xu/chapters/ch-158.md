Late October brought the cold, and dawn came later with it. Before the rooster's crow, the Su family estate stirred from sleep, gradually coming alive in what would inevitably be a busy and chaotic day following last night's upheaval.

When Ning Yi woke, faint light flickered beyond the window. Xiao Chan had already risen in accordance with his rhythm, her light footsteps carrying through the corridor as she hummed a soft tune while heating water in the small kitchen, her slender shadow dancing across the walls.

Last night's many events had involved all three maids, and by the time Ning Yi and Su Tan'er returned from outside the city, it was already very late before everyone finally slept. Ning Yi possessed the internal energy Lu Hongti had taught him, which benefited his health in daily life and allowed him to recover his spirit with just two hours of sleep, but for little Chan such a schedule inevitably taxed her body. Yet the little maid's spirits seemed good enough, though a moment later he heard her cough softly—whether from the smoke or something else, he couldn't tell.

Not long after Ning Yi lit the oil lamp in his room, Xiao Chan arrived right on cue with hot water. As the door opened, the morning wind wailed through, making the lamplight shudder before she quickly kicked it shut. Having just risen, she wore a thin pink jacket, her hair not yet properly arranged, which only made her look more fresh and charming as she set the basin on its stand and came to the bedside to help him with the mosquito netting.

"The wind's strong this morning and it's a bit cold—it might rain. Will Young Master still go running?"

"Mm, it's not raining yet." Hearing the wind rushing past the eaves outside, Ning Yi looked her up and down before placing his hand on the little maid's forehead, his brow furrowing. Xiao Chan blinked in confusion. "Young Master, what's wrong?"

"You seem to have a cold." Ning Yi got out of bed and draped an outer garment over Xiao Chan's shoulders, then pressed her down to sit on the bedside. After studying her for a moment, he made his decision. "Go back to your room and sleep more. It's cold—pile on another blanket."

Xiao Chan pressed her hand to her own forehead for a long while. "No... no, I'm not hot at all."

"Of course you can't feel it yourself when you have a cold. You only slept at that hour last night, and this morning with such strong wind, you're wearing so little."

He walked to the stand and wrung out a towel to wash his face, his expression serious, while little Chan protested from behind: "It's fine, Xiao Chan is very healthy..."

In truth, though the maids looked delicate, their daily work kept them considerably healthier than ordinary people, and even Su Tan'er was far from being as fragile as typical wealthy young ladies. But Ning Yi wasn't about to argue with her. When little Chan came to take the basin after he finished washing, he simply grasped her hand and pulled her from the room.

Though Xiao Chan and Ning Yi shared deep intimacy of heart and had already experienced much physical contact, having long promised to be his, the matter remained unconfirmed until the young miss truly consummated her marriage with Ning Yi. Being held by the hand like this, she immediately flushed red and didn't dare argue, lowering her head as she followed him out.

The courtyard remained quiet at this hour. Juan'er and Xing'er didn't need to serve the early-rising Ning Yi, and having been exhausted last night, they hadn't yet woken. When Ning Yi pulled Xiao Chan to the bedroom door, she finally protested quietly: "But... there are still things to do. Since I'm already up, I need to heat more water... I'm really not sick..."

Ning Yi smiled and pushed open the door, nudging little Chan inside before pointing at the bed. "Sleep. No backtalk."

Little Chan sat on the bedside wrapped in Ning Yi's outer garment and pouted. "Young Master didn't sleep much either."

Ning Yi laughed. "I'm a first-rate master possessing peerless martial arts. How can some nameless nobody like you compare yourself to the Bloody Butcher? Behave."

Though he wasn't much older himself, he always treated little Chan like a child when they talked, with commands like "behave" and "no backtalk." Little Chan deeply disliked this in her heart—mainly because she didn't want Young Master treating her as a child—yet whenever Ning Yi actually said such things, she could only obediently comply. She stared at him with pursed lips for a moment before finally removing her shoes and, still wrapped in Ning Yi's outer garment, rolling herself into the blankets with only her small face peeking out.

Ning Yi approached the bedside and smiled at the girl's resentful expression, watching her for a long while before finally leaning down and kissing her forehead. Xiao Chan blinked, her little face instantly burning up as she became too dazed to speak.

After Ning Yi turned to extinguish the lamp and left, closing the door behind him, little Chan finally drew her hand from beneath the covers to touch where he'd kissed her forehead, then pressed her palms against her burning cheeks. In the dark, quiet room with the wind sounds from outside penetrating the cooling air, the little maid lay wrapped in her blankets feeling as though Young Master's presence had enveloped every part of her in warmth. Only that dizzy, intoxicated feeling made her wonder if perhaps she really had caught a cold after all...

******************

In truth, Xiao Chan's health was fine with no signs of illness, but she'd been working hard these past days, which Ning Yi had noticed, and now that matters were settled, she deserved some rest.

He returned to his room, carried the basin out to empty it, then went to the small kitchen where the firewood in the stove still burned. When little Chan had said she was already up and needed to heat water, she'd meant to prepare extra for Juan'er and Xing'er. The water would take a while yet to heat, so with nothing else to do, Ning Yi stayed nearby to watch and toss in a few sticks of wood. Then he heard a soft creak from the courtyard as a white figure emerged from a doorway and walked toward him.

In the dim light, the figure's bearing was unmistakably Su Tan'er. She wore a white undershirt and long pants, the white silk trouser legs adorned with two small yellow flowers, moon-white embroidered shoes on her feet—clearly just sleepwear with a long outer coat draped over it, which she held closed with her hands as she approached the small kitchen. When she confirmed it was Ning Yi, she smiled slightly and entered the room, crouching beside him before the stove, probably also feeling the cold. As the firelight illuminated her, it revealed her graceful curves to Ning Yi's eyes.

"Where's Chan'er? I thought I heard her heating water here earlier."

"She didn't sleep long either, so I sent her back to her room to rest."

"It really shouldn't be my husband doing such work..."

Su Tan'er wasn't particularly concerned about pampering the maids, but for Ning Yi this was mainly about not considering going to the kitchen to tend the fire any great matter. He grabbed another stick of dry wood and tossed it in, the fire crackling and popping.

"It's nothing—they've all been tired these past days. And you too, why are you up so early?"

"I..." Su Tan'er crouched there, rising slightly on her toes as she gazed at the firelight, but instead of answering his question, she asked quietly, "Husband's going running again this morning?"

"Mm, it's not raining yet."

"These next few days... perhaps you shouldn't go?"

Su Tan'er glanced at him, and after a moment's thought, Ning Yi understood. Last night's events weren't truly finished yet. The Hundred Blades Alliance lacked the orderly discipline of regular troops, and when they'd finally surrounded Ou Peng and his twenty-some men, the desperados' desperate breakout had allowed four or five to escape through blood and slaughter by the time the authorities arrived. Ou Peng himself had dragged the severely wounded Xi Junyu away in their flight.

The Hundred Blades Alliance's influence throughout the Jiangning area was considerable, and they'd continued the pursuit, but the outcome remained unknown. When those government soldiers arrived, Ning Yi, Su Tan'er, Su Boyong, and the others could only leave as quickly as possible.

Su Tan'er hadn't slept long either, but this matter weighed on her mind, and since she hadn't managed to discuss it with Ning Yi last night, she'd hurried from bed without even changing clothes or arranging her hair, throwing her outer coat over her sleepwear to come warn Ning Yi not to leave the house for a few days until they could assess the situation. Despite how seriously she took this, her manner of speaking remained as casual as everyday conversation. Ning Yi smiled, assuring her the matter posed no real danger and she needn't worry.

In truth, some fierce individuals would immediately launch a counterattack after suffering a loss, catching people off guard. But given yesterday's bizarre circumstances when they'd come to capture him, those people were probably still trying to figure out what had happened. Even if they escaped, they were severely wounded, and their companions would face pursuit from the Hundred Blades Alliance. To move against him now wouldn't be fierce courage but stupidity, and the possibility was minimal. Having tasted the benefits of martial arts training, Ning Yi's confidence had grown considerably, and he wasn't about to hide away for such an unlikely threat.

The two chatted quietly for a while, then laughed about some household trivialities. After the water boiled, Ning Yi dampened the fire somewhat, and Su Tan'er gave him a few more warnings before wrapping her coat tightly and returning to her room. Though her retreating figure looked slender, when she turned back her smile was warm and serene. With so many matters now settled in her heart, the nineteen-year-old girl finally showed the bearing of her nineteen years.

That morning he ran his usual route for exercise, and indeed few people bothered him. When he visited Nie Yunzhu at her small building for conversation, they discussed only matters concerning Zhu's branch location selection and renovation, plus the strong liquor, without mentioning last night's events. But then Yuan Jin'er bounced out lively as ever to tell him about the new poem he'd written, leaving him stunned for a long moment.

Ning Yi hadn't mentioned these matters, but of course Nie Yunzhu knew about all the recent changes in the Su family—she'd naturally been paying attention, and with Yuan Jin'er as her energetic little gossip, Jin'er had heard about last night's poem the very first instant it emerged.

Last night while Ning Yi rushed outside the city, the two women had discussed these events in their chamber, piecing together everything that had happened throughout the evening.

Jin'er had a sharp tongue but a soft heart. Though she had no kind words for Ning Yi himself, she'd come to regard him as very special "one of us" because of Sister Yunzhu—for instance, competing with Ning Yi for Sister Yunzhu's affection was one thing, an internal contradiction, but external matters were something else entirely. As for Yunzhu's mood, that went without saying.

Strictly speaking, they hadn't participated in these events and bore no great connection to them, yet Jin'er chattered on while Yunzhu listened with a smile, occasionally interjecting a comment. Though the small building stood quite far from the Su estate, the two women's mood in that riverside dwelling seemed more celebratory than if they'd won some victory themselves. Since Ning Yi still didn't know about the "Settling Wind Waves" affair, Yuan Jin'er embellished dramatically as she told him about last night's commotion at Changyun Pavilion and Moon Fragrance Tower, reciting "With bamboo staff and straw sandals lighter than horses—who's afraid!" Yunzhu also smiled and occasionally added details.

In the end, Ning Yi could only laugh and spread his hands. "Well, I'm famous again."

"Ten steps, one scheme—Ning Liheng." Yuan Jin'er delivered her critique. "This person is too devious and cunning. Sister Yunzhu, you should stop talking to him, or he'll sell you and you'll still help him count the money."

Yunzhu smiled as she looked at Ning Yi without answering. In truth, having already given her heart to Ning Yi, she figured it wasn't much different from being sold to him, but she trusted Ning Yi's character—the notion that he'd sell her again was something she didn't even need to consider, her heart holding no such misgivings. After a long moment, she smiled at Jin'er: "Hardly anyone recognizes me anymore, so I wouldn't sell for much. If anyone's being sold, it would be more worthwhile to sell you, Jin'er."

Jin'er rolled her eyes. "Hmph, I won't let anyone sell me."

After this leisurely chat and laughter, he left the small building. It was still morning when he arrived home, around the usual time they'd gather to eat breakfast together. These past few months the small courtyard had been rather deserted, but today even on the way back people greeted him frequently, and when he reached the courtyard entrance he found the family's maids and servants gathered in numbers, with voices and conversation drifting from the reception room inside as maids carried tea in and out through the doorway.

When Ning Yi reached the door and glanced inside, he discovered Su Tan'er had already risen and finished her grooming. Several cousin brothers had come to visit, along with two clan uncles. Su Tan'er sat in the lower position, smiling as she conversed with them—her smile composed, intelligent, elegant, the clarity belonging to a nineteen-year-old girl once again concealed beneath the surface.

In the past when Su Tan'er received guests, Ning Yi typically had little presence, but this time his mere appearance in the doorway was enough—before he could even leave, everyone inside noticed him, and in an instant the entire small courtyard fell silent. Su Tan'er turned to see him and rose with a smile: "Husband's back." Ning Yi then exchanged greetings and courtesies with each relative. These people, now taking Ning Yi seriously, suddenly realized they didn't really understand his character and weren't sure what to say first. Ning Yi smiled: "Please continue your conversation. I don't really understand these matters—I'll have Xing'er and the others prepare breakfast." Then he simply left as usual.

Leaving that reception room and returning to the opposite building, when he went to find Xing'er and the others to prepare breakfast, he first discovered little Chan humming a tune as she carried things over. Seeing Ning Yi, her face flushed before she pouted: "Young Master, I'm not sick."

A moment later she added seriously: "I slept until just now before getting up"—clearly afraid Ning Yi would push her back to bed again.

Before long, the married couple Ning Yi and Su Tan'er ate breakfast in the adjoining room with these relatives. Most of them were close to the First Branch but not intimately so—though they chatted about household matters to strengthen connections, naturally they also wanted to discuss business.

During the conversation with Su Tan'er, everyone watched Ning Yi's expression, paying attention to whether he'd respond. The implications in their words, the hints, their thoughts—Ning Yi heard them all perfectly clearly in his ears, but he truly didn't concern himself with such matters. Throughout the entire breakfast, aside from occasionally encouraging people to eat, the rest of the time he simply buried his head in his porridge and dishes. Unable to read his attitude, some wondered—could this Ning Yi really care nothing about family affairs?

In fact, what occupied Ning Yi's mind at that moment was probably just the ripples that "Settling Wind Waves" might create after spreading, plus that "ten steps, one scheme" evaluation and other such trivial matters. When he went to teach today, he really should give that little girl Zhou Pei who couldn't keep secrets a talking-to. But thinking about how her father had been outmaneuvered so thoroughly by him, she probably wasn't having an easy time either. Perhaps he should be magnanimous and forgive her, offer some comfort instead.

Naturally some people interpreted his silence as not expressing opinions lightly, figuring he'd still discuss with Su Tan'er behind the scenes. Among those present, probably only Su Tan'er could roughly understand Ning Yi's temperament, and she too found it amusing.

Just what kind of personality her strange husband truly possessed—it would probably take everyone quite a while to really understand. For many people, they'd likely never figure it out in their entire lives...
