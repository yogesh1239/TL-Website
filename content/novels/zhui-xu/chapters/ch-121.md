Her words dissolved into the deep night's rain. When the breeze blew in, the candlelight flickered. Such an expression must have taken enormous courage for Su Tan'er. She lay there, momentarily silent with embarrassment. Much of her illness stemmed from psychological factors—after speaking like this, the pressure in her heart would likely ease considerably. But moments later, perhaps thinking of something, she still gently bit her lower lip.

"If... if I fail in this, will you... will you think less of me...?"

"Think less of you?"

"Will you..." Su Tan'er struggled to get the words out, then finally shook her head. "No, it's nothing. Let's not talk about it."

"Such a mood killer..." Ning Yi looked at her thoughtfully, then probably guessed what she was thinking and shook his head. "I'm not saying this because of whether things go well for the Su family. Whether this matter works out or not, between us... it's like this anyway... Besides, getting through this situation is actually quite simple."

Su Tan'er nodded, her expression relaxing slightly. After a moment, she smiled with some difficulty at his latter statement: "This matter... husband doesn't understand..."

"I understand." Ning Yi glanced back. "I've roughly reviewed the account books from the past three years. The Su family's foundation is still solid. No matter who our opponents are, their trick of stabbing someone then playing victim is indeed vicious, but it won't affect our customers. The Su family's business spans the entire country—no one really discusses whether the owner far away in Jiangning has good character before entering a shop. The impact would only be on nearby partners and suppliers. The Su family will be shaken in these areas, but relative to such tactics, the significance isn't great. With the Su family's foundation, it's hard to collapse directly from complications in certain segments—at most we'd lose a small portion. For real impact, it's mainly around Jiangning. In the near term, what would be most affected is the Imperial Merchant status. Once the matter is confirmed, the government will consider reputation and not cooperate with the Su family..."

"So it's the Imperial Merchant status..." Su Tan'er murmured, repeating his words.

"So... the main thing is still resolving the Imperial Merchant issue."

"Husband doesn't understand..." She turned her gaze toward the inner side of the bed, repeating in a low voice, not letting Ning Yi see her expression. Ning Yi sighed and pulled out that piece of cloth from his clothes, placing it in her hand. "Don't understand the Imperial Merchant, or don't understand this cloth?"

Su Tan'er turned back, looking at the cloth in her hand, then at Ning Yi. "Husband... already knows?"

"Honestly, I'm not entirely clear." Ning Yi shook his head. "Xing'er seemed troubled and couldn't speak freely, so I didn't press her."

Su Tan'er held that piece of cloth, looking at it for a while, occasionally glancing aside in thought. When she looked back at Ning Yi, there was a slight smile on her face, but her eyes had grown desolate. Clearly, thinking of this matter recently had often brought her close to tears—perhaps she'd already cried more than once in secret.

"Husband... we can't become Imperial Merchants anymore." Her voice was heavy with defeat. "I started this project three years ago, in secret. All that preparation... for such a beautiful color. I was so sure it would work, but... it ended up like this. It feels like I've been cheated. We didn't add Imperial yellow dye. We used new methods to create it—cinnabar, madder root, alum, gardenia... This must be a formula no one has used before. Over two months ago, I thought bringing this out would shock everyone, but in the end... in the end it just..."

She sniffled and bit her lip. Ning Yi thought for a moment. "When did it start fading?"

"Almost two months. We have no idea why." Su Tan'er shook her head. "After making it, we tested many things too. Sun exposure, fire, washing with water over and over—nothing happened. It stayed that beautiful. Originally there were no problems at all, but in the end... it faded. I don't know what to do. After Father collapsed, they finally couldn't help coming to say it probably couldn't be solved. I told them to keep trying, but I know too—there's no way..."

"This color is very hard to match. Few ingredients use yellow. If the formula is even slightly off, the color differs greatly. We have no idea where to adjust. Only that formula we stumbled upon by chance produces bright yellow..." She paused slightly, tears in her eyes. "There's no way, husband... we can't get it..."

The textile industry had developed for many years and actually had its own methods for determining what modern times would call color fastness. But with the formula Su Tan'er had found, these methods had clearly failed. Perhaps some delicate chemical reaction at precise proportions had produced that bright yellow—though with his current chemistry knowledge, he certainly couldn't solve this technically.

Su Tan'er wasn't one to give up easily. But when three years of effort were judged meaningless, when hopes for Imperial Merchant status suddenly vanished, combined with exhaustion, pressure, illness, and various other factors, her sudden collapse was no longer surprising. Human mental states were like this—one moment you're at the peak, thinking that even with Father down, getting Imperial Merchant status would naturally resolve the crisis. The next moment you discover you have no chips left. When suddenly struck down from the peak, everything explodes more violently.

However, Ning Yi was now looking at that cloth with considerable interest. He took it from Su Tan'er's hand. "Isn't there also the loom improvements? I saw in the accounts that you allocated money..."

"Can't improve much. It was originally prepared for the Imperial Merchant position too. But in these areas, you have to invest a lot of money for little profit. If we only get the tribute portion, it becomes a burden instead. The Imperial Textile Bureau just treats people like manual laborers..."

"But this is enough. Ultimately it's still about resolving the Imperial Merchant matter..."

"But it can't be resolved... husband..." Su Tan'er said this, then paused, looking at Ning Yi's unaffected expression. "Hm?"

"It might be hard to obtain, but that doesn't mean it can't be resolved." Ning Yi smiled. "Non-fading has non-fading solutions. Fading has fading methods too. As for how to use it, that needs consideration..."

Su Tan'er thought for a moment. "Husband... surely you don't mean to say fading is good? That won't work..."

She was clever after all, knowing that sometimes matters could be resolved through words, through promotion. Ning Yi had the reputation of a talented scholar—she thought he wanted to promote fading as a feature of the cloth. This might work in some situations, but here it would just be making fun of the imperial family. Ning Yi shook his head. "That's not how to do it. There are still things I'm unclear about—mainly which Imperial Textile Bureau officials are involved in this Imperial Merchant matter, what chips each family has for competing, what exactly we've done, how far the loom improvements have progressed... If you still have energy, you can tell me now. I'll explain my plan after, but..."

He looked down at the cloth. "The Imperial Merchant position is key. Whether our opponents show themselves or not, we can use this to draw them out and catch them all. So no matter what, this Imperial Merchant matter... we must fight for it to the end..."

*******************

"...Why? Because we have the strength!"

In the morning, rain still fell. In the neighboring courtyard's meeting room, Ning Yi was addressing a group of managers and stewards with a serious expression. Honestly, this was his first time speaking so prominently in a "formal" setting since arriving at the Su family. But in his blue robe and scholar's cap, he looked quite presentable, seeming to have the bearing expected of someone taking charge in crisis—at least... he appeared to be trying very hard.

He tapped with a folding fan in his hand, glancing left and right.

"...Resolve the Imperial Merchant matter, and the schemes of these petty opportunists outside, along with all the gossip within our family, will be silenced at once—permanently. As for exactly who's watching us from inside and out, don't worry about it. Others will handle those matters. Old Master will resolve them. We just need to do our own work well—stabilize the situation and take the Imperial Merchant quota by any means necessary."

"So for the next month or so, I'll be taking charge of this matter. Of course, I know I lack experience in this area. For major decisions, I'll consult with Tan'er. All you managers have more experience than I do in these matters. I'll seek your guidance when the time comes. I hope Manager Liao and everyone will instruct me generously..."

Ning Yi clasped his fists respectfully, then smiled.

"However, regarding the Imperial Merchant matter, we need to start making breakthroughs now. I'm a scholar who hasn't touched commerce before, but some things in this world are universal. I understand simple rules. For instance, last New Year, because I guessed certain things, a casual comment helped settle the He family's business. Haha... So I probably know one thing that's definitely right."

"Quality," he said, tapping his folding fan on the table for emphasis. "True quality will always be recognized. No matter the market, no matter the place."

"Just like us scholars—those with talent will shine anywhere. Others will always recognize them. So when selling ourselves, no need for modesty. Manager Liao and Manager Nie have recently been handling relations with several lords from the Imperial Textile Bureau. We've shown our cards, everyone knows. But I feel one point isn't enough..."

"We've only shown our determination to take the Imperial Merchant position. The Xue and Wu families have seen it, but we haven't clearly displayed our chips. I hope going forward, whether you managers are hosting dinners or discussing future business, you can tell others clearly: we've been preparing for this opportunity for several years! We don't fight battles without confidence! We already have the best cloth! This is strength no one can match!"

"Now Great Wu and Great Liao's situation is tense. There will definitely be problems and friction with the annual tribute. Whenever such changes occur, business opportunities arise. Before... like the Xue and Wu families, they viewed the Imperial Merchant matter as dangerous territory. But seeing the situation changing, seeing us compete, they suddenly want to compete too. It's just opportunistic speculation—what preparations do they have? But we're different. We began preparing years ago, and now we can tell everyone!"

"Just tell them this—how the Xue family is, how the Wu family is, how we are. Though we can't fully reveal our chips yet, we can promote this way. Let everyone know we came prepared. Let the Imperial Textile Bureau officials know we're the best, most thoroughly prepared. We've already developed loom improvements that can greatly increase efficiency, guaranteeing no impact on our business or the Imperial Merchant duties. We have the best cloth... Oh, what comes next requires everyone's confidentiality, but I think it's time to show you all. Juan'er, bring the box."

The little maid Juan'er standing nearby nodded, turned and carried over a box, placing it on the table. Ning Yi pressed his hand on the box. "To repeat, please keep what you're about to see confidential... Of course, you're all our Su family's own people who understand these matters better than I do. Heh, I'm being redundant..."

After speaking, he smiled and slowly opened the rectangular box. A bolt of bright yellow silk appeared before everyone. As several people frowned in amazement, Ning Yi pushed it forward grandly, then picked up a knife and somewhat clumsily cut off a section.

The rain continued. The doors had closed. Ning Yi's voice carried out in waves from inside.

"It's fire-resistant, wash-resistant, and sun-resistant. The color is perfectly fast," he declared, his voice ringing with confidence. "We perfected this only two months ago. We had hoped to keep it under wraps, but circumstances have forced our hand. With this, we solve all our problems. Who else has cloth like this? This color... tells me there is no reason to panic. With this color, if the Imperial Merchant position isn't ours, whose would it be? Though I'm a scholar, I know this will definitely work. It's not us begging those officials—those officials will come begging us... Oh, don't spread that statement, but anyway... we benefit, they benefit even more. It's obvious... Alright, for the next month, I'll work with all of you to accomplish this..."

Rain still fell. In the bedroom, Su Tan'er gazed at the rain curtain, looking toward the meeting room in the neighboring courtyard. She seemed to hear activity, but naturally only rain sounds reached her. Xiao Chan entered, sitting by the bedside to chat. After a while, she said, "I wonder what husband looks like right now..."

"Sister Xing'er just came by. She said Master is speaking very impressively. Those managers were all convinced by Master's words. Mm, Master makes good sense..."

"Oh, really..." Su Tan'er smiled, imagining what those "very sensible" words must be like. Soon after, the meeting ended. The sounds of managers leaving drifted over in bits and pieces—just footsteps and movement. If she could go out now, she'd probably hear some of their whispered conversations in the rain.

"It's just the idealism of a scholar..."

"He makes some good points, though."

"Business isn't that simple."

"But that cloth... it's undeniable."

"There's no other choice. With the Master and Second Miss both ill, someone has to take charge. As long as he doesn't interfere too much, we should be fine..."

"Young Master is clever, he understands some things..."

"But he's still just a scholar. Commercial matters are too complex..."

Such discussions gradually faded, disappearing into the rain. Ning Yi returned to the small building, clapping his hands as he watched these people depart through the rain from the window. Then he turned and went downstairs to visit the ill Su Tan'er.

Another day passed. As Su Tan'er's fever gradually subsided, Ning Yi began taking her place, driving out daily in a carriage, mimicking her previous routine. With the attitude of a diligent but bumbling novice, he began "meddling" in the Su family's business...
