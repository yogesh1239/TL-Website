The city gates remained closed, and the endless autumn rain seemed to have dissolved the entire city into itself, leaving hurried figures on the streets who carried with them a deep sense of exhaustion and weariness, for with the gates sealed, little could be accomplished, and even simple daily tasks now required far more effort than usual. As rice and grain prices climbed ever higher and various conflicts gradually increased, everyone had grown weary in these dispiriting days.

However, if one were to examine Jiangning's cloth merchant circles independently, the situation appeared somewhat different—a storm had begun brewing, with every household engaged in vigorous maneuvering, forging new connections and alliances while preparing to gauge the winds, identify trends, and secure their positions. The Wu family, originally Jiangning's foremost cloth merchant, had secured this year's Imperial Merchant position, signaling their likely preparations for expansion, though it would probably take several months before any major moves materialized since securing the Imperial Merchant position brought enormous responsibilities, requiring the Wu family to first make adjustments for the imperial annual cloth quotas before they could stabilize and inevitably begin advancing with great strides.

In stark contrast stood the now-shaken Su family. After that fateful night of the Imperial Merchant selection, Su Tan'er had finally begun making appearances, actively working to stabilize the turmoil the Su family would face by seeking out past business partners in attempts to secure their relationships. The Su family possessed some foundation, and the responses received so far remained positive, but beneath these calm waters, it was impossible to know how many had already begun having second thoughts, how many had secretly initiated contact with other merchant houses.

The Xue family found themselves powerless in these matters, able only to wait quietly, laying their plans and accumulating strength to potentially capture more market share that might be released by the Su family in the coming upheavals. Having prepared most extensively against the Su family in the past, they might now seize this opportunity to reap enormous benefits.

Though these changes hadn't yet fully materialized, they had already begun rapidly corroding the previous structure like termites appearing in wood. Within a month or two, the entire situation might truly collapse, with the Wu family ascending to new heights while the Su family retreated from its position among Jiangning's three major cloth merchants, shrinking back to medium-scale merchant status, and then... in the eyes of those who understood such matters, perhaps continuing to weaken further—indeed, the internal upheaval within the Su family had, in a certain sense, already begun.

The Su family compound now teemed with discussions about that night of the Imperial Merchant selection. The First Branch, Second Branch, and Third Branch¹ had begun drawing clear boundaries, with voices both open and hidden speaking of Ning Yi's incompetence, of Su Tan'er's failures. Of course, Su Tan'er had spent these past days rushing about everywhere, too busy to deal with these family members, and those people temporarily lacked the courage to confront her directly. But within the Su family, calls demanding an end to Su Tan'er's management of business affairs had already risen, with daily arguments erupting.

Not only from the worthless younger generation of the Second and Third Branches—such opinions had also begun emerging from the mouths of some Su family elders. The power that Su Zhongkan and Su Yunfang had accumulated over the years had finally begun its release, preparing to deliver a fatal blow to the First Branch after Su Boyong's fall. With the Su family beset by internal troubles and external threats, even Old Master Su found himself unable to suppress these matters through high-handed methods.

While it would take time for these matters to reach their conclusion, in most people's eyes, Su Tan'er's withdrawal from the Su family's commercial stage seemed an inevitable trend in the near future. No matter how hard she worked to maintain stability and prevent collapse, some things truly crumbled like a mountain avalanche, and as a woman herself, she found it even harder to provide others with a sense of stability during such a crisis—many might acknowledge Su Tan'er's business acumen and believe she could recover from this failure, but they found it difficult to believe the Su family would continue allowing her to helm their ship.

During this period, criticisms of Ning Yi were perhaps the most numerous. Though these hadn't been brought to the surface demanding any particular action from him, privately, even many originally close to the First Branch spoke unfavorably, with some even beginning to say this scholar was unworthy of the Second Miss. After that night, Su Tan'er had completely reclaimed her original position, leaving Ning Yi with nothing to do, and these days he had returned to his previous idle state. With rain falling outside and the private school closed, he simply read books and practiced calligraphy at home, occasionally fiddling with a small cylindrical device, appearing no different from before.

However, though the city gates remained closed and thus the private school stayed shut, within the Su compound, several people had already approached Yushan Academy's headmaster Su Chonghua, requesting their children be moved to other classes. These children had originally been Ning Yi's students, but their parents had apparently decided to align with the Second or Third Branches and no longer wished for Ning Yi to continue teaching their children. Within the Su family, matters concerning the private school had always been what the Old Master valued and controlled most strictly, so when factional activities reached this point, it clearly indicated this was no mere game. These matters had already spread widely throughout the Su mansion within just a few days.

As the ninth month approached, the weather had cleared again that day, and word spread that the city gates might open within the next few days. The tense atmosphere within the city seemed to ease slightly, but within the Su family compound, this atmosphere deepened daily. Beneath the corridor, two maids walked past carrying items, whispering as they went.

"Making such a mess of things, that live-in son-in-law still acts like nothing's wrong..."

"Still calling himself the number one talent, completely useless..."

"Second Miss got dragged down by him too..."

"Who knows what will happen to the Su family..."

In such an atmosphere, passing maids gossiping had become commonplace. Today's pair seemed particularly unlucky though—as they neared the corridor's corner, a cold, pretty face suddenly appeared before them.

"You two, go help over there. They're moving to the neighboring courtyard and still don't have enough hands."

"J-Juan'er..."

"Didn't you hear what I said? Everyone's working, hurry up!"

"But... Fourth Miss told us to..."

"Fourth Miss's side isn't urgent, I'll send others... Hurry up!"

"Yes..."

The two maids looked displeased but finally hurried off as Juan'er frowned and strode forward quickly. Before long, she heard people inside talking about Ning Yi at another courtyard entrance—naturally nothing good. This time she pursed her lips but finally didn't go in. Everyone was talking, and these matters ultimately weren't all within her control. She just lowered her head and walked quickly toward the courtyard. At this time in the small courtyard, Chan'er was holding a broom sweeping the ground. Juan'er walked over and looked at Ning Yi's room, then looked upstairs: "Xiao Chan, where's the Young Master?"

"Uh, he went out." Xiao Chan hugged her broom. "This morning he said since it finally cleared up, he'd go out for a walk. Does Juan'er need the Young Master for something?"

"Just now when I passed the gate, the Zhou family's young miss and young master came looking for the Young Master."

"Oh, but Juan'er, your complexion doesn't look too good."

"Just now I ran into some people who don't understand anything..."

Juan'er coldly recounted what she'd just heard, and Chan'er pursed her lips, her expression also darkening—over these past few days they'd all heard plenty of such talk, and even standing up to scold people proved useless, while the other matters they knew about absolutely couldn't be spoken.

"The Young Master really has been wronged..." Juan'er furrowed her brows slightly, the usually quiet girl now genuinely feeling sorry for Ning Yi.

"Sister Xing'er scolded people yesterday too..." Chan'er added. "But the Young Master seems quite leisurely. Yesterday I also asked if he was angry, and the Young Master was fiddling with that telescope thing. He just casually shook his head without saying anything."

Chan'er imitated Ning Yi's casual head-shaking gesture, though it was hard to say if it really resembled him—clearly she cared too. After exchanging a few more words, Juan'er hurried off to reply to the Zhou family's two siblings, leaving Xiao Chan hugging her broom and gazing at Ning Yi's room for quite a while before biting her lip and murmuring softly, "Young Master ah..." as she took up the broom and swept the ground vigorously.

At this time in the morning, Ning Yi and Nie Yunzhu met in the small building.

He had gone to fetch some items from the small laboratory beside the academy, then wandered over here, not expecting to find Nie Yunzhu at home in this first meeting since the twenty-fifth of the eighth month, and upon suddenly seeing him at the door, Nie Yunzhu's expression showed obvious relief. They didn't exchange many greetings—Ning Yi simply carried a small bag and waved casually, while Nie Yunzhu stood on the steps, revealing a smile that afterward seemed almost like a wife welcoming her weary husband home.

"How have things been lately?"

"The shop's doing well, Jin'er's there, so I'm resting." Nie Yunzhu tilted her head, letting Ning Yi enter. "What about you?"

"Also fine, just couldn't go out these past few days because of the rain. Now that it's cleared up, I came out for a walk."

"That's good then." The doors to the living room were open, leading directly to the terrace extending over the river. Autumn sunlight spilled across there, and a crooked-neck tree grew leaning against the small building, casting shade on the terrace at this time. Nie Yunzhu thought for a moment: "Actually... I heard about these past few days' events."

"Oh." Ning Yi glanced at her, then smiled and shook his head. "Uh... things definitely aren't as terrible as the outside rumors say, though these past few days have indeed been a bit noisy..."

"How about... I play some music for Liheng to listen to, to ease your mind?"

"Wouldn't that be troublesome? You finally have a day off..."

"It's no trouble." Nie Yunzhu smiled, then lowered her eyes. "I... I can only do this much..."

The terrace faced the river with pleasant scenery all around at a glance. The shade from the crooked tree wasn't much—most of the terrace still basked in lazy sunlight. Ning Yi took a cushion and sat casually at the terrace edge. When Nie Yunzhu brought over a tea tray, she saw him sitting there on the terrace floor, back against the wall, one leg bent up as he gazed at the distant scenery. She couldn't help but smile, setting down the tray.

"I'll go get the qin²."

She spoke softly. Ning Yi looked at her and nodded.

Moments later, the qin music began...

************************

Without realizing it, he had fallen asleep.

A warm feeling, as if floating in water, his mother's hand gently brushing over him... He didn't know what pieces Nie Yunzhu was playing—he was completely tone-deaf in this area and had never particularly enjoyed these ancient qin melodies, but now he found himself immersed. Nie Yunzhu occasionally hummed along with the music, various melodies like a young woman's murmured fragments. Looking over occasionally, autumn light spilled down as if scattering gold dust upon her, her white garments like snow, black hair gently moving. The woman's expression remained focused, yet when he looked over, she would smile at him tenderly during pauses in her playing.

She had changed clothes when she came in... By the time he hazily realized this, Ning Yi had already begun drifting off to sleep. Across the river, willow shade formed a screen as autumn wind blew, river water flowing past beneath the terrace's river bend. On the terrace, leaves rustled and occasionally one would fall. The qin music merged with these sounds of water and leaves, the woman's soft singing from her throat graceful and ethereal.

The music eventually stopped at some unknown time, and the woman sat there motionless for a long while, gazing at the sleeping figure not far away. Over these years, this was the first time she had played continuously for so long. Even when moved by inspiration in the past, entertaining herself, she had never reached this extent. But in those days, even earlier when she was in the pleasure quarters, her performances had mostly been for herself. Not long ago at Yanxui Tower, her performance had been driven by competitive spirit, with little genuine playing. Only now did she play here wholeheartedly for another person, for an extended time, letting him sink into sleep, hoping he would feel comfort and peace, finding solace.

Wind blew across the river's surface as she pushed aside the guqin and stood, followed by soft footsteps as she quietly cleared away the teapot, teacups, and snacks, worried that Ning Yi might topple over while sleeping. Then she sat nearby in the autumn light, quietly watching that sleeping face.

At some point the wind seemed to grow stronger, and she went inside, returning shortly with a thin blanket. Crouching beside the man, the woman hesitated slightly, unsure if placing the blanket would wake him, and this blanket belonged to her and Jin'er, carrying a distinctly feminine scent. During this brief hesitation, Ning Yi's eyelids moved several times before he groggily awakened, rubbing his eyes and pushing against the floor to stand.

The woman in white dress and skirt, holding the blanket, also stood involuntarily, hesitating slightly in uncertainty.

"Ah, sorry, I don't know what happened, I fell asleep... It must be because you played so beautifully."

Ning Yi smiled, still somewhat drowsy, but Nie Yunzhu didn't answer. Turning her head, this white figure stepped forward, rising on white-stockinged tiptoes, tilting her head up to press her lips against his.

The soft, warm, slightly trembling and inexperienced sensation lost the autumn scenery between this wooden building by the river bend. Wind passed through as sunlight pierced the eaves' corners, a leaf drifting down in the breeze, quietly witnessing this moment...

**Cultural Footnotes:**

¹ First Branch, Second Branch, Third Branch (大房/二房/三房): Traditional Chinese extended families were organized into branches descended from different sons of the founding patriarch. The First Branch (eldest son's line) traditionally held primacy, but competition between branches for family resources and leadership was common, especially during succession crises.

² Qin (琴): Often translated as "zither," the guqin is a seven-stringed classical Chinese instrument associated with scholarly refinement and emotional expression. Playing the qin was considered one of the four arts of the Chinese scholar (along with calligraphy, painting, and chess) and carried deep cultural significance in expressing one's inner feelings.
