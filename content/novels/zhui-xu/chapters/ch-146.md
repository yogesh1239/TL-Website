Over the past few days, Su Chonghua found strange emotions occasionally sweeping through his mind, though even he couldn't quite articulate the specific reasons behind these feelings.

For years, the Old Master's favor had secured Su Chonghua a respectable position within the Su family, and since Su Zhongkan served as the true administrator of Yushan Academy, he had gradually become an important participant in the Second Branch's affairs. Recently, the Second and Third Branches had joined forces to move against the First Branch, preparing to eliminate this numerically small but seemingly most threatening faction first, and he had participated in these efforts alongside the others. During various gatherings, when discussions turned to the Second Branch's current situation—though external threats remained, at least internal troubles had somewhat stabilized, marking significant progress in their struggle for true control of the Su family—everyone's mood had been quite cheerful.

Today marked an especially significant day, and from the moment he'd awakened that morning, he had understood this reality, sensing that everyone's emotions carried a different quality than usual. When he'd encountered Su Zhongkan in a nearby courtyard that morning, along with several other managers and stewards aligned with the Second Branch, everyone had been smiling and chatting pleasantly.

He understood clearly that tonight's events had already been decided, knowing that Su Tan'er had spent enormous sums preparing for the Imperial Merchant opportunity without generating any benefits, which had now led to those outside merchants beginning to distrust the Su family. These matters could all be brought forward tonight for discussion, and many Su family members would exert their influence together, while some elders who had originally disagreed with female leadership¹ or had wavered on the issue were now beginning to side with the Second and Third Branches—even the traditionally formidable Third Uncle found himself powerless at this juncture.

Yet during this time when the Second Branch hearts overflowed with anticipation, those strange emotions would occasionally surface, particularly when he glimpsed Ning Yi's leisurely, carefree figure from the sidelines over these past few days, inevitably triggering unexpected thoughts in his mind.

*Settling Wind Waves*...

What he occasionally recalled was that poem he'd seen several days ago—truly exceptional verse.

Su Chonghua possessed genuine literary talent, having earned modest fame as a scholar in Jiangning, and after years of writing poetry and composing verse, naturally few works could strike him with immediate awe upon first reading. Yet coincidentally, Ning Yi's previous two pieces had achieved precisely this effect—leaving aside "Drinking Wine with Pei Di," of course—and this *Settling Wind Waves* he'd recently encountered proved equally impressive. Naturally, viewing it in isolation, he would consider this poem merely a scholar's self-consolation and self-intoxication, portraying himself as triumphant despite obvious defeat, with the author's reluctance to present it publicly serving as clear evidence of this interpretation.

But... every time he actually saw Ning Yi, especially when combined with this verse, or after reading other people's poetry, the feeling always seemed somehow different, and Su Chonghua now sat beside the private classroom, lost in contemplation as he observed.

"...This brings us to mathematical studies, which you'll learn about this afternoon, though I won't tell you how to calculate it, but some of the logical systems within mathematics—the principles and methods of thinking—are quite fascinating... In the far western lands there's a country called Greece, where there's a story known as Zeno's Paradox. One day, a fleet-footed great hero encountered a tortoise, and the tortoise said: 'If you race against me, you'll never be able to catch up...'"

At the front of the classroom, Ning Yi lectured with a smile, drawing lines on the blackboard with chalk: "The great hero said that even if he ran slowly, his speed would still be ten times yours, so how could it be impossible to catch up? Then the tortoise explained, 'Let's use an example: you're a hundred zhang away from me, your speed is ten times mine, and then you chase me. When you've run a hundred zhang to reach my current position, I'll have moved forward ten zhang, so you continue chasing those ten zhang, but by then I'll have moved forward another zhang, and after you cover that zhang, I'll still be ahead of you... You can keep getting closer to me, but you'll never actually catch up.' The great hero felt the tortoise was absolutely right and found himself completely baffled..."

His lessons always followed this pattern—supposedly teaching classics like the Great Learning and Doctrine of the Mean, yet invariably incorporating all sorts of seemingly unrelated material, though it usually proved quite engaging. A new student named Zhou Junwu raised his hand and asked, "Teacher, where is Greece located?" So Ning Yi began explaining about Greece with another smile.

Watching this leisurely figure, who seemed utterly unconcerned with the day's events or the family's recent turmoil, Su Chonghua felt that strange feeling surface once more as he thought of *Settling Wind Waves*. He frowned for a long moment, marveling at how profound Liheng's skill in verse composition was to influence him so deeply with a single poem, then finally turned to leave, shaking his head as such thoughts occupied his mind.

***

The morning gradually passed into afternoon, and certain courtyards within the Su residence filled with people, bustling with activity like New Year's festivities. By this time, the various factions had finally begun to form clear divisions, eliminating the need for excessive caution—now they simply needed to wait for tonight's proceedings. Members of the First, Second, and Third Branches continued arriving in groups.

Su Yu's courtyard also received a steady stream of visitors throughout the day.

"...I also feel that the Second Miss shouldering so many family responsibilities has been under tremendous pressure. Everyone certainly recognizes her capabilities, and if the First Branch had a male heir who could take charge, even after this incident, we'd feel comfortable letting her continue managing affairs. But unfortunately..."

"Given the current situation among the three branches, it really isn't wise to continue this stubborn standoff, Third Brother..."

"Sigh, if only Boyong hadn't suffered that accident..."

The reception room featured modest rather than luxurious furnishings, yet conveyed an atmosphere of steady dignity. Su Yu sat in the seat of honor, leaning on his walking stick with eyes closed in meditation, while those below exchanged comments and observations. These were all old family brothers, and since tonight's clan assembly² would ultimately require them to step forward and make the final decision, it seemed best to exchange thoughts now and establish a general framework beforehand.

Setting aside various positions and vested interests, how could they not recognize Su Tan'er's capabilities? Yet the Su family's current situation fundamentally represented a three-way struggle for inheritance; with Su Boyong fallen, if Su Tan'er continued her stubborn resistance, the result might eventually devolve into a vicious cycle of internal strife. Su Yu clearly understood these realities, but even at this point, he had yet to express a clear position.

This elderly patriarch's prestige was simply too great—without his stated position, no framework could emerge for tonight's events, potentially leading to arguments. These were all elderly men who preferred to avoid such confrontations, and while the three-way inheritance struggle still had Su Yu presiding over it, if the old master's thinking became inflexible and he insisted on supporting his granddaughter's position in tonight's confrontation with everyone else, the consequences for this family could prove dire.

Although Su Yu had remained quite lucid throughout these years, with age came uncertainty about whether he might suddenly become obstinate tonight.

"So, Third Brother, you really must give us some indication of your intentions regarding these matters."

The Old Seventh below seemed somewhat anxious as he stood and spoke, glancing at the others before several more elderly family members echoed his sentiments. Su Yu opened his eyes slightly, squinting at them: "Give what indication?"

"Regarding the Second Miss's situation, you must have some plan in mind—we need to know your position so we can have some sense of direction..."

"I haven't decided anything myself, so how can I give you direction?"

"But... Third Brother, regarding this matter... you can't remain undecided. All these years, everyone has been following your lead."

"Tonight we'll need to hear what the First, Second, and Third uncles have to say, what others have to say, what the Second Miss has to say—only then will the situation become clear and everyone will see things plainly."

"Third Brother, you're talking nonsense now. We all know what they're going to say—of course we should listen when the time comes, but everyone already understands the general content. If you don't take a position first, we'll be..."

"Old Seventh." The walking stick struck the ground as Su Yu gazed at his fifty-something seventh brother, then his expression softened as he sighed, "Until the very end, no one knows exactly how things will turn out. In any case, when the time comes, you should support whatever seems reasonable and reject whatever seems unreasonable—as long as everyone avoids harsh words, that will suffice. I can't see clearly how this matter will unfold right now."

The old man closed his eyes and continued his meditation: "In any case, we'll discuss it tonight."

Afternoon sunlight streamed through the doorway, casting a large bright area on the floor, and the humming sounds of discussion resumed...

***

The scratching sounds of brushes filled the somewhat secluded teahouse as three maids and several managers busily flipped through numerous ledgers, copying information before them, while across from them sat several core members of the Wu family. Sunlight filtered down past the eaves, wind occasionally blew through, and quiet conversations took place intermittently.

Su Tan'er sat to one side, quietly sipping tea. Since the Wu family had submitted, everything had proceeded smoothly, and both parties had nearly established a cooperative understanding—though naturally, the cooperating party was far from pleased.

Wu Qilong also sat nearby, quietly drinking tea while gazing at the patches of light on the ground near his feet. Since that first day, Wu Chenghou had stopped coming, leaving Wu Qilong to handle the primary negotiations.

"Tonight, I hear Xue Yan and the others have arranged to dine at Moon Fragrance Tower on Persimmon Street, and the Lü and Chen families will likely send representatives as well." Wu Qilong spat out a mouthful of tea, speaking as if discussing matters completely unrelated to himself. "They're quite concerned about these developments, and their expressions afterward should prove quite amusing." Despite calling it amusing, his own expression showed no hint of humor.

Su Tan'er had already grown tired of using such matters to provoke him—the first day had involved pointed confrontations to establish dominance, but afterward such things became unnecessary: "According to our previous agreement, you should tell me about those other matters today."

Wu Qilong glanced sideways: "In a while—I can delay telling you as long as I want, and I intend to enjoy that privilege."

"As you wish." Su Tan'er turned her gaze elsewhere. "But if you let those people escape, while I might swallow this insult, my father certainly won't."

"Hmph."

Wu Qilong snorted coldly, then after a moment asked: "What's your husband doing right now?"

"Walking around, playing chess with friends, or listening to some young lady sing opera." Su Tan'er looked up with a smile. "As for my husband's activities outside, I—as his wife—shouldn't pry too much... managing the household well is sufficient."

***

Ning Yi was indeed watching a young lady perform.

On the second floor of Zhu's establishment, Ning Yi sat beside a table, drinking tea and eating small pastries. The tavern now regularly featured performers singing and playing in the front area, though naturally, the performance Ning Yi was watching wasn't that one.

Yuan Jin'er sat right beside him, while diagonally across in the distance, the talented scholar Liu Qingdi occupied another seat, directing his gaze toward them.

Liu Qingdi had found his way to Zhu's establishment several days earlier—through what channels, no one could say—but regardless, he'd been coming frequently lately. Today, with Yuan Jin'er present and Ning Yi also here, she had gracefully seated herself beside Ning Yi, adopting quite an intimate manner.

Jianghu rumors claimed Yuan Jin'er had previously been involved with both Cao Guan and Liu Qingdi, though the depth of feeling between these talented scholars and beautiful women remained difficult to assess. Perhaps it hadn't reached the level of pathological obsession that Gu Yanzhen had once displayed, but Liu Qingdi's resentment toward Ning Yi had clear foundations, stemming from various complex reasons—their mutual status as literary talents, Yuan Jin'er's performance that time, and so forth. Being constantly stared at in this manner left Ning Yi feeling somewhat helpless. This feud had been established back at Smoke and Emerald Tower and seemed unlikely to resolve anytime soon.

"Do you find this interesting?"

Ning Yi smiled as he leaned toward Yuan Jin'er.

"Very... interesting."

Yuan Jin'er likewise leaned closer, presenting a picture of feminine dependence, though in reality Ning Yi couldn't gain the slightest advantage—courtesans remained courtesans, maintaining distance with subtle hand movements while appearing to lean closer.

"Where's Yunzhu?"

"Sister Yunzhu said she wouldn't come out to join this commotion and is inside organizing the account books. So this humble woman had to come out and accompany you, great hero."

With winter's approach, both wore somewhat thick clothing, and Yuan Jin'er was dressed beautifully. The two appeared to be leaning together, squeezing back and forth across a small space, while Liu Qingdi watched with fire in his eyes.

"Since our situation has become so ambiguous, don't you think it would be perfectly reasonable for me to take some liberties with you?"

"Certainly—this young lady is prepared to sacrifice everything, including her virtue, so Sister Yunzhu can see what kind of person you really are."

"Should I be afraid?"

"Go ahead and try."

"If I don't take advantage when it's offered... you're putting me in a very difficult position..."

Yuan Jin'er smiled demurely, the picture of purity, and their gazes met in midair, sparking with electricity. The next moment, just as Ning Yi was preparing to attempt something with a high risk factor, Yuan Jin'er twisted her body and delivered a crisp slap that rang throughout the second-floor hall. Liu Qingdi, who had been unwilling to continue watching this pair's shameless behavior, immediately looked over, along with everyone else whose attention was drawn by the sound.

In their field of vision, the pure and beautiful young woman had risen and retreated two hasty steps to the side, causing the items on the table to clatter noisily. She covered one side of her face with her hand while gazing at Ning Yi with tears already flowing—truly a picture of tearful beauty that stirred sympathy.

"Scoundrel!"

*Damn, she beat me to it...*

That slap hadn't actually connected at all. Yuan Jin'er had appeared to suddenly stand and swing her hand toward him, but in reality only her sleeve had brushed past Ning Yi's cheek. However, Yuan Jin'er came from a dancing background, and with her current outfit featuring large sleeves, she had clapped her hands together beneath them to create the sound, making it appear to onlookers like an extremely humiliating slap.

"...Beast, lecher, libertine..."

Yuan Jin'er wiped her tears while winking at Ning Yi with one eye. Ning Yi grimaced: "Ruthless." Liu Qingdi had already shot to his feet, and Yuan Jin'er continued: "I haven't given my heart to you yet, and you... how could you be so forward..."

Then she ran away.

Not only Liu Qingdi, but probably others in the tavern felt indignant, though hearing Yuan Jin'er's final tone—which sounded almost like affectionate complaint—left everyone uncertain about the true nature of these two people's relationship. Ning Yi sighed and raised his teacup, turning his face away.

Several staff members who understood something about the relationship between their employers, Ning Yi, Yuan Jin'er, and Nie Yunzhu, stood there stunned for a long time, unsure what new scheme their bosses were hatching.

*This tea had become undrinkable...*

Yuan Jin'er pattered into the inner rooms, feeling quite pleased with herself as she walked down the corridor, then spent a moment working up some emotional tears before wiping her eyes and running inside. She pushed open the door to the inner room, covering her face with utterly sincere sobbing: "Sister Yunzhu, Ning Yi is getting more and more outrageous. I was just joking with him, but he took liberties with me, and many people saw it. If you don't believe me, go ask Little Ding and the others..."

Yunzhu stared for a long moment: "In broad daylight... how did he take liberties with you?"

"He kissed me on the cheek." Yuan Jin'er sat down beside Yunzhu, sniffling, her gaze defiant. "It was supposed to be just joking, but he definitely did it on purpose!"

Yunzhu cupped her face and examined it for a moment, then leaned over and kissed her cheek: "There, I've helped him take liberties with you too."

"Really!" Yuan Jin'er protested. "Sister Yunzhu, you always believe him and never believe me!"

"In broad daylight, he'd never behave like that. You want me to believe you... Come help me with these account books."

"These are so hard to calculate... No wait, why wouldn't he? Men are all like that—he thinks he's being subtle about it. Just because it's broad daylight doesn't mean you shouldn't believe it. He calculated that exact point, which makes him incredibly devious. What if next time he actually... actually does that to me in broad daylight? Then Sister Yunzhu won't believe me either..."

Although both had previously been courtesans of noble status³, the atmosphere of the pleasure quarters through constant exposure had certainly been quite influential, and such words could never be spoken by other women. Yunzhu burst into laughter: "If he actually... if he actually does that to you in broad daylight, well, regardless of what it is, I won't believe it..."

Yuan Jin'er maintained a serious expression, then couldn't help laughing as well: "You're just biased toward him anyway." She turned to help with the account books.

"He has business tonight, so stop going out there to bother him."

"I like him, so I bother him. It's not because I dislike him..."

***

With a bang, the teacup hit the table, and the afternoon sunlight had begun taking on a warm yellow hue as it illuminated this teahouse. Su Chonghua was also startled awake by the sound and looked toward the middle-aged man across from him.

"Brother Chonghua has seemed preoccupied these past few days—could you be worried about tonight's family affairs?"

The middle-aged man before him was tall and lean, sporting a goatee. He was one of Su Chonghua's regular poetry companions, named Chen Lu, who styled himself Empty Mountain Hermit⁴ and enjoyed some reputation in Jiangning. He had encountered Su Chonghua on the street that afternoon and suggested they have tea together.

"Ha, tonight... there probably won't be much happening..."

"Brother Chonghua, don't try to deceive me. These past few days I've heard that your Su family's clan assembly is approaching and will bring significant changes. You seemed somewhat distracted at poetry gatherings the past two days, showing little interest—if you're not concerned about this matter, what else could it be? If tonight truly holds no significance, why don't you skip those mundane affairs and join me at the Flourishing Cloud Pavilion⁵ gathering instead?"

"Regardless of the clan assembly's outcome and its relevance to me personally, I still must attend." Su Chonghua smiled, then considered for a moment: "Ha, but speaking of those poetry gatherings the past few days... actually, I've been contemplating matters of poetry and verse, truly requiring some talent for the craft. A few days ago I encountered a piece of verse that left me with complex feelings, and I've thought of it constantly these past few days, which has actually diminished my interest in writing poetry."

"Oh?" Chen Lu showed interest. "From your tone, this verse must be exceptional?"

"Extremely good." Su Chonghua shook his head. "It's just that when you consider the person who wrote it alongside the verse itself, it truly leaves one sighing."

"Brother Chonghua's words have only increased my curiosity—please stop being mysterious and explain."

"Ha, this was written by a family nephew, none other than that Ning Yi, Ning Liheng whose exploits you've heard about before. My Su family's current situation partly stems from his actions... A few days ago he casually wrote a verse and simply gave it to a nine-year-old child in the family for private viewing. I happened to see it by chance. This *Settling Wind Waves*... its artistic conception is something I've rarely encountered in my lifetime, and compared to his previous two compositions, it shows no inferior quality whatsoever. Therefore, whenever I see him, or read others' poetry, I can't help but think of it, and regarding poetry composition, I find myself somewhat dispirited. Yet this person is truly... inadequate..."

Su Chonghua shook his head while extending his finger to dip in tea water, and in the afternoon sunlight approaching its end, he sighed as he wrote out the verse on the table, as if wanting to savor the work once more through this method. The middle-aged man across from him listened and watched these lines, his expression gradually growing serious...

***

On the other side of the city, at a small teahouse, horse carriages had arrived. Su Tan'er and Wu Qilong stood beneath the eaves, preparing to depart separately. Wu Qilong gazed at the sunlight.

"The people you want are..."

Su Tan'er's gaze had been cool throughout, but hearing Wu Qilong speak these words, her eyes trembled slightly at some moment, and she frowned briefly before remaining silent. Only after he finished speaking did Su Tan'er pause to consider, then ask: "Just them?"

"Believe it or not, it's up to you."

"No, I believe you."

"Mm?"

"Some of those people we already knew about. If you were hiding anything, there might really be problems." She smiled slightly and said, "Do you know what the first thing my husband said when he came home after confronting you that day?"

"What?"

"Qi Guangzu is the inside man."

"..." Wu Qilong frowned as he looked over.

"Because the first thing you said to my husband was: 'So it really is you.'"

"So what?"

"He had Manager Zhou gather information, and Manager Zhou wasn't drunk. Once problems started on your end, you'd naturally try to gather intelligence. My husband had already designed several methods for Manager Zhou to inadvertently leak information to Qi Guangzu, with Manager Zhou telling him that the person he most admired was Grandfather and my husband... My husband said you shouldn't have delivered that 'So it really is you' with such elaborate emotion—he immediately knew whether you were guessing or had confirmed information... I just didn't expect there were also them..."

A silence fell like a cold cavern swallowing Wu Qilong. Su Tan'er glanced at him.

"I'm leaving now. Let's cooperate well from here on—I don't want to completely destroy your Wu family, as that would reflect poorly on my Su family's reputation."

As Su Tan'er turned away with a cold expression, Wu Qilong stood frozen, watching her carriage disappear into the distance. The sunlight brought him no warmth; in his mind's eye, he could only see Ning Yi's casual figure standing in that same direction, casting a long shadow over the entire Wu family...

***

Within the Su residence, people had already begun emerging from various courtyards with smiles and laughter, the noisy sounds carrying relief, worry, amusement, and secret satisfaction—all sorts of emotions gathering together like New Year's festivities as people greeted and exchanged pleasantries.

The evening banquet was nearly ready, and after the meal would come that clan meeting capable of determining the Su family's direction for years to come. Throughout the city, Xue Yan, Xue Jin, and others had already left their homes, heading in groups toward tonight's gathering place.

"Hurry up, everyone! Tonight's gathering features a top courtesan specially hired at great expense—you're all in for a treat, so make sure to perform well..."

"A top courtesan? Could it be Lady Qilan?"

Since they were merchants, the Xue family maintained relatively close relationships with the Puyang aristocrats. This year's courtesan competition had seen the Puyang family support Qilan as the winner, and since it wasn't peak season, she was probably the most likely candidate they could hire. But Xue Yan shook his head.

"We originally intended to invite Lady Qilan, but Puyang Yi is also hosting a banquet tonight with a group of literary scholars—Cao Guan, Liu Qingdi, and others will attend—so we needed Qilan to maintain the Puyang family's prestige. Instead, I managed to arrange for Luo Miaomiao..."

Meanwhile, Su Chonghua, who had spent the afternoon outside, was also riding his carriage home. Ning Yi had bid farewell to Yunzhu and was walking through the streets toward home as well. Other Su family members still outside were also converging on the residence.

***

As the carriage passed through the streets, Su Tan'er sat with her eyes closed, contemplating many matters before taking out a piece of paper and writing three names on it. Lifting the curtain, she handed the note to Guard Geng on the driver's seat, her expression cold. "Proceed as planned," she ordered, "but be careful not to let the Wu family turn the tables on us." With a nod, Guard Geng tucked the paper into his clothing, jumped down from the moving carriage, and ran off in another direction.

The sunlight streaming through the lifted curtain provided no warmth.

Not long afterward, in a designated meeting room, Guard Geng showed the three names to another person, then placed the paper in the fire to burn.

At the entrance to one of the Su family shops, Xi Junyu sat in the sun, eyes closed as he contemplated all the arrangements made along the way. After a while, he sighed but also smiled, then rose and walked toward the Su residence.

"It's almost dinnertime—everyone should prepare to go." In Su Yu's courtyard, in the reception room, the elderly patriarch finally opened his eyes and spoke with a smile. Then everyone began standing up, filing out amid scattered conversation.

Su Boyong, still pale-faced, sat in his wooden wheelchair as his wife and concubine pushed him outside. In the courtyard, many First Branch managers including Su Yunsong and Su Danhong were waiting for him, and he smiled and waved weakly: "Let's go, let's go—tonight will be quite busy..."

Su Zhongkan, Su Yunfang, Xi Anzhong, Yu Daxian, Su Wenxing, Su Wengui, Su Wenji... dozens and hundreds of people, representing various interest networks, began to tighten their grip.

The area outside the Su residence also showed increased activity as Su Tan'er stepped down from her carriage, then spotted her husband not far ahead, who had just finished greeting and chatting with a Su family relative. She smiled and walked toward him.

"Husband, let's go inside."

**CULTURAL FOOTNOTES:**

¹ Traditional Chinese society strongly favored male household management. Female leadership was controversial and seen as disrupting proper Confucian family hierarchy.

² Clan assemblies were formal gatherings where extended family members made important collective decisions about inheritance, leadership, and family direction in traditional Chinese society.

³ "Clean courtesans" provided entertainment and companionship but not sexual services, holding higher social status in the courtesan hierarchy.

⁴ Empty Mountain Hermit (Kongshan Jushi 空山居士) - A literary pseudonym typical of scholar-poets' adopted names.

⁵ Flourishing Cloud Pavilion (Changyun Ge 昌云阁) - A gathering place for literary and cultural events.
