Over the next few days, the world around him got a bit noisier, but everything else remained more or less normal.

Back at the Su residence, there were more greetings from people who were a bit more enthusiastic, more invitations from family members that required more polite refusals, and even a few students who'd previously left his class at the academy now wanted to return, with family members coming by to plead their cases. All of it was entirely expected, just human nature really, nothing particularly strange.

After "Settling Wind Waves" spread and people started telling increasingly dramatic versions of the Su-Wu family battle, visitors to Yushan Academy increased as well, similar to when "Water Melody Prelude" first appeared. The difference was that the visitors now were considerably more varied.

For instance, Puyang Yi from the Pu merchant family came by once to invite him to some gathering on a painted boat, mentioning that Lady Qilan would be there to keep them company. Though Ning Yi had a decent impression of the Pu family, he declined these invitations as usual.

After all, Qilan held little appeal for him. He'd occasionally drop by the small building where he could listen to Yunzhu play the qin and sing. Yunzhu's accomplishments in this area far exceeded Qilan's, and she'd already grown familiar with Ning Yi's tastes, sometimes even singing a few rather unique songs in the modern style he'd taught her.

And even if Qilan held the title of top courtesan, this side had Yuan Jin'er as one of the four leading courtesans. She wouldn't even dance for him to see, instead spending her time bickering noisily with him every day, which completely drowned out any sense of a courtesan's lofty mystique. Still, at least she showed genuine personality.

A few days later, he met up with Old Qin and Old Kang. The two elders teased him about his "Ten steps, one scheme" nickname, but when discussing the entire series of maneuvers, they praised him for making it look effortless, showing the bearing of a great general. Afterward, Kang Xian said with a laugh, "It's just that compared to 'Ten steps, one scheme,' that bandit title 'Bloody Butcher' seems rather odd. I've had people making inquiries for several days now, but no one's ever heard of anyone earning such a reputation before..."

The forces backing Kang Xian were considerable, so it wasn't surprising that he'd learned about the Ten Steps Ridge incident after taking an interest in Ning Yi's recent activities. When he told Old Qin about Ning Yi's involvement that night, Old Qin frowned. "In these matters, you must eliminate the roots. If we've really provoked these martial world types and a few escaped, there'll be future trouble. Has there been any resolution yet?"

Kang Xian smiled. "After learning of this, I notified the authorities to issue wanted posters for these criminals. They've been rushing them out to the neighboring prefectures and counties as well. This morning I heard they already caught one of them. He was severely wounded and got killed when they tried to arrest him."

Old Qin nodded. "If we're going all out, these people probably won't last more than a few days."

Both men were considered great scholars of Confucian learning, and when they'd gathered in the past to play Go and chat, their eloquent discussions mostly centered on principles of benevolence. Yet now, with Old Kang bringing this up and Qin Siyuan following along, neither said a single word of disapproval or admonition about the deadly fighting and killing. From the very start, they'd treated it as something that simply needed handling.

Kang Xian had actually been using his influence to intervene in the matter over the past few days, though Ning Yi hadn't known about it until now. Hearing it, he couldn't help but shake his head with a smile.

They'd played Go together casually before, so he'd already had a rough sense of their characters. Now he was just seeing it more clearly. These two old men wrote moral essays in their daily lives, but when it came to actually doing things, neither one of them hesitated for a moment.

In fact, when they discussed the matter, they only focused on Ning Yi's appearance at that moment, the skill he'd shown in pulling all twenty-some people who'd nearly broken through the encirclement back into the net. As for merchant families taking action, gang warfare, how many people died—Old Qin and Old Kang both seemed unconcerned. After all, Su Boyong had been attacked first, so this retaliation was entirely justified. What really concerned them, or at least what concerned Kang Xian, was that Ning Yi should be someone who did great things, with no need to personally risk himself over such trifles. If something had actually gone wrong, a few petty bandits' lives couldn't repay the loss to the family and nation.

"You invented that telescope, and I've already had people send it to the Eastern Capital. We're also rushing to study and manufacture them here. We've already made several working models that are proving quite useful when deployed with the army." Old Kang and Old Qin were playing Go, and he shook his head to toss aside those petty bandits from Ten Steps Ridge, bringing up what he truly considered important.

"It's just that you keep such a low profile, it makes requesting merit for you difficult. It's infuriating... I have a whole group of craftsmen and artisans at my estate. If you're interested, I'd like to assign them all to you. Whatever you want to make, just have them do the work. I've heard from Pei and Junwu recently that you've been working on things related to gunpowder. Honestly, the military hasn't neglected research in this area either, and I know you have ideas, but it's extremely dangerous. Those fire lances and such, even if you really use iron for them, they can still explode and injure you. The military has tried it before. Why don't you just share your ideas and let others handle the actual work?"

The Wu Dynasty military was indeed researching gunpowder weapons, with all kinds of chaotic designs floating around, but they all inevitably earned the assessment of being flashy and impractical. Kang Xian naturally regarded Ning Yi differently and didn't want him injured researching this. Just thinking about the disaster relief manual plus the telescope, Ning Yi's value was already staggering, not to mention all the strategic planning ability he'd displayed in countless small ways during this time. He wanted to recommend talent to serve the nation, but Ning Yi stubbornly had his own set of ideas, ideas that he and Qin Siyuan still hadn't fully grasped. For now, they could only let him do as he pleased.

Ning Yi's enthusiasm for firearms currently stopped there, mainly due to technological limitations. It wasn't yet time to truly develop this. No matter how much he developed firearms, for now they still wouldn't match powerful crossbows. He hadn't yet decided what to work on next, so he could only shake his head and politely decline Kang Xian's kind offer. If he'd really accepted, it would have just become another constraint.

"By the way, how much longer until the war?"

When Ning Yi brought this up, Kang Xian also shook his head with a smile. "Not certain. They're still negotiating. Minister Tong has been overseeing this matter for several years now. Though I don't usually ask much about it, looking at the situation, it should be soon. It's just that winter's arrived now, and the weather in the Liao territories must be even harsher. If they can reach an agreement, perhaps there'll be results after spring arrives... What does Master Qin think?"

Old Qin thought for a moment and nodded. "Though Tong Guan is... *ahem*, though he's a eunuch, he does handle matters well. However, I'm somewhat worried now..."

"Worried about what?"

Old Qin held up a Go stone for a long time before finally placing it, then let out a sigh. "Worried it's too hasty."

Kang Xian had never entered official service, but Old Qin had once been a high-ranking and powerful official. The current Minister Tong had initially served under him and perhaps had to answer to his authority. Yet Old Qin rarely discussed these matters normally, and even now only said a few words before changing the subject. Still, Ning Yi could roughly see that the old man probably cared deeply about this, which was precisely why he was reluctant to say much.

Playing Go with Old Kang and Old Qin rarely involved much political discussion. Most of the time, they talked about academic questions, various trivial events happening around Jiangning, and made jokes about them.

Time passed through such days until late October. The relationship between Ning Yi and Su Tan'er became even more harmonious. After the weather turned cold, everyone gathered in the living room at night to chat, play Go, and tell stories—warm and cozy. Su Tan'er still seemed busy these past few days, but she'd basically finished the matters weighing most heavily on her mind.

Looking at the Su family as a whole, things had actually been quite busy recently. Normally, the Su family calculated performance and distributed dividends around late November or early December each year. But this year, all the important managers from various locations arrived a month early, and year-end work had already begun trickling in. With all these friends and relatives gathered, the Su household bustled with activity daily—teahouses and taverns during the day, pleasure quarters and singing houses at night. Thanks to this late October reversal, the Su family's reputation in Jiangning's merchant circles had grown even more prominent for a time.

Because of all this, Su Tan'er couldn't really find much free time. After the clan assembly, her cousin Su Danhong often came to keep her company, so Ning Yi didn't actually spend much time alone with her. Still, the relationship between husband and wife had deepened regardless. Su Tan'er had her own mind, so there wasn't much need to worry about her. It was just that when he returned home on the afternoon of the fifth day of the eleventh month, Ning Yi saw Su Tan'er doing something strange.

Though the temperature had already dropped, the weather that afternoon was pleasant. Ning Yi got home a bit early, and the small courtyard seemed peaceful and quiet. He'd initially thought no one was there, but after looking around, he discovered Su Tan'er sitting in the pavilion, facing the small building where he lived, lost in thought about something.

Theoretically, after the clan assembly, there shouldn't be any major issues worth making her frown and look troubled like this. Ning Yi watched for a few moments, somewhat puzzled, but Su Tan'er seemed absorbed in her thoughts, her expression shifting, not noticing him. After a moment, he saw her press her lips together as if making a decision, then stand up. She looked deeply at the small building once more before turning toward the small kitchen nearby.

That expression of decision-making carried a certain childishness, the kind of expression that belonged to a nineteen-year-old girl, though what difficult decision she'd just made remained unclear. Ning Yi shrugged his shoulders and went back to his room first. Shortly after closing the door, he saw Su Tan'er's figure hurrying out of the small kitchen, apparently having thought of something. She walked quickly to the courtyard gate and looked both ways several times. After confirming no one was around, she returned to the small kitchen and came out carrying a bundle of firewood.

Su Tan'er normally paid considerable attention to propriety and appearances. While she might lend a hand moving goods in the warehouse, at home she absolutely maintained the image of a refined young lady from a good family. She basically never did things like starting fires or handling firewood. But the atmosphere right now was definitely rather mysterious. Ning Yi secretly watched from his room as Su Tan'er, carrying that firewood, examined the small building on his side once more before walking toward the back of it.

Ning Yi closed the door and quietly followed. He saw Su Tan'er pile the dry, easily flammable kindling she'd selected by the window at the back of his room, seeming to deliberate for quite a while about the placement.

Ning Yi was somewhat dumbfounded. This woman spent all that time making a decision, and she's planning to murder her husband? By burning him alive, so cruelly?

Before long, he discovered that things weren't developing quite the way he'd thought...
