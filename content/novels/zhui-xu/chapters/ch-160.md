Su Tan'er had been troubled lately.

It was a private sort of trouble, one that brought with it feelings rather unfamiliar to her—now that the major household matters had been settled, she'd find herself blushing whenever certain thoughts crept into her mind over the past few days, though she hadn't discussed much of it even with Cousin Danhong, instead forcing down her embarrassment as she struggled to think through certain... matters.

If only she hadn't run away on their wedding night...

She was regretting that decision now, though she knew regret was a fairly useless emotion since so many things in life turned out in ways impossible to predict, and since these thoughts had nothing to do with business anyway, after all her anxious deliberation she could only purse her lips and blame herself for her lack of foresight and childishness. Looking back on it now, she could barely remember what she'd been thinking at the time—she'd known what would happen, known it was unavoidable whether she stuck her head out or pulled it back in, yet in the end she'd still fled pointlessly, and if she'd just closed her eyes back then and endured being bitten with resignation, she wouldn't be agonizing over such embarrassing matters every single day now.

Consummating a marriage... naturally required the right atmosphere and a natural opportunity, but these past days had been so busy with household affairs, and even though she'd achieved what she'd set out to achieve, there were still countless things she could occupy herself with if she wanted to stay busy—her husband probably assumed she'd continue being preoccupied for a while longer, and being a proper gentleman, now that their relationship had settled into a comfortable pattern, he wouldn't suddenly decide to push her onto a bed one day, though honestly, if he actually did that now, she wouldn't really mind anymore...

But she was forced to consider and plan these things now—surely between husband and wife... they couldn't possibly drag this out past winter. Yet when she truly thought of themselves as "husband and wife," when she examined the matter from that angle, she had to admit that the way they interacted now, this relationship they'd built, had started with her own choices. Before, she'd thought that having the appearance of a family and treating her husband well would be enough, but looking at it now, as a wife in the truest sense... she really hadn't fulfilled much of that responsibility.

Still, she wasn't the type to wallow in self-pity all day—her years in the business world had forged a certain decisiveness in her, so after several days of fretting over the problem, her first thought was that she needed to solve this matter herself. Of course, she couldn't exactly go talk to her husband directly about it, she did care about face after all, and now that she'd begun to care what he thought of her, she valued his opinion of her even more.

In any case, she needed to solve the problem... it was a bit troublesome, but once her mind started working, it wasn't difficult for this young woman with her reputation as a capable businesswoman—that morning an idea came to her, and while it might seem absurd and making a mountain out of a molehill to most people, to Su Tan'er it was no different from making any business decision, so with the same decisiveness she'd use when orchestrating deals worth hundreds of thousands of taels of silver, she committed to this small scheme. She returned home early at noon, then arranged for Chan'er, Juan'er, and Xing'er to go handle various tasks while mobilizing the servants and guards from several nearby courtyards, making sure they wouldn't all come rushing over at once, and once everything was prepared, she gritted her teeth and began.

She carefully examined the small building's structure, then hauled dry, thin kindling from the woodshed and piled it around the perimeter, initially considering many factors—the fire needed to burn evenly, the ignition points required careful thought, whether she should make it look like an accident—but when it came to arson she was completely inexperienced, so she shook her head: who cares, the house was hers anyway, if she wanted to casually burn it down that was her business, and as long as she forbade any investigation afterward, who would dare say anything?

In the quiet afternoon, the young woman who held power over the First Branch went about these furtive tasks with nervous concentration, then remembered a few more things and ran into her husband's room, apparently planning to do something else.

Outside the door, Ning Yi pressed his ear against it in confusion and saw the woman inside opening his cabinet, hurriedly pulling things out to examine and sort through.

"Brush, ink, paper, inkstone, clothes... is this... writing my husband did before?"

"Fans... this fan should probably be kept..."

"This outfit... forget it, burn it..."

"What's written on this..."

"Paintings... diagrams..."

"Vernacular novels... uh, should I burn these or not?"

She muttered to herself as she sorted through things, placing items she wanted to keep at a distance from the rear window while casually tossing old clothes near the window sill, then retrieved a brush holder and hung several writing brushes on it, found an inkstone Ning Yi had used and thought for a moment before switching it for one he'd barely touched, poured some tea into it and ground the ink stick a few times. She'd first moved the large stack of vernacular novels to the far side of the room away from the window, but after a moment's consideration she carried them all back and placed them on the doomed table by the window—Ning Yi watched with some regret since there were several volumes he hadn't finished reading yet.

Once she'd roughly organized everything and adjusted the positions of various furniture pieces with much grunting effort, she wiped the sweat from her forehead and nodded in satisfaction. Seeing that she was about to come out, Ning Yi quickly fled to the neighboring empty courtyard, climbed onto a pile of earthen bricks in the corner, and scrambled up the wall to watch what would happen behind the building.

Before long, Su Tan'er came coughing with a large lit branch held in both hands—it was thick enough that it probably hadn't dried completely, and being hollow, one end burned with flames while the other end's small hole poured out smoke relentlessly, and Su Tan'er, who'd apparently grabbed it thinking it would work as a torch, was now being smoked quite severely as she squinted and waved her hand to fan away the fumes, looking thoroughly uncomfortable.

Ning Yi covered his forehead, laughing silently.

Su Tan'er moved decisively without overthinking it, lighting the piled kindling cluster by cluster with a frown, her expression focused despite constantly being bothered by the smoke. The building was mostly wooden construction, so igniting it this way made catching fire a certainty, and she also lit two windows before tossing that smoking branch that had tormented her through a small opening in the window and dusting off her palms—Ning Yi rather liked the crisp way she clapped her hands together at that moment, though just then the tiles on the wall shifted slightly, and when he reached out to steady them, he froze for an instant.

When he looked toward Su Tan'er, she was also looking in his direction, her delicate oval face showing a stunned expression, lips pressed together as if they might turn into a harelip, and what showed on the face of this once composed and intelligent young woman was nothing short of catastrophic panic.

Ning Yi's half-visible head probably made eye contact with her for about half a second before his first action was to decisively duck back down, the laughter reaching his mouth making his cheeks puff out with a "pff," and with escape being the most important thing right now, his foot suddenly slipped—the pile of bricks had been sitting there a long time and one corner had loosened, making him tumble down with a thud in an ungainly heap that nearly twisted his ankle, then he fled while suppressing his laughter.

********************

Ning Yi stood guard on the nearby roads for about two minutes, leaning against a wall to digest that series of entertaining scenes he'd just witnessed while making sure no one came by yet so the fire in the small building could burn a while longer. Su Tan'er hadn't chased after him, and predicting her emotional state right now was difficult—of course, if the first emotion she saw on his face when they met was helpless laughter, then tonight this courtyard would probably have better than an eighty percent chance of witnessing an actual "husband murder" tragedy.

A smart man should know when to have a sense of humor, when to be serious, when to act confused, when to look agonized... but still, it was really funny... hahahaha...

He got all the laughing out of his system internally while keeping his face mostly expressionless, and when two servants passed by he sternly stopped them: "Hey, where are you going?"

"Ah, Young Master, we went to deliver some things earlier and we're heading back to report to Manager Zhou now."

"You finished delivering everything?"

"Yes."

"I might need to go out in a bit, so don't report to Manager Zhou just yet—go prepare a carriage for me at the side gate, I need to handle something... but if I don't come over within half an hour, that means there's nothing after all and you can go report to Manager Zhou then."

By now no one in the household dared ignore him, so once he'd spoken the two servants immediately agreed and turned to leave. Ning Yi glanced back, figuring the fire had probably reached a decent scale by now, and just then he heard his wife's voice from over there.

"Someone come quick, there's a fire, there's a fire! Someone come quick..."

Her voice sounded pretty calm, all things considered.

***********************

With the fire started, the first two servants who heard the news came running through the courtyard gate—the flames were still burning at the back of the building for now, but smoke had already begun billowing out from the front. In the center of the courtyard, Su Tan'er frowned: "There's a fire, hurry and find a way to put it out!"

"Yes, buckets, water barrels... where are the water barrels, Second Miss..."

"Someone come, there's a fire!"

The two servants panicked somewhat, but the Second Miss who'd just taken over the First Branch was someone who handled major affairs, and she remained composed: "Wait, wait, there's no water in the kitchen... you two, go call for people first, and hurry up and move things out of the rooms so they don't get burned! Quickly."

As she spoke, another servant came running over, and Su Tan'er said: "Hurry, you go too..." Her words hadn't even finished when Ning Yi's figure also appeared at the courtyard gate, and while she couldn't pay attention to her husband's expression right then, her face suddenly flushed bright red as she stared wide-eyed in a daze before looking at that servant: "You go help too!"

Her head turned back to look at the smoking building, her chest rising and falling as her heart pounded frantically inside—thump thump thump, thump thump thump.

Then Ning Yi came running over too: "What happened! What happened! How did a fire start!" He looked completely shocked and distressed, and hearing his voice, Su Tan'er also froze for a moment and turned to glance at him, seeing Ning Yi panting with furrowed brows: "How... how did a fire start..."

As Ning Yi said this and looked at her, Su Tan'er's face flushed red again with a "whoosh" as she turned away, struggling to put on her professional business face: "I... I don't know either, it suddenly caught fire, maybe it was... when no one was around, I heated some water and then... then didn't put it out properly... I was resting just now..."

"Oh," Ning Yi nodded, then fell silent for a moment. "Uh... it's winter now, the weather's dry, fires... fires are quite normal." He was clearly grasping for something to say when one of the servants rushed out from the small kitchen carrying a water bucket and told the other servant: "Ah Shan, there's only two buckets of water left here! We need to check the neighboring courtyard..."

As he spoke he was about to charge into the building, but Ning Yi ran over: "What are you doing?"

"Putting... putting out the fire..."

"Two buckets of water won't put out the fire, move things first, move things! Find some blankets and soak them so you won't get burned..."

"Oh..." The servant nodded and with a splash dumped the entire bucket of water over himself before charging straight in.

The flames roared as they burned while smoke billowed up, and servants and maids rushed over one after another, carrying things out from the building. Su Tan'er and Ning Yi stood in the center of the courtyard, the young woman's expression serious and frowning though she didn't really look at Ning Yi much, their voices ringing out amid all the shouting.

"Move that chair out first... *cough cough*..."

"By the bed, by the bed there's a box..."

"Don't worry about the Eight Immortals table!"

"Books, books, those bamboo scrolls, right..."

"Put things under the eaves over there first..."

"The things in Xiao Chan's room! Right... hurry hurry, be careful not to get burned..."

"Anyone who gets burned can collect ten taels of silver for medicine afterward..."

"Everyone gets a reward..."

Amid all the bustling activity, Ning Yi gave instructions on which things the servants should move while Su Tan'er pointed here and there directing servants to put items on the other side of the courtyard, and firefighting had already begun too, with people carrying water buckets and basins running back and forth continuously, and before long Xiao Chan and the others came rushing over shouting "What happened, how did a fire start" in a flurry of activity, while one manager who apparently hadn't seen the resting Su Tan'er or Ning Yi who'd started helping with firefighting burst in bellowing: "How did a fire start, how did a fire start! Did anyone get burned..." making the afternoon even more chaotic.

"Well... it's winter, you know, the weather's dry, fires are quite normal..." Ning Yi dumped a bucket of water into the flames, walked over and patted the manager's shoulder. "Everyone's already very anxious and annoyed right now, so stop asking why, talk less and do more, there's no need for you to keep questioning, here, take this bucket and go fight the fire."

At the courtyard gate, Chan'er came running in with a washbasin along with several other maids, and Su Tan'er called out: "Xiao Chan, don't go, don't get burned." Xiao Chan turned her head: "I'm fine," then with a crash fell to the ground with water splashing everywhere and the basin rolling around as voices rose in alarm, creating another wave of chaos...

*********************

After the fire burned and then was extinguished, that scorched smell permeated the air, and as the sunset cast warm yellow light, that building—its upper half charred black and a small section collapsed—still stood in the courtyard, though it was definitely no longer habitable.

The courtyard was filled with rescued items—tables, chairs, cabinets, and all sorts of miscellaneous things. Servants worked on cleaning up the fire site while various relatives arrived one after another, and Su Yu and Su Boyong had come by earlier as well, with the important people talking in a babble of voices while the less important ones didn't dare make much noise, and under a manager's direction servants began moving those items into the building on the other side. Xiao Chan could be arranged into a room next to Juan'er and Xing'er's, and so that was done, with servants moving things while Xiao Chan inventoried the rescued items with Juan'er and Xing'er's company.

Her belongings were clearly sorted, but more items had ambiguous status.

When the manager went to ask Su Tan'er for instructions earlier, she'd been talking with a cousin-uncle and casually pointed: "Just put them on the table in the room for now." So the manager did exactly that.

Those were some of Ning Yi's miscellaneous belongings, and she'd pointed to what had originally been her own bedroom, though of course at that moment she was just answering offhandedly, and in such a chaotic scene no one paid any attention to what consideration might lie behind that casual response.

She was busy after all, and besides, who would notice what thought she'd put into an offhand answer in such confusion—she was busy.

As the sunset gradually faded and oil lamps and torches were lit all around, when dinnertime approached the servants handling cleanup were ordered to stop work for now, and in the small courtyard a certain atmosphere began turning strange.

Nothing remained scattered in the courtyard anymore—even the last chair Ning Yi had been sitting on had been pulled by him into the dining room, and only then did some people realize that earlier, most of those items had been temporarily stuffed into Su Tan'er's room, which was now packed rather full with things placed somewhat haphazardly.

No one understood how things had ended up this way—throughout the afternoon Su Tan'er had been dealing with numerous relatives and hadn't directed anyone about where to move what, while Ning Yi had been busy with other cleanup tasks, yet gradually those things had simply disappeared, and thinking back on it, they couldn't even figure out whose idea it had been or who'd given which instructions.

Su Tan'er went to look at the crowded room and seemed somewhat troubled though she didn't say anything, while Ning Yi also went to look and was probably equally troubled about where to sleep tonight, and Chan'er, Juan'er, and Xing'er went to look as well but similarly had no idea how to organize things.

No one could quite pinpoint when the atmosphere had started turning peculiar.

What followed might be an awkward evening. Or perhaps... it would be an interesting one...
