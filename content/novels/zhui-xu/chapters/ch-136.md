"In response to Second Young Master, everyone initially thought the problem was limited to the first batch. After the incident, Manager Qin locked the affected fabrics in the warehouse beside the workshop to prevent further issues before delivery, personally checking on them daily. While at first no one noticed anything amiss, I've since heard that his spirits seemed off for the past few days. It wasn't until we discovered the full extent of the issue today that we realized he had likely been aware of the daily fading ever since the first batch showed problems. He probably held back, clinging to the hope that the fading wasn't severe, but as more fabric changed color each day, he knew we had a major crisis on our hands..."

Inside the carriage, Wu Qihao furrowed his brow and clenched his left hand into a fist, seeming about to curse, but ultimately held back. "Why didn't he say something earlier..."

Yet the answer to this question was something he already understood in his heart.

"Has the information been locked down?"

"It was immediately sealed after discovery—not many people know. Manager Qin's condition doesn't look good though; we've already called for a doctor..."

"Uncle Qin... is ultimately still dutiful..."¹

Wu Qihao spoke with furrowed brows, finally saying these words before sitting there without speaking further. Pulled from a meeting at a cloth merchant's home by a family servant, Wu Qihao couldn't yet grasp the full situation, but connecting it to the problems with the first batch gave him the terrible feeling of being struck from behind without warning. He didn't dare assume the worst, hoping instead that it was merely an isolated incident caused by some internal mistake—after all, some issues were to be expected with a new fabric.

Lifting the carriage curtain, he saw they were nearing the workshop as a Su's Cloth Store sign came into view. The sight of that sign anywhere in Jiangning usually made him want to laugh, especially when he was with others who would inevitably start discussing it. The others would "objectively" discuss various problems the Su family might face in the future and how their profits would flow away, while he would shake his head and smile beside them without much comment, enjoying a certain sense of achievement. As a member of the Wu family—even one of the heirs—he truly felt like he was standing at the summit looking down at all the other mountains, where neither the Su family nor the people talking beside him were worth considering anymore. But now, he let the curtain fall, waving away the surge of irritation rising in his heart.

It couldn't possibly be related to them—it had already been over a month...

Without thinking further, the carriage arrived at the small workshop, where at the entrance he encountered Manager Luo's carriage. He exchanged greetings with Luo Minzhi, but from each other's eyes, they could both see that feeling of worry, so they didn't talk much and entered together. In the adjacent workshop, bathed in the evening sun, the production of Brilliant Gold Brocade continued in full swing. Bright yellow fabric billowed dazzlingly in the air, showing no hint of trouble as workers shouted, heaving cloth from massive dye vats. A manager stood by, calling out: "Easy does it! Not a single problem—we're making fabric for His Majesty himself..."²

Wu Qihao and Luo Minzhi went through to the warehouse on one side, where security had always been tight but was now reinforced with additional guards. Making their way into the small warehouse, lamps had already been lit, and including Wu Qilong, several of the Wu family's senior managers had arrived—these were the trusted, elder-level members responsible for various processes from start to finish. Before them stood a wall of Brilliant Gold Brocade, with the fading of some fabrics immediately obvious.

Luo Minzhi took just one glance before beginning to check various markings on the fabric with two other managers.

"How... how is Uncle Qin?"

Wu Qihao looked up at the fabric wall, furrowed his brow, and closed his eyes briefly, but his first question to his brother was still about this matter. Wu Qilong was sitting on a stool at this time and shook his head, remaining silent for a long while before saying: "The doctor says he's fine, just too exhausted..."³

"Why is it fading?"

"We don't know, but..." Speaking of this, Wu Qilong suddenly stood up and walked toward his brother with a wave, taking several steps toward the fabric wall before picking up a bolt of cloth leaning nearby and placing it against the wall.

"Look," Wu Qilong began, placing a freshly made bolt against one from over a month ago. "After a month, they're identical—no fading at all. We tested them, and the dye is completely stable. But these faded ones..."

He smirked and pointed to the bolts Luo Minzhi's team was examining. "We've checked the timing, and the fading occurs sequentially in fabrics produced between a month and a half and twenty days ago. The waste fabrics from that period show the same pattern. And then there's this..."

He held up a wrinkled yet still brilliant piece of cloth that showed slight color inconsistency against the others. "This bolt was produced right on the time threshold. It looked normal, but after we soaked it in water and dried it by fire, it began to fade. I cut this piece to show you—the rest are still being tested."

"How could this happen..."

"Indeed." Wu Qilong smiled somewhat mockingly and sat back down, looking around the warehouse: "Is there a problem with the dyeing formula?"

This question came out simply, and in an instant everyone fell silent, exchanging glances. After a long while, Wu Qihao finally asked: "Is it possible?"

"How is it possible?" Wu Qilong frowned and shook his head. "We have more than one person placed in the Su family—if we still got the wrong formula under such circumstances, then unless this person... unless this person could see everything we were doing from the very beginning. How could there be such a person over several years' time? Even if it were Su Yu, it wouldn't be possible. Our decision to compete for the imperial merchant status was only made this year—are you going to tell me now that someone was laying plans years ago... The only one who could have been planning years ago would be Su Tan'er, but how could she have targeted us years ago..."

"If she really had been watching from behind all along, the benefits she could gain from taking the imperial merchant status herself would be far greater than this..."

"For now... it might be a problem on our end..." Wu Qilong rubbed his forehead, then looked at the several managers in front: "Uncle Luo, Uncle Nie, the current matter still requires everyone to contain this news and have the master dyers at the dyeing workshop check the formula and analyze possible problems. This matter is too strange to draw conclusions yet. Everyone should do their own work well. I will communicate more with Director Dong at the Imperial Textile Bureau⁴ along with father to postpone the delivery date. The Imperial Textile Bureau has already granted the imperial merchant status to our Wu family and won't stand by watching us fail... Our Wu family has reached this point over decades, encountering countless difficulties large and small along the way, with everyone weathering storms together⁵. In Jiangning's cloth industry, if we claim second place, no one dares claim first. This time, as long as everyone works hard, we'll be fine as well... The matters here are entrusted to all you uncles."

The people in this room weren't just Wu family confidants but were also seasoned business veterans who had experienced various storms, roughly on the same level as the Su family's Manager Liao and others. Even without Wu Qilong speaking, they generally knew what they should do. They now responded in unison and began gathering together to discuss.

As the last crimson of sunset faded and the workshop's torches and lanterns were lit, the two Wu brothers walked out together. Though the shifts changed and production continued unabated, their mood was impossible to describe. All this fabric was still being produced only to be sent to a warehouse where it might all fade—what, then, was the point of it all?

For the past month, every action had felt exceptionally meaningful, but now, looking back, they realized the very foundation of their work was flawed. The sudden loss of purpose was disorienting—what had all their frantic effort been for?

"Brother, is someone really targeting us from the shadows?"

Wu Qihao had been thinking for a long time and now, looking at this scene where meaning had been lost in an instant yet temporarily had to continue, finally asked. Wu Qilong's brow was tightly furrowed as he shook his head and looked back at the warehouse entrance.

"How would we know now? It shouldn't be like this. Now... now we can only hope it's a problem on our end. If not..."

He frowned, unable to understand. Indeed, looking around, they couldn't see any enemies—before the imperial merchant competition, they hadn't sensed any hostility, and after winning it, even if there was some animosity, it couldn't be put into practice. They had indeed made one move, but all their planning had been in secret. Theoretically, no one should have detected their preparations—they were like a tiger that had devoured a goat with lightning speed. The entire process had no problems, no arrows, no hunters, no blades or spears, and even that goat had no time to resist. Everything was perfect and smooth, but in the end, he discovered wounds on his body yet had no idea when or where those wounds had appeared. Moreover, these wounds were so severe they might even be fatal.

Who exactly was it...

The tiger suddenly awakened and began looking around into the surrounding darkness, yet still couldn't see anything as the forest began filling with hostility...

"If not... perhaps someone has been behind us all along for the past few months, watching us..."

Wu Qilong murmured these words, and Wu Qihao instinctively glanced behind him, turning in a circle: "Then who exactly would it be? Su Yu? Su Tan'er? Who else? Some of the Su family elders?"

"It doesn't seem like it..." Wu Qilong shook his head. "It doesn't seem likely... this doesn't look like a trap they would set. Xi Junyu is also impossible—what we took wasn't just his things. This time... who exactly schemed against us?"

"Stop thinking about it, brother. Maybe it's just some small issue that went wrong. We can't panic now—let's investigate clearly first."

Wu Qihao consoled his brother, and Wu Qilong subsequently nodded: "Yes, let's go back and start investigating. For now..." He looked at the workshop ahead still at work, and further at Jiangning city where various lights were coming on. "For now... let's just wait and see."

In the sky, night fell as darkness had just descended.

They passed through the dim passageway outside the small workshop, emerging at the workshop entrance where lamplight shone, boarding their carriage and heading home with incomprehensible anxiety. The roads alternated between light and dark, while many, many more people still had no idea about what had happened that afternoon in this corner of Jiangning.

Back at the Su residence, Ning Yi was cooling off in the courtyard pavilion after his bath, playing a childish guessing game with Xiao Chan over a bowl of cooked peanuts. Soon, the sound of voices and footsteps announced the return of Su Tan'er with Juan'er and Xing'er. Despite another busy day running errands, she broke into a contented, tight-lipped smile the moment she saw Ning Yi.

On evenings like this in the past, children would often come to play, or cousins close to the main branch would come asking for money or to chat, but such people had become much fewer these days. Chan'er went to prepare some simple dishes, and before long Su Tan'er had also bathed and come out, with Juan'er taking her turn. Everyone sat together in the pavilion chatting, talking, and eating—even many business matters Su Tan'er would now casually discuss with Ning Yi without concern, though of course, Ning Yi usually just made casual jokes that everyone would laugh about.

Under the stars and moon, it was another leisurely day...

**Cultural Footnotes:**

¹ **Dutiful (尽责)**: In Chinese business culture, managers taking personal responsibility for failures to the point of mental breakdown reflects deep-rooted Confucian values of loyalty and duty to one's employer. Manager Qin's silence about the problem stems from shame and hope to somehow resolve it without bringing dishonor.

² **Imperial cloth production**: The gravity of producing textiles for the emperor cannot be overstated. Any defect in imperial goods could result in severe punishment for the entire merchant family, making this crisis potentially catastrophic for the Wu family's newly acquired Imperial Merchant status.

³ **Too exhausted (心力交瘁)**: This euphemistic diagnosis for what appears to be a mental breakdown reflects traditional Chinese medicine's holistic view linking physical and mental health, avoiding direct discussion of psychological distress.

⁴ **Imperial Textile Bureau (织造局)**: The Imperial Textile Bureau was a powerful government institution overseeing textile production for the court. Having their support as Imperial Merchants was crucial, as they could provide protection and extensions when problems arose.

⁵ **Weather storms together (风雨同济)**: This phrase embodies the Chinese business philosophy of collective loyalty through hardship. It reflects the expectation that long-serving employees and managers will stand by the business family through crises, viewing the company's fate as their own.
