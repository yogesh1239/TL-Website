By the time Wu Qilong returned from the restaurant to the Wu residence, the evening sky had already darkened. The Wu family mansion was brilliantly lit inside and out, its festive atmosphere lingering from the past month and a half of celebration—visible in the very bearing and demeanor of every servant and attendant, though perhaps only those serving closest to the family's highest-ranking members could vaguely sense that something was amiss. As he entered through the main gate, a servant stationed at the entrance carefully approached.

"First Young Master has returned. Second Young Master and the Master arrived home half an hour ago. Additionally, Third Uncle, Fifth Uncle, Sixth Uncle, Manager Luo, and Manager Nie have all arrived and are currently discussing matters with the Master in the side hall."

It was just after dinner, when most households would be settling in for the evening, yet the various managers—who over the past month had typically been out entertaining clients until late into the night—had gathered early, something that had only occurred these past two days. Wu Qilong nodded and proceeded silently toward the side hall, only to hear a crash from inside as he reached the corridor—the sound of a teacup smashing against the floor.

"So you're telling me it can't be resolved? After only three days? You're saying it can't be resolved?"

The one who had thrown the teacup and was now speaking was his father, Wu Chenghou—rarely had Wu Qilong seen him lose control like this in all these years, but the consequences of this problem were far too severe and the reversal far too sudden. They'd been struck with a crushing blow out of nowhere, leaving everyone still reeling. In the side hall, his father was confronting Fifth Uncle, who oversaw the dyeing operations despite Manager Nie's technical responsibility, and was likely the only one who could engage in any negotiation with Father at this point.

"But... it truly cannot be resolved. This formula wasn't something we developed ourselves. In the two months since we obtained it, the master craftsmen in our household have all been attempting modifications, but this formula is extremely sensitive—even the slightest changes cause dramatic color shifts. The Su family even used ingredients originally meant for dyeing blue fabrics. Right now... it's not that it absolutely cannot be resolved, perhaps with some luck..."

Fifth Uncle Wu Chengke was clearly in a difficult position as well. Wu Qilong entered through the doorway, and his father glanced at him from above, casually waving for him to sit down at the side before turning back to confront Fifth Uncle.

"...Luck?"

"Heh, the Su family spent two or three years developing this formula, and we currently have no leads whatsoever. Master Chen and the others say... perhaps we can only rely on luck..."

In the business world, when asked to solve a problem and told that one could only rely on luck at this point—Wu Chenghou glared with wide eyes from his position above, and the entire main hall fell into complete silence. After a long moment, Wu Chenghou opened and closed his mouth several times before finally sinking back into his seat: "So we can confirm it? This isn't a problem on our end—we've truly been... outmaneuvered by the Su family?"

Among those present in the side hall, no one dared to speak, no one dared to draw such a conclusion. Perhaps everyone had considered it, but if this were truly the case, the price they would have to pay afterward would be terrifyingly steep. After a moment of silence, Luo Minzhi shook his head:

"There are still suspicious elements in this matter that are difficult to understand. If the Su family truly laid such a trap, wouldn't it have been better for them to directly secure the Imperial Merchant¹ position? Su Tan'er spent years working on this—anyone can see that. Look at the Su family now, in complete disarray. Even if there's truly some turnaround, the commotion over this past month and a half has been enough to cause them substantial losses. I've discussed this with Third Uncle and Manager Nie—if there was indeed some conspiracy two months ago, the risk would have been far too great for the Su family..."

Wu Chengyuan, who held the third position in the Wu family hierarchy, nodded at this point: "Brother Luo is right. We originally had no intention of using the Su family's formula—we only decided on a whim over two months ago. If the Su family truly had another formula, we couldn't possibly have been unaware of it. Our subsequent deductions proved there were no issues before we used this Brilliant Gold Brocade. If the Su family had planned all this from the beginning, how could they have been so certain we would take the bait? To say they calculated everything so perfectly—I don't believe it. Su Tan'er doesn't have such capability, and even Su Yu, as scheming as he is, hasn't reached such a level..."

"But regardless of everything else, our current situation is what it is..." Wu Qilong had been sitting quietly to the side since entering the room, appearing rather dejected, his gaze merely drifting indifferently over the people in the side hall. Wu Qihao glanced at his elder brother several times before sighing and beginning to speak as well.

"Since things have already come to this, we must start considering our response going forward. My father and I spoke with Director Dong today. Postponing the delivery date shouldn't be a problem, but the current issue is this: once we formally request a postponement from the Weaving Bureau², this matter will have to be recorded in official documents. At that point, it won't be something Director Dong can suppress. News of the Wu family's troubles will inevitably spread, and what happens then will be hard to predict. We have ten days until the agreed delivery date—after ten days, we'll need to have figured out how to handle this."

He paused briefly: "And regardless of whether we postpone by one month or two months, to ultimately resolve the problem, we still need to perfect this formula. Fifth Uncle, Manager Nie, whether through desperate effort or relying on luck, we can only try. Additionally, if the Su family has the real formula, we'll probably need to attempt that approach as well. When that time comes... Elder Brother, it will depend on your side..."

Wu Qihao glanced toward his brother, while on the other side, Sixth Uncle from the clan shook his head: "If it's not the Su family laying a trap, then we could indeed proceed that way. We can't confirm this at present."

"But we can only handle it this way for now." Wu Chengyuan interjected, "We truly can't confirm it now, but if this isn't the Su family's scheme and is genuinely due to coincidence—if we had a problem on our end that we could have addressed but didn't, wouldn't we ultimately become a laughingstock?"

During the somewhat heated debate, Wu Qilong stood up at his younger brother's prompting. He patted Wu Qihao's shoulder: "Father, uncles, I... I've recently been handling matters regarding our northwestern expansion and haven't been paying close attention to affairs in Jiangning. I know some things, but not in great detail. May I ask... what exactly has the Su family been doing lately?"

Though Wu Qilong possessed Wu Chenghou's bearing, his voice wasn't loud at this moment, but he clearly had reached some conclusions in his mind. Everyone glanced at him, and Wu Chengyuan thought for a moment before sitting down in his seat: "Internal strife, I suppose."

"The situation isn't good. Su Zhongkan and Su Yunfang have made their move, and things are in complete chaos right now." Wu Chengke also shook his head, "Su Tan'er is running around frantically, apologizing everywhere, trying to pull strings and maintain their original partnerships."

"I've heard... it doesn't seem to be having much effect, does it? Su Tan'er is capable, but previously she had Su Boyong behind her. Now I hear Su Boyong is paralyzed and still can't get out of bed after all this time. The Li and Nian families that were originally allied with them are preparing to cease cooperation with the Su family's First Branch. Various smaller businesses have also been affected. The main issue is that everyone's saying Su Tan'er won't be able to maintain control of the First Branch's business for much longer. The Xue family has also been courting these people recently. Once the Su family's business contracts, those who weren't particularly close to begin with would be better off cooperating with the Xue family first..."

Everyone had recently been busy with more substantial matters and didn't know too many specific details about the Su family's situation, though Luo Minzhi had attended quite a few dinner parties recently and paid some attention, so at this point everyone was speaking in bits and pieces. Wu Qilong frowned slightly: "What about Su Tan'er herself?"

"Maintaining their previous partnerships, of course." Wu Chengyuan smiled, "With Su Boyong down and the current situation, the Second and Third Branches of the Su family are seeing their businesses shrink. She still wants to maintain those previous businesses, to keep hold of everything Su Boyong originally controlled—how is that possible..."

Wu Qilong looked at his third uncle, his expression unchanged: "But what is the entire Su family First Branch doing?"

"The entire Su family First Branch, she..." Wu Chengyuan looked at his nephew, waving his hand, then stopping mid-air. After a moment he shook his head without speaking, as if he understood something—or perhaps had thought of it earlier but was unwilling to voice it, for however one thought about it, it all seemed too incredible, too difficult to comprehend. The faces of everyone in the room shifted slightly as they realized what they were thinking of now was nothing good.

"Actually..." Wu Qilong's expression carried an indescribable bitter smile as he slowly began to speak, "Actually... Third Uncle, Fifth Uncle, you all probably considered this possibility days ago, didn't you?"

"That's reasoning backward from the result—it's impossible." Wu Chengke's face was dark as he spoke curtly.

"At this point, we have no choice but to reason backward from the result..." Wu Qilong shook his head, "Three years of preparation, the imperial merchant position with a one-time investment of two hundred thousand taels of silver, and they'd probably invested even more before that. Su Tan'er upgraded all those looms under her command—we originally thought she was preparing for the massive annual cloth quota demands, dead set on securing the imperial merchant position. But she didn't stop even after the imperial merchant matter was settled. Everyone outside thinks she's lost her mind, unable to dismount from the tiger she's riding, wanting to target our Wu family by increasing production and dumping goods at low prices..."

"A woman whose mind has gone wrong can do anything," Wu Qilong muttered softly, "but what if that's not the case? Father, uncles, dumping goods at low prices ruins the market—everyone would unite against her, so we've never feared these moves of hers. But what if from the very beginning, this woman hasn't been targeting the market but specifically targeting our Wu family's market share, just waiting for us to vacate it ourselves?"

Wu Chenghou stared at his son for a long while before finally speaking, his voice hoarse: "If that's truly the case, she's almost fully prepared by now."

"Yes." Wu Qilong smiled wearily, his voice rising: "Right now, no one in the entire market is watching our Wu family—they see us expanding and are already considering how to step aside. Led by the Xue family, everyone is fixated on the Su family's apparent collapse, waiting to divide up their market shares. But what if the Su family doesn't collapse at all? What if this woman has simply been waiting ever since we secured the imperial merchant position..."

He waved his hand: "No, she might have been waiting even before we secured the imperial merchant position. Once we have problems here, the moment we release our market share in Jiangning's cloth trade, while those watching the Su family are still getting their big laugh, Su Tan'er will have already consumed seventy to eighty percent of it before the others can even react—the rest can only stare helplessly. For a month and a half, we thought everything was fine, treating them as defeated opponents we'd left in the dust, but actually? They'd already diverted the entire Jiangning weaving industry's attention, quietly completing all their preparations. Su Tan'er upgraded those looms under her command, increasing what now seems like useless production capacity—everyone was laughing, but only we would realize after the fact that they'd already been preparing for over a month..."

The suffocating feeling enveloped the entire side hall. After a moment, Wu Chengyuan still shook his head: "This is the worst-case scenario. If this is true, then this scheme is too outrageous. How could we not have sensed anything at all..."

Wu Chengke frowned: "Even if this is true, the Su family isn't having an easy time either. How long can Su Tan'er hold out? How long can we hold out? At worst, we'll delay—drag it out for two months and wear her down..."

"No matter how outrageous it is, what's before us is exactly this situation. Moreover..." Wu Qilong looked at Wu Chengke, "Fifth Uncle, they won't wait for us to collapse on our own. The day before yesterday the workshop had just developed problems and Manager Qin had just fallen ill, yet yesterday someone was already spreading rumors outside, saying our Wu family had encountered issues with the imperial merchant position. Of course, wild speculation and baseless conjecture are common in business, but this was remarkably fast—everyone knows someone is spreading rumors behind the scenes, though not many people are paying attention yet. But..."

He pulled out a sheet of rice paper from his chest: "Before coming back, I actually already investigated Su Tan'er's movements over the past month and a half. Now perhaps we can see things more clearly. After the imperial merchant matter, all adjustments and expenditures have been targeted at our Wu family—calculated and deliberate. In a month and a half, while we've been charging forward blindly, they've already silently arranged all their swords and spears, each one aimed at our vital points... Father, Fifth Uncle, can you sense it?"

Wu Qilong smiled bitterly, shaking his head as he looked in the direction of the Su residence: "That woman has already stealthily completed all her preparations... she's begun to make her move." The rice paper circulated through the hall. Over the past month and a half, all of this had seemed like a joke—as long as the Wu family didn't have problems, all these arrangements would be meaningless. But what problems could the Wu family have? It was under this assumption that they'd been singing triumphantly all the way toward the best possible outcome, only to discover when the path ahead turned into a dead end that the surroundings were already bristling with sharp edges. Looking at that paper listing all of Su Tan'er's targeted arrangements against the Wu family over the past month and a half, everyone felt an indescribable chill running up their spines, cold air surging upward. If this was real...

"I still don't believe it." Wu Chengyuan suddenly waved his hand. "If this is true, then we didn't walk into this ourselves—they lured us in. They've been calculating against us since two months ago or even earlier? Su Tan'er worked hard for three years to set up such a scheme? We weren't without consideration before about getting counterfeits, and if things were just a bit more deliberate, everyone would have seen through it! No one can set up such a scheme! This matter... isn't something you can accomplish just by thinking about it. The entire process—how difficult it would be, how many contingencies there would be—everyone understands clearly. Su Tan'er couldn't do it, and Su Yu wouldn't risk something like this! If they could secure the imperial merchant position, why wouldn't they? If they couldn't, why did they build momentum to that extent beforehand? They were just a hair's breadth from failing to trap anyone. Just obtaining the formula alone—if it were too difficult, we wouldn't have gotten it; if it were too simple, we wouldn't have believed it. How many times did we verify afterward before confirming there were no problems? Who could achieve this level of sophistication!"

"She's already begun to act. In a few more days, we'll be able to see. Actually, I also hope I'm just overthinking this..." Wu Qilong sat there, shaking his head somewhat quietly, "But if not, then thinking about the whole affair becomes... heh, quite interesting..."

"Su Tan'er's illness was real, and Su Yu couldn't handle such matters during that period—he is getting old, after all. But there was one person we might have all overlooked, or rather, apart from the beginning, we never took him seriously. Look... throughout this entire affair, he seemed to do nothing, yet after Su Tan'er fell ill, he was actually handling everything. He led us in circles, practically telling jokes every day, while behind it all, he was the one who boldly came forward saying they wanted to promote that yellow cloth with great fanfare, promote how capable the Su family was... Now that I think about it, there's actually one very interesting point. Whether it was me or people from the Xue family, we all had a habitual thought at the time—every time we talked in teahouses and taverns about what foolish things Ning Yi had done recently, we never forgot to remind others: Su Tan'er is very capable, the Su family is still using the most straightforward methods to compete for the imperial merchant position, so don't be fooled by Ning Yi's performance. As a result, everyone was clever, everyone was watching that piece of cloth from the Su family."

"Third Uncle, do you remember what you told me when I was young? If you want someone to see something, the best method isn't to place it in the most conspicuous spot, but to put it there and cover it with something, or bury it underground with a layer of sand. 'Trying to cover up only makes it more conspicuous,' 'No three hundred taels of silver buried here'³... Ning Yi was telling us from start to finish: the Su family has the best cloth, the Su family has the best cloth, the Su family has the best cloth! And... we all thought we were the clever ones, seeing the key point behind it all, slowly dropping our guard..."

"He's supposed to be a fool, right? A business idiot, right? With Su Tan'er ill, some oversights were inevitable. If it had been Su Tan'er herself, we might have been more vigilant because some small mistakes shouldn't have been made. But he kept making small mistakes, and none of us thought it was abnormal. Heh... in the end, he didn't do anything. We got the yellow cloth formula anyway, and he just watched. Then... 'Old friends still grip their swords'⁴... He finished his business, tossed everything aside, and left. For this past month and a half, he's been just like before, not even bothering to glance at these business matters. But even now, no one knows—perhaps he never took any of this seriously at all..."

Wu Qilong finished as if talking to himself, sitting there with a mocking smile. Wu Qihao beside him frowned: "Ning Yi? That's... impossible."

"I hope I'm mistaken too," Wu Qilong said, raising his head. "But today our contact in the Su family told me something. Two nights ago, Manager Zhou—the one in charge of their dye formulas—got drunk and admitted that the only people in the Su family he truly admired were Su Yu... and their son-in-law, Ning Yi."

Someone's eyes widened.

Wu Qilong paused: "Anyway... we still have a few days. No matter what, within ten days we'll have to request an extension from the Weaving Bureau. By then, if the Su family really did set this trap, everything will come at us. Then we'll know whether he was acting back then or was perfectly clear about everything in his mind. If it's really like this..." He looked toward the darkness outside the door, remembering that scholar's figure, "I'd be somewhat afraid of him..."

Recalling Ning Yi's behavior during that period and more recently, everyone remained silent and utterly speechless, exchanging glances of disbelief—but if it were true, that spine-chilling feeling would truly be bizarre to the extreme.

After a moment, Wu Qilong finally rubbed his forehead and sighed softly: "Poor Xi Junyu, he still doesn't know..."

***************

¹Imperial Merchant (皇商): A prestigious designation for merchants officially recognized by the imperial court to supply goods to the palace. This position brought immense wealth, political connections, and social status that could elevate a merchant family for generations.

²Weaving Bureau (织造局): The imperial textile manufacturing office responsible for producing fabrics for the court, managing quality standards, and overseeing contracts with merchant suppliers. Control over Weaving Bureau contracts was crucial for dominance in the textile industry.

³These are Chinese idioms about how attempts to hide something often draw more attention to it. The second refers to a story where someone buries silver and puts up a sign saying "No 300 taels of silver buried here," thus revealing exactly what they tried to hide.

⁴From Wang Wei's poem "Pouring Wine for Pei Di," expressing how even longtime friends remain wary of each other. The full line "白首相知犹按剑" (Even white-haired friends still keep hands on sword hilts) speaks to the perpetual distrust in human relationships.
