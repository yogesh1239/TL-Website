Sitting in a restaurant overlooking the bustling streets in the early morning hours, you could often watch people coming and going at the entrance to Auspicious Street not far away. This Auspicious Street was a famous area in Jiangning where brothels clustered together. At this time of day, you could see quite a few men who'd spent the night there emerging from that direction. Some were still adjusting their clothes on the street, while others looked rather flustered—probably having urgent matters—running off at full speed still fastening their robes. Though admittedly, there weren't that many of these.

In this era, patronizing courtesans¹ was after all quite a normal activity. Some young scholars walked out refreshed and clearheaded, greeting friends they recognized on the street before hooking arms to discuss which young lady's chambers they'd entered last night. There were also those with upright, proper appearances—refined and elegant like perfect gentlemen—whose bearing revealed few clues.

"Oh, Xiao Chan, look at that guy. He even bought meat porridge to take with him. This guy's planning to bring it home for his wife and kids... quite the family man."

"That young master? He doesn't look like it. You can't just say that about someone because they're coming from that direction, Young Master."

"You don't understand," Ning Yi said, tossing a silver thread pastry into his mouth as he sat by the second-floor window. "On the surface they all look about the same, but there's a definite difference in the bearing and complexion of a man who slept at home versus one who slept out."

"Young Master knows how to tell? Then teach Xiao Chan too, okay?"

"What are you planning to do?"

"Then Xiao Chan will be able to tell if Young Master stays at a brothel and doesn't come home."

"Heh."

The reason they'd been coming to this restaurant the past couple days to observe who hadn't come home for the night in such a boring manner was mainly because they'd discovered this restaurant's breakfast tasted pretty good. Xiao Chan had nothing to do today, so she'd also come along. Right now the two of them sat by this second-floor window pointing and commenting, occasionally making remarkable discoveries.

"Young Master, Young Master, look—that old man also came out from the brothels!"

"And how can you tell?"

"Because the young lady at that little building's window over there is still waving at him..."

"Such vigor in old age... truly makes one... envious..."

"Young Master isn't envious at all. You never go to those kinds of places."

The master and servant joked around like this for a while. By this time, quite a few other people had also arrived at the restaurant. Several young scholars—who under Ning Yi's identification were indeed men who hadn't come home for the night—sat at a table to the left. Their clothing was neat and their spirits good as they used poetry to describe the previous night's scenes, occasionally breaking into hearty laughter. When Xiao Chan heard those verses, she would carefully glance back, her face flushing red before turning back around.

At this moment, these talented scholars were discussing romantic affairs, often displaying rather frivolous attitudes. After a little while, someone made a gesture of lowering his voice: "Hey, you all—do you think any of us will have a chance to meet that Lady Shishi during this visit?"

"I heard Li Shishi is only visiting friends and not receiving guests, so it'll be hard to catch even a glimpse of her."

"That's what they say, and she's probably only meeting with important figures anyway."

"That's true. Though people like Cao Guan and Liu Qingdi will surely be able to meet her."

"Brother Jichang comes from a scholarly family with deep roots, and your clan uncle serves in the prefecture office—you'll probably be able to meet her too."

"Haha, just joking, just joking. Last year when Gu Yanzhen returned, he said he'd seen Li Shishi during his time in the Eastern Capital. I don't have such good fortune..."

"I wonder how Li Shishi, who's praised as the capital's number one courtesan, compares to our Jiangning's Qilan, Luo Miaomiao, and the others..."

"Compared to Qilan and the others, I'm afraid they fall somewhat short. Moreover, I feel Li Shishi's choice of timing for this visit to friends is truly perfect. Think about it—right now that top courtesan Qilan was deliberately promoted by the Puyang family. Although her artistic accomplishments are indeed remarkable, there's still too much of the taint of commerce about her. Luo Miaomiao has splendor in abundance but actually lacks sufficient grandeur. In the past, Feng Xiaojing was called an orchid in an empty valley², but... alas, after being driven by that brute Chen Yong to jump from her building, she's now fallen into obscurity. And that lively and pure Yuan Jin'er is said to have already left the life³. With two of the four former top courtesans gone, Qilan and Luo Miaomiao simply can't hold the field against someone of Li Shishi's caliber. So tell me, don't you think she deliberately chose this moment? She might *say* she's just visiting friends, but what if she's actually here to issue a challenge?"

"...If that's truly the case, we Jiangning scholars must unite. We can't let her get her way."

"Perhaps you're all overthinking this..."

That group's conversation used tones suggesting they were discussing highly confidential matters, but in reality their voices weren't lowered at all. Hearing names like Li Shishi and Gu Yanzhen, Ning Yi also became somewhat interested. Seeing his expression, Xiao Chan also listened for a while: "Young Master, Young Master—if you wanted to meet that Li Shishi, you'd be able to, right?"

Ning Yi blinked: "I don't even know her. Uh... you already heard about her coming?"

"Mm, Xiao Chan heard about it several days ago. They say this Lady Li Shishi⁴ is coming to visit friends and relax, and it was supposed to be a secret trip, but somehow word got out and it spread like wildfire. Even Young Miss knows about it. Yesterday when chatting with the Flourishing Fortune House manager's wife, Young Miss mentioned it. Young Miss said: 'If my husband wanted to meet her, he could probably manage it. But you know, my husband's temperament is different from others—he has little interest in such things as getting close to courtesans just to gain fame. He often says these romantic talents are just hyped up. Oh, "hyped up" means the same thing as meaningless hype.'"

She imitated Su Tan'er's manner of speaking, but her tone overflowed with pride—our Young Master isn't unable to go, he just doesn't care about such things. Ning Yi laughed. Xiao Chan thought for a moment, then added: "Actually, if Young Master wanted to go, Xiao Chan thinks it would be good for you to participate in these things sometimes. Young Miss has said the same thing too."

Ning Yi smiled: "Isn't that saying one thing but meaning another? If I actually agreed, I'd definitely suffer for it."

"No, really." Xiao Chan shook her head vigorously. "Young Master never pays attention to those women anyway. After Young Master became famous, both Young Miss and Xiao Chan were very happy. Although Young Master says these gatherings are just meaningless hype where everyone flatters each other, when people get together to flatter each other they do feel happy. If Young Master went and participated, wrote a poem or two to shock everyone, then felt pleased about it for a while—that would be quite fun. Actually, Xiao Chan is like this too. If someone praises Xiao Chan for being impressive, Xiao Chan would also be very happy."

Xiao Chan's words weren't false pretenses. Although as a woman of the household she certainly wouldn't be delighted to see her man flirting around outside, this was the reality of the era—it was also a matter of degree. Ordinarily when these talented scholars and beautiful women gathered together, it was also considered a type of socially encouraged entertainment activity. As Xiao Chan said, everyone flattering each other for a while would make the parties involved feel happy.

If he participated in such things too much and genuinely became infatuated with those famous courtesans, Su Tan'er, Xiao Chan, and the others would naturally be displeased. But if Ning Yi didn't participate at all, by this point people like Su Tan'er would instead feel as if she'd somehow interfered with Ning Yi's path to happiness. She might even feel that having Ning Yi occasionally participate once or twice, greatly displaying his poetic talents to make crowds look on in admiration and courtesans fall for him, was actually the truly healthy lifestyle. In short, they understood that Ning Yi had nothing going on with those courtesans—if Ning Yi gained fame from it, the family was actually quite proud of it too.

Ning Yi pondered this for a while in his mind, then laughed for quite some time before saying: "Xiao Chan is really impressive." He reached out to pat her head. Xiao Chan giggled in delight.

"So does Young Master really not want to meet that Lady Li Shishi?"

"Your Young Master's charm is too great. What if after meeting me she falls in love and cries and refuses to leave Jiangning..."

"Then..." Xiao Chan's head dropped, her face flushing completely red as she stammered in a voice as tiny as a mosquito's, "Th-then... she'll have to wait until after Young Master has taken Chan'er as a concubine before she can come through the door..."

Where most people would have laughed and mocked his boast, Xiao Chan's earnest reply was so far outside his expectations that Ning Yi simply froze. After a moment, he could only shake his head with a smile, though in his heart he couldn't help wondering how this Li Shishi compared to Yunzhu and Jin'er.

This comparison wasn't actually because of her status as a courtesan. It was really because Li Shishi's reputation was so great—presumably her foundation in song, dance, poetry, and music ran deep. Originally Ning Yi hadn't been particularly interested in ancient song, dance, and musical arts, but after hearing Yunzhu's singing, his opinion had completely changed. He understood that truly skilled people were ultimately skilled. Of course, this was also because after hearing the musical styles he'd taught her, Yunzhu had changed her own singing performances, catering to his tastes.

Yuan Jin'er was unwilling to perform dances in front of him. After all, she'd retired from that life—she felt there weren't many audiences worth performing for, and such things would smack of trying to please this stinking man. But just from the few dances he'd seen, they were ultimately pleasing to the eye. Supposedly when Ning Yi wasn't around, she would bounce around dancing in front of Yunzhu for her own amusement. Being in a cheerful mood with inspiration bursting forth, she'd constantly create new dances to show Yunzhu. Sometimes after Jin'er left, Yunzhu would mention it with a laugh and imitate them a bit.

The two had comparable skills. Yunzhu was extremely skilled with instruments and singing, but in dance she wasn't much inferior to Yuan Jin'er. However, when she danced there was no one to play music, so she could only let Ning Yi appreciate the graceful rhythms of her body. If she performed a bit more, often Yuan Jin'er would have already run back and loudly interrupted, protesting that Yunzhu was using her dances to please her lover—a shameful act of valuing romance over friendship. Actually, Yunzhu was gentle and refined while Jin'er was lively and energetic, so even performing the same dance, there were some differences in their presentations.

The scholars at this table were still discussing matters related to that Li Shishi. Over on the other side, three more men came up from the stairway. Under the server's guidance, they sat down nearby. All three wore short traveling clothes and carried weapons. One even had injuries on his body—clearly they were all jianghu people. After sitting down, they ordered food.

"Damn it! Yesterday dozens of people set up an ambush but still let those Liao dogs escape. What terrible luck!"

"They can't get away. Old Master Cheng from the Hundred Blades Alliance has personally led people to pursue them and set up an inescapable net. The authorities are also cooperating. These Liao dogs entered deep into our heartland to kill and assassinate people—if they can still escape with their lives intact, that would truly mean there's no one of worth in our Central Plains."

"Though that lead Liao man was truly formidable—to actually kill his way out from the encirclement of so many experts..."

Hearing this conversation, Ning Yi frowned and became concerned...

Yesterday an elderly person in this area passed away. They set up a mourning tent, and all afternoon they've been singing songs like "Love Trade"—"Betraying my love, watching me leave"... Oh, right now they're singing "The First Snow of 2002"...

FOOTNOTES:

¹ During the Song Dynasty, visiting courtesans (狎妓) was a socially acceptable practice for scholars and officials. Courtesan houses functioned as cultural salons where literati gathered for artistic performances, poetry composition, and social networking. This practice emphasized artistic and intellectual companionship rather than simple prostitution, and was considered a legitimate form of entertainment promoted by society.

² "Orchid in an empty valley" (空谷幽兰) is a classical Chinese metaphor for rare, refined beauty untainted by worldly concerns—a beautiful and virtuous person living in seclusion or obscurity, maintaining purity despite lack of recognition.

³ "Retiring from courtesan life" (从良, literally "following propriety") refers to a courtesan leaving the profession, typically through marriage or retirement. This was considered an honorable path representing a return to conventional social respectability.

⁴ Li Shishi (李师师) was the most famous courtesan in Chinese history, active during the Northern Song Dynasty in the Eastern Capital (Kaifeng). She was renowned for her exceptional artistic accomplishments in poetry, music, and qin performance, and is legendary for her relationship with Emperor Huizong of Song. Her name became synonymous with the peak of courtesan culture, representing artistic refinement and cultural sophistication rather than mere entertainment. Her visit to any city was considered a significant cultural event.
