Temperatures gradually dropped after the Mid-Autumn Festival, and when the weather cleared after several days of rain, a refreshing breeze seemed to breathe life into the city that had been locked down for nearly a month. During the day the sky was bright and clear, and at night the starlight shone pure and bright with cotton-like clouds floating across the heavens one after another.

Over this past month, the situation of refugees inside and outside the city had been pushed to its limits. Of course, they said there had been even more difficult years than this one—the string was already pulled taut, but exactly where the breaking point lay was still hard to say. The government occasionally distributed grain while some wealthy households helped by providing porridge and meals, taking care of both those inside and outside the city. During these charity distributions, soldiers helped maintain order, preventing any major chaos.

However, gangs and factions had formed among the disaster victims, and fights over food were common. After the government and wealthy households finished distributing grain and porridge, such troubles would often emerge and were difficult to manage. Since the city closure, some people had died—though few actually starved to death. Most died from fights and robberies or gradually succumbed after having no money for medical treatment. But overall, they said it was still better than previous years.

Living in this era, everyone had heard countless times about how things were in past years. Most people felt compassion, but the current situation was already considered good and life had to go on. Business negotiations continued and social gatherings were still attended—it was just that the atmosphere of the entire city had become somewhat quieter. During the recent autumn rains, the feeling of silent stagnation had grown even heavier.

After the Mid-Autumn Festival, daily life returned to its original track as people waited day by day for the flood's impact to pass while the city's various merchants and shops continued operating in this atmosphere. This morning a refreshing breeze blew through, and around eight or nine o'clock, in a small room beside a Su family cloth warehouse in Jiangning, several people were busy with something. This warehouse was connected to the adjacent shop, though business wasn't particularly good given the current situation. A maid named Juan'er occasionally ran in to take a look.

Working in the room were Ning Yi and the Zhou siblings—Zhou Pei and Zhou Junwu—dressed as shop assistants in blue clothes and small caps. Despite their attire, their fair skin immediately revealed they came from some background. These two had been inexplicably following Ning Yi for a month now, and some Su family members had grown accustomed to their presence, assuming they were either children from the main family or Ning Yi's disciples being shown around. Sometimes Ning Yi had them serve tea and pour water, sometimes he even had them help move goods—though nothing heavy, of course.

For the siblings, this kind of life was quite novel. Two days ago Ning Yi had even given them their first month's wages—one tael and two qian of silver¹ each, the standard rate for child workers. They'd compared it to outside prices afterward, and while one tael and two qian wasn't much use to the siblings, it was still quite interesting.

Of course, using them as child labor was just occasional amusement. Most of the time Ning Yi fulfilled his responsibilities as a teacher, giving them lessons when free and teaching Zhou Pei modern arithmetic, casually connecting addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division with traditional calculation methods. Initially Zhou Pei had been dismissive of those Arabic numeral symbols, but now she frequently asked questions about them.

The reason the three were tinkering with today's project was that a few days ago, when they'd gone to the laboratory, the two had followed along, so he'd given them a tour and roughly explained the concept of physics. Ning Yi had mainly found some glass pieces that could work as convex lenses, planning to make a telescope for fun. At the time he'd shown off the principles of focusing and magnification to them on a whim. Zhou Pei had been rather unimpressed, saying it was simple and everyone knew about it. Since the telescope was still being made, Ning Yi decided to do something very simple that not everyone might know about.

After having someone help construct a wooden box earlier, he now set up a black cloth tent for shade while the three of them hid inside and lit a candle. Ning Yi then covered one side of the candle, and since the box only covered half, light still escaped. He placed a piece of thick rice paper with a hole cut in it, stretched on a bamboo frame, creating a simple pinhole imaging² demonstration.

As Juan'er stood at the door watching the black cloth tent with some confusion, she heard voices from inside after a moment: "Look, the light here is upside down."

"Uh..."

"Ah, Teacher, how can it be like this!"

"It must be a magic trick."

"Even magic tricks have principles."

There was a period of chattering inside before Juan'er approached and Ning Yi emerged from the black cloth, smiling at her: "Go in and take a look. It's not super interesting, but most people probably haven't seen it..."

Juan'er went in doubtfully, then saw the upside-down candle flame displayed on one side of the wooden box.

Over the past month, Ning Yi had been working like a laborer—holding morning meetings daily, walking a fixed route, then improvising freely. He appeared diligent but accomplished little. Most of the time Chan'er accompanied him, sometimes Juan'er, and the several maids had already gotten to know the Zhou siblings, with the polite Junwu often calling them Sister Chan'er and Sister Juan'er. Zhou Pei was more reserved, but she too had developed a familiar attitude toward them and Ning Yi.

"Some people would call this strange and frivolous techniques³, so for now don't take it too seriously. But some things are quite interesting. For instance, these two glass pieces—when placed at this distance apart, they can magnify things... Um, I've already had Carpenter Chen help hollow out a good tube, then we'll figure out how to fix them in place..."

Ning Yi habitually liked teaching through casual conversation. This morning the long bamboo tube sat on the desk by the window as Little Pei, Junwu, and Juan'er who'd come in took turns peering inside, then stared wide-eyed. The lenses weren't fixed yet—Ning Yi had only found the approximate focal length and wrapped the lenses in circles of rice paper to temporarily place them in the bamboo tube. Without being secured, the lenses could easily fall over, so this small telescope couldn't be moved yet, but at least from the effect, it was already quite astonishing.

"Light passing through a small hole to form an inverted image actually demonstrates that light travels in straight lines. But in some situations, like putting a chopstick in water, it bends—here, light curves. If you say seeing an inverted image lets you do something, that's difficult, because this telescope is the product of many different things and principles combined. Once people can research to this degree and understand everything clearly, then you don't need to slowly try your luck like me—you'd directly know what kind of lenses you need for a telescope, what the concave and convex surfaces should look like... When you know more principles, you'll also know how to precisely create those surfaces and control them accurately."

"But you don't need to consider how to make these things. What I want you to know is... a way of thinking about things, the combination of cause and effect. Don't know the how without knowing the why. Many craftsmen have used old methods for years without knowing why those methods produce certain effects. If you understand the principles, you can create clearer glass, telescopes that see farther and more clearly. Efficiency will increase tenfold, a hundredfold. It's the same with anything..."

"Zhou Pei, the calculations you like work this way too—they're clearer. From one plus one equals two, two plus two equals four, it can extend endlessly. We live in a human world, but that's a world of numbers. Actually, calculating how light refracts, how it magnifies, how much it magnifies—all require numbers to assist. The world of numbers is pure cause and effect, clear logical relationships... I don't want you to become craftsmen or anything, but I hope you can understand these logical relationships. They should be very useful."

"Of course, in calculations there are also some rather extreme examples that are interesting to think about. For instance..."

After finishing the experiment, he roughly extended into various random topics. Junwu kept looking back at the pinhole imaging device while occasionally fiddling with the telescope. Zhou Pei also found the earlier experiment amazing but now listened more seriously to the conversation. Juan'er listened for a while then went out to check the shop situation, sighing to herself where no one could hear: "Young Master is so amazing..."

After a while longer, Xi Junyu passed by and came in to chat with Ning Yi for a bit. Junwu and Little Pei went over to pour tea and arrange seats—this had already become an understanding between the two children and Ning Yi. In fact, besides attending lessons, they had also been wondering this past month why Ning Yi seemed to be doing nothing.

Xi Junyu was just passing by today because the cloth guild meeting was only three days away, when each household would have to officially show their cards, so he came to see Ning Yi's current situation. Actually all the managers were now busy running around, and Xi Junyu had just met with a shop owner this morning and was preparing to attend another social gathering.

"Although after this month it looks like our Su family has the highest support, the business world is full of deception and we must guard against various situations. Though we now have Lord Han's support and Lord Dong also favors our Su family, it's hard to say if some change might occur. The Xue and Wu families also have quite thick networks in official circles—hard to say if they might flip things at the last moment. If there's any backup plan, it should be arranged as early as possible."

Xi Junyu belonged to the younger generation in the Su family, ambitious and progressive but also clear-headed. After he finished speaking, Ning Yi nodded: "As for official matters, Old Master has already done his best. Manager Xi, I don't really understand the ins and outs—have there been similar situations before?"

"In the cloth guild over the years, there haven't been competitions this fierce before. Of course, nobody can say for sure about schemes hidden beneath the surface... Heh, perhaps I'm overthinking it. The Su, Xue, and Wu families all have foundations. Since things have reached this point, there probably won't be too many more changes. If they could still flip everything at this point, covering the sky with one hand, then they probably would have swallowed our Su family long ago."

"Probably impossible now." Ning Yi smiled. "Opening the door to do business, after so many years—when the time comes and we lay out our goods, even if they have some private moves, they can't just lie with their eyes open and say our things aren't good. If we were small merchants that would be one thing... Heh, actually this time we were forced into it too. One assassination, one frame-up, then seizing the Imperial Merchant status, and we still don't know who it was. If not for this, this month probably wouldn't have needed to be so high-profile. Anyway, we're burning our boats—if we can get through, there's a future; if not, there's nothing to discuss. The one hundred and fifty thousand taels we painstakingly saved before are now all being thrown in at once to improve the looms, all betting on the Imperial Merchant status. There really is no retreat..."

Xi Junyu nodded and sighed, then raised his head with a smile: "Only three days left—there shouldn't be any problems. Don't worry. Also, please tell Second Miss that no matter what, we've already done our best with this matter."

"Do your best and leave the rest to fate." Ning Yi nodded. "Manager Xi has also worked hard recently. Thank you."

"It's my duty." After exchanging a few more pleasantries, Xi Junyu invited him to attend the social gathering together to meet an official from the Imperial Textile Bureau⁴. Ning Yi shook his head and declined—there wasn't much point in him going. The moment Xi Junyu left, Zhou Pei and Zhou Junwu frowned and spoke up.

"Why didn't you go?"

"Maybe you could have convinced that person..."

Ning Yi tidied up while smiling: "I have so many things to do—why should I waste energy on that?"

"But you have nothing to do at all." Zhou Pei pouted.

"Who says I have nothing to do? In a bit I need to go eat lunch, then stroll around the streets this afternoon, stop by Carpenter Chen's to get the telescope casing, and figure out how to fix it properly. Uh, I'm also planning to paint a layer of lacquer on the outside, and go to East Market to see if there are any new serialized novels for sale. Which of these isn't more important than social gatherings..."

"But if the social gatherings don't go well, Teacher's family will have problems."

"But they are handling the social gatherings well, aren't they? My going wouldn't help much. The preparations for getting Imperial Merchant status are already complete—you two know this too. We don't need any tricks. We just build relationships and ask everyone to speak according to their conscience. We don't need those lords from the Textile Bureau to show us special favor. We've sent money and various gifts, and we're not making things difficult for them—as long as they don't speak against their conscience, we're confident we'll get it."

"Wouldn't it be better if they spoke against their conscience in favor of the Su family? That would be even more certain."

"Well, that wouldn't be bad either..."

"Anyway, I think Teacher isn't trying hard enough..."

Junwu was somewhat unhappy, but Ning Yi just smiled: "Relax, relax. I've already done everything that needs doing. It was originally a small matter—I don't know why you're so anxious. It's getting late, let's go. I'll take you for lunch..."

As he prepared to leave, Zhou Pei suddenly came over and blocked his way, smiling: "Um, wait—there are only three days left. Can we see that cloth?"

Ning Yi thought for a moment, then tilted his head. "Heh, alright." Taking out a key, he opened the nearby cabinet, removed a brocade box, and opened it for the two children to see. Little Pei and Junwu gathered around to touch it.

"Wow, it really is more beautiful than what we've seen at home..."

"I've never really seen cloth in this color before."

"Trade secret." Ning Yi smiled, then laid down the ground rules: "But let me be clear first—you two aren't allowed to go home and talk about this randomly, aren't allowed to help by finding people, aren't allowed to try hinting anything to those lords from the Textile Bureau... Of course you don't have that kind of influence now anyway, but I want fairness."

"Show-off, we wouldn't help anyway." Zhou Pei smiled and rolled her eyes.

Little Junwu nodded beside her: "If you don't get the Imperial Merchant position, it must be because that Dong Decheng took money—took lots of money."

"Haha, let's go eat... Juan'er, let's go together!"

At noon the group of four left the cloth shop, and royal guards disguised as cloth shop workers or passersby followed from all around. Sunlight poured down as chattering voices spread through the air.

"Is this what they call 'the skilled warrior gains no great fame'⁵... But Teacher, you really haven't done anything... Right, Sister Juan'er?"

"Um... Young Master has done many things..."

"Of course you'd speak up for your own young master, but my sister and I haven't seen anything... Though it's true, there wasn't much that needed doing anyway. We originally thought it was a big crisis, but step by step by step it's reached this point. This is called an open strategy, right Sister?"

"I don't know..."

"Why not?"

"Those people only did one thing, then there were no more schemes. Isn't that strange?"

"Who knows what they're thinking. Teacher's approach of not playing any tricks at all is also quite strange."

"Let's eat, let's eat—eating is important. Juan'er, which restaurant has the best food? Little Pei and Junwu have money now, they can treat us!"

"Young Master is teasing again. How could those wages be enough..."

"Fine, fine, I'll treat—but really, one tael and two qian of silver, what would you use it for?"

"Save it up..."

"For a dowry?"

"Ah? How could... how could it be a dowry? Of course for spending later!"

"As a dowry isn't bad either. You could open a steamed bun shop, live frugally for decades and save quite a bit. When Juan'er gets old, you can run your steamed bun shop, watch the kids running around. Or a tavern would work too—eating the leftovers would save money. Oh, actually a cloth shop wouldn't be bad either, though apparently the initial investment is quite large..."

"A steamed bun shop would be so tiring..."

"Right, opening a brothel would be good—it's recession-proof. In good times officials and merchants go for entertainment, in bad times they sell their sons and daughters..."

"How awful! Sister Juan'er, don't listen to Teacher's nonsense..."

Voices rose and fell along the long street. Going against the flow of people, the four walked toward the city center. Painted boats floated on the distant Qinhuai River, and up ahead the restaurants grew in number—the atmosphere of the world spread among them...

As the group gradually went far, the flow of people continued surging through the streets. The hidden guards who'd been protecting them returned to their original positions. A full shi chen⁶ later, at a restaurant called "Old Rain Tower" that was said to have changed hands to the Pu family, a meal for two was underway in an elegant private room. One of the diners was the Wu family's second young master Wu Qilong, talking and laughing as he carelessly ate his way through fresh beef, tender lamb, and other delicacies. Across from him sat Xi Junyu, the same man who had been at the Su cloth shop at noon.

As Wu Qilong ate his meal, cold dishes and hot dishes kept arriving as he complained with a smile: "You arrived too late—I've already eaten quite a bit, and this food just doesn't taste as good anymore. I was just thinking earlier whether I'd dismissed everyone too soon. If I'd kept a beauty to keep me company, perhaps this meal would taste better. Haha, but... Brother Xi always brings me good news."

Xi Junyu also smiled as he raised his cup: "As Second Young Master requested, everything has been prepared. Here's to Second Young Master."

"Yes, after this is done, Brother Xi can come work at our Wu family." Wu Qilong smiled as he clinked cups. "But I'm curious—that Ning Yi, he really hasn't made any preparations this month?"

After finishing their drinks, Xi Junyu said: "He's just a scholar who entered through marriage. Second Young Master has seen his antics this month. It's laughable—he's been of no help to the Su family at all. All he knows is buying people's hearts, going through the motions daily, showing he's doing something. He's nothing but a country bumpkin who's read a few books. How would he understand the ins and outs? Just as Second Young Master anticipated, all the Su family's preparations are on the surface. Now things are about to conclude, and he has no clue. Still thinks that in three days..."

"In three days it will all be over. Nobody will see it coming." Wu Qilong raised his chopsticks with a smile. "It's a pity about that fabric. If the Xue family wasn't so insistent and we could wait a bit longer, find the source... but there's no helping it. However, the Su family has been in the textile business for many years after all—they must have more than one trick. We'll see if Su Tan'er can recover afterward. Brother Xi hasn't completely burned his bridges, so if the Su family rises again later, you can return. They'll probably treat you even better."

"I'm overwhelmed by Second Young Master's consideration."

"Well then, this meal can't continue—I have other matters to attend to. Come, Brother Xi, I'll toast you once more."

They raised their cups and drained them, speaking and laughing in the tranquil private room. Through the window, the city's scenery was elegant and peaceful—another ordinary day in this grand city...

FOOTNOTES:
¹ *Approximately a month's wages for a child worker in this era*
² *Camera obscura principle - Light passing through a small hole projects an inverted image, documented in ancient Chinese texts centuries before Western discovery*
³ *Strange tricks and excessive techniques - A traditional Confucian dismissal of technology and innovation as distracting from moral cultivation*
⁴ *The Imperial Textile Bureau - A government department overseeing textile production and quality control, with power to grant official merchant licenses*
⁵ *"The skilled warrior gains no great fame" - A principle from Sun Tzu's Art of War emphasizing subtle victories over dramatic ones*
⁶ *One shi chen equals two modern hours*
