Ning Yi ran into Yunzhu and Jin'er that afternoon while wandering around. With the New Year festivities in recent days, construction on Zhu's branch store had paused for a bit, but with the Lantern Festival approaching, work had to resume, and since Ning Yi had been pretty busy for a while, when he had some free time he got dragged along to take a look. Yunzhu had some purchasing matters to handle along the way and needed to give someone instructions, so Jin'er came over to the branch store with him first.

Actually, by this point in the renovation, the store's style had basically taken shape and there weren't many things left that needed Ning Yi's decisions—as for whether to call the shop "Second Store" or "Jin'er Store," Ning Yi really didn't mind either way. The restaurant was built along the river, and many of the windows hadn't been installed yet, so on the side facing the wind, some snow had even drifted in and piled up. Fortunately, both of them were dressed warmly. Ning Yi's second-rate martial arts skills had achieved some success—at least he felt that way—while Yuan Jin'er had always been lively. Not long ago she'd bragged that she could bathe in the river even in heavy snow, so she didn't feel cold at all. The two chatted idly.

"After we get this place finished, I've decided to personally perform on stage for three days to build up some buzz."

"You haven't performed in ages, you're probably rusty."

"Screw you. I only perform for Sister Yunzhu... uh, what do you think?"

"I've never even seen you perform, so it's definitely terrible."

"I meant me performing on stage."

"...You know the answer to that yourself. Stop making trouble."

Although they seemed incompatible personality-wise, Jin'er actually admired Ning Yi quite a bit when it came to major decisions. She naturally had high hopes for this store bearing her own name. She'd mentioned wanting to perform on stage to attract customers, but when Ning Yi said this, she just pouted and dropped the subject.

"Then I'll just have to get some of my old sisters. It'll cost a lot though." Jin'er dragged a stool to find a spot in the hall sheltered from the wind and sat down—she had good connections and had played a big role in finding relationships for Zhu's, but her concept of money wasn't very concrete. Sometimes when Yunzhu did the accounts, she'd watch from the side and get angry about every expense, incredibly stingy.

"It'll build reputation, and they won't have to accompany stinking men like me. Win-win." Ning Yi put several stools on a round table to clear out some space, smiling as he spoke. "And besides, if Zhu's really gets big in the future, we can train our own batch of performers."

"Train..." Jin'er blinked. "You want to open a brothel?" she asked quietly.

"Your thoughts are so perverted!" Ning Yi glared at her. "In the future, when Zhu's scale gets really big, we can train some girls ourselves—even boys would work—for all kinds of things that can be taught and learned: form a troupe covering everything from opera and singing to magic tricks and acrobatics. There are plenty of kids outside who can't get a meal anyway, so it'd be doing some good, solving the surplus labor problem."

Listening to Ning Yi's plans and prospects, Jin'er stared blankly for a long moment. "That... would cost a lot though. If you don't open a brothel and just do performances, those traveling troupes¹ can't earn much money at all. And... how big would it have to get..." She had no way to imagine these things—if they just traveled around performing, wouldn't that be no different from a traveling theater troupe, and who would be willing to spend that much money on something like that?

"After we open three or more branch stores, the chicken-and-egg cycle will speed up, and at that point we'll create a system and let it gradually multiply on its own." Ning Yi wrote some things about the store's interior layout on paper as he spoke. "The important thing is leveraging government connections—Yunzhu's pretty familiar with Old Qin's family now, and she knows Kang Xian—the Prince Consort—too. That old man owes me quite a bit lately, so this way we can at least guarantee the whole process runs smoothly, follow proper procedures without too much government interference, and if we need to pull strings, we'll have the connections..."

He paused. "The key is to go upscale and expand southward—head to places like Suzhou and Hangzhou and gradually build up the complementary entertainment. As long as management and marketing are done right, there'll always be business—this Wu Dynasty is poor in everything but money anyway. Of course, it also depends on whether you all want to make it that big. We can stop anytime if you don't."

In business matters, Ning Yi had sufficient planning ability, but what's more, doing business these days didn't rely so much on planning as on backing—having Zhu's business operate under the Prince Kang mansion's influence meant they didn't need to be too polite about it, no big problem. He hadn't really talked much about these things with Yunzhu and the others before, so now that Jin'er heard it, she looked completely astonished, troubled as she imagined herself maybe becoming a tycoon in the future, then thinking this guy was talking way too big—she didn't believe him at all.

While they were talking, Yunzhu came in from outside the restaurant, closing the door while still looking toward the side road. Unlike Jin'er, who sometimes dressed like a man, Yunzhu usually wore simple women's dress, at most in plain colors that looked a bit dowdy from a distance, though up close her beautiful features couldn't be hidden. Seeing her come over, Jin'er laughed and rushed over, then looked around too. "What are you looking at, Sister Yunzhu?"

"Uh, I think I just saw... Miss Qilan pass by here. Maybe I was mistaken."

"Qilan?" Jin'er pushed open the door and looked several times. "Probably a coincidence. We weren't that close to her anyway. There was that grudge from the courtesan competition back then, so she definitely has nothing to do with us."

"When did you get a grudge with Qilan..."

"She won top courtesan, and that guy surnamed Ning bought her two thousand flowers to support her, making me lose face. Isn't that grudge big enough?"

Yuan Jin'er originally hadn't planned to compete for top courtesan back then, but she still brought this up whenever the mood struck, using it to accuse Ning Yi of being shameless. Yunzhu listened and laughed with a soft sound. Ning Yi helplessly slapped his forehead. He wasn't far from the pile of snow by the window on one side of the hall. He quietly walked over and grabbed a snowball. Jin'er's expression froze and she tried to run. Yunzhu laughed. "Alright, hit her."

Ning Yi had no mercy for someone like Yuan Jin'er who was always provoking him—his hand swept out and the snowball whistled through the air as Jin'er hugged her head and let out a low "Ah," thinking that after taking this hit she'd declare her revenge. The next moment, snow exploded everywhere—but not on Jin'er.

Yunzhu shrank her neck back, not having reacted at all as the snowball burst apart on her head, while Ning Yi maintained his throwing posture, frozen for a moment too. Jin'er held back her laughter, and moments later, her whole face had puffed up.

"Some martial arts master... can't even hit snowballs..." Yunzhu patted the snow off her head, shoulders dropping, her eyes slightly reproachful. Then she pursed her lips and started walking outside. Jin'er followed out with a grin, and the two women began forming snowballs under the eaves.

"Hey, it's like flooding the Dragon King's temple²—this is a misunderstanding... Yunzhu, you're more sensible than Jin'er. You can't do this..."

*****************

As it turned out, even sensible people wouldn't willingly take a beating for nothing—not long after, when the three of them came out of the hall again, Ning Yi was brushing snow off his body with a somewhat helpless expression.

"Hidden weapons need practice too, you know..."

"This proves your hidden weapon skills aren't as good as ours." Yuan Jin'er was fixing her hair, looking like she'd just been thoroughly roughed up, then turned back to look at the store still awaiting renovation. "It can open in the second month, right?"

"Mm." Ning Yi nodded. "Second Store."

"Jin'er Store!"

"Fine, whatever you say..."

It was getting late, and they'd basically said everything that needed saying about the store's renovation details—though this hadn't really been the main point anyway. The three of them parted ways on the street, with Yunzhu and Jin'er taking a carriage back while Ning Yi headed home from another direction.

The weather remained cold with thick snow accumulated throughout the city, and as he walked home, looking at the shops and teahouses along the roadside with their doors open and the expressions on pedestrians' faces, it seemed to foreshadow another peaceful year. Ning Yi thought about Zhu's development—these things might sound like business, but for him, they were more like playing house.

Rationally speaking, he preferred developing southward—after all, the Wu Dynasty was weak, and whether it was the Liao or the Jin, whichever direction the situation developed, they might eventually attack from the north, making the south far more peaceful. But thinking this way reminded him of what he'd told Lu Hongti about doing business all the way to Lüliang Mountain, which would be really hard to pull off, especially since that area was both impoverished and currently plagued by Tian Hu's rebellion. If he really wanted to do business there in the future, he'd probably have to use a different model.

Ning Yi did plan to expand the business, though not for something as boring as building a national chain—the main reason was actually to prepare for future weapons inventions he'd definitely work on eventually. If he could make them but didn't want to hand them directly over to Kang Xian, the reasons were complicated.

First, half-baked firearms didn't mean much—if they were really going to play some role in turning the tide, Ning Yi would need to get involved in many areas, which would definitely require him to come out and do things. The imperial court was full of scheming and infighting with an emperor on top, and while Ning Yi was used to being in charge and could handle the scheming, it would definitely be annoying—he wouldn't like this mode where people were always pointing fingers at him. Second, he had no sense of identification with this imperial court, though he did identify more with Lu Hongti—he appreciated this strong and resilient woman, and if there was a chance, he might as well help her out.

Of course, when a thought expanded to the scope of "nation," specific considerations always seemed extremely insubstantial—right now with just two stores, thinking about a national chain did seem somewhat grandiose. Ning Yi's current range of activity was just within Jiangning city, where he'd recently been accompanying Tan'er running around paying New Year visits, and what he usually encountered were just household matters or business affairs.

This also wasn't an era of information explosion where any random person could talk about politics and patriotism—looking back at history, you might see how many patriots there were and how tragic and moving their stories were, but for current society, the wars up north were probably an extremely vague concept. In business circles they might interact with Liao merchants, but what the Jin were really like, even those scholars spouting off in brothels and pleasure boats didn't actually know.

Ning Yi only understood bits and pieces of intelligence from occasional conversations between Old Qin and Old Kang—what he encountered more were just the leisurely days in Jiangning city: the quiet bamboo groves near the academy, a group of kids swaying their heads while reading, his wife's smile or playfulness when keeping accounts and chatting at home. These things, after all, were what felt most real.

But some feelings were actually gradually expanding. During this New Year period, the officials visiting Old Qin had made the old man's status more complex and multifaceted—one thing was probably certain: in the future, Old Qin should have no way to go back to setting up a Go stand by the Qinhuai River. Ning Yi wasn't clear on exactly what Old Qin had done and could only roughly sketch an outline based on what others said, since the old man was extremely steady in these matters and never talked about them in everyday conversation. But since New Year, Ning Yi could very clearly sense something tightening—both Old Qin and Kang Xian, everyone was waiting for certain things to happen up north.

But what they were waiting for hadn't come yet.

That spring, the Jin and Liao nations concluded a ceasefire agreement³. The war that had seemed imminent suddenly became distant and uncertain once again...

(End of Chapter)

FOOTNOTES:
¹ *Traveling performance troupes had low social status in traditional China and operated on thin profit margins, moving between towns for small audiences.*

² *"Flooding the Dragon King's temple" (大水冲了龙王庙) - Chinese idiom meaning members of the same family/group unknowingly fighting each other. The Dragon King controls water, so flooding his own temple represents allies attacking allies by mistake.*

³ *Historical event: In spring 1120, the Jin (Jurchen) and Liao (Khitan) kingdoms signed a temporary peace treaty, unexpectedly postponing the warfare that Song Dynasty officials were anticipating.*
