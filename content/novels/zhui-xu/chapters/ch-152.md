After the clan assembly dispersed, chaos scattered in all directions like moviegoers leaving a theater—except no one could simply munch on snacks while casually discussing what had just happened before heading home. Everyone had far too much to deal with in the aftermath.

What people feared most were unexpected developments they had no way to prepare for. There had been extensive predictions and arrangements beforehand, but when the cards were finally revealed, the entire situation had developed in complete opposition to their preparations—a rare occurrence even in past business conflicts. When the sense of loss from several wasted months combined with the bewilderment of watching carefully nurtured expectations collapse into nothing, the resulting mental exhaustion created an enormous burden that made everything feel futile, yet many things still had to be done regardless.

The Second and Third Branches needed to find ways to suppress the centrifugal force and negative effects of this outcome, while those who had previously mocked the First Branch would now have to consider how to repair relations with them. Old Master Su had to busy himself soothing his sons Su Zhongkan and Su Yunfang, harmonizing the thoughts of his old brothers, and ensuring the matter passed as smoothly as possible.

As for the First Branch, they couldn't assume things were settled either. Su Tan'er needed to seize this opportunity and act decisively to completely stabilize the First Branch's position that had begun wavering over the past two months—stabilizing, reassuring, and winning people over to maximize her own interests.

Additionally, those who had been persuaded by Su Zhongkan and Su Yunfang to jump out during the clan assembly needed proper handling—after appropriate discipline and punishment, they still had to be put at ease. As Ning Yi had said, these people weren't necessarily disloyal; they simply lacked confidence in Su Tan'er. These people had ability, so the discipline shouldn't be excessive. After this incident, they might actually become more steadfast than others when similar situations arose in the future.

Beyond those within the family, news of the clan assembly's outcome had already begun spreading from the Su household in fragments, rippling outward toward the various large and small commercial forces throughout Jiangning that were concerned about the Su family's fate, stirring up wave after wave of shock. Xue Yan and his companions' dumbfounded reactions at Moon Fragrance Tower might not have been unique. At Changyun Pavilion, when Puyang Yi received the message from inside the Su household during a heated argument, he stood frozen in bewilderment for a long moment, never imagining that this information he'd only casually inquired about could deliver such an enormous shock. Afterward, he recounted the series of events that had unfolded among the cloth merchants over these past months to the others who were still debating.

The Puyang family was Jiangning's wealthiest, with far deeper resources than either the Su or Wu families. Puyang Yi excelled at commerce, and his friendly overtures toward Ning Yi on various occasions had been due to admiring his talent and learning. These past months of intrigue and maneuvering in Jiangning's cloth industry had merely been a spectator sport for him, watching various players perform. He hadn't held much expectation for Ning Yi's involvement from the start, and subsequent developments had unfolded exactly as he'd predicted. Still, seeing such a talented scholar venture into commerce only to fail left him with complicated feelings—there was both sighing and a certain satisfaction, a sentiment roughly equivalent to: you're excellent at poetry and composition, which I admire, but in this arena, I'm far superior, and you shouldn't have gotten involved.

Tonight's predictions about the Su family's situation weren't any different for Puyang Yi than for everyone else. Of course, this wasn't particularly important to him and hadn't occupied much of his attention. But when the news arrived, he was genuinely startled. Nearly everyone in Jiangning who had been following this matter had been confident for months, yet all of it had fallen completely into empty space—the feeling was truly indescribable.

Everyone had assumed he was an insignificant participant in the affair, perhaps not even a participant at all, but now they discovered this person had been at the center of events all along. Over these months, he had pulled the strings that moved the entire situation forward like a puppet master, yet no one had noticed...

Those present had little emotional investment in the Su family's affairs—more than half didn't even know about it—but when Chen Lu wrote out "Settling Wind Waves," and Liu Qingdi's face flushed red and white in turns, they laughingly mocked it as merely self-consolation. Liu Qingdi recounted the Su family situation at length, and then both sides launched into another round of argument, until Puyang Yi emerged and said: "The Su family just produced a result..."

Then everyone was truly shocked.

What had initially seemed like an unintentional series of actions transformed into meticulous calculations executed step by step. Months of what everyone had witnessed as forbearance and frustration suddenly took on another layer of meaning—calm and composed, magnanimous and broad-minded, an enormous game, the most spectacular reversal, like something straight out of a historical romance. After the group of literati and scholars recovered from their dumbfounded shock, they began expressing their amazement, while on Liu Qingdi's side, there were no words left to speak. All the various attacks from earlier had suddenly become one enormous joke. Most importantly, Ning Yi wasn't even present—he'd merely written a poem for a nine-year-old child, and after enduring various attacks, it had finally transformed into an enormous slap that landed solidly across their faces.

The Su family matter spreading outward was only the first wave of shock this night would stir up in Jiangning. No one yet knew what form these waves would take by tomorrow, or how Xue Yan's assessment of "calculating ten steps ahead" would evolve. As the person at the center of it all, Ning Yi couldn't possibly know that someone had taken "Settling Wind Waves" and created some kind of literary performance, or that a group of literati and scholars had also been swept into these waves.

He was currently still rather optimistic.

The fact that Old Master Su had publicly revealed his role at the clan assembly certainly left him feeling somewhat helpless, but calling it truly startling or unexpected would be disingenuous. Ginger gets spicier with age—Su Yu making this move was nothing surprising. It was a decent idle piece on the board, and if Ning Yi were in his position, he would have played it the same way. If Ning Yi had ambition, this move would make everyone in the Su family wary of him. If he lacked ambition, then however many people were wary made no difference, and it could conveniently serve as a bargaining chip backing Su Tan'er to intimidate others.

The thing was, he really didn't have any ambition to speak of. Of course, his complete indifference stemmed from knowing that if he truly wanted to accomplish something one day, the old man's arrangements would hold no real meaning for him. For someone with the ability to break out of any situation at will, naturally he wouldn't care about such small matters. Old Master Su was thinking of the Su family's interests, which was both reasonable and proper. You couldn't expect people to extend complete goodwill without any reason—being able to confirm most of their goodwill was already sufficient.

At this moment, waves were continuously spreading outward beyond the Su household. Inside the Su residence, there wasn't much left for Ning Yi to handle. All three maids were busy with their own tasks, and Su Tan'er had already gone out. Ning Yi wasn't clear on her arrangements for tonight, but presumably, with everything back on track, Su Tan'er's ability to handle detailed matters wasn't much inferior to his own, and his intervention was no longer needed.

After seeing his wife off, he wandered around the Su residence for a while, observing the flustered atmosphere in various places and the crying of children who'd been spanked for upsetting adults. His mind began drifting toward his recent chemical research and thoughts about expanding Bamboo Records to a second location.

After getting the telescope working, he'd roughly explained the principles and then traded it with Old Kang for some materials. The current gunpowder and military items like fire arrows and fire lances could be modified somewhat to serve as self-defense tools with the finished products in hand. Additionally, he was mainly considering the coming war—telescopes would be somewhat helpful for the Wu Dynasty's military forces, so he was casually doing his part for a peaceful life. Old Kang was probably still carefully studying it over there.

As for Bamboo Records, they were preparing to open the second location after the alcohol prohibition was lifted. According to current projections, the prohibition would most likely be lifted before New Year's, at which point high-proof alcohol could also be introduced along with some publicity stunts.

Actually, what occupied more of his thoughts was Lüliang Mountain. Telescopes, alcohol, gunpowder, guns—the latter two he hadn't started on yet, but improving on the Wu Dynasty's foundation presented no major problems. However, at present, he was considering whether he should avoid telling Old Kang about these things. The Wu Dynasty's problem wasn't really with military equipment but rather that their army fundamentally didn't work. When Jin forces fought Liao forces, they could defeat seventy thousand with twenty thousand, while Wu Dynasty troops heard wind and fled at the mere sight of Liao armies. Everyone was human—the main issue lay in people's hearts. Even if he improved the current fire lances to a practically useful degree and equipped the army with them, whether that would be fortune or disaster remained hard to say.

He simply wanted to experience the sense of accomplishment from improving these things. If he could contact Lu Hongti later, he'd rather provide a batch of equipment to them. He just didn't know how long this process would take—months had passed, and Lu Hongti should have returned to Lüliang Mountain by now. He wondered what things were like there currently.

Before long, Su Yunsong, who had discovered Ning Yi wandering about, came over to chat with him for a while. Afterward, this pivotal figure in the Su family's First Branch was somewhat bewildered to discover that this fellow truly didn't want to manage anything about what came next. After learning from his daughter about Ning Yi's performance throughout the entire process and then noticing this, Su Yunsong's expression as he looked at him was genuinely rather peculiar—he'd originally thought Ning Yi was a crouching tiger within the Su family's First Branch. Although he didn't participate in too many matters, he would definitely not be completely unconcerned. He surely helped Su Tan'er manage many things secretly, but now... what kind of person was this?

It was in this atmosphere that certain entities neither Su Tan'er nor Ning Yi had anticipated quietly set their sights on him, creating complications for this evening.

After finishing his conversation with Su Yunsong, Ning Yi prepared to return to his room to read while incidentally organizing everything he knew about modern firearms. Before he reached the small courtyard, a household servant came running over: "Young Master, Chan'er... she was hit by something over there and got injured. Second Miss isn't around, Young Master should quickly go take a look..."

At these words, Ning Yi's expression changed as he followed the servant toward the courtyard the man indicated. This road led to one of the Su residence's side gates, relatively quiet. Just before reaching the side gate at a path intersection, the servant fell half a step behind and pressed a blade against Ning Yi's back: "Young Master, don't walk too fast. Do as I say next."

The Wu family's gone mad... Ning Yi frowned. Although the possibility seemed remote, at present that appeared to be the only explanation. This area wasn't far from the side gate's small courtyard where carriages were parked. Ning Yi raised his hands: "Chan'er isn't injured?"

"We're not quite sure who exactly Chan'er is, but... Young Master, if you tell us, we wouldn't mind letting her get hurt a bit."

Ning Yi nodded and smiled: "Excellent."

Outside the city, Ten Steps Ridge.

"Why did you send people to assassinate my father?"

After Su Tan'er asked this question, Xi Junyu blinked, stunned for a long moment as if finding it somewhat absurd: "Where did you... hear such a thing? How is that possible?"

"Admit it." Su Tan'er looked at him, then shook her head. "When I was coming over, I was wondering what you would say, Junyu-gege. I learned so much from you when I was young. Back then I thought, Junyu-gege, you'd be a good teacher and friend for life. I'm very sad right now. Is it because I'm a woman that you have to humiliate me like this? Xi Junyu!"

As she spoke the final two sentences, the coldness emanating from Su Tan'er became pronounced as she looked at the man across from her, nearly enunciating each word.

"You... the imperial merchant matter fell through, the clan assembly turned out like this, I can understand your thinking, but..." Xi Junyu paused. "Have you lost your mind?"

"You're still thinking about all that, but you calculated everything wrong..." Su Tan'er shook her head. "I've already taken control of the First Branch. Second Uncle and Third Uncle have nothing to say. Tonight's clan assembly results will shock everyone—unfortunately you weren't there earlier..."

"How is that possible? The entire First Branch is finished, with so many people—you can turn it around? Even if the old master forcibly puts you in charge, there's no solution anymore... Haven't I thought of ways to help you? When things fail, you have to accept it. You..." Xi Junyu looked at Su Tan'er with some hesitation. For a moment he truly thought the woman before him had become unstable. Compared to her actually knowing certain things, this explanation seemed more reasonable. He glanced at Guard Geng, then at the tall, thin man holding a blade nearby.

"The Wu family's cloth faded." Su Tan'er tilted her head, waiting for Xi Junyu to digest what that meant. "Everyone's been in the game for months. When Father was assassinated, you made people believe someone was targeting the Su family—attacking and suppressing us, making Grandfather spend so long calming everything down. You did it on purpose, to make the Su family let down its guard at that moment. With your abilities, if you relaxed even a little, you could do many things. You thought you controlled the whole situation from the middle..."

As she spoke, suddenly there were two clashing sounds from outside—weapons striking together, followed by shouting voices.

"They're here!"

"Kill him!"

"Don't let them escape!"

Chaotic sounds. Xi Junyu looked in that direction while Su Tan'er also tilted her head to glance. Outside, it seemed people wanted to break in but were discovered, triggering intense combat and flight.

"Your people?" Su Tan'er asked Xi Junyu. "They actually came to rescue you. Do you believe now? Even they think you can't possibly escape by luck."

Xi Junyu turned his head back without speaking, bewilderment in his eyes, not understanding why things would truly develop to this point.

"The day Father was attacked, my husband and I were supposed to handle the charity work. Father wasn't planning to go. The assassins were arranged there early, and so many excuses prepared to ruin the Su family's reputation—that couldn't have been meant to kill me with a last-minute change of plans. There had to be an insider who knew Father would stop there when he returned, someone very familiar with the Su family who could understand everyone's habits, so they decided to assassinate Father then. You were too clever for your own good. This huge mistake—when we fooled everyone that the Su family's yellow cloth was good, that insider would definitely believe it, because he could see it with his own eyes..."

"Now the Wu family's cloth has faded and they have no way out. The crime of deceiving the emperor—they can only come begging to the Su family. Wu Qilong could only tell everything... I'd thought the Su family had an insider, but I never imagined you were the mastermind. What impressive maneuvering—you must have been quite proud, right? Unfortunately, you were already in the game too. This game is bigger than yours..." The sounds of fighting outside continued, suddenly another scream. It seemed more than one person was trying to force their way in, but many were lying in ambush around this courtyard. Su Tan'er took out several sheets of paper from her robe. "This is what Wu Chenghou signed. I made a copy—do you want to look?"

Xi Junyu leaned back against his chair, listening to the fighting outside without looking at the items on the table, his expression complex. After quite a while, he finally looked at Su Tan'er: "Crime of deceiving the emperor..." He understood what power that could wield if things reached this point. He could roughly guess what was on the table without looking, but he shook his head. "This wasn't your doing... you haven't reached that level yet..."

Su Tan'er was silent for a moment, then smiled softly, that smile gentle as she quietly gave her answer.

"Yes, it wasn't me..."

Su residence, near the side gate.

Two figures followed the two people ahead, pausing here briefly.

The figures ahead had stopped too. One was dressed as a household servant, the other walking in front with his left hand wrapped in bandages, now raising both hands.

"Number Two succeeded. Go get Number Four to bring the carriage. We'll leave immediately."

"Got it... You keep an eye on Number Two though, don't let him go too hard. He's just a weak scholar—it'd be bad to kill him."

"I know."

The two figures ahead started walking forward. They checked their surroundings and quickly followed. Soon, Number Two and the scholar turned past the courtyard gate ahead. The path there was darker. As they reached the gate, there was a thud—a figure collapsed on the road ahead.

"Number Two's temper..."

One of them muttered a curse and walked faster, then both stopped at the gate.

Under the starlight, the scholar's silhouette was outlined. He stood there, head tilted looking at the figure on the ground, his bandaged left hand waving a few times in the air. His right hand held the servant's knife, and he scratched his head somewhat awkwardly. He turned to a different angle, seeming to want to bend down and drag the body aside, then he looked back and saw the two figures at the gate. He stood up.

Six eyes met, gazing tenderly at each other...

(End of Chapter)
