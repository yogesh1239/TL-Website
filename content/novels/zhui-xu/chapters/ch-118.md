Night had not yet fallen, but an uneasy restlessness already permeated the Su mansion. Lamplight flickered in the main hall, casting wavering shadows and spilling soft light from the windows. Inside Su Tan'er's bedroom, Chan'er and Juan'er kept vigil at the bedside, pressing warm towels to their mistress's forehead as she tossed fitfully in her sleep. The white-bearded physician sat beside the bed, fingers on Su Tan'er's wrist as he took her pulse. Ning Yi stood in the doorway with his arms crossed, lost in thought. Outside in the courtyard, only the medicine apprentice who'd accompanied Doctor Sun remained—no one else had been allowed to enter.

The examination didn't take long. The old doctor released Su Tan'er's wrist and rose to leave. Juan'er hurried after him, and Xing'er appeared at the main entrance, her eyes rimmed with red.

"Second Miss has caught a wind-cold," the doctor explained. "Judging by the symptoms, she's had it for several days. Her condition was complicated when her monthly affliction began while she was already ill, making the wind-cold more severe. If that were all, I wouldn't be concerned; a few doses of medicine would bring down the fever. But there's more to it. Second Miss has been overworking herself, and the shock of what happened to Master Su has taken its toll. Under such strain, she has become utterly exhausted in both mind and body. This is not something that will heal in a few days."

"Utterly exhausted?" Ning Yi frowned at the phrase.

The old doctor nodded gravely. "Yes. Rather than calling this a wind-cold, it's more accurate to say it's the result of long-term fatigue and pressure—physical and mental exhaustion building up over time. The heart of the problem lies in her spirit. The wind-cold simply triggered everything to erupt at once. This cannot be taken lightly. I'll prescribe medicine to bring down her fever first, but for true healing... Second Miss needs to find peace in her heart. Ah..."

Doctor Sun sighed heavily. As the Su family's retained physician who'd been overseeing Su Boyong's treatment, he understood the current situation all too well. Getting Su Tan'er to find peace of mind would be no simple matter. Shaking his head, he wrote out the prescription in the sitting room, gave detailed instructions, then took his leave. Xiao Chan followed to fetch the medicine while Juan'er and Xing'er rushed to Su Tan'er's bedside. They looked back at Ning Yi with tears threatening to spill.

"What should we do?" The question seemed directed at Ning Yi but might have been rhetorical. On normal days, the three maids managed many of the main branch's affairs with considerable competence, but now with Su Boyong down and Su Tan'er collapsed as well, they were finally at a loss.

Ning Yi studied the prescription for a while before asking, "What exactly happened these past few days?"

Su Tan'er's wind-cold probably started that afternoon when she'd fallen into the bathtub. The symptoms had appeared in the following days but seemed mild at first. After Su Boyong's attack, Su Tan'er surely faced enormous challenges, yet she'd shown no signs of retreating or breaking down. Just a few nights ago at dawn, she'd confidently spoken about securing the imperial merchant status. Her spirit and confidence had been at their peak, her responses flawless.

It was like a major corporation facing attacks and conspiracies of varying severity—a normal occurrence. When the attacks came, you adapted and responded. Su Boyong's attack was a sudden blow, but Su Tan'er would never have reached her current position if she were the type to collapse from a single setback. Someone who can only handle smooth sailing would never be able to lead the Su family.

Su Tan'er wasn't that type of person—Ning Yi knew this perfectly well. For her to suffer a mental blow, it couldn't have been from those earlier events. She should have been psychologically prepared for more schemes and attacks. Something more significant must have happened in these short four days...

At his question, Juan'er looked puzzled and turned to Xing'er, who'd spent most of these days with their mistress. Still crying, Xing'er looked between Juan'er and Ning Yi before choking out, "I... Miss said not to tell..."

Ning Yi thought for a moment, then sat down in a nearby chair with a sigh. Rubbing his forehead, he murmured, "Utterly exhausted... the imperial merchant matter, something went wrong with the imperial merchant deal. An unsolvable problem... external or internal. External would mean mortally offending some high official at the Weaving Bureau, but the managers have been handling those visits these days. It wouldn't escalate to open hostility... Must be an internal problem then. A problem that can't be solved. I'm not particularly interested in the technical details, so let's leave that aside for now..."

The two maids listened to his murmured analysis. Xing'er's crying intensified. "Actually, actually... a few days ago..."

Before she could finish, footsteps sounded outside. Juan'er and Xing'er hurried to the door. It was Manager Liao, the most senior of the main branch's managers, who'd already heard about the situation from Doctor Sun. The Old Master hadn't returned to the compound yet, and the maids certainly couldn't make major decisions, so they conferred with Manager Liao in hushed tones. Ning Yi sat in the room, speculating about possibilities while occasionally glancing at Su Tan'er's sleeping form.

The bedroom was usually tidy, but these past few busy days had left it slightly disheveled. As Ning Yi walked toward some notebooks on the desk, he noticed something on the floor by the bed. Picking it up, he found a small triangular piece of cloth in pale yellow with a simple pattern.

The cloth had probably fallen there recently—no dust had gathered on it. Ning Yi held it near the oil lamp, and memory stirred. One day on the second floor of the building across the way, Su Tan'er had shown him a piece of cloth, beaming with delight: "Husband, isn't this color beautiful?"

"Oh... beautiful indeed, though common folk can't use this color..."

A bright imperial yellow cloth... Su Tan'er hadn't discussed it much then, but the vivid color had stuck in his memory. This piece seemed to have faded from bright to pale yellow...

Manager Liao mentioned Ning Yi's name outside. Ning Yi sighed and slipped the cloth into his sleeve. With both Su Boyong and Su Tan'er incapacitated, calling on people from the second and third branches wasn't an option. Though Ning Yi didn't usually involve himself in business matters, he still held the position of master in the Su household. Manager Liao chatted with him briefly, apparently wanting him to take a stance. Ning Yi nodded.

"It's nothing serious. Continue as usual—we'll cross each bridge as we come to it. Just don't spread word about Tan'er's illness. For now, don't let too many people know she's sick. That's all."

"I understand Second Miss needs rest and can't be troubled, but... if something urgent comes up requiring decisions..."

"Then bring it here. We'll figure something out. For the rest... I'll have to trouble Manager Liao and the other managers."

"Yes. Actually, even if changes come, everyone has experience dealing with them. We're all old hands in the cloth trade after all. Please help put Miss at ease, Young Master..."

Manager Liao's words were merely meant to comfort. For business in Jiangning alone, any single manager could handle things, but changes affecting nationwide operations required a central authority. For now, though, there was no other solution.

After Manager Liao left, Chan'er returned with the medicine. Not long after, the returning Old Master hurried over to check on the still-unconscious Su Tan'er. This development struck the elderly man hard as well, but there was nothing to be said now. He offered words of comfort to Ning Yi and the three maids before departing with heavy steps.

Once the Old Master left, the courtyard finally grew quiet. The oil lamps flickered, and the scent of brewing medicine filled the air. Similar quiet evenings had passed before in this courtyard—everyone sitting together, chatting and laughing over games of Five-in-a-Row. They were a family. Before Ning Yi's arrival, the four young women had probably formed their own family unit, but now the atmosphere was oppressively silent. Xiao Chan brought in the medicine bowl, and in their silent vigil, Juan'er couldn't help but sob, "What should we do? Young Master..."

Su Boyong had fallen, Su Tan'er lay unconscious, and even if she woke, her body wouldn't recover quickly. The Old Master might have to temporarily assign someone else to manage the main branch's affairs. The future suddenly seemed terrifyingly empty and unpredictable. No one knew why things had suddenly turned this way. All they could rely on were the few people around them. If Ning Yi were still the bookworm of before, he might have been excluded, but after more than a year together, at least in matters like this, he'd been accepted as part of the family... Of course, Juan'er's question came purely from helplessness. Ning Yi was a man, different from them, but expecting him to have real solutions was surely impossible.

Ning Yi stood by the window, tidying some items on the desk. The room's slight disorder prompted him to put things back in place, casually tossing some items onto shelves out of sight. His movements were unhurried, and he'd nearly finished organizing when he spoke. Without turning around, he folded a sheet of unused rice paper several times, then unfolded it and poured water into the inkstone, slowly grinding ink.

"Haven't you been taught how to handle these situations before?" he asked quietly.

The three young women shook their heads.

Ning Yi picked up a brush and paused. "Next... I need the Su family's account books from the past seven to ten years. I need records of the Su family's development in all areas, correspondence with regional managers. I want to know every business matter that's occurred, how it was handled, the final outcomes, and why those outcomes happened... Also, I need more rice paper and ink. I need some thin string. Prepare some pastries—filling ones, not too sweet. And prepare a large pot of tea... That's about it for now..."

Silence fell behind him. The three maids stood frozen, unsure what he intended. Ning Yi turned around.

"Father-in-law is in no condition to help. The Old Master... might send someone, but that won't work either." He gestured casually toward Su Tan'er on the bed. "Your miss won't let go. When she wakes, her first action won't be taking medicine—it'll be getting out of bed. No one can replace her... So the conclusion is simple."

He gave them a smile that was part helplessness, part boredom—the same look he wore when telling stories or playing chess. "I suppose I'll give it a try..."

Juan'er and Xing'er still hesitated, but Chan'er drew a sharp breath. Though her eyes had been misty with tears, a hint of a smile now appeared. She wiped her eyes and turned to leave. "I'll get the account books and records..." The Su family had a central accounting office, but the main branch kept its own separate records next door. The three maids, who managed these affairs daily, had the authority to retrieve them.

After Xiao Chan left, Juan'er thought for a moment and followed. Then Xing'er wiped the tears from her face and asked softly as she headed out, "Young Master... will you work here?"

"What if your miss wakes up?"

The young master wanted to help solve their miss's problems. Whether this was good or bad, what results it might bring—Xing'er and Juan'er couldn't say. But they knew with absolute certainty that if their miss woke elsewhere, she'd immediately try to get up and handle business. Keeping her in bed was something perhaps only the young master could manage.

She smiled awkwardly and left. When only he and the unconscious Su Tan'er remained, Ning Yi finally sat down and sighed at the rice paper and brush before him.

"You people are too much," he sighed to himself. "Can't even let a live-in son-in-law have a moment's peace..."

His complaint seemed directed not at his wife, but at the unseen players behind the scenes.
