As night fell, the atmosphere at Princess Cheng's Imperial Son-in-law's mansion carried the refined dignity befitting the royal residence.

Since the founding of the Wu Dynasty, the Zhou imperial family had flourished into a substantial network of wealthy relatives who, due to strict imperial management, were largely barred from significant military or political power, making the title of imperial son-in-law one of the most awkward and constraining positions.

Though Jiangning housed several imperial sons-in-law, Princess Cheng's husband, Kang Xian, stood apart, for unlike the men of ordinary ability who typically married princesses, he was a renowned contemporary scholar of genuine talent and learning. Most importantly, by this time both he and his wife had achieved higher seniority in rank compared to most other imperial family members.

While an emperor's daughters were princesses and his sisters elder princesses, Princess Cheng Zhou Xuan, as the emperor's aunt, bore the title of Grand Elder Princess¹—a combination that conveyed impressive authority. Few people today remembered why the brilliant scholar Kang Xian had originally chosen to become an imperial son-in-law, and the parties involved had likely long since put such matters behind them. The Grand Elder Princess Zhou Xuan and her husband, Kang Xian, were old enough for a peaceful retirement, yet the shrewd and formidable pair secretly controlled wealth and businesses beyond anyone's imagination—fortunes that would astonish everyone if brought into the open.

Naturally, wise individuals understood the principle of protecting themselves through discretion. Throughout the Jiangning region, Princess Cheng's influence remained carefully detached from major political affairs, with her various enterprises maintaining a low-profile approach to accumulating vast wealth. Because of this stance, many surrounding imperial relatives preferred to maintain close relationships with them. This afternoon, after Prince Kang Zhou Yong had taken his children and various talented scholars for tea, he casually dropped by for a visit.

Long after the dinner hour, Zhou Yong sat chatting with his imperial aunt in the courtyard, his restless children having long since dragged Kang Xian away to the treasure pavilion to examine rare artifacts. Zhou Yong rarely visited the imperial son-in-law's mansion, but in childhood he had maintained close intimacy with his aunt, the Elder Princess Zhou Xuan, so after engaging in extensive family conversation, he finally left the courtyard and encountered Kang Xian near the garden area. As for the children, they had apparently wandered off elsewhere again.

Zhou Yong had always maintained respectful regard for Kang Xian, and now the two men strolled toward the garden area to enjoy the cool evening air. After discussing various minor matters, Zhou Yong casually mentioned the afternoon's events: "Today while accompanying Zhou Pei and Junwu at the Fragrant Warmth Tea House, joining with various talented scholars, I happened to observe someone—the individual you had previously mentioned..."

He recounted the entire sequence of events, including Liu Qingdi's appearance and his manner when speaking: "Ha, achieving the title of premier talent is no easy feat, and this Liu Qingdi, though he appears magnanimous while repeatedly praising Ning Yi's remarkable literary abilities, likely harbors jealousy in his heart and seeks to spread malicious gossip. Such statements contain much that's untrue, yet afterward it seemed many people were aware of these matters. Regarding that Ning Liheng, since you suggested some days ago that Zhou Pei and Junwu might study under him, I thought to meet him personally, though I wonder if you're aware of this matter concerning the Su and Wu family cloth merchants."

After they settled in the pavilion and Zhou Yong finished speaking, Kang Xian was already chuckling with amusement. In truth, Kang Xian wouldn't have believed any prior concern from Zhou Yong was genuine, as Kang Xian himself had handled the bulk of the children's education over the years. When he had casually suggested the city's premier talent as a tutor, Zhou Yong had simply nodded agreeably, content to have the teacher summoned at his convenience without regard for his character, trusting Kang Xian to handle the evaluation. Perhaps only today's observations had finally sparked some genuine interest.

Imperial family members, at their core, ultimately valued practical ability.

"Heh heh, I actually asked Liheng similar questions over a month ago. At that time the Su and Wu families' imperial merchant competition was creating quite a stir, and when the dust initially settled with the Su family having been outmaneuvered at the last moment, he angrily composed the poem 'Pouring Wine for Pei Di'² in public. I assumed he felt frustrated, thinking that if the matter couldn't be resolved, he would eventually need to come seek my assistance, so I waited at home for several days. Later when I encountered him at Old Qin's residence, he seemed preoccupied with worries, and his Go game was completely distracted, yet he stubbornly refused to ask for help—it was truly infuriating..."

"If that's the case, he does seem to possess considerable pride," Zhou Yong nodded, then asked, "But it sounds as though you and this Ning Yi are quite familiar? And... Old Qin?"

He recalled Ning Yi's appearance—barely over twenty years old, truly young—and had originally assumed his adoptive father merely appreciated the young man's talent, but now it seemed their relationship was quite unusual.

"Heh heh, we began as Go partners, nothing too involved initially, but later Liheng provided tremendous assistance—tsk, many people benefited from his help." Kang Xian's expression grew serious as he nodded, then broke into laughter. "But I later discovered it wasn't pride at all. Ha, that day I told him, 'Given our friendship, surely you wouldn't need to be so cautious about asking for help just once?' This matter involved his reputation, and for the Su family the implications were enormous—I had originally decided to intervene once to help him resolve everything, yet his subsequent response left me unable to mention the matter again for over a month, heh heh..."

Finding the memory amusing, he laughed heartily while Zhou Yong frowned in confusion: "One response?"

"Ha, 'That cloth fades.'"

Kang Xian shook his head, and these simple words drifted lightly through the pavilion air while Zhou Yong's expression showed continued puzzlement. For a moment, silence fell around them, and after quite some time, he finally processed the meaning and reacted with genuine shock: "Ah?"

******************

While conversations continued at the imperial son-in-law's mansion, in the Wu family's main hall a heated argument and discussion erupted with desperate intensity. Household servants stationed themselves at a distance around this area, occasionally glancing back to glimpse the shifting shadows of figures, yet unable to comprehend the unfolding crisis. Inside that room where people determining the Wu family's entire fate had gathered, strange atmospheric currents never before experienced now permeated and spread—emotions utterly unlike any previous day: anger, bewilderment, fear, absurdity, even occasional outbursts of hysteria.

"No matter what, the one-third demand is impossible... absurd! There's never been such a thing!"

After Wu Qilong's explanation broke through the long-raging argument, the hall fell into a stunned silence that soon gave way to discussion tinged with a growing sense of absurdity. Even in the notoriously greedy commercial world, such situations rarely occurred—one merchant brazenly telling another, "Give me one-third of your family property." This kind of demand seemed so ridiculous at first hearing that it hardly merited discussion, yet as the atmosphere gradually settled and they understood from Wu Chenghou and others' expressions that the situation was no joke, and as time provided them space for consideration, these people finally managed to organize their thoughts and consider the entire matter's severity.

"Give them one-third? Then spend another third or even half of the family property on activities and bribery? What would the Wu family become then? We... even if we died, how could we face the Wu family ancestors who worked so hard to accumulate this fortune! The title of Jiangning's premier cloth merchant..." Wu Chengluo, who managed affairs in the Hezhou area, shook his head desperately. "It's just fading fabric—I don't believe it could lead to confiscation of family property! As long as we engage in more activities and bribery, our Wu family might not be unable to weather this crisis!"

"When the wall falls, everyone pushes, Seventh Brother," Wu Chengyuan said grimly.

From another direction, Wu Chengke spoke with an iron-gray expression: "Give them one-third, then lose half or one-third afterward, abandon future market share to become exclusively imperial merchants—that's what the Su family envisions. After this incident, if things don't proceed this way, do you think the Su family will easily let the matter rest?"

"You also said the Su family won't easily let it rest—who knows whether they might secretly leak information or sabotage us behind the scenes!"

"They'll need time to digest what they take, and moreover... this approach preserves their reputation better..."

"Not destroying our family's reputation is better for their reputation?"

Wu Chengluo stared at Wu Chengke with utter bewilderment.

"At least people won't say they took our property and then drove us to death..." Wu Qilong's voice was low as he joined the battle.

"Whether they can drive us to death is still a question!"

"Seventh Uncle, don't speak in anger..."

"I'm not speaking in anger—you've been frightened by that scholar! Everyone knows those so-called talents just engage in empty rhetoric!"

"But there really might be war, and when the wall falls everyone pushes, Seventh Uncle! Now a group of people are watching the Su family, still not making their move because they're waiting for the Su family to collapse on its own! The Su family hasn't made any major external mistakes yet. But if our family encounters this kind of trouble, everyone can seize evidence, everyone can add insult to injury! Does our Wu family have fewer enemies than the Su family?"

"It might not reach that extreme! If we do as he suggests, what's the difference between reaching that point and..."

"Shut up!"

Bang! A walking stick struck the ground, and after enduring so much noise, amidst all the surrounding chatter and discussion, Fifth Uncle Wu Zhen, seated above, finally exploded. The elderly man stood up trembling.

"Stop talking nonsense! The issue now isn't about what degree. It's confiscation of property and extermination of the clan!" He struck the ground with his walking stick: "Confiscation of property and extermination of the clan!"

All surrounding voices fell silent momentarily as the old man surveyed everyone around him, then collapsed back into his chair, speaking between labored breaths: "Still don't understand? It's not about what degree—if you make the wrong choice afterward, it's not a difference between giving thirty percent or sixty percent. If you choose wrongly, it's confiscation of property and extermination of the clan. Everyone here, everyone outside, your wives and children at home—death for those who die, exile for those who get exiled. At this point, you all understand, don't you... Stop arguing and say something useful."

"We can only... try to approach some high officials through connections..." Wu Chengyuan hesitated before speaking.

Wu Chenghou above shook his head: "Ten days isn't enough time—even with vast amounts of money, we couldn't reach officials at the Three Chancelleries and Six Boards³ level."

Fifth Uncle Wu Zhen caught his breath: "Actually, if negotiations truly break down, would it really be this severe? Everyone should consider this first."

"The Chen and Lü families are also watching us—they used to be imperial merchants and now want to expand further. They have... previous official connections. If our Wu family falls and yields market share, they would definitely be delighted..."

"When the wall falls, everyone pushes—that's certain..."

"Moreover, there really might be war. If it were like before..." Wu Qilong frowned deeply, "there would likely be more room for maneuvering..."

"War doesn't necessarily mean something will definitely happen. What are the actual odds?" Wu Chengluo asked.

"I don't know," Wu Qilong spoke honestly, then looked around: "What do you uncles and elders think? Do... do you dare take this gamble?"

Confiscation of property and extermination of the clan ultimately depended on the emperor's mood. If it were just a single issue, perhaps they could still take the risk, but with the combination of pre-war timing, the exposure and subsequent pushback from various cloth merchants, plus the emperor's possible comprehensive reaction after hearing about this, no one could maintain good spirits. A period of oppressive silence fell. Fifth Uncle struck his walking stick once: "Then there's nothing more to discuss about this matter..."

"There might still be opportunities." Wu Chengke had been thinking for a long time before speaking. "That Ning Liheng's approach is simple—essentially making our Wu family use money to buy time, but business can always be negotiated. We don't know how much of his statements involve bluffing, and we need to see how much the Su family wants to ensure a smooth transition. If it's not smooth, how much effort they'll need to expend—in this process, who exactly is planning things, who's making decisions, we need to clarify these matters first..."

Wu Chenghou nodded: "It's essentially Su Yu, Su Tan'er, and Ning Yi—these three people..."

Wu Chengyuan frowned: "I'm not clear about what Ning Yi is like, but Su Yu and Su Tan'er are no easy opponents. What if we collapse negotiations in a single attempt?"

Wu Chenghou remained silent for a moment: "We need to see how determined they are, how much they want..."

"Su Tan'er hasn't been having an easy time lately either." Wu Qihao raised his head from his position. "Our Wu family ultimately has opportunities to avoid problems, and even if the situation deteriorates, we could currently hold out for several months or even most of a year... Su Tan'er now desperately wants to prove her capabilities. Since she's leading this matter, I believe... there will definitely be room for negotiation... The timing of when the postponement news becomes public is most critical. If we can delay past these few days, we might be able to spread false information and keep the market uncertain about what to believe."

"This would only avoid detection by some people. Once the Su family releases information, there will definitely be believers. Spreading rumors just deceives a portion of people, and we'd need to engage in activities for at least one or two months..." Someone shook their head. "To find weak points, we should first consider who's actually controlling the situation, but I don't think this scheme resembles something Su Tan'er would control."

"Su Yu never used such methods before either, it doesn't seem like... but aside from them, it couldn't really be that Ning Yi, could it? Such matters can't be accomplished through intelligence alone—they require people like Su Yu and Su Tan'er, and we investigated before—he has no experience whatsoever..."

Amidst the chatter and buzzing of discussion, Fifth Uncle sighed and gestured toward Wu Qilong, who had been sitting silently again: "Qilong, you've had the most contact with that Ning Yi. Even if he really arranged this scheme, what kind of person is he exactly?"

Wu Qilong glanced at him with some hesitation: "I... I have some thoughts, but they're..." He shook his head. "This is difficult to express..."

In the business world, one could only truly begin strategizing after grasping an opponent's outline. To devise strategies and escape dangerous situations required similar understanding—if an opponent had set what appeared to be a perfect trap, then one could only search for weaknesses in their character, guessing what elements they might not have anticipated. The Su family had competed with the Xue family for many years, with the Wu family observing from the sidelines throughout, yet regarding this Ning Liheng, no one could truly understand him even now. Perhaps only Wu Qilong, who had interacted with him most extensively, could barely piece together some rough impressions at this critical moment.

"Looking back, there's one matter we all overlooked." He sighed. "We all know Su Tan'er's personality. When she fell ill right after Su Boyong's assassination, we simply assumed she was overwhelmed by the enormous pressure and suspected nothing, but there were problems with that story."

Someone nearby reacted as Wu Qihao said: "She didn't appear for that entire month..."

"Exactly." Wu Qilong nodded. "With Su Tan'er's personality, after the initial few days of cold symptoms passed and her fever broke, she couldn't possibly have remained bedridden in such circumstances. Yet Ning Yi took control then—somewhat laughably—but behind the scenes, the Su family continuously promoted yellow cloth with high-profile publicity, never missing a step. Our assumption was that Su Tan'er was managing the overall situation while letting Ning Yi handle the details, and since he only seemed to manage the broad strokes, we were certain his detailed work would contain exploitable gaps..."

"Speaking of it now might seem like hindsight analysis, but considering Su Tan'er's character, how could she possibly have remained at home during those circumstances? If Ning Yi didn't understand, she certainly would have... It wasn't until a few days ago when Ning Yi mentioned those things that I realized—he said Su Tan'er collapsed because of the fabric fading. A woman like that, with Su Boyong's assassination and the Su family's internal and external troubles at the time, these issues alone couldn't possibly have kept her bedridden for a month. We overlooked these details, but thinking back, she collapsed when the Su family's main branch lacked a central decision-maker, and her condition at that time couldn't have enabled such calculations. So it must have been during this period that Ning Yi completed his planning, which is why even after her fever broke she didn't get up, and that old master didn't interfere either..."

"Then everyone fell into this trap. At the time it appeared that Ning Yi had done nothing, but thinking back now, we didn't even feel the slightest unease—we never even considered the possibility. Even after the imperial merchant decision was made more than a month later, when Ning Yi completely stepped away from this matter, we calculated everything multiple times without the slightest suspicion... Uncles and cousins, everything was perfectly timed. If I must describe what kind of opponent he represents, then quite simply... quite simply..."

He frowned with a hesitant expression for a long time, unable to find appropriate words, yet everyone around him could roughly perceive the outline being sketched...

*****************

"...Actually, when explained clearly, it's nothing more than simple borrowing flowers to present to Buddha⁴. Speaking of it casually, many people could probably conceive such plans, but genuine implementation requires extraordinarily high difficulty—inducing others to develop specific thoughts without being overly deliberate, ensuring every link proceeds perfectly. Otherwise, the Wu family, being experienced commercial veterans after years of struggle, would detect problems from the smallest details..."

In the imperial son-in-law mansion's pavilion, Kang Xian chuckled while shaking his head.

"At that time the Su family had internal traitors, so Ning Yi couldn't possibly have instructed various people in acting. Given his position as an live-n son-in-law, controlling the entire situation seemed impossibly difficult, yet he managed to gradually kindle others' interests step by step. On the surface, nobody detected anything unusual—the Wu family assumed they had deliberately stolen the Su family's formula, completely unaware of how many strings Ning Yi manipulated behind the scenes. At the time I also had people monitoring the Su family. Ha, I noticed nothing either. After he spoke those words that day, I sat stunned like you for quite a while. When I finally understood, my scalp tingled... truly remarkable..."

"Lifting heavy weights as though they were light, threading this scheme together strand by strand... Many things appear miraculous, their concepts perhaps simple, but success or failure often depends on these details that others cannot see or detect. Similar achievements might only be possible for someone like Old Qin... cough..."

He paused mid-sentence and sighed slightly. Zhou Yong frowned: "Uncle refers to Master Qin—could that be..."

Kang Xian shook his head. Actually, the matter he referenced no longer counted as particularly strict secrecy, though it remained inadvisable to discuss casually: "Liheng's accomplishment in this affair truly merits admiration. I imagine the timing is almost right—we should soon witness the final outcome. Heh heh, then you and I can observe those people's dumbfounded expressions... Having Zhou Pei and Junwu study under him would indeed be fortunate. Dexuan must not treat this matter lightly."

"This matter goes without saying—I absolutely dare not be negligent." Zhou Yong performed a respectful bow, demonstrating his newfound appreciation for Ning Yi. "However, I heard he declined becoming a guest scholar for the Prince's mansion—I wonder why."

This matter he had heard some time ago and subsequently forgotten, but now it returned to mind.

"Ha, Liheng's temperament differs from others—in time you'll understand. It's not that he holds any objections toward the Prince's mansion. Dexuan, do you know why he remained somewhat distracted during our Go game that day, despite handling commercial matters with such casual ease?"

"Could he have encountered some trouble?" Zhou Yong frowned, determined to remember this matter.

Kang Xian smiled with amusement: "Not exactly... well, one might call it trouble, though not the kind others could resolve. At that time he had handled life-and-death matters involving the Su and Wu families with complete nonchalance, yet still faced difficulties. Both Old Qin and I felt somewhat curious, so when he revealed the answer, heh heh, we found it truly amusing. It turned out that while out one day, a woman had confessed her feelings to him. Since he was already the Su family's in-marrying son-in-law, he felt somewhat uncertain about how to arrange the future..."

Zhou Yong blinked, then chuckled with incredulous amusement: "Such a trivial matter! Men naturally take three wives and four... eh..." He had originally intended to speak boldly, but considering that his adoptive father maintained only one wife—though his relationship with his aunt was profound—he ultimately halted this comment and shifted direction: "Cough, this person truly demonstrates sincere devotion to relationships..."

"Heh heh, speaking of which, Old Qin and I both know that woman—she's truly excellent. Originally from the pleasure quarters though she consistently maintained moral conduct, she later redeemed her own freedom..."

*******************

On this peaceful night, Ning Yi and Su Tan'er stood on the second-floor corridor of the Su mansion's small courtyard, gazing at the round moon in the sky while eating and enjoying the breeze as they talked.

Today the two were boredly munching on uninspired food—flatbread.

"When negotiating with the Wu family, you should speak more domineeringly."

"Mm... but how exactly should one speak domineeringly?"

"Uh, for example... 'Don't be sad, after all, people are alive...'"

"Husband would infuriate people to death."

"No I wouldn't—they're all commercial elites... Mm, the fourteenth day's moon is also quite round..."

"Too bad it's not the fifteenth..."

"Why did you suddenly think of the fifteenth?"

"I didn't get to go to this year's poetry gathering, so it's a pity. I couldn't see husband write another moon poem that would leave those talented scholars with nothing to write."

"It wasn't that exaggerated..."

"Why don't you write one today, husband, to celebrate the Wu family's downfall."

"Sure."

"Eh, really going to write one?"

"Heh, as a talent, naturally writing poetry is something I can do at the drop of a hat..."

"..." Waiting attentively.

"...Ocean, you are all water!"

"Mm..."

"Horses, you have four legs!"

"..." Her face began to twitch.

"Moon, you are so round!" His expression remained calm.

"..."

"Wu family, you're finished."

"..." Her head had already lowered, desperately stuffing flatbread into her mouth to suppress her body's trembling.

"Finished. See, moon poetry and celebrating the Wu family's downfall."

"Mm... eh... *cough cough*... eh..."

"What's wrong with you?"

"Eh eh eh..."

"Are you trying to strangle yourself..."

Under the moonlight, Ning Yi patted his wife's back with helpless laughter, noticing again in that moment how slender her figure was.

She seemed about to choke to death and had started hitting Ning Yi's chest with her head.

As she abandoned herself to the kind of completely unladylike behavior she had never engaged in before, she thought, *This is probably the year I've laughed the most...*

¹ **Grand Elder Princess**: An imperial title. The emperor's daughters are called princesses, his sisters are called elder princesses, and his aunts are called grand elder princesses. This represents a higher rank in the imperial hierarchy.

² **"Pouring Wine for Pei Di"**: A Tang Dynasty poem by Wang Wei about the fickleness of human relationships and betrayal. The key line "Human feelings flip like waves" captures the theme of how people's loyalty changes unpredictably.

³ **Three Chancelleries and Six Boards**: The main administrative structure of imperial government, representing the highest levels of bureaucracy below the emperor.

⁴ **Borrowing flowers to present to Buddha**: An idiom meaning to gain favor or credit by using someone else's resources or efforts.
