"You'll just cause trouble. You can't follow me."

In the narrow alley, Ning Yi shook his head, feeling helpless against this sudden turn of events. But it was clear these two little devils weren't going to be put off by a simple shake of the head.

"Why not?" This time it was Zhou Pei who spoke up.

"These are business secrets. You can't just talk about them casually. You'll understand later that it's not what you think."

"Then... then what does Teacher want to do? How can you convince that He Fang?"

"Can't you see he won't even meet me? What's there to convince or not convince?"

"Then... we can find a way to help Teacher meet He Fang..."

Ning Yi squinted slightly as he looked at the child in front of him. Junwu also smiled as he looked over, and after a moment, seemed somewhat puzzled: "Uh, is that not okay?"

Ning Yi laughed: "You're a little prince¹ and a little princess² - aren't you bored enough that you care about these matters?"

"No, no! We might be a little prince and princess, but we're really just useless freeloaders." Junwu explained earnestly, glancing at his sister before turning back, his eyes blinking. Feeling he'd been a bit too hard on himself, he quickly corrected, "Uh, not completely useless... it's just that our father isn't involved in state matters, so we'll have nothing to do when we grow up. My sister and I don't want that. We want to do great things, so we want to learn from you how to threaten people... uh, no, negotiate, negotiate..."

"But I'm not planning to threaten anyone."

"Ah? Then how will Teacher get the Imperial Merchant status?"

"That's complicated..."

The adult and two children negotiated for a while before finally seeming to reach an agreement. Ning Yi left the alley, nodded to a palace guard he recognized, and then the siblings emerged and boarded a carriage that had been following from a distance.

Around the corner, in a nearby teahouse, Xue Jin and others sitting by the second-floor window spotted Ning Yi and began chatting and laughing.

It wasn't so much what Ning Yi was doing that mattered. It was the fact that he, as the steward of the Su family's First Branch, had been visiting He Fang for the past few days with a persistence that suggested he wouldn't give up. This had finally started to attract some attention, since there was always a chance a fool's persistence might pay off. Xue Yan had business today and couldn't make it, so Xue Jin and the others sat in the teahouse, laughing and speculating whether Ning Yi would even get to see He Fang, and if he did, whether he could accomplish anything.

But the third day's events exceeded everyone's expectations... or in some sense, were exactly what they expected.

******************

"Ha, you're saying he... gave up? Left after just half an hour?"

That evening, in Swallow Jade Tower, Xue Yan, Xue Jin, and the others were discussing the afternoon's events. Xue Jin laughed and shook his head. "I was betting with Ah Xiang and the others that since it was the third day, He Fang might finally agree to see him. I bet that he'd get to meet Master He but wouldn't accomplish anything. Ended up losing five taels of silver... Nobody expected he'd only stay for half an hour before leaving, without even saying if he'd be back tomorrow. He just gave up."

"The funniest part is what we found out later," a cousin chimed in with a laugh. "Master He already knew Ning Yi had been coming. He figured that even though a live-in son-in-law doesn't have much pull, his persistence showed some real sincerity. So this morning, he told the gatekeeper that if Ning Yi showed up and waited the full hour again without giving up, to let him in. Who would've thought... haha..."

"A scholar will always be a scholar," Xue Yan said, shaking his head. "They start with ambitions higher than the sky but have no clue what they're doing. They're the most troublesome kind. I bet Su Tan'er has a real headache on her hands. You can't help someone who won't be helped³. If you've got a knack for writing, you should just stay home, write poems, and look at the moon. It's just like those poets who go on about serving the country and complain that no one recognizes their talent. But if you actually put them in charge, it's not that they don't have the heart for it—they just don't have the ability. Heh, mediocrity is mediocrity. Just a bunch of armchair strategizing."

"But having someone so mediocre in the Su family is good for us," Xue Yan laughed again after a pause. "Everyone should try to get closer to this Ning Liheng. By the way, Ah Jin, find a chance to invite him out for me. We're all in the same business. Business is business, but connections matter. Things got a little heated last time, and Ah Xia and I were also at fault. Let's have a meal together, and I'll apologize to him in person, hahaha..."

While Xue Jin, Xue Yan and others discussed this afternoon's events, Su Tan'er wasn't actually troubled by it. The bigger reaction to this news came from the Su family's other two branches. It was said that Su Zhongkan slammed the table that evening and nearly threw things.

"Ridiculous! He's just a scholar who doesn't understand a thing! If he'd just kept at it and shown Master He some sincerity, that would've been one thing. But what was this? What will Master He think of our Su family now? He's... he's just dragging us all down!"

This sentiment spread from the Su family's Second Branch, and people throughout the mansion were talking about it all evening. Of course, neither the Second nor Third Branch made their feelings public. No one went to the Old Master to complain or even spoke to Su Tan'er about it, because the real issue was that Su Tan'er's bid for the Imperial Merchant status was a threat to them both.

That same night, even Nie Yunzhu and Yuan Jin'er were puzzled when they heard the news. Ning Yi had brought a brother and sister over for a meal that afternoon and seemed completely unfazed, but he probably didn't know yet that Master He had been ready to see him.

"Sigh, Sister Yunzhu, I bet he's definitely unhappy tonight."

"He probably... won't be. Liheng is pretty easygoing..."

"No matter how easygoing he is, he'd still be upset. And... he was just half an hour short! What a pity. Why didn't he stick it out?"

"Maybe he... felt that Master He really didn't want to see him, or perhaps those siblings were waiting outside and he was hurrying to meet them..."

"Who are those siblings? Could they be his children born outside⁴?"

"Nonsense, Liheng is only just over twenty - where would he get such old children..."

"Maybe he had a child bride before becoming a live-in son-in-law..."

Despite their good intentions, their speculations weren't much different from others. In Jiangning City at the moment, the only place with different guesses was perhaps in the Imperial Son-in-law's mansion on one side of the city.

"He did it on purpose?" In the pavilion, Kang Xian listened to the siblings' story with some surprise. The two had gone home for dinner and a bath before coming over. Kang Xian had already heard the news that Ning Yi had missed his chance to meet He Fang because he'd left half an hour early on the third day.

"Mhm," Zhou Pei nodded. The little girl wore a fresh autumn dress, her face flushed with excitement, clearly thrilled to have been part of something big. She and her brother had already spent the carriage ride over speculating about it. "Imperial Son-in-law Grandfather⁵, why would Ning Liheng do that?"

"Heh, I can't figure it out either." After thinking for a while, Kang Xian finally shook his head in puzzlement. "He didn't tell you?"

"Mm, he refused to say. Junwu said he could help him meet He Fang, but he immediately refused. This person... never intended to meet that He Fang from the beginning. I can't understand how he knew He Fang would see him today, so he simply left half an hour early."

"He didn't know for sure, but today was the third day..." Kang Xian sighed. "Liheng... he was probably counting on the significance of the number three⁶. An ordinary person would likely persist for three days. After all, He Fang isn't the type to refuse all visitors; he just won't discuss the Imperial Merchant business. If he were to meet someone, he'd want to make it look like he was moved by their sincerity, so he'd probably wait until the third day. Ning Yi deliberately created the appearance of giving up halfway. He chose the third day and then left just half an hour early. Heh, that guy..."

Kang Xian shook his head and laughed, but his eyes still showed puzzlement: "But why would he do this? Leaving He Fang with such an impression - how can he still resolve the Imperial Merchant matter? Little Pei, Junwu, what else did you discuss with him?"

"We made a deal," Junwu piped up with a laugh.

"Exchange?"

"Mhm. He wouldn't let us go in or help, which we thought was strange. We asked him if he could really solve the Su family's crisis, since you said he was so capable, Granduncle⁷. He was probably worried we'd cause trouble. Finally, he admitted he was working on it, but said it would take a long time and business is unpredictable, so he couldn't tell us the details. So, we made a deal! We promised not to cause trouble, and in return, we get to follow him around for the next month to see what he's up to at the Su family. He eventually agreed, as long as we behave. We're planning to go undercover as shop assistants at the fabric store, or maybe as book boys. I'll be Book Boy A, and sister can be Book Boy B... oh... fine, you can be Book Boy A, sister..."

Little Junwu chattered innocently and earnestly, then got bonked on the head by his sister and quickly corrected himself. Across from them, Kang Xian's eyes had narrowed: "You two little rascals, because I recently haven't let you go out and made you study in the mansion every day - you did this on purpose, didn't you?"

"Not at all, we want to see just how capable the teacher Granduncle praises so highly really is..."

The little boy looked completely sincere, tugging his sister's clothes underneath. Zhou Pei beside him also quickly nodded: "Yes, Imperial Son-in-law Grandfather, if I know he's really capable, I can wholeheartedly acknowledge him as my teacher. You also said the Su family's current troubles are very difficult to handle. If he can resolve them without asking for help, then Junwu and I will acknowledge he's truly capable... Mm, we promise not to cause trouble or run around."

"Mm mm mm mm..." The little boy beside her imitated a pecking chick.

Kang Xian squinted at them for a long time before letting out a dry laugh. "Fine. 'Each field has its specialists,' after all. It's no surprise if he can't solve this, but if he does, you might as well see how he does it. Since he agreed to this, I trust he won't lead you astray. But remember one thing: once you're out there, you are not to leave Guard Mu's sight for a second. I'll have people checking on you, too. If there's a single incident, you'll both be grounded here until the city gates open. Got it?"

"Mm." Two heads nodded vigorously, then the siblings exchanged a smile. Finally free.

*******************

The Su family was, after all, one of Jiangning's three major textile giants, so the story of what Ning Yi had done over the past few days spread quickly through the city's textile industry. The next afternoon, setting aside the immediate fallout, Ning Yi went to Zhu's to meet Nie Yunzhu before heading to the Qin mansion to apologize to Old Qin as promised.

A few days earlier, Yunzhu had actually been looking forward to this. Ning Yi taking her to the old man's home to apologize felt significant. But today, she felt none of that excitement. After her conversation with Jin'er last night, her heart was filled with other feelings. As they walked, making small talk, she kept glancing at Ning Yi ahead of her, trying to think of something comforting to say.

But in the end, these words were never spoken...

FOOTNOTES:
¹ Little prince (小王爷): Junior title for children of royal blood, indicating their status within the imperial hierarchy.

² Little princess (小郡主): Junior title for daughters of princes, indicating their status within the imperial hierarchy.

³ Can't support someone who won't be supported (扶不起的阿斗): An idiom meaning someone who cannot be helped or improved despite assistance, referencing the incompetent son of a famous historical figure.

⁴ Children born outside (在外面生的儿女): Refers to illegitimate children born to concubines or mistresses, a common social reality in traditional Chinese society.

⁵ Imperial Son-in-law Grandfather (驸马爷爷): A respectful address acknowledging both family relationship and imperial connection.

⁶ Number three (三这个数字): In Chinese culture, three often represents completeness or the proper duration for showing respect and sincerity.

⁷ Granduncle (姑爷爷): A generational title indicating family relationship through marriage or adoption.
