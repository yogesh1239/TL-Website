The early winter afternoon bathed the teahouse in gentle sunlight, yet the atmosphere between the two men crackled with tension as sharp as drawn blades.

"So it really... was you..."

To be honest, this wasn't how Wu Qilong had imagined things would unfold. Though he'd been speculating about Ning Yi and Su Tan'er beforehand, analyzing the situation the Wu family faced and reaching preliminary conclusions, he hadn't expected—at least not today—that Ning Yi would say something like this.

Speculation was one thing, but verification was another. For the past two days, even as he sat down across from Ning Yi just now, his mind had been racing, calculating how to probe for the slightest hint of truth. When Ning Yi mentioned chemistry and metals moments ago, his thoughts had leaped to the connection—so the fabric discoloration really was related to him? This should have been a gradual process of raising stakes toward certainty, yet the man had toppled everything with a single glance and a quiet admission, confirming all his suspicions in an instant.

This made no sense—after laying such an elaborate trap, why reveal his hand now? Not in a formal setting, not after careful deliberation, but here in this casual teahouse on a lazy winter afternoon, speaking of such momentous matters as if discussing the weather. Various emotions churned through Wu Qilong's mind in bewildering succession, and even though he'd mentally prepared himself for the possibility of Ning Yi's involvement, the sudden surge of confusion was overwhelming and impossible to articulate.

Ning Yi merely looked at him for a moment before lowering his head to pour tea.

"You seem angry. Why?"

The question came softly as Ning Yi lifted his cup and took a sip, his expression unchanged, while Wu Qilong found himself clenching his jaw so hard his teeth ached.

Why was he angry? Obviously because...

Because...

He suddenly laughed, glancing around before settling back against his chair. "So it really was you... Everyone miscalculated..."

Ning Yi shook his head with apparent indifference. "Things between the Su and Wu families have already reached this point—does it really matter who's responsible? How bad is the situation over there?"

Wu Qilong couldn't have anticipated that Ning Yi would ask such a question with such calm certainty, as if it were the most natural thing in the world. He stared for a moment before letting out an incredulous laugh. "You're asking me how bad things are? Don't you know?"

"Not really—things have been chaotic at home lately, and besides, I haven't concerned myself with this matter for over a month."

"You..." Wu Qilong tilted his head, eyes widening. "Haven't concerned yourself with it!?"

This situation had been brewing for months—one could even say the Su family had been preparing for years—and now it affected countless people and matters, with who knew how many still scrambling desperately to salvage something. In these few months alone, the silver involved probably amounted to hundreds of thousands, perhaps even millions of taels, yet when he'd finally found a key figure, the man sat here casually saying 'I stopped paying attention over a month ago'!?

He fixed his gaze on Ning Yi, the absurdity of it all beyond words. After studying him for a while, Ning Yi smiled and closed the book beside him. "Well, even without following closely, the timing is pretty clear—Tan'er has mentioned it at home several times recently, and I hear she's started spreading rumors outside, so it must be about now."

"...So the rumors really were her doing?"

"Yeah, that's right." He nodded with such sincere earnestness—as if admitting to helping an old lady cross the street—that his quiet voice carried clearly despite its softness. "It's not specific information yet, but she'll consider releasing that soon. Your contract with the Imperial Textile Bureau—the first delivery deadline should be approaching. That's when things will really begin."

An atmosphere of absurdity permeated the teahouse, leaving Wu Qilong struggling to reconcile the words he understood with the completely unreal environment he felt trapped in. Ning Yi's tone was so peaceful and his attitude so sincere that he projected an air of transparent detachment, as if he were a mere observer describing events from the outside. Su Tan'er was spreading rumors now and would release specific information later—he stated all this without the slightest attempt at concealment. Wu Qilong had never encountered anyone like this before, never experienced such a conversation, such negotiation, such confrontation. The sheer absurdity of it defied comprehension.

Yet the most bitter irony was this: even knowing these steps with perfect clarity, he was utterly powerless to prevent them from unfolding.

Wu Qilong stared at Ning Yi in this absurd daze, temporarily unable to organize his thoughts into words, while Ning Yi sipped his tea and gazed out at the street, patiently waiting for him to collect himself. After a long moment, Wu Qilong drew a deep breath and nodded through gritted teeth. "So you think it's time to show your cards?"

Ning Yi pondered this, shaking his head then nodding. "Not exactly... but also yes, I suppose. Overall, this isn't really my affair—you'll need to reach an agreement with Tan'er."

"Then... what is this?"

"A chance encounter... a momentary whim... call it whatever you like." Ning Yi smiled. "You've already figured it out anyway. Since you came today and I happen to be free, and this is what's on your mind, there's no harm in telling you. Actually, knowing earlier gives you time to consider and prepare, which I think would be better for the Su family."

He raised his cup for another sip as Wu Qilong steadied his emotions and leaned closer to the table. "Then tell me, Ning Liheng—what do you think would be best?"

"Give Tan'er whatever she wants." Ning Yi shook his head. "It'll save everyone a lot of trouble."

"How am I supposed to know what your wife wants?"

"Sincerity¹. Everything you can offer that the Su family's First Branch can swallow..."

"...Everything!?"

"Yes, everything."

They stared at each other for a moment—Wu Qilong's smile cold and mocking while Ning Yi's expression remained calm and earnest as a three-year-old child's—before Wu Qilong drew another deep breath. "Very well, Brother Ning. Why don't you give me some examples of what this 'everything' entails?"

"Everything means everything. The territories where the Su family has already positioned themselves recently—let's see... Luzhou, Shouzhou, Guangzhou, Hezhou, Xuanzhou..." Ning Yi counted on his fingers as if reciting a shopping list. "The business shares you can cede in these places, some conveniently located properties, various fabric formulas—I've heard Tan'er mention several, including one with especially tight stitching that's apparently a Wu family secret technique. After all, some shares and businesses need proper coordination for smooth transfer, and then..."

Before he could finish, Wu Qilong's palm slammed down on the table. "Ning Liheng! Do you understand what you're saying!? You're sitting here, to my face, demanding half the Wu family!?"

"It shouldn't be half." Ning Yi glanced at him. "The Su family has had its own upheavals recently. The Wu family has deep foundations—the First Branch alone couldn't digest half of it. They'll just take what they can manage. Even a third of the Wu family would be approaching their limit. With that third, though they'd lose the Imperial Merchant status, it would prove Tan'er's qualifications to lead the Su family. But the most important thing isn't even this—you should understand what she's truly angry about, what the Su family cares about most. When the time comes, you must be sincere. You'll only have one chance..."

"In! Your! Dreams!" Wu Qilong ground out through clenched teeth. "You really think you've got the Wu family cornered over this trivial matter? After all these years, my Wu family has..."

"Human nature—everyone thinks that way at first." Ning Yi cut him off mildly. "Dreaming, daydreaming, delusions of grandeur, trying to swallow an elephant whole—who would simply hand over such things? That's why I said we can speak plainly now. Actually, after a month and a half, everything that needed doing is mostly done. The fading fabric..."

"We'll request an extension from the Imperial Textile Bureau. Let's see how long your Su family can last."

"Yes, an extension..."

"So go ahead, spread your rumors about our fabric fading. Spread them all you want—even if there's temporary impact, nothing can be confirmed until the Wu family delivers. What can you possibly do?"

Ning Yi looked at him and sighed. "Well, what's meant to come will come. Actually, we'd prefer not to wait ten more days, because once word spreads, other cloth merchants will have time to prepare. Taking what we want then becomes more troublesome—it's better if you bring it to us... Oh, by the way, Manager Liao has already gone to the capital. I wonder if you knew that..."

"You..."

"I heard he used to travel back and forth to the capital frequently, knows several major cloth merchants there, has many connections. He took some silver with him this time—the First Branch has little left, so he took it all. The main goal is to frame the Wu family for deceiving the emperor²."

Through Wu Qilong's twisted expression, Ning Yi smiled and shook his head. "I know what you're thinking. Fabric fading—at worst it's deceiving the emperor, but His Majesty has always been lenient these years. Orders for confiscation and execution³ won't come easily. The Su family has connections, the Wu family has connections too, and neither are particularly strong. With both sides maneuvering, it depends on the mood above. Still, it's leverage. Throwing around tens or hundreds of thousands of taels will definitely have an effect. If the Wu family admits guilt, the outcome might be lighter. Actually... if you show sincerity, Tan'er would have Manager Liao speak on the Wu family's behalf too. The penalties won't be light, but confiscation and execution is too extreme after all..."

Wu Qilong laughed through gritted teeth. "Even you know charges of deceiving the emperor aren't handed down lightly..."

"But war is coming, Qilong," Ning Yi said, reaching over with the teapot to fill Wu Qilong's cup. "Throughout history, what does war require most? Money. Everyone sees business opportunities in war—haven't you considered this point? The Wu Dynasty may claim prosperity, but the state treasury is perpetually empty, and when troops move, provisions must go first—once military action begins, how much silver will it take to fill that bottomless pit? There's never enough. We don't know when fighting will start, but all decisions will be made beforehand. Has anyone confirmed whether decisions have been made yet?"

He looked at Wu Qilong. "If not, there's still room to maneuver. But then again... might they decide tomorrow? Or by month's end? Next month? Feel free to drag things out. Once those decisions are made, His Majesty, the ministers, and every level of the Three Departments and Six Ministries will be desperate for funds. As for your Wu family—with such deep foundations, how much silver can you produce? I'm not entirely clear on these matters, but surely it's substantial? Several million taels? Over ten million? Am I overestimating... What perfect timing..."

Wu Qilong's face had gone completely pale. Ning Yi sighed, his voice becoming gentle and almost comforting as he delivered the final blow: "Don't overthink it. Once this escalates, your Wu family will definitely face confiscation and execution. There's no escape..."

Footnotes:

¹ Sincerity (诚意 chengyi): In Chinese business culture, this goes beyond mere honesty to encompass genuine good faith, substantial gestures, and tangible commitments that demonstrate one's serious intent to resolve matters.

² Crime of deceiving the emperor (欺君之罪 qi jun zhi zui): One of the most serious offenses in imperial China, encompassing any form of fraud, false reporting, or deception involving imperial interests. For merchants dealing with the imperial court, providing substandard goods could be construed as this capital crime.

³ Family extermination (抄家灭族 chaojia miezu): The ultimate punishment in imperial China, involving the execution of the entire family line, confiscation of all property, and erasure of the family name. This collective punishment extended to multiple generations and branches of the family.
