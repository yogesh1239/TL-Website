News of the assassination attempt on Ning Yi near the side gate didn't spread far. After some time passed, several household guards arrived along with Xing'er—currently the easiest to find—and secured the small courtyard.

The clan assembly had just concluded tonight when this sensitive incident immediately occurred. With the instigators behind the assassination—or rather, kidnapping attempt—still unclear, the matter couldn't be publicized and had to be handled internally by the First Branch's forces. By the time Xing'er arrived, Ning Yi had already led several guards to search the nearby areas and caught a suspicious newly hired coachman.

The manager overseeing this area had been drinking and apparently didn't know what happened at the clan meeting. Seeing Ning Yi leading people over, he tried to obstruct them without understanding the situation. Xing'er happened to arrive just then, and seeing Ning Yi was safe, she finally breathed a sigh of relief and bowed to him. Then she furrowed her delicate brows and coldly told the manager she was going to report him to the Head Steward. The manager sobered up instantly and apologized profusely.

Though only seventeen years old with a pretty face, Xing'er had always held the position of eldest sister among the three maids, her personality strong and forceful. When dealing with those she could afford to offend, she would always imitate Su Tan'er's manner with a few cold sentences. If it was someone from another branch with similar status whom she couldn't afford to offend but who was being unreasonable, she would still argue with them relentlessly for quite some time. Several times she'd apparently nearly been punished for standing up for the First Branch's guards and maids. Over time, others had grown familiar with this maid's stubborn toughness. Ning Yi found her anger today rather amusing, but the urgent matter at hand was naturally to consider issues related to this kidnapping.

"This was premeditated, so it's hard to say who's really behind it. I'm fine for now, but do you know where Tan'er went?"

Whether it was the Second Branch, Third Branch, the Xue family or Wu family planning something, the main target would always be Su Tan'er. Ning Yi had originally thought everything was settled, never expecting such complications now. He immediately worried about his wife. Hearing him mention this, Xing'er seemed to remember something.

"Miss... Miss should be fine, but I don't know where she is right now..."

"Hm?" Ning Yi frowned. "What's going on?"

"Miss went to handle the matter of the assassination attempt on Old Master. Juan'er should know—I'll go get her."

Xing'er's expression was awkward. She stuck out her tongue and ran off. Ning Yi felt puzzled while in the adjacent room, guards were still interrogating the four captured infiltrators. A moment later, Juan'er came running over, out of breath. "Is Young Master all right?" She'd clearly heard from Xing'er about the assassination attempt. From the neighboring room came the agonized screams of the assassins.

Tonight Chan'er, Juan'er, and Xing'er all had responsibilities. Ning Yi had originally thought that given the violent nature of this situation, Xing'er might have higher tolerance for such things, but now he discovered that despite hearing the screams from inside, Juan'er showed little discomfort. She merely glanced toward the room with furrowed brows before anxiously asking whether Ning Yi was injured. After he explained what had happened and his concerns, Juan'er hesitated briefly before sharing what she knew.

"Miss went to Ten Steps Ridge with Old Master and the others to handle the assassination attempt on him. They're planning to find those responsible and eliminate them completely. They even brought in Alliance Master Cheng from the Hundred Blades Alliance—there are many people there, so it should be safe... Miss only learned today that the mastermind behind the original assassination on Old Master was... was Manager Xi, Xi Junyu. He has someone backing him..."

When mentioning Xi Junyu's name, Juan'er—who'd been keeping her head slightly lowered—stole a glance at Ning Yi. Their eyes met, and she quickly dropped her gaze and pressed her lips together. Among the three sisters, Chan'er had the gentlest temperament, Xing'er was the most forthright, while Juan'er remained the quietest and most reserved. Though she handled tasks competently, she sometimes gave the impression of timidity and shyness in daily life. Yet these matters were unknown to Xing'er, while Juan'er apparently had full knowledge of them.

Ning Yi regarded her with the look one might give a secret agent before frowning and asking about this Hundred Blades Alliance she'd mentioned. It turned out this organization was one of Jiangning's major gangs, keeping a relatively low profile in ordinary times while possessing considerable strength. Alliance Master Cheng Lie shared deep friendship with Su Boyong, representing the greatest underworld force the Su family could mobilize.

"This whole thing was actually arranged jointly by Old Master and Miss. Miss hasn't dealt much with these matters before, and Juan'er doesn't know much either. We didn't tell you because we were afraid you'd worry..."

Juan'er offered her explanation, and Ning Yi gradually understood the situation. Su Boyong wasn't someone without a temper—after this assassination left him paralyzed, revenge was naturally required. With Su Tan'er preparing to take over household management, she needed to begin engaging with more of these matters as well. Juan'er's tone when mentioning Xi Junyu carried intriguing undertones, though Ning Yi could roughly guess some of the reasons. What he hadn't previously considered was that the assassination had actually been orchestrated by one of the family's own managers.

"Someone backing him." Ning Yi nodded. "Who?"

"Uh, that's still unclear..."

"Uncle Cheng, who exactly are these people?" In the courtyard at Ten Steps Ridge, Su Tan'er posed the same question to someone beside her.

Outside the courtyard, fighting continued, though with limited participants obscuring any clear view of the overall battle situation. A Hundred Blades Alliance member had just crashed through the gate covered in blood, though both sides remained in the probing stages. Several wounded now lay within the courtyard, bleeding and groaning—a scene that would prove extremely distressing for most women. Su Tan'er stood there with unchanged expression, though one hand secretly gripped the corner of her robe. This wasn't her first exposure to such violence. Years ago when leaving Jiangning, their party had encountered mountain bandits, and when negotiations for passage failed, the resulting battle had shown her rivers of blood. Still, regardless of past experience, this type of scene could never become comfortable.

Beside her stood the middle-aged man who'd spoken with her father earlier—a tall, powerfully built figure in his early forties with half-graying beard and hair. His appearance resembled a lion or tiger, radiating steady, authoritative presence, a great saber resting at his side. This was Cheng Lie, Alliance Master of the Hundred Blades Alliance, currently turning his head to listen to sounds from outside.

"Still hard to say. They have quite a few people, and we didn't manage to set up the ambush properly at the start. Now it's just going to be a hard fight. Hmph, they're not Jiangning locals."

"Not locals?"

"Unfamiliar faces, willing to fight hard. Judging by their style, they're outsiders who came here before—probably one of several groups of desperate types who arrived during the flooding."

Jiangning's prosperity naturally attracted those working the underworld and darker paths, with outsiders arriving each year to struggle for territory. During disasters and calamities, even more of these desperate souls appeared—people who'd lost everything, then descended on Jiangning with crossing-river-dragon momentum. For numerous small gangs and minor forces, such individuals posed enormous threats, as those already pushed to starvation could be terrifyingly destructive when they stopped caring about their lives. But for an organization like the Hundred Blades Alliance, the impact remained minimal, and Cheng Lie merely tilted his head slightly.

"Niece, don't worry. Strong dragons don't suppress local snakes. These wild-road types aren't worth concern. They think they have numbers tonight, but we'll let them die clean and complete. This year's outsiders trying to make a living in Jiangning—we know every batch. Once we identify which group they are, those who didn't come tonight won't be leaving Jiangning alive either. This matter... mm..."

Cheng Lie's words carried steady authority and absolute confidence—naturally, he possessed the strength to back it up—though midway through his speech, he seemed to realize he was talking to a young niece and hesitated before simply waving his hand. "Stop wasting time with them. Attack!"

Throughout the courtyard's eaves and shadows stood positioned figures invisible from outside, transforming the small yard into a heavily fortified bunker. At his gesture, someone beside him immediately opened a bamboo tube. The instant fireworks burst skyward, shouts erupted from outside. "Kill—"

That brief moment's call received sudden tidal response, the roar filling the night sky. "Kill—"

"Kill them—"

Hundred Blades Alliance members stationed throughout Ten Steps Ridge launched their coordinated assault like furious waves sweeping toward the forest surrounding the small courtyard. Close combat erupted as the outsiders previously lurking nearby were finally forced into the open, fighting intensifying instantly. From the courtyard, six or seven figures charged out through the gate. Within the massive chaos, Su Tan'er suddenly remembered something and called out a question to Cheng Lie.

"What?" Cheng Lie hadn't heard clearly and shouted his question.

"Uncle Cheng! I wanted to ask—among these outsiders, are there any from the Ezhou region?" Su Tan'er raised her voice.

"Ezhou?"

"Yes! I remember now—the person who framed Father originally came from Ezhou!"

"They come from all over, though as for Ezhou... there is one group with many from around Ezhou. The leader's called Ou Peng... Ah! The roof!"

Cheng Lie's words cut off abruptly as he roared and spun around, his left hand seizing the great saber while his right grabbed an iron ring sitting nearby and hurled it howling toward the roof above the room where Xi Junyu was being held. A human silhouette had somehow already reached that rooftop. The iron ring thundered through the thatched roof, scattering grass as the figure reacted instantly, reaching behind to draw what proved to be another great saber and slashing down hard at the projectile.

CLANG—

Sparks flashed against the night sky as the ring was smashed aside. The impact sent the figure staggering several steps backward across the roof, trampling through thatch before tumbling into the room below.

"Ah—"

"Die!"

Sudden chaos erupted inside. Su Tan'er had stepped outside moments earlier when Su Boyong had a guard wheel him into that room—what conversation he'd had with Xi Junyu remained unknown. He was just emerging now, glancing back once. Cheng Lie's massive form had already charged toward the room, smashing through half a window frame as he crashed inside. The chamber held only a single oil lamp, and human shapes dissolved into chaotic tangle—CLANG CLANG CLANG CLANG! Blade-light whirled, sparks erupting constantly with roars of "Ah—" as tables, chairs, and wooden racks were hacked apart, fragments flying through the air illuminated by firelight.

CRASH—

A figure burst from the window—the tall, lean Hundred Blades Alliance member who'd been restraining Xi Junyu and guarding the room. Though a skilled fighter himself, he'd clearly been beaten out, rolling several times across the ground before coughing blood and staggering back to his feet. Su Tan'er had started toward her father's position when Su Boyong waved her off, signaling it was unnecessary since Guard Geng had already emerged with drawn blade. Now three remained inside the room: Xi Junyu, Cheng Lie, and the infiltrator who'd been knocked from the roof. Fighting continued, sparks flying spectacularly as it remained unclear whether Xi Junyu had been caught in the violence and cut down.

"Die!" From within the room, Cheng Lie suddenly bellowed. A tremendous crash followed as another figure was cleaved through the window. This man gripped a steel blade, half his body already drenched in blood, his headwrap knocked away in his utterly disheveled state—clearly the infiltrator. Scrambling up from the ground, he roared and charged toward Su Tan'er's position like a madman. The tall, lean fighter shifted several steps to intercept, swinging his short blade to block. Their weapons clashed, and the infiltrator howled while spinning his great saber rapidly in hand—CLANG CLANG CLANG CLANG—a cascade of sparks exploded forth. This time the tall fighter had gained experience, and after two exchanges, he forced the man back.

Hundred Blades Alliance members from the eaves and shadows closed in from all directions.

"Go!" Cheng Lie's shout came from inside the room. Xi Junyu was kicked stumbling through the doorway, and before he could regain his footing, a great saber settled firmly against his neck. Cheng Lie emerged from the entrance gripping his blade single-handed, fixing his gaze on the blood-soaked swordsman now surrounded in the courtyard. "Who are you?"

The half-blood-drenched blade wielder swept hair from his face and bit out through clenched teeth, "Grandfather's name is Ma Lin!"

"Good. Kill him."

Cheng Lie wasted no words, tilting his head slightly. Near the courtyard gate, fighting suddenly intensified—the whistling sound of something cutting through air preceded two Hundred Blades Alliance members being struck and sent flying. Simultaneously two others were forced to scatter back. Cheng Lie's blade flashed, deflecting an incoming projectile before settling smoothly back against Xi Junyu's throat. At the gate appeared another tall, powerfully built man wielding an iron spear. He stepped across the threshold and halted there, surveying the courtyard situation—his trapped companion in the center, and... the yard full of enemies.

"I've seen you before. Ou Peng, so it really is your group." Cheng Lie shook his head. "You outsiders have been playing quite happily in Jiangning."

"Just trying to make a living." The tall man raised his spear. "Anyone who blocks my meal, I kill their whole family. I know your surname is Cheng. Will you let me pass or not?"

Cheng Lie's brows drew together before his expression twisted into something faintly savage, each word deliberate. "I think... that's not happening."

Throughout the courtyard, every Hundred Blades Alliance member understood their leader had truly lost his temper, preparing to surge toward Ou Peng for the kill. Ou Peng slowly backed across the threshold, and after a brief moment, suddenly spun and sprinted off to one side.

"Kill these suicidal fools!"

Face darkening, Cheng Lie's great saber flickered once—CRACK—striking Xi Junyu to the ground, nearly half his face already swollen. Gripping his blade, Cheng Lie advanced toward Ma Lin in the courtyard center while some members began charging outside in pursuit, joining those already there to hunt down Ou Peng. In that instant, throughout the Ten Steps Ridge area, the sounds of slaughter and deadly combat intensified until they practically boiled over!

At the same moment, two carriages rolled through the Su family's side gate, racing toward the outskirts of the city...
