Like the end of a dance when the music gradually fades, the night sky deepened while the wind stirred the clouds that had been hovering over the city, slowly unfurling the oppressive atmosphere strand by strand. The gathering in the Su family clan assembly hall had finally reached its conclusion, and though the evening's events had twisted and turned through multiple reversals, most of the Su family members still hadn't managed to fully digest or accept the reality before them. Yet regardless of their personal feelings, after months of enormous turmoil caused by the Imperial Merchant incident, the curtain hiding the mastermind behind it all had finally been pulled back, revealing the most unexpected outcome imaginable.

Though it would still take time for many people to truly process everything and unravel the threads one by one to piece together the true outline of what had happened, even this single corner that had been revealed tonight was enough to leave everyone watching these events utterly astounded. The series of schemes and counter-schemes, the silent planning behind the scenes, the heavy expectations that had come to nothing—especially the names of those who had orchestrated everything: Su Yu, Su Tan'er, and most astonishing of all, Ning Liheng, who had always seemed to stand outside the entire situation. His involvement behind the scenes was something no one had ever anticipated.

The members of the First, Second, and Third Branches, along with everyone else inside and outside the assembly hall, were still engaged in animated discussions as they tried to come to terms with this reversal of fortune. Perhaps the only conclusion one could draw was admiration for Su Yu's commanding presence—this old man who had remained relatively silent for the past four months yet still held enormous control over the family. Once the truth was revealed and the results of the Imperial Merchant incident laid bare in undeniable terms, he had managed to persuade the surrounding clan elders to accept this reality, and by extension, force everyone in the family to accept Su Tan'er's ascension to power.

In truth, had the family's situation over these past few months not been so dire, these elders wouldn't have come out to negotiate with Su Yu at all, but now that they could see Su Yu's control over the entire situation remained as solid as ever, after their initial shock, they naturally accepted the current state of affairs—after all, this wasn't the first time Su Yu's leadership had guided the family through various difficulties.

"Then... regarding letting Tan'er take over the duties that Boyong originally managed, if anyone has any thoughts, you can speak up now."

When Su Yu spoke these words amid the crowd's discussion, riding the momentum of this complete reversal, few people dared to raise any objections. Several clan elders subsequently expressed their support: "Given such achievements, there's nothing anyone can say about Tan'er taking on these responsibilities."

The matter gradually settled, the original crisis transformed into an opportunity, and this clan assembly that had been called to address the crisis no longer had much left to discuss. While the elders sat above speaking about the aftermath, the people in the assembly hall harbored hearts full of complex, indescribable emotions. Su Zhongkan, Su Yunfang, and others kept glancing between Su Tan'er, who had returned to her seat, and their father sitting above, while on another side, Su Yunsong let out a long sigh as he looked at Su Tan'er, thinking of that scholar his daughter had mentioned, the one who supposedly spent all day wandering around idly.

"Zhongkan, Yunfang, after this concludes, come to my place for a bit."

As things drew to a close, Su Yu came over and spoke to the two of them, and the brothers nodded their agreement—after all their maneuvering over these past months, they had become complete losers. But their father's authority remained absolute, and even if they felt resentful about it, getting angry wouldn't solve anything.

On the other side, feeling all those shocked gazes and hearing the murmurs of discussion, Su Tan'er—who had finally laid all her cards on the table and completely won this round—found herself in surprisingly poor spirits.

It wasn't any lingering tenderness after a great victory, nor was she dwelling on how much her father had sacrificed by becoming paralyzed. When she had stepped forward carrying that box, her heart had pounded with the excitement and anticipation of a queen being crowned, her entire heart trembling as she pulled out those banknotes and contracts. But now, this woman in her light blue dress occasionally glanced toward the assembly hall entrance, suddenly unsure how she would walk through those doors.

She had something weighing on her mind, something that could normally be considered a small matter, but in this moment it had somehow completely filled the woman's thoughts, making her unable to taste even a hint of the sweetness that should have come with success.

Finally, the elders announced the meeting's conclusion, and people stood up, conversing and discussing, casting their gazes toward each of the key participants. Su Tan'er hesitated for a moment before standing as well, following her grandfather and father as they walked toward the exit.

As she left through that main door, she glanced around but didn't see the figure she'd been thinking about, which brought her both a measure of relief and another wave of worry. Su Danhong was coming from another direction, but Tan'er didn't notice her as she separated from her father and the others in the corridor, following her grandfather and several elders in another direction.

After turning a corner, her grandfather noticed her and called her over, and two of her grand uncles spoke with her for a while, which she answered politely. When the grand uncles walked away, Tan'er furrowed her brow slightly and looked up at the old man.

"Grandfather, how could you say that..."

"Hmm, say what?" the old man asked with a kind smile.

"About Liheng."

Looking at his granddaughter's expression, Su Yu fell silent for a long moment. "What was wrong with what I said about him?"

"Grandfather, he's my husband. I think... it could be simpler." With people passing by all around them, Su Tan'er frowned. "Besides, he can understand these things. Grandfather, how am I supposed to explain today's events to him?"

The old man sighed. "Liheng married into our Su family, and as his wife, you're the one he should naturally protect, should act as a shield for. This matter was ultimately too intense, and your Second Uncle and Third Uncle will certainly harbor resentment—rather than letting it all fall on you, it's better if Liheng can share some of that burden. Besides, with Boyong's current physical condition, having Liheng standing behind you means you won't be completely isolated. While this may not be entirely fair to Liheng, after all, helping his wife do these things is his duty, his proper role as your husband."

Su Tan'er closed her eyes and said forcefully, "But Grandfather, what you're doing is making the entire Su family watch him. He'll understand that."

She had always had a strong personality, and even when facing the most serious matters, she rarely showed any excessive weakness, especially in front of her grandfather—even during that period when the yellow cloth had faded and she'd felt utterly powerless, she had never shown such a helpless expression but had instead held on until her body gave out and she collapsed from illness. Yet now, after completing something with such enormous implications and securing the First Branch's authority, she was nearly on the verge of tears over this matter.

There were some things she couldn't tell her grandfather, couldn't explain to him that her relationship with her husband had only just reached the level of true husband and wife, couldn't tell him that she and her husband had only just decided to consummate their marriage, couldn't explain how their feelings had developed over these past days or what kind of person her husband truly was. But she knew in her heart that he would certainly hear the implications behind her grandfather's words.

After he had done so much for her, when even her father had said similar things and now her grandfather had grown frightened as well, starting to warn him, starting to make everyone in the Su family watch him—even if there was no real malice in it, how would he feel about all this? And how could she even begin to explain these things to him? No matter how magnanimous he might be, how was she supposed to have that conversation...

The old man watched her for a long time before finally raising his hand to pat her shoulder, then smiled—this smile was somewhat different from usual, carrying both understanding and relief. "So... that's how it is..."

"Grandfather..."

"Zi'an has a fine grandson. When I originally decided to let you run this household, I thought about it for a long time—you certainly had the ability, but I was only worried that you were too strong-willed. A woman who takes charge needs to be stronger than others, but I was afraid that in being so strong, you'd lose the feeling of having a real home, that you'd have no one who truly cared about you. Now, though, I can finally set my mind at ease. When Liheng first married in, I didn't want to treat him as a mere son-in-law because I was worried he wouldn't have much ability to adapt, but this time I spoke up about him because he does have that ability now, and my main concern was indeed for him—but more than that, it was because with this ability, others can't hurt him, and with this ability, he can stand in front of you. Your concern for him is naturally a good thing, and it makes me happy as well, but in my view, he's your husband, and even if he married in, now that he can shoulder these responsibilities, he should bear some of this burden for you. This is also my own selfish wish as your grandfather. As for men in this world, these responsibilities will come pressing down on them regardless, without any room for negotiation—you're his wife, so caring for him more is naturally your duty too. Heh, and it's a good thing. That's what being husband and wife is all about."

The grandfather and granddaughter walked forward together. "As for your brothers, they're all mediocre—none of them could last two or three moves against him if they tried to hurt him. After what happened today, even though you may seem isolated and vulnerable in the business world going forward, anyone who wants to scheme against you will always have to remember who stands behind you. In the future, if you truly care for him, your second child could take the Ning surname—how you handle that is entirely in your hands, and I'll have given Zi'an an explanation... ah, there he is over there."

As he spoke, Su Yu gestured ahead, and Ning Yi was indeed walking toward them from not far away, though he'd been waylaid by one of the grand uncles who seemed to be offering words of encouragement. After the grand uncle left, Su Yu brought Su Tan'er over, then took Su Tan'er's hand and placed it in Ning Yi's. "This granddaughter, I entrust to you."

Ning Yi laughed.

After Su Yu departed, Su Tan'er held Ning Yi's hand in silence for quite a while. "Husband, we... succeeded."

"I'm angry."

"Uh..." Su Tan'er's palm instantly went cold as she roughly understood what Ning Yi was referring to, though for a moment she couldn't find words to respond. Ning Yi glanced around, pulled her forward, and shook his head. "I'm sleeping in a separate room from you tonight."

"..."

"That old man's completely unreasonable."

"..."

"No negotiation. When I say I'm angry, I'm angry."

"..."

"I'm unhappy, so I'm taking it out on his granddaughter."

"..."

"You can cry all you want, but you're still sleeping alone tonight."

"..."

"Hahaha... hey, don't actually cry. You don't need to be like this."

The two had already walked some distance down the corridor to a place where there weren't many people, and Su Tan'er had lifted Ning Yi's sleeve to dab at her face a few times—she really had shed tears, and now in the lamplight, her faint smile mixed with those tears before she regained her usual calm expression. "I wanted to apologize to you on Grandfather's behalf..."

"I reserve the right to pursue this and stay angry." Ning Yi smiled and patted her shoulder. "But for now, you should handle the aftermath. You have a lot to deal with tonight?"

Only then did Su Tan'er relax, nodding. "Mm, there are still some things I need to take care of..."

"Then go quickly."

In the lamplight, Ning Yi smiled and waved his hand, and Su Tan'er stood there watching him for a long while, seeming to hesitate, but eventually she nodded and turned to leave.

Ning Yi watched Su Tan'er's figure recede into the distance—he wasn't entirely clear about Su Tan'er's plans for what came next, though he assumed it was just some cleanup work that didn't require his participation, and Su Tan'er hadn't mentioned much about it in their casual conversations either.

In a courtyard at the front of the Su residence, Su Tan'er changed into a set of inconspicuous men's clothing before boarding a carriage that departed from the area, accompanied by several of the Su family's most trusted guards. They caught up with two carriages ahead and together headed out of the city.

The time was approaching midnight, and in a room near Ten Steps Ridge, the bean-sized flame of an oil lamp flickered slightly. Xi Junyu sat at the table with both hands placed flat on its wooden surface, and there were two other people in the room—one was Guard Geng, who sat directly across from him, while the other was a rather lean man with sharp eyes who leaned against the shadows by the door holding a knife, clearly not someone to be trifled with.

The Su family's business was extensive, and they often escorted goods to other regions, which naturally involved them in some underworld entanglements from time to time, so Xi Junyu knew it was best not to make any rash moves at a time like this.

The passage of time was dull and tedious as Xi Junyu listened to distant bell tolls, trying to guess at possible developments on the Su family's side, though he really didn't have many leads to go on.

"The Su family should have some kind of result by now, shouldn't they?"

He spoke up with this question, but Guard Geng merely shook his head. "I don't know."

"How else could they possibly turn this around?"

"That's Second Miss's affair."

"I really can't figure it out though."

Xi Junyu sighed—he truly disliked this feeling. "I wish someone would come quickly and give me an answer."

After he said this, the room fell silent again, and they could only continue waiting quietly like this as occasional sounds came from outside. Xi Junyu spoke again: "Guard Geng, do you know? I've been with the Su family all these years, watching Tan'er gradually come into contact with all these things—sure, many people taught her, and she was willing to learn about everything, but if we're being honest, she was practically a student I raised myself. Yet now, I understand her less and less each day, and this feeling really isn't pleasant..."

"There will come a time for that."

"But it's still a bit early for..." He glanced around. "For these sorts of things, I really can't make sense of them..."

Silence, stillness, then a voice spoke from outside: "I can't make sense of some things either."

The voice was rather cold, and after a moment, a figure pushed the door open and entered—Su Tan'er wore a black short outfit with a headscarf wrapped around her head, looking clean and efficient, ready for action. She stood in the doorway looking in their direction, though this masculine outfit ultimately made her appear somewhat short and slight. To Xi Junyu, it almost seemed like that small figure from so many years ago when she had first looked at him with that same kind of strange, questioning gaze.

"Brother Junyu... I remember when I was very small and first met you, Father had me call you that. You taught me many things, and this may be the last time I call you this." Su Tan'er walked to the table and sat down, her gaze holding an unfamiliar coldness in its depths—this too was something Xi Junyu had taught her, that in negotiations, you had to establish clear distance. He tried to find some pain in that unfamiliarity, but unfortunately there was only puzzlement. "What was it all for? Why did you go to such lengths?"

"What exactly happened?" Xi Junyu furrowed his brow and looked around. "After tonight's clan assembly turned out like this, you can't possibly think I was behind it?"

"No, I'm asking..." Su Tan'er shook her head quietly, her gaze clear as she looked at him. "Why did you have someone assassinate my father?"
