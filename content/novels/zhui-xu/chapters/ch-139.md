Warm sunlight filtered through as Xi Junyu walked along the corridor beneath the eaves, pausing briefly before entering the adjacent room and nodding to the two men inside.

"Manager Chen, what's the reaction from Rong's side?"

The man he addressed as Manager Chen was named Chen Youhe, who shook his head when Xi Junyu asked. "Rong Li still insists on raising prices—they're planning to go up to four taels and two qian."

"That's straight-up robbery." Xi Junyu frowned, though having grown accustomed to such bad news recently, he showed little emotion beyond sitting down at the table with a darkened expression. "Lü's can negotiate down to four taels."

"We've already discussed it, but Rong Li says that with the Old Master¹ no longer managing affairs and Second Miss about to be removed, the Su family will undergo major upheaval and struggle to recover—they're expecting to operate at a loss, so they absolutely must have this price."

"Second Miss won't be removed!" Xi Junyu paused before continuing with absolute certainty. "Su Yu isn't that short-sighted—even if he has to struggle, he'll help prop up the main branch, and with Second Miss's abilities, she'll rise again sooner or later!"

His words were firm and decisive, yet the room fell into silence. Everyone knew their own affairs best, and the current situation was indeed dire—the second and third branches were desperately trying to dismantle the main branch, and even Su Yu seemed somewhat powerless now, unable to suppress so many voices. The Su family's main branch had suffered damage to its reputation around Jiangning over the past month, primarily from suppliers and distributors demanding price increases or higher profit margins. Most were currently stalling, with the Su family stubbornly refusing to budge, asking this side to wait and that side to be patient, but the hidden impact had likely already dragged down the main branch's total profits by twenty percent, with the most frightening aspect being future development problems.

"Damn it... one wrong step leads to another..."

Xi Junyu cursed while holding back his frustration, then shook his head with a dark expression and began handling the documents on the table—shipping invoices and the like. The two managers beside him also wore gloomy expressions, with Chen Youhe shaking his head: "If not for that oversight during the imperial merchant period..."

No one continued, as they all knew what this referred to: a narrative that had recently begun circulating within the Su family's sphere, mainly arising after they conducted a self-investigation into the situation at the bright yellow fabric workshop during the imperial merchant period.

For instance, while many matters could be handled independently by various managers, quite a few required someone to oversee and control the overall situation. That small workshop responsible for researching new fabrics had originally been under Su Tan'er's personal supervision without issue, but after Su Tan'er fell ill, oversights appeared at certain levels. Looking back now with the Su family's experienced managers' operational expertise, if Ning Yi hadn't been so overly excited and aggressive in operating the bright yellow fabric during that period, many minor details and problems should never have occurred.

If Su Tan'er had personally overseen things during that time, when they chose an aggressive promotional strategy, she would certainly have made adjustments to the small workshop's security measures to accommodate this direction. But after Ning Yi took control, he ultimately didn't understand these minor details—the workshop's policies remained unchanged while Manager Zhou and Manager Bai had to work hard at maintaining secrecy while also cooperating with the high-profile promotion from above, inevitably leaving many traces that allowed the Wu family to exploit vulnerabilities at these most fundamental strategic levels.

The thought was infuriating—they had done everything right, yet ultimately lost because of that greenhorn. Experience, all the problems came down to experience—at that time, if any other manager had taken control of the overall situation, such problems likely wouldn't have occurred.

And that scholar had the nerve to write indignantly about "old friends still grip their swords"²—how embarrassing...

Previously, everyone had mostly ignored Ning Yi, feeling that if they won it certainly wouldn't involve him, and if they lost there was nothing to be done about it. But such narratives had only become clear in the past two weeks, and consequently, rhetoric against Ning Yi within the Su family had intensified recently. Manager Xi had originally been relatively cordial, saying it was understandable, but at this moment he finally felt frustrated too—in Manager Chen and the others' eyes, Xi Junyu's slight loss of composure at this time stemmed from this.

As the room fell quiet, filled only with the rustling of paperwork, Xi Junyu glanced aside, his previously angry gaze now calm.

Everything was probably almost ready—the clan assembly would begin within half a month, and when Tan'er was removed from her position, that would be the best time to let such rhetoric reach its peak. If it were people from the second and third branches making such accusations, it might actually trigger her combative spirit, but if those speaking were consistently people close to her, in her dejected state she would inevitably end up redirecting her anger. That was just human nature.

After the imperial merchant affair, the Su family had exploded into chaos with all manner of disorder and rhetoric. No one knew that all these narratives about Ning Yi had been promoted and guided by him from behind the scenes—there was no need to hire people to spread propaganda, just casually mention a few thought-provoking comments during conversations in certain settings, letting them think and deduce for themselves. On the other hand, increasing everyone's feeling of "we did nothing wrong yet still lost," amplifying that sense of grievance and frustration... From beginning to end, he had remained an outside observer in the role of a rational person, even helping to speak up for Ning Yi, though of course, these words ultimately only produced a contrary effect.

Everything had been laid out perfectly, for he was skilled in such matters. The more ideal situation would have been for Ning Yi to finally lose control and act out a few times at home, but unfortunately that scholar was indeed very patient. Facing various provocations, knowing that explanations were useless, he said nothing and even put on a leisurely attitude. But no matter—he had already begun hinting to these people in the household: that fellow feels no guilt whatsoever.

As he casually pondered these thoughts, Wu Qilong's face suddenly flashed through his mind, making him frown.

"Manager Chen, how's the Wu family been lately? I heard they've had some problems."

"Not very clear—I heard one of their managers worked himself sick, Qin Ye I think, we dealt with him a couple years ago." Chen Youhe looked up. "Some people are gossiping that the Wu family will have this problem or that problem—probably the Xue family spreading rumors from behind. The imperial merchant deadline is approaching, and the Wu family will be busy with the annual tribute cloth next. The Xue family probably wants to take advantage."

"Oh."

The Su family certainly didn't like the Wu family right now, but as defeated opponents who had just been schemed against by them, they absolutely didn't want to constantly hear gossip about the Wu family either. Chen Youhe spoke casually, and Xi Junyu simply nodded, then thought—what could possibly happen to the Wu family... Never mind them, whatever was happening to the Wu family now was no longer on the same level as the Su family; they would drift further and further apart. Let them handle whatever it was—right now, he just needed to finish his own business.

At any rate, everything was going smoothly.

He glanced at the afternoon sunlight in the courtyard outside the window—the atmosphere was peaceful yet held a vague sense of lifelessness. Less than half a month remained until the Su family clan assembly when everything would be settled. Those belonging to the main branch were all somewhat dejected now, doing their human best while leaving fate unknown. Perhaps only he knew his own position and purpose with such clarity, the only one among them with a true destination.

So he smiled, as relaxed as that patch of sunlight.

The quiet afternoon room seemed somewhat dim with doors and windows closed. The room was filled with medicinal scents, and at this moment before the heavily covered sickbed, an elegantly beautiful woman held a porridge bowl in her hands, blowing on the spoon she raised before bringing it to the patient's mouth.

"...the clan assembly... I heard Seventh Granduncle has also decided to have me step down; Second Uncle finally convinced him... Third Uncle's side is busy poaching people, saying these past few days what a pity that Manager Liao has been sent away... Rong's wants to raise prices, Lü's too, such a hassle—in a while they'll have to run back and forth again..."

Feeding the patient porridge spoonful by spoonful while slowly speaking of household matters, the woman at the bedside actually looked somewhat weary herself, though her lips carried a faint smile that held a touch of irony amid the fatigue. Reaching up to smooth her hair, she tilted her head to look at her father on the bed, a trace of bitterness in her heart. She had only come to visit the sick and hadn't wanted to speak of these troublesome matters, but thinking back, the exchanges between father and daughter had always been like this—aside from purely social pleasantries, it seemed there was only this.

Two and a half months had passed since Su Boyong's attack, during which time his paralysis had been confirmed—both legs had lost feeling, and his left hand's movement was also somewhat affected. Physically weak and unable to leave his bed, he required daily changes of medicine, and his spirit seemed to have suffered as well, leaving him frequently in foul moods. Su Tan'er visited daily, but father and daughter mostly discussed these matters, often just exchanging formal greetings when her mother and concubines were present. At this moment, Su Tan'er fell silent for a bit.

"Today on the street, I saw many selling tangerines—Father used to... like eating them, right... Um, there's not much wind today, Father might want to open the windows a bit... Husband³ says opening them is better, um... something about air circulation... then sunlight coming in will improve one's mood..."

These were all the heartfelt words she could find to say, and when mentioning Ning Yi, she smiled more happily. Su Boyong nodded, then shook his head: "Better... not open them, it's cold... feeling cold... Also, Tan'er, what has Liheng been doing lately?"

Su Tan'er smiled faintly: "Doesn't Husband come to see you every day too? Lately he's just been teaching those children, going out for walks and leisure each day..." Su Tan'er's smile had actually already faded as she spoke lightly of Ning Yi's recent activities. She understood in her heart why her father asked these things—previously from this sickbed, her father had sighed several times: "I can't see through him." It was actually a reminder to her.

These matters couldn't be explained clearly—just how capable Husband was and what he could accomplish, Su Tan'er was curious about too, but her father's thoughts differed from hers. After chatting a bit more, she called out the door for her father's maid Xiuhe to come in, handed her the porridge bowl, then began taking her leave.

This past month and a half, she had many things to do daily—stabilizing the current situation wasn't easy and she had expended great effort, but unlike before the imperial merchant affair when she had been directionless, she now felt settled in her heart while working, mentally well though physically busy. After leaving, her accompanying maid Juan'er came to greet her, able to see that her mistress's mood wasn't particularly good.

There were still places to visit and matters to handle in the afternoon. Mistress and maid left the courtyard but heard urgent voices from the neighboring courtyard.

"Already like this, what are you still thinking about..."

"It's not that simple, Sister..."

"I've already heard everything. But the First Branch isn't hers alone—whatever she wants to do, everyone goes along? It's already like this now..."

"I know..."

"If it weren't for that Ning Yi not understanding anything, sticking his nose in..."

These were the vague sounds of conversation, and when two figures appeared at that doorway, they suddenly froze. Su Tan'er performed a dignified courtesy: "Second Mother⁴, Uncle Gu..."

"Uh, Tan'er, you came... came to see the master..."

"Second Miss."

The speakers were the household's Second Concubine and her brother, currently also one of the main branch's managers. After awkwardly exchanging greetings, both parties went their separate ways. Once they reached a deserted area, Juan'er pursed her lips: "Miss, Second Madam and Manager Gu are discussing surrender..."

"I know," Su Tan'er said with a faint smile, "and it's not just them..."

Recently with all these matters in the main branch, there had actually been considerable internal disagreement. Some people felt the main branch definitely couldn't obtain the family head position—better not to fight further and preserve whatever interests originally belonged to them. Even if Su Zhongkan or Su Yunfang led the family in the future, they surely wouldn't drive them out; the more that could be retained, the better everyone's future days would be. The two concubines, even her own mother, seemed to hold this attitude. Of course, if Su Tan'er really wanted to have an open discussion, they would likely claim "absolutely not" and "we women don't concern ourselves with business matters."

From another perspective, they were actually acting for the main branch's good, for Su Boyong's good, for their own good—just somewhat short-sighted and disheartening, currently using their limited mindset suited only for household politics to consider these matters.

"Juan'er, it's good you weren't born into a great family..."

"Hm?" Juan'er blinked.

"This household has no human warmth."

Su Tan'er smiled faintly while Juan'er pursed her lips—as a maid, it wasn't appropriate for her to comment. The two paused on the small bridge by the pond near the garden, Su Tan'er looking at her reflection in the water while tidying her hair, murmuring: "Actually, I don't have much human warmth either..."

"No, Miss and Master⁵ are both very good people."

"With Father, I can't be close either... Only wronging Husband. Mm, he's been outside during the day recently?"

"Yes, some people at home speak very harshly. Master doesn't bother arguing with them but surely doesn't like hearing it either... Last time Chan'er was even made to cry."

Su Tan'er frowned as a flash of severity crossed her eyes, but after a long moment she took a deep breath and smiled at Juan'er: "You've recorded all the names?"

"Uh..." Juan'er froze slightly, then lowered her head. "How did Miss know?"

"Because I'm the elder sister."

Su Tan'er stated matter-of-factly while Juan'er looked embarrassed, lowering her head with an awkward smile: "Chan'er and I have recorded them all—last time... last time we were secretly writing names at home and Master saw us."

"Oh? What did Husband say?"

"Said we were petty."

"Heh." Su Tan'er burst out laughing before restraining her smile. "Record them well and show me. Don't tell Husband."

"Mm." Juan'er nodded, and having received this golden immunity, she then put on a stern face to complain: "Miss, actually these past few days they've been speaking more and more excessively—some even telling Master to leave the Su family himself... When will we resolve things... Actually Master is the most wronged one, and sometimes even Juan'er can't stand listening anymore..."

"Husband, he..."

Su Tan'er paused, recalling these past two months of events, especially after the imperial merchant annual meeting—her own busyness was actually secondary to Ning Yi's calm composure amid everyone's abuse and accusations. It wasn't just Juan'er, Chan'er, and Xing'er who couldn't bear it; she herself wanted to stand up and curse every time, and several times it was Ning Yi who patted her shoulder to send her back. Only she could clearly feel the power, steadfastness, and determination concealed behind that casual figure, and with each incident, she felt more strongly that she had wronged her husband. At the beginning of their marriage, she had thought these were all things she would shoulder herself and had worked hard to achieve that, but in the end, it was still that figure who stepped forward to casually block it all.

"It won't drag on much longer—the information has already started being released, and if the Wu family's reaction is normal, roughly..." She looked around at the scenery. "Roughly these next few days we can start laying our cards on the table..."

After saying this, her heart seemed somewhat relieved as she sighed softly: "Such a long two and a half months..." "After things are finished, after they're finished..." Thinking of something, a blush suddenly flashed across her face. She lowered her head to look at her reflection in the water, then unconsciously raised her hand to arrange her hair, tilting her face to examine herself in the water. Indeed, she'd have to wait until after everything was finished—she was so haggard now, unworthy of him like this...

She smiled at herself in the water, then released her hair and strode forward.

"Let's go—time to prepare for the showdown."

In the afternoon sunlight, the woman's confident and carefree steps stood out, with the little maid happily following behind. On this fourteenth day of the tenth lunar month in the eighth year of Jinghan, the decision for a showdown had been made, though naturally there would still be a few days' wait after going through the procedures. But in reality, on this very afternoon, certain events had already occurred—events that seemed quite natural but upon later reflection might strike one as somewhat abrupt.

This afternoon in a teahouse, Ning Yi perhaps on a whim casually tied up loose ends for a small matter, though of course what seemed a small matter to him might be unimaginably significant to others.

A day earlier, Ning Yi had encountered Wu Qilong on the street—the meeting appeared coincidental, and both parties exchanged casual greetings. In the month and a half since, this might not have been their first encounter but was indeed their first greeting. Wu Qilong was chatting with a cloth merchant about something when he saw Ning Yi and cupped his hands from afar: "Brother Ning, have you been well?" Ning Yi returned the courtesy: "Quite well." They then went their separate ways, and at the time Ning Yi hadn't bothered taking the matter to heart.

Then today, after finishing class he went to East Market to buy an old book—probably brought in by foreign merchants from the western Arab regions, containing many diagrams likely related to alchemy and such knowledge. Ning Yi bought it casually, deciding to find someone to translate it later. After purchasing the book, he went to a teahouse where he ordered tea, asked for paper and a brush, and began studying the diagrams, guessing at their meaning as a way to recall past knowledge. While bent over writing and drawing, a figure approached beside him.

"Brother Ning, what a coincidence. What's this?"

Ning Yi looked up—indeed a coincidence, it was Wu Qilong, so he nodded in greeting. However, Wu Qilong then sat down on the other side of the tea table, prompting Ning Yi to casually introduce it.

"Should be from the Arab region—this is Persian script from a bit north, can't read it, but looking at the diagrams, it should involve chemical reactions of several metals... Do you understand chemistry? Uh, similar to alchemy..."

"Brother Ning truly has broad interests. Er... chemical reactions of metals?"

"Purification and transformation of metals and such."

"Oh?" Wu Qilong's expression grew serious. "Then... wouldn't that be extremely useful?"

"Just average—the things discussed here, our blacksmiths and such should mostly already know. It's just that stones from other mountains can polish jade⁶—their way of thinking is different and can serve as reference." Ning Yi said this while raising his head to look at Wu Qilong. "Is Brother Wu waiting for someone?"

"Heh, nothing particular, just wandering idly when I happened to see Brother Ning here and grew curious."

Ning Yi nodded, his gaze steady, and as Wu Qilong smiled, the two men held each other's gaze for a long moment. After a moment, Ning Yi narrowed his eyes, nodded again, and waved to call the waiter: "Add another cup."

He lowered his head again, beginning to copy one of the book's diagrams onto paper while frowning in thought. After the cup arrived and without looking at Wu Qilong, he simply extended his hand: "Brother Wu, please help yourself."

"Thanks." Wu Qilong smiled while pouring himself tea. Ning Yi continued working with those diagrams with his head down, sighing softly as if hearing some other answers Wu Qilong had just given, then murmured in sudden realization: "Oh, the cloth is starting to fade..."

Wu Qilong's smile instantly froze there, his hand also trembling, though he ultimately held the teapot steady and gently set it down. Tilting his head, he stared at Ning Yi with a serious and somewhat fierce gaze, his face twitching slightly. Something surged up from deep within—the sensation of a nightmare finally becoming reality, along with some bewilderment. In his expectations, these conversations shouldn't have been like this, and Ning Yi shouldn't have spoken these things so casually.

Yet everything presented itself just like this, with Ning Yi still writing and drawing as if it were all as ordinary as the snacks and tea on the table. He had uttered the words that confirmed Wu Qilong's nightmare—'Oh, the cloth is starting to fade, no wonder you came to sit down'—in the simple tone of casual conversation.

Just another perfectly ordinary matter.

"So it really... was you who did it..."

Wu Qilong had to exert tremendous effort to suppress his tone from becoming gnashing or trembling.

In the early winter sunlight, Ning Yi set down his brush, raised his head, and met his gaze...

² "Old friends still grip their swords" - A reference to a poem about betrayal among longtime companions
³ Husband (相公) - An affectionate, refined term for one's husband, showing Su Tan'er's genuine feelings
⁴ Second Mother (二娘) - The formal address for a father's concubine, showing the complex family hierarchy
⁵ Master (姑爷) - How servants address the son-in-law who lives with his wife's family
⁶ "Stones from other mountains can polish jade" - A proverb meaning external perspectives can help improve oneself
