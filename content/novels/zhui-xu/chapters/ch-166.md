The sky hadn't yet brightened, and in the lamplight, the bedroom was already bustling with activity.

"...After that news arrived, the last few days have been noisy everywhere, especially those scholarly students—they've been making quite a fuss..."

Wrapped in her blanket, Su Tan'er sat up in bed and reached out to help her husband straighten his clothes. Still within the first month of the new year, the freezing cold outside had naturally cooled the room despite the fire that had warmed it the night before. Wearing only an undergarment, Su Tan'er stuck out from the blanket briefly to help him, then quickly burrowed back in, leaving only her head exposed to talk with Ning Yi.

Though she was already the helmsman of a major business and had managed various affairs for several years, she'd only shared a bed with her husband at the end of last year. While she naturally carried herself with confidence and composure, at this moment wrapped in blankets talking with her husband, she still showed an innocent charm. This was simply how things were in this era—even though Su Tan'er had experienced so much in the business world, in the bedroom, she still seemed like a young maiden.

Ning Yi got out of bed as Chan'er and Juan'er brought in washbasins and face towels. Su Tan'er's bedroom wasn't particularly large to begin with, and with all of Ning Yi's belongings moved in before the new year, the space felt somewhat cramped despite their tidying efforts—but Su Tan'er wasn't particular about such things, and before the new house was built, she said it actually felt warmer this way. It certainly was quite warm indeed. Ning Yi took the face towel Xiao Chan handed him and sat on the edge of the bed making small talk.

"The academy's been discussing these matters the last few days too. Everyone's just anxious, worried that the Jin Kingdom and Liao Kingdom won't actually go to war. Some people are also saying that since Jin is small and weak, even though they've won a few battles, they're ultimately still afraid of Liao. They say our Wu Dynasty isn't being proactive enough—if we were more proactive, Jin would probably become more resolute too... Heh, these people certainly have some ideas..."

"Yesterday at the cloth shop, I heard Teacher Qi from the Qi family mention that Master Zhongheng and the others at Qingyuan Garden want to rally a group of renowned scholars to petition¹ the government. They came to ask about your thoughts, husband..."

"A couple of old teachers did come to the academy looking for me yesterday... But what kind of renowned scholar am I?"

"Husband, you're Jiangning's number one talent. It's natural they'd come looking for you."

"Uncle Chonghua vouched for me—he wanted to show off, so I agreed to sign my name when the time comes. It's just publicity anyway, not much use."

"United in purpose, though²."

"Heh, probably not that united..."

Several people moved about in the bedroom as Ning Yi took the towel to wash—when Xiao Chan tried to reach out and take it from him, he waved her off, leaving the little maid pouting as if he'd stolen her work.

Since information about the Jin and Liao kingdoms' tense standoff had been brewing in the Wu Dynasty for the previous year, when news of the two nations' peace talks arrived now, the public immediately felt disappointed. Many scholarly students thought it regrettable. Some felt the Wu Dynasty should take the initiative to send troops, seizing this opportunity to ally with Jin. In any case, discussions were quite heated, and Ning Yi and Su Tan'er's household had also been affected by it, bringing it up as casual conversation while getting up.

"This concubine actually thinks it would be better if they started fighting a bit later."

"Does the family also have business with Liao?"

"Mm, there's always some. But that's not really the reason..." Su Tan'er nodded in her bedding, then shook her head. "It's just that the Wu family's business was only just taken over and hasn't settled down yet. If war broke out right now, this concubine is afraid complications might easily arise... Of course, I'm just saying..."

As she spoke about this, Juan'er, who'd been organizing the wardrobe on the other side of the room, suddenly laughed. "Speaking of the Wu family, Young Master, Miss, the Wu family must be absolutely furious right now, don't you think?"

This maid was normally well-behaved and quiet, but occasionally had a dark sense of humor. When she said this, everyone in the room couldn't help but laugh—Ning Yi, Su Tan'er, Chan'er... Back when Ning Yi had threatened the Wu family, he'd leveraged the momentum of impending war to frighten them into not daring to gamble with their entire family's lives, and now, not long after the new year with the handover already complete, others only thought the Wu family had cut losses to preserve what remained³—if they knew the actual inside story, they'd probably die laughing.

It was early morning, and the oil lamps rendered warm yellow light in the room while everyone's laughter, though not particularly loud, truly felt warm. After a moment, Su Tan'er brought up something else: "Husband, if you have no other plans, how about our whole family takes a trip around this summer?"

"Summer?"

"Mm, after we finish with the spring silkworms in a few months, we could make a trip toward Suzhou and Hangzhou, touring along the way. As the saying goes, 'Above is heaven, below are Suzhou and Hangzhou⁴.' The family also has some properties in those places, and when it gets hot, we could go there to escape the summer heat."

"Sounds good."

"Then this concubine will start making arrangements."

Ning Yi nodded in agreement, and Su Tan'er became happy. In truth, it was only late January now—if they were going to travel in summer, there was no real need to start planning at this time. However, Su Tan'er had her own reasons: in the past, as she'd managed the First Branch's properties, she would make a trip out each year (initially following her father, later bringing along household guards herself) to familiarize herself with the specific circumstances of properties in various locations, so she wouldn't be working behind closed doors when truly taking over the business later.

Back when she'd married Ning Yi and left home, it had been precisely because she already had experience traveling far from home, though now, of course, the situation was different. This year she'd already taken over the entire First Branch and shouldn't really be running around everywhere. But with her relationship with Ning Yi having progressed to this point, Su Tan'er's heart was divided—on one hand she valued the family business, while on the other she also wanted to manage this marriage well. In the future, she planned to be a good wife and loving mother, and for that, she was even willing to reduce some of the calculating that came with business. She wanted to use the excuse of following past "precedent" to combine business with pleasure⁵ and take a trip together.

Beyond that were some deeper, more complex reasons. Last year, that move against the Wu family had been played beautifully by her and her husband Ning Yi, greatly establishing their reputation and stabilizing her position within the Su family. But after her father recovered from his injuries, he actually still maintained sufficient control over the First Branch. Though Su Tan'er had learned her father's style and, as a woman, excelled at subtle operations, she still lacked his mature steadiness—and Su Boyong, despite his paralysis, wasn't that old and retained a clear mind. After the Xi Junyu affair was exposed, he'd also been the one to order the Hundred Blades Alliance to nearly wipe out the other party completely. The old man's ruthlessness and prestige were ultimately things that couldn't be replaced in the First Branch. Su Tan'er's position had now been established and couldn't be shaken, but over the next several years, it was foreseeable that her father would still need to protect her path for some time.

Though Su Tan'er and Su Boyong maintained a certain emotional distance, there was no real friction when it came to transferring power—Su Boyong handing over authority wasn't the issue, but whether Su Tan'er could handle everything yet. While they had a tacit understanding internally, the external situation was more complex: Su Boyong still controlled the First Branch even as the Old Master had announced Su Tan'er's takeover, which would inevitably create divisions among outsiders. Because of these factors, Su Tan'er made her choice: after the great victory against the Wu family, she would pause, restrain her edge, and consolidate the results first to avoid any mishaps. On another level, she'd already gotten enough of the spotlight—she was only twenty years old, and there was no need to rush forward anxiously. She still needed to keep her father at the forefront, and when her own image faded, people would see the Su family as a whole rather than just the First Branch, allowing the Second and Third Branches to gain benefits as well. At this point, she could start considering distributing advantages to the other branches rather than limiting herself to First Branch concerns alone. Some people would always remember her brilliant methods. Additionally, with war in the north, she was also thinking about shifting the focus of various businesses toward the southeast. Suzhou and Hangzhou were precisely the most prosperous region, certain to be the top priority in the future. She could make the Su family prominent in Jiangning while observing the situation in Suzhou and Hangzhou herself—it would be killing multiple birds with one stone.

Of course, there was no need to bring up too much of this. Once she'd thought it through in her heart, she simply remembered that this would be a long journey with her husband. She'd made a big sacrifice, after all. Having been lying in bed earlier not wanting to get up, she now got dressed and rose, preparing to start planning ahead for the summer trip. Chan'er and Juan'er also liked going out to play. As they attended to their young miss getting dressed and washing her face, they discussed things with her in low voices.

Ning Yi bid them farewell and pushed open the door for his morning exercises. The sky had just begun to show a dim light, revealing snow piled in the courtyard where several snowmen outlined vague silhouettes. Ning Yi did a few stretching movements under the eaves while over in the temporarily constructed small kitchen, Xing'er sat tending the fire and stuck her head out: "Young Master's up."

The morning wind was cold, and amid the sounds of chickens and dogs, the entire Su family compound was gradually waking up.

"Just what the hell is that Zhu's anyway..."

It was just approaching morning, and the streets along the Qinhuai River had already grown lively. Boats sailed across the river surface through the cold air, and on the streets, pedestrians, carts and horses, peddlers and laborers had already begun rendering the day's bustle and clamor. In front of a restaurant called Gathering Guests Restaurant, a carriage stopped there. The man who stepped down from the carriage was in his thirties, with a slightly pointed chin and dressed in the expensive robes of a wealthy landlord, with a folding fan tucked behind his back. To be honest, carrying a fan in this season was a pretty idiotic thing to do, but he pulled it out from behind him and even opened it, fanning himself a few times in the freezing morning wind. At this moment, he was frowning as he looked across the road at that beautiful restaurant by the river.

This pointy-chinned fellow looked somewhat like a wealthy landlord in his clothing, somewhat like a scholarly gentleman as he waved his fan, somewhat like an idiot waving his fan in this weather, and in appearance and spirit bore some resemblance to street ruffians and scoundrels. Behind him, the Gathering Guests Restaurant had its doors closed at the moment, though lights were on inside for pre-opening preparations—after this walking contradiction arrived, the doors opened and a manager hurried out: "Fourth Master Chen, you're here. So early?"

"What do you mean early? Just came from Swallow Jade Tower, was gonna head home and catch some sleep, passed by here... This Zhu's Brocade Restaurant or whatever, isn't this just stealing food from the tiger's mouth⁶? Who opened it? What's their background?"

The manager paused for a moment, then bowed: "Replying to Fourth Master, Zhu's manager came by earlier to deliver a calling card. Their manager's surname is Lin, an old man. But the actual owners behind it seem to be two women—I see them coming by every day. Haven't heard of any particular background... Oh, but I did hear they're two girls who retired from the pleasure quarters⁷."

"Retired?" That Chen Si laughed. "You're bullshitting me... What courtesan actually retires?"

He looked at the restaurant for a few more moments, then his expression darkened as he shook his head. "This restaurant being here won't do. It's stealing business, clearly going against our Chen family... Make them move."

(End of Chapter)

FOOTNOTES:

¹ Traditional Chinese scholars had a Confucian obligation to advise the government through formal petitions (上书), especially during national crises. This scholar activism was both sincere political engagement and a form of reputation building.

² Famous saying "众志成城"—when people are united in purpose, they become as strong as a fortress.

³ "壮士断腕" (warrior cutting off his wrist) and "弃车保帅" (sacrificing the chariot to save the general in Chinese chess)—both idioms meaning to make painful strategic sacrifices to avoid greater loss.

⁴ "上有天堂，下有苏杭"—Famous Chinese proverb meaning "Heaven above, Suzhou and Hangzhou below," indicating these southern cities were considered earthly paradises, renowned for centuries for their scenic beauty, cultural refinement, and economic prosperity.

⁵ "假公济私"—literally "using public matters for private benefit," here meaning Su Tan'er plans to combine her official business inspection duties with personal leisure time with her husband.

⁶ "虎口夺食"—literally "snatching food from a tiger's mouth," meaning engaging in dangerous competition by taking business from a powerful rival.

⁷ "从良"—literally "becoming virtuous," the traditional term for courtesans leaving their profession. This was socially difficult and rare due to stigma and economic barriers, making Chen Si's skepticism reflect harsh social reality rather than mere cynicism.
