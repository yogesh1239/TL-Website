The setting sun gradually disappeared behind the horizon, leaving behind countless lanterns casting their warm glow over countless tables where countless people gathered. This was dinner time, resembling those grand annual feasts when Su family relatives and friends assembled during the New Year season. The participants remained roughly the same as always, but tonight's atmosphere carried a distinctly different undercurrent.

The din of voices filled the air, and the gathering maintained its surface liveliness, yet it lacked the carefree atmosphere of previous years—those evenings of flowing wine, clinking cups, and unburdened laughter. Members from the first, second, and third branches unconsciously separated into invisible territorial divisions. Only the most carefree individuals dared to grab wine pots and drink freely while everyone else smiled, spoke pleasantly, and exchanged greetings with familiar faces, though remarkably few actually consumed alcohol. Beneath this festive facade, people from all factions scrutinized one another, measuring and speculating while hidden currents surged through the tensely charged atmosphere.

Su Yu sat in the place of honor, quietly observing everything before him. His gaze swept across the second and third branches before turning toward the first branch, where he could distinctly sense an atmosphere of defeat and resignation. Only Su Yunsung and a few others maintained cheerful dispositions while working to lighten the mood. He noted Su Tan'er and Ning Yi sitting to one side, conversing quietly over their meal. They both remained utterly calm, and while Su Tan'er's serene expression occasionally glanced around the hall, her attention in conversation was focused entirely on her husband.

Su Yu's gaze lingered on Ning Yi for several moments before someone approached with a wine cup. It was Xi Anzhi, the manager from the second branch, which prompted the old man to smile slightly, withdraw his attention, and nod in acknowledgment as he began speaking with the approaching figure.

This evening banquet didn't last long.

Once everyone had eaten their fill, the dismissal phase commenced. There were no solemn ceremonies or symbolically significant speeches—everyone already understood what would follow next. Nevertheless, several stewards went around individually notifying the members required to attend this clan assembly. The banquet area became somewhat chaotic as some people rose first and began moving toward the council hall beside the ancestral shrine. Groups of two and three conversed among themselves, creating a buzzing drone that momentarily seemed quite disorderly, with some people standing while searching through the scattered crowd for specific individuals, issuing various instructions.

Approximately fifty people would participate in this clan assembly, while the remaining banquet attendees consisted mostly of family dependents or Su mansion managers and stewards. Though these individuals couldn't attend the formal proceedings, most would wait for news in nearby courtyards or gardens. Beyond the front eaves, lamplight extended throughout the Su mansion's small courtyard, where Su Boyong was pushed forward in his wheelchair through the crowd. Slightly behind them, Su Tan'er and Ning Yi also headed in that direction.

"Will husband find tonight... somewhat boring?"

"Not at all."

"However..." Su Tan'er began, lowering her head as if to say more, but she finished with only a smile. As they walked intimately side-by-side in the night breeze, she reached out to grasp his sleeve, and after a moment, playfully swung his hand. Then, as if remembering something, she turned her gaze aside, her expression settling into a calm resolve.

Xi Junyu was also speaking with a first-branch manager among the crowd, occasionally glancing toward Su Tan'er while expressing concerns about tonight's events and questions regarding future allegiances. When they had walked roughly halfway across the small courtyard, someone emerged from the crowd and greeted him with a smile—Guard Geng, one of the most trusted personnel under the first branch.

"Miss has arranged certain matters for tonight. Around 7:15 PM, I'll need Manager Xi to accompany me on an errand. This matter is significant, and we have approximately fifteen minutes remaining. If Manager Xi has any affairs to arrange, please handle them now, as tonight will likely run quite late."

Guard Geng spoke quietly as they moved aside.

"Significant?" Xi Junyu frowned. "Tonight... what sort of matter?"

"I cannot say for certain at present, but it's Miss's arrangement."

Xi Junyu considered this briefly, his expression brightening with hope. "Are there still opportunities for turning things around?"

"Hard to say. Manager Xi will understand when he accompanies me..."

"Very well."

Xi Junyu nodded. As his gaze shifted to Su Tan'er, he saw her leave Ning Yi to lean down and speak quietly beside her father's wheelchair. Noticing his attention, she offered a slight smile and a nod, prompting Su Boyong to turn and acknowledge them as well.

Xi Junyu responded with his own smiling nod.

By this time, the two groups had considerable distance between them, and when he watched Su Tan'er's figure disappearing into the crowd heading toward the ancestral shrine's council hall, he realized he should have approached earlier to offer some consoling words about tonight's proceedings. However... never mind, he could address that after returning.

He watched her silhouette vanish into the crowd, frowning in deep contemplation. A chance for reversal? How could that possibly be?

Shortly after, the sounds of the first round of ancestral worship began echoing from that direction.

*******************

The council hall blazed with brilliant illumination, its bright lights casting radiance into every corner of this spacious chamber.

"...According to established tradition, we gather here at least once annually to decide important matters. In previous years, we convened after year-end accounting was completed. This year, we've moved the meeting forward by more than a month, requiring the clan leader and various elders to intervene personally while asking everyone to travel from distant locations specifically for this occasion. This is because our Su family has encountered numerous problems recently—problems that might be minor but could also prove quite serious... though everyone currently fears these issues will become quite troublesome..."

A resonant voice filled the council hall while complete silence reigned among the various seating areas. Below the group of clan elders sat members from all three branches. Su Boyong, seated in his wheelchair, appeared in poor spirits, maintaining the meditative posture of observing his nose with his eyes and his heart with his nose. Su Zhongkan sat rigidly upright while Su Yunfang seemed absorbed in his own thoughts. Speaking from the center of the hall was Su An, whom several addressed as "Seventh Uncle," and he paused slightly at this point.

"Regarding these matters, concerning affairs within this household, ultimately the clan leader understands the situation most clearly..." He turned back. "Third Brother, would you like to speak?"

Su Yu frowned as he surveyed the assembled members of the council hall, then after a moment raised his hand slightly. "Old Seventh, please continue."

Su An nodded, then after a pause turned aside and gestured toward another individual. "For the specifics... perhaps we should have the Head Steward explain, as he understands the situation most thoroughly."

Su An gestured to the Head Steward, a middle-aged kinsman who typically maintained a low profile. Although the three branches concealed certain matters, the steward's records remained the most objective, as most of the mansion's administrative work ultimately flowed through him for consolidation. A moment later, the steward's voice filled the hall.

"Regarding whether these issues are truly serious, I cannot say definitively, but concerning recent matters that have reached my attention, the situation is approximately as follows: First, over the past three months, our Su family's sales of various goods in the Jiangning area have experienced some decline in market share, though overall it remains less than five percent... The primary problem lies in future profit margins. Recently, nearly sixty percent of suppliers and business partners in Jiangning have begun demanding negotiations with our Su family to increase raw silk prices while reducing wholesale costs. Listed in my records: the Qi family demands..."

The Head Steward's voice carried on the night breeze, echoing faintly into the nearby courtyards where people waited. Su Wengui and his group listened from close by, discussing the proceedings, while further away, Su Danhong spoke with several family dependents, her brow occasionally furrowing.

Everything proceeded exactly as anticipated. Su Danhong understood clearly the problems facing the Su family, the demands being raised from various quarters, and the crises lurking behind these demands. Turning her head, Su Danhong noticed Ning Yi walking past toward a more distant location, looking bored as he stretched his arms and legs. She followed him.

Passing through the courtyard gate, the voices from the distant hall became barely audible, with only the lights around the council hall visible beyond the courtyard walls. Ning Yi's demeanor tonight seemed somewhat different from usual—still relaxed, but... as if he were experiencing something specific. He sat down beside a pavilion in this courtyard, lifting his head to gaze at the star-filled sky while footsteps occasionally passed through nearby alleys. Su Danhong frowned.

Could this man be savoring the final moments before the first branch's fall from power?

She furrowed her brow...

******************

In the council hall, the narration continued. Anyone with business understanding could sense the danger behind these circumstances—the Su family's problems had attracted circling wolves and stone-throwers taking advantage of their vulnerability. The Head Steward spoke for an extended period before completing his account of these matters and returning to his seat. No one below spoke while several clan elders at the head table began commenting.

"This suggests they believe our Su family is without hope, that our Su family will encounter serious troubles..."

"Problems must be resolved, so everyone should discuss and seek solutions..."

The elders surveyed their surroundings before the hall fell silent once more. Su Chonghua sat among the crowd, also watching silently. He could roughly predict what would happen next, though these developments didn't require his commentary or formal statements, so his mood remained somewhat relaxed as he simply observed. When his gaze swept past the entrance, he suddenly thought of Ning Yi.

Where was he now, and what was his state of mind? That poem "Calming the Storm"...

"This situation ultimately began with the assassination attempt on First Uncle, though naturally the responsibility doesn't lie with First Uncle himself. I believe our Su family should work diligently to identify the real mastermind behind that assassin. However, if we're discussing the root causes, Wenxing has some thoughts..." Below, Su Wenxing, the man who would light the fuse, stepped forward. Although only of the third generation, his recent management of several second-branch affairs had qualified him to participate.

"This incident fundamentally stems from our Su family's high-profile but unsuccessful bid for imperial merchant status..."

"Such tremendous fanfare, such enormous investment, only to achieve nothing in the end..."

"Therefore, outsiders have begun harboring suspicions..."

Following the predetermined script, Su Wenxing initiated this topic, and waves of discussion finally began spreading. After Su Wenxing finished speaking, several other members from the second and third branches joined the discourse, followed by Su Zhongkan and Su Yunfang. Their words contained both discussion and questioning as voices transmitted outward in succession.

"So the current question becomes: exactly how much did Tan'er spend on this imperial merchant operation..."

"The first branch... starting with Manager Liao, the specific situation... unfortunately Manager Liao isn't in Jiangning today..."

"Our situation here is such that we've also encountered certain problems that cannot be remedied. If this continues long-term..."

"Over the past two years, no, three years, we've known this operation had some accounting irregularities. This matter should be clearest to Elder Brother's side..."

The predetermined drama unfolded as one person after another began speaking while the first branch remained relatively silent throughout, with Su Tan'er and others offering only occasional responses. Under the low-hanging starlit night, this evening's proceedings were destined to consume considerable time. Outside the council hall, Su Wengui and others chatted and laughed as people departed and returned: "Tonight has only just begun..." they said.

*******************

Several streets away from the Su mansion, at Moon Fragrance Tower, Xue Yan and his companions dined while discussing recent events with amusement. At this moment, they also glanced toward the Su mansion's direction: "Speaking of which, things over there should have begun by now."

Luo Miaomiao, one of Jiangning's four premier courtesans, smiled from nearby: "Young Master Xue and gentlemen, tonight your concerns seem quite different from such romantic diversions."

"Haha, Miaomiao's perception is keen as always. Tonight, we indeed have certain matters of concern. Does Miss Miaomiao know of the Su fabric merchants?"

Luo Miaomiao considered this, and a glimmer of light flashed in her eyes: "Could Young Master Xue be referring to the Su family where Ning Yi—Ning Liheng—married in?"

Fabric merchant affairs naturally concerned only industry insiders, and though Luo Miaomiao now held premier courtesan status, she knew little about such matters. However, her first thought was of the greatest talent famous for "Prelude to Water Melody" and "Green Jade Cup." Xue Yan and his companions paused briefly before bursting into laughter.

"Indeed, indeed. Speaking of which, this does relate to him somewhat. Has Miss Miaomiao heard about the assassination incident that occurred during Jiangning's siege several months ago, causing quite a sensation..."

While the Su family clan assembly proceeded according to its predetermined pattern, Moon Fragrance Tower began recounting the ups and downs within Jiangning's textile industry over recent months. Under the same starlit sky, one location remained initially unconnected to these affairs—Flourishing Cloud Pavilion, located not far from Moon Fragrance Tower, was a substantial restaurant where tonight a gathering hosted by the Puyang family was taking place.

As Jiangning's wealthiest family, the Puyang household had cultivated relationships with many of Jiangning's talents through years of management and with premier courtesan Qilan presiding over their establishment. Today wasn't any special occasion, so when the gathering commenced, many renowned talents naturally attended, including Cao Guan, Liu Qingdi, and others—this could be considered a literary gathering among scholars. Puyang Yi, who hosted the assembly, was thoroughly accommodating, though some elements remained beyond his control, and at this moment, a minor incident occurred.

For unknown reasons, Liu Qingdi was in poor spirits today. After drinking some wine, his poetry became rather wild, and during the gathering, he accidentally bumped into another participant, leading to an argument between them. Though Puyang Yi subsequently mediated and calmed the situation, certain individuals at the gathering now harbored underlying tensions.

A middle-aged man calling himself "Empty Mountain Hermit," whose literary talents weren't particularly outstanding, was also present. He originally intended to intervene diplomatically, but subsequently found himself drawn into Liu Qingdi's conflicts as well.

The poetry gathering continued amid the somewhat discordant atmosphere created by this incident, with both sides beginning to compete in literary talent and poetry, gradually intensifying their rivalry. Puyang Yi was consequently quite pleased.

Naturally, at this point, their trajectories hadn't intersected with the Su family's path in the slightest...

********************

As Ning Yi peeled a peanut and tossed it into his mouth, he began humming the opening notes of "Devils Enter the Village," which gradually transformed into the "Wedding March," much to the irritation of Su Danhong, who was approaching from the side.

"Cousin Danhong, please sit. No need for formality. Peanut?"

"I don't understand what you're thinking."

"Experiencing this atmosphere..."

"Don't you realize that everything Tan'er has fought for over so many years without giving up is about to disappear?"

"You've guessed wrong." Ning Yi answered calmly, turning to glance toward the council hall's direction where light spilled forth and extended outward, carrying restless energy within. "Things... should be about ready now..."

(End of Chapter)
