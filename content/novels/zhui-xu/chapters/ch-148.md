The lanterns blazed brightly as the first round of discussions about the Su family's recent problems drew to their inevitable conclusion, with the business ventures of the Main Branch¹, Second Branch, and Third Branch all suffering declining profits while suppliers and distribution channels began demanding increased compensation. At its core, the turmoil stemmed from the Main Branch's catastrophic miscalculations in major strategic decisions—the Imperial Merchant affair had begun with such tremendous fanfare only to collapse spectacularly, and now with a woman managing these critical affairs, the instability had finally erupted into open conflict.

Of course, this represented a careful deflection of the real issues, since the external upheaval primarily resulted from the Third Branch's inheritance grab, but in this context, saying this much already sufficed for their purposes.

"Gentlemen, I believe I should say a few words here." Su Zhongkan rose to his feet in the great hall, his voice cutting through the whispered conversations and discussions around him. "In business, when we set a plan and attempt to complete a deal, it's impossible to guarantee success simply because we have the initial idea—many times, despite everyone's best efforts, failure is a common occurrence. Regarding why we didn't secure the Imperial Merchant position this time, everyone present understands the true reasons: it was due to the Wu family's underhanded methods, not any fault of our strategy². Tan'er's abilities and business acumen are evident to all—this failure wasn't due to anyone's mistakes."

"However, even though this wasn't anyone's fault, now that things have developed to this point, we must have a proper accounting and explanation. Regarding this Imperial Merchant affair, exactly how much money was spent and how large a deficit was created—some people claim that our efforts to secure the Imperial Merchant position drained the reserves from many of our operations, and whether this is truly the case, everyone needs to understand clearly. Previously, all these matters were handled by Tan'er from behind the scenes while my third brother and I didn't interfere, so I believe today's primary task must be to clarify the extent of the losses before we can proceed with anything else..."

The moment he finished speaking, Su Yunsong stood up from across the room: "I find this approach inappropriate." Behind him, another voice joined in: "You're actually asking our Main Branch to reveal its accounts at a time like this?"

"This is kicking someone while they're down!"

"The Su family's Main Branch, Second Branch, and Third Branch haven't divided so clearly yet!" Su Zhongkan frowned deeply. "Moreover, this matter has affected the entire family—shouldn't the clan elders³ have some concrete figures in mind today? If the Imperial Merchant affair remained unresolved, naturally these accounts would stay confidential, but now that the matter is concluded and the dust has settled—if we failed, we failed, so what's the point of hiding anything further!"

Su Yunsong glanced toward Su Tan'er and Su Boyong's direction: "The Imperial Merchant affair involved extensive complexities, and the specific details weren't previously disclosed, but how can these accounts be summarized today, Zhongkan? This matter surely requires us to wait until..."

"Why not wait until next year!" someone from the Second Branch stood up, and Su Zhongkan gestured for quiet while someone from the Main Branch also rose: "What are you saying? Doesn't Yunsong make sense?"

The scene descended into chaos once again as Su Tan'er stood up attempting to speak, but Su Yu suddenly struck his walking stick sharply against the floor: "Stop arguing!" The room finally quieted, and as people began settling back into their seats, Su Tan'er was opening her mouth to speak when another figure emerged from among the Main Branch's group—this was Su Tingguang, a relatively important manager within the Main Branch and a cousin of the Su family, holding some documents with a somewhat hesitant expression as Su Tan'er looked at him: "Uncle Tingguang..."

Su Tingguang met Su Tan'er's gaze and sighed deeply: "Regarding today's matter, I... I actually agree with Second Cousin's position, and I have some accounts here that should be brought forward."

Everyone's eyes fixed on him as the meeting hall fell into the most complete silence yet, as if something had reached a critical threshold and was finally about to emerge—the Main Branch, Second Branch, and Third Branch, along with the patriarch and elders above, all wore varying expressions.

Only Su Tingguang's voice broke the silence in the next moment.

"While the Imperial Merchant matter remained undecided, these accounts were still active, but given the current state of the family, claiming we could still make changes would be self-deception. Everyone knows of Tan'er's efforts over these past years—she established plans early and made preparations well in advance for the Imperial Merchant bid, spending considerable money in the process. This truly wasn't a strategic failure..."

He sighed again: "What I have here are the accounts for funds secretly transferred from the Yuanzhou area over the past several years—the current deficit amounts to approximately fifty thousand taels⁴ and can no longer be replenished. Elder Brother, Tan'er, everyone..."

Su Yu at the head table narrowed his eyes while Tan'er closed hers and turned her head away, Su Boyong lowered his head so his expression remained hidden, and on the other side, Su Zhongkan's gaze grew stern as Su Yunfang listened carefully.

Su Tingguang continued speaking, but his words could no longer be heard clearly as the entire meeting hall erupted in an uproar, the commotion spreading beyond the lantern-lit interior to ripple through the crowds gathered in the surrounding courtyards.

*****************

The clamor carried over the courtyard walls so that it could be heard even in this adjacent area—the meeting hall had finally reached its breaking point, or rather, the people who had been planning to create an explosion had finally made their move.

"Guess what wrong?" Su Danhong glanced toward the noise before turning back to look at Ning Yi.

Peanut shells lay scattered on the table as Ning Yi kept his head down.

"Starting from... several years ago." He seemed to consider for a moment before beginning to speak, his words coming slowly, "When Tan'er decided she wanted to compete for the Su family head position, everyone already knew this, but regardless of her capabilities, she's still fundamentally a woman—this basic fact can never be changed. Even within the Main Branch, while most people genuinely trust Su Boyong, their feelings toward her have always been somewhat uncertain, with many people remaining conflicted."

"So even if the old master helps her secure the family head position, problems will persist—there's no telling when these people might lose confidence in Tan'er, and while this represents natural human behavior, rather than simply watching it happen, it's better to take action while we still have the means to address it decisively."

Su Danhong furrowed her brow with a completely puzzled expression, unable to understand what he was actually saying.

Ning Yi raised his head to look toward the distant lights, where countless fragments of discussion could be heard: "Today's events are primarily triggered by the Third Branch's inheritance grab, but that can't be discussed openly. To establish that the Main Branch no longer has the capacity to manage so many business operations and to pressure the clan elders into cutting their losses—rather than continue dragging things out, they want to remove Su Tan'er as an unstable factor, and the only way to accomplish this is by focusing on the losses from the Imperial Merchant accounts, which presents the obvious approach."

"Su Zhongkan and Su Yunfang have been actively maneuvering, so certain people were bound to step forward—this isn't entirely about loyalty issues but simply a matter of confidence in the Main Branch and in Tan'er. During crisis moments, they inevitably remember that Tan'er is a woman, and if these people don't cause problems now, they could become troublesome later, so... it's possible to issue them a warning before Tan'er's position becomes officially confirmed, conducting a trial run to demonstrate that even when facing such difficulties in the future, Tan'er will be capable of resolving them."

"What exactly are you talking about?"

"The thing you guessed wrong about." Ning Yi smiled slightly, and at that moment, several figures approached from the other direction, led by Su Wengui, who had attempted some clever provocation the day Su Boyong was attacked only to get his head bloodied by Su Yu's walking stick—since then, his expression had remained dark whenever he saw Ning Yi, but now upon seeing Ning Yi with Su Danhong, he simply paused momentarily before breaking into laughter and walking over.

"Liheng! Why aren't you over there watching? Do you know what's happening? They're arguing inside, haha!" Su Wengui laughed before lowering his voice, "There's internal strife, you understand? Uncle Tingguang and Uncle Mianyun have both come forward, presenting accounts of the Main Branch's losses while everyone's arguing—it's complete chaos! Tan'er is isolated and helpless, nearly getting scolded, and you're her husband but you don't even go look—this is really... tsk tsk tsk... completely heartless..."

Su Danhong still wore that puzzled expression from before, but hearing Su Wengui's description combined with what Ning Yi had just said created an almost horrifying realization as she looked between Su Wengui and Ning Yi. Su Wengui noticed her expression: "Eh? Is Cousin Danhong worried?"

Su Danhong simply stared at Ning Yi while he smiled back: "You see, you felt it too..." Then he turned to Su Wengui, pulling out a handful of peanuts: "Want some peanuts?"

Su Wengui stared at him for a long moment before shrugging: "No thanks."

He still had to return to watch the show.

*******************

On this same evening, at Changyun Pavilion⁵.

The sharp crack of a wine cup shattering against the floor rang out.

"Liu Qingdi, don't be so arrogant—I'm telling you!"

"So what if I am arrogant?" Liu Qingdi's face flushed red with drink as he spoke each word deliberately.

The atmosphere had grown somewhat chaotic, leaving Puyang Yi with quite a headache as the host—tonight's situation had developed rather dramatically, with Liu Qingdi drinking heavily for unknown reasons and now completely losing control, systematically mocking each person he'd quarreled with tonight while composing poem after poem in what seemed like a literary battle against all comers. Meanwhile, those who might normally match his abilities, like Cao Guan, simply sat quietly drinking and watching the spectacle without participating, making the scene increasingly difficult to manage.

Of course, while tonight's atmosphere was unpleasant, word of these events would likely transform into quite a literary legend afterward, certainly bringing Liu Qingdi tremendous fame—amid the frenzied arguments, another person finally lost his temper and began making bold claims.

"Do you really think you're the most talented person in all of Jiangning? I know of someone whose casual writing for a nine-year-old child surpasses your work by a thousandfold!"

"Then tell me who you're talking about!" Liu Qingdi shouted.

"Ning Yi, Ning Liheng!"

The moment this name was spoken, everyone present fell silent, Puyang Yi frowned, Cao Guan raised his wine cup with narrowed eyes, and Liu Qingdi's face cycled through red and white before his expression turned vicious.

Someone nearby asked: "Has Ning Yi produced new poetry?"

"Where did Brother Empty Mountain Hermit⁶ learn of this?"

"Quickly bring it out for us to see..."

The room immediately buzzed with discussion as Qilan in the back stretched her neck curiously. Liu Qingdi waved his hand dismissively, taking a long time to catch his breath before beginning to roar: "Bring it out then! Is it another 'Pouring Wine for Pei Di'⁷? Has that Taoist at his gate finished reciting the third verse?"

The man called Empty Mountain Hermit—Chen Lu—grabbed a long table with a flourish, his own face red with anger as he seized a brush that was about to fall to the ground and slammed it forcefully against the table's surface.

"I, Chen Lu, am no poetic genius! I write poetry and song lyrics merely for self-cultivation! Perhaps I cannot write as well as you, but I cannot stand your behavior! This poem isn't mine, but I want you to see it and understand that there are heavens beyond heaven and people beyond people!"

"Good!" someone applauded.

"Then write it! Let me see what this fellow can produce now!"

Chen Lu glared at him, dipped the brush vigorously in the ink, grabbed a sheet of paper, and scrawled three large characters: Calming the Waves⁸!

His brush moved continuously without pause as a crowd of agitated, red-faced people gathered around while Liu Qingdi held his breath, his chest rising and falling dramatically—the poem appeared on the paper with a flourish!

Don't listen to the sound of leaves rustling through the forest,
Why not chant and walk at leisure?
Bamboo staff and straw sandals lighter than horses—who's afraid?
A raincoat through misty rain, letting life unfold!

As he reached this point, Chen Lu looked up at Liu Qingdi before continuing with his brush.

A raincoat through misty rain, letting life unfold!

FOOTNOTES:
¹ *Traditional Chinese family business organization divided wealth and responsibility among main family lines based on generational hierarchy*

² *非战之罪: Classical expression meaning "not a fault of war/battle"—a failure due to external circumstances beyond one's control rather than personal incompetence*

³ *Clan elders: Senior family members who collectively made important family business and inheritance decisions in traditional Chinese families*

⁴ *Traditional Chinese silver currency measurement; fifty thousand taels represented an enormous sum*

⁵ *Changyun Pavilion: A refined gathering place for scholars and poets to hold literary competitions and cultural discussions*

⁶ *"Empty Mountain Hermit" (空山居士): Literary pseudonym following traditional Chinese scholarly naming conventions*

⁷ *Reference to Ning Yi's previous poetry that had gained recognition in literary circles*

⁸ *定风波 (Ding Feng Bo): Classical Chinese ci poetry form, literally "Calming the Storm/Waves," often used for poems about maintaining composure through difficulties*
