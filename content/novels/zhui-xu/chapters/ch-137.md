Every morning, he would run to the banks of the Qinhuai River to meet Nie Yunzhu for a brief moment, sometimes discussing their plans for the day ahead. In the afternoons, he might visit the main Bamboo Records store or come here for tea and music. Recently, there had always been a third wheel present during his time with Nie Yunzhu—or more accurately, sitting beside them. Yuan Jin'er, who appeared to have nothing better to do, would invariably position herself next to him as he listened to Yunzhu play the qin and sing. Though the relationship between Ning Yi and Yunzhu had already been clarified and could have potentially developed in decidedly impure directions, this situation forced them to remain frustratingly pure, much to Ning Yi's regret.

Of course, stepping back, having two courtesan-level beauties sitting beside him wasn't an experience available to ordinary men. Yunzhu's playing and singing were indeed exceptional, and if Jin'er had deigned to dance for him, that would have been quite enjoyable too. But Jin'er wouldn't even grant him this small pleasure—she would sit beside Ning Yi like a boy, legs crossed and chin propped on her hand, listening with apparent satisfaction, looking thoroughly content with herself, like a little monk. When Yunzhu left to fetch tea or snacks, she wouldn't follow but would remain seated beside Ning Yi, looking properly serious and utterly insufferable.

After several rounds of mutual mockery and verbal sparring, the two had engaged in some frank conversations, which usually happened whenever Nie Yunzhu stepped away, leaving them staring at each other.

"Go down and dance for us, little girl," Ning Yi said, seeing no reason to choose his words carefully since things were already off between them.

"Not dancing. I'm just here to listen to Sister Yunzhu sing... You should be grateful—do you have any idea how much it used to cost to have this young lady's company at Golden Wind Tower?"

Ning Yi rolled his eyes, unwilling to engage with her on this topic. Recently, Su Tan'er had given him a key, making him a kept man who could access money freely, though this actually made him more reluctant to take any, leaving him rather poor lately. He wasn't about to discuss money matters: "Tsk, this won't do—you're ruining people's romantic prospects..."

"How am I ruining anything? Aren't you and Sister Yunzhu just proper friends with a gentlemanly relationship? So just be that way. But if you want to take advantage and do bad things, I won't allow it. You're not a good person—you have a wife at home. Can you abandon that Su Tan'er at home to be with Sister Yunzhu?"

"Honestly, that would be very difficult." After a moment's thought, Ning Yi gazed at the river and muttered, "There are many problems, and men are never good creatures—always eating from their bowl while eyeing the pot..."

Jin'er had been about to say exactly this, but seeing Ning Yi so shamelessly admit it, her eyes went round with indignation, though being well-versed in such matters, she quickly recovered her composure and pouted: "Eye it then, eye it all you want—I'll just make sure you can look but not eat."

Ning Yi looked at her somewhat wearily: "I wasn't particularly hungry to begin with, but with you constantly reminding me like this, I suddenly feel very hungry indeed. What should we do about that..."

"Then we'll see who's more capable..." Jin'er threw him a flirtatious glance, adorably charming. Ning Yi laughed: "A thief can steal for a thousand days, but you can't guard against thieves for a thousand days."

"Hmph." Jin'er turned her face away with a stern expression, not wanting to hear this, yet afterward continued accompanying Ning Yi to listen to songs, acting like someone who feared neither boiling water nor scalding oil. Yunzhu found it amusing and didn't chase her away, and when Ning Yi left, she would cling to Yunzhu and earnestly tattle on him. Under the current circumstances, however, Yunzhu wouldn't get angry over such things. Even when Jin'er described Ning Yi's shameless attitude of eating from his bowl while eyeing the pot, Yunzhu would just smile without comment, even asking with interest if he really said that, as if wondering, "Does he really want to eat?" This left Jin'er somewhat deflated.

In truth, Ning Yi didn't care much about such matters. As a man, he naturally had desires—it was impossible not to—and Nie Yunzhu was beautiful with a gentle temperament, her resilient side holding great attraction for him. They had already reached this point with Yunzhu clearly willing in these matters, and if Jin'er hadn't suddenly appeared that day, things would have naturally progressed.

On the other hand, these matters didn't occupy much of his thoughts. From Yunzhu's perspective, she was enjoying the feeling of their interactions even more. To be frank, in this era, while there might be romantic love stories or tales of fidelity that people praised, the modes of interaction between men and women couldn't achieve true equality or respect. Even when men cherished women, it was built upon the framework of this era's conventions.

What Ning Yi could truly make certain people feel was perhaps that extremely "peculiar" and "unconventional" style of behavior. He could walk away so nonchalantly after saving Nie Yunzhu only to be slapped, later chat casually with her about anything, sleep through entire afternoons to her qin music without bothering to display his talents or scholarly side. Just as he could sit with Su Tan'er on the balcony for an entire evening when bored, making jokes about wanting to be kept.

Though casual, what Ning Yi displayed wasn't that of a rogue or someone without principles—he always maintained his own bearing and demeanor while simply being relaxed. The genuine components of calm, equal emotion within these interactions, or what they might consider elements of love, were probably something women of this era could never otherwise experience, though whether one liked it was a matter of personal preference—for instance, a certain young lady named Zhou Pei constantly felt her teacher lacked dignity and wasn't imposing enough.

The cold mockery and covert rivalry between Ning Yi and Jin'er often left Yunzhu somewhat flustered. When they were together, she seemed to return to her days as a singing girl, playing and singing on her qin to entertain when she had nothing else to do. While the shameful Jin'er who only acted as an audience member and refused to help seemed quite content with herself, the equally shameful Ning Yi would occasionally take out his integrity to polish it, inquiring after her during Jin'er's brief absences. Yunzhu would only smile and say, "I'm happy in my heart." She would often share Jin'er's tattling with Ning Yi, though not the overly sensitive parts like eating from one's bowl while eyeing the pot.

Afternoons at the small building were naturally still infrequent. After morning classes, he would either teach the Zhou siblings more in the laboratory beside the academy, researching physics and chemistry, or stroll around with Xiaochan, eating various foods. Sometimes he'd visit Elder Qin's home to chat and play chess, sometimes sit in Bamboo Records' shops. The city gates had reopened, and the flood's impact had entered its recovery phase. Once the alcohol prohibition was lifted, Bamboo Records would begin selling their high-proof liquor.

Occasionally he would encounter people he'd met in business circles—from the Wu family, the Xue family, or other friends and enemies of the Su family, as well as some of the Su family's shopkeepers. Jiangning didn't seem so large after all, though there wasn't much to say between them as these people either mocked or disdained Ning Yi, who understood this well enough and was too lazy to bother with them.

At the Su family compound, however, various messy situations would constantly arise—for instance, not long ago a young shopkeeper with some distant relation to the Su family had accused him, saying the Imperial Merchant matter had all gone wrong because he hadn't properly managed the fabric dye formula, and that if not for his inexperience and insufficient attention to this matter, the Imperial Merchant business should have been virtually guaranteed by the end.

Similar incidents weren't uncommon, and he had already prepared himself psychologically. Whether internal conflicts within the Su family's First Branch or forces from the Second and Third Branches, they had all emerged by now. As long as they could spare no effort in attacking anyone connected to Su Tan'er, it might count as a kind of victory. Though Ning Yi was now a live-in son-in-law in the Su family and had already withdrawn from business matters, he was still Su Tan'er's husband. If they could use any means to drive him from the Su family, it would obviously be the most powerful blow to Su Tan'er.

Whether they could actually achieve this was another matter, but pressure from all sides was inevitable. Ning Yi's current response could only be seen by others as adopting a completely non-resistant turtle-shell attitude—being in the wrong, he could only act this way, though the frustration in his heart wouldn't be small and would eventually explode, creating even bigger weaknesses. What people were waiting for now was that day when Ning Yi would have problems, making the already overwhelmed Su Tan'er's situation even worse. However, the situation had seemed to become somewhat strange in recent days.

"Recently, Fifth Uncle and Seventh Uncle agreed to hold another clan assembly in half a month to formally discuss the family's recent problems, at which time they'll also re-address the issue of Tan'er's involvement in family business as a woman—the First Branch matter should be decided within the year."

In the afternoon, several shopkeepers and cousins close to the Second Branch, including Su Chonghua, sat drinking tea with Su Zhongkan in his courtyard, casually discussing recent family matters. Despite the Su family's turmoil over the past two and a half months significantly affecting the Second and Third Branches' businesses, these Second Branch members showed little dejection as Su Zhongkan spoke of these matters, prompting one cousin to speak up.

"I'm just afraid that when the time comes, Third Uncle still won't change his mind—given our Su family's situation, even if all the other uncles stand with us, one word from the old man might still drag things out."

Su Zhongkan shook his head, understanding that this "Third Uncle" referred to Su Yu, who ranked third among the older generation.

"Father is unlikely to say more—given the family's situation this past month, he would have already intervened if he intended to. He understands there must be a conclusion for the First, Second, and Third Branches, as he seeks stability and family harmony above all. While he's likely genuinely angry about Elder Brother's actions, he must also see that with Elder Brother's current condition and Niece Tan'er's mistakes, it's not good for the First Branch to remain under this pressure. Their stepping down would preserve Niece Tan'er's future."

"That should indeed be the case." One cousin nodded. "Now everyone in the family has generally come to this understanding. These days, I've been contacting several First Branch shopkeepers about their future intentions, and most have expressed willingness to come to our side if separated from the First Branch. Unfortunately, the most central figures haven't declared their positions yet—Shopkeeper Xi is young and hot-blooded, saying he'll share the First Branch's fate, and his feelings for Second Miss are known to many in the family, while Liao Kaitai also refuses to state his position..."

"If Shopkeeper Liao would speak up, I believe many would change direction," one shopkeeper said. "But he's truly loyal to the Elder Master. Even after such events, he still harbors no resentment toward the First Branch... Oh, he only said during the few nights after the fabric merchants' annual meeting that Ning Liheng's scholarly temperament was far too willful, otherwise there might have been a chance... We haven't been able to find him recently, so we can't work from that angle..."

"Heh, Ning Liheng..." Someone laughed, prompting Su Chonghua to also lean back in his chair with a smile: "This person has talent, but unfortunately knows nothing about business..."

"Speaking of which, I heard Shopkeeper Liao was sent to the capital by Niece Tan'er?"

Su Zhongkan nodded: "What exactly for is hard to say, but what was reported to the family was indeed a trip to the capital. I even laughed that day—had Niece Tan'er lost her mind, thinking that since Jiangning connections wouldn't work, she wanted to go to the capital to file complaints? But I suspect she has other ideas. Filing complaints without solid evidence, and we don't have particularly good connections in Dongjing¹—she should know it's impossible."

"She seems to be targeting the Wu family lately, wanting to flood the market with low prices to create chaos. Perhaps she really has lost her mind and wants to make a desperate gamble?"

"Flooding the market with low prices would be pushing our entire Su family into a fire pit. Even fools know what the result would be—the entire fabric industry would unite against us." Su Zhongkan laughed. "Even if she wanted to do it, the family wouldn't allow it. The first day such an order was issued, there would probably be a clan meeting that very night, saving us the trouble."

"However... there have been some strange rumors outside these past two days," a shopkeeper surnamed Ren mentioned, suddenly remembering something.

"Hmm? What rumors?"

"The Wu family's situation seems somewhat strange—these past two days have been different from their previous approach of adjusting supply and demand while extracting annual tribute cloth and boldly discussing development with other merchants. There are rumors they've encountered problems with the Brilliant Brocade, and in any case, Wu Chenghou and the others seem somewhat distracted when discussing business these past two days. People from the Imperial Textile Bureau have even rumored they're negotiating with Dong Decheng to delay the delivery time for the first batch of Brilliant Brocade, though it can't be confirmed yet."

"What's that about?" Su Zhongkan frowned.

"They've probably encountered some minor problems. Such things are common. Yesterday I seemed to hear that Qin Zhongnan, the Manager Qin responsible for their Imperial Merchant batch of fabric, suddenly fell ill. Because of various relationships, some people always spread things as if they're miraculous." One shopkeeper shook his head.

The shopkeeper who had spoken earlier also shook his head with a laugh: "It should be that. I think the Xue family must be spreading rumors. This afternoon I even heard people saying the Wu family fell into our Su family's trap regarding the Imperial Merchant matter, that Second Miss was secretly plotting against them, and now there are problems or something."

"Fell into a trap?" Su Zhongkan was stunned before laughing with his head thrown back. "This must certainly be the Xue family spreading wild rumors. If there really was a trap, it would either be Elder Brother's or Second Niece's, but Elder Brother wasn't even conscious during those days. As for Second Niece, if she was faking illness, perhaps she really might have been using some scheme, but last time she genuinely fell ill from overwork and suddenly collapsed—Doctor Sun himself said she was under too much pressure and then suddenly faced with Elder Brother's collapse, so there's no question about this. If there really was a trap, I heard everything was handled by Liheng at the time—could they have fallen into Liheng's scheme?"

When he said this, everyone smiled somewhat helplessly—honestly, it wasn't good for family members to laugh at their own, but regarding Ning Yi, they were all already familiar with him. While others might say this person was mysterious or incomprehensible, they all lived in the same large compound and knew clearly what he did every day.

All day long, he just taught classes to children, told ridiculous stories, and reportedly conducted unorthodox experiments while playing chess and wandering around eating everywhere. Before Su Tan'er fell ill, he had almost never touched business matters, and after that annual meeting, he never set foot in the fabric shop again. If such a person who idled away every day had really done something during that month, only to be discovered now while enduring various annoyances and insults without moving a muscle, he would hardly seem human—moreover, if he had really done something, various changes could have occurred during the subsequent month and a half, making it impossible to completely ignore them.

Everyone drank tea and laughed for a while until one of the cousins frowned: "But... what if it's true?"

"Uh..." Su Zhongkan was slightly stunned as the room's atmosphere cooled somewhat, everyone looking at each other while that cousin thought for a moment.

"Thinking about it now, it does seem strange—this Ning Liheng previously didn't manage any business matters at all, and while he indeed put his heart into trying to handle things well after Second Niece fell ill, he suddenly withdrew completely after August 25th when Second Niece took over again. It's possible he was struck by what happened that day, but his withdrawal was far too thorough—afterward, he never asked about business matters again, maintaining a carefree appearance when others criticized or cursed him, continuing his life as before as if he hadn't taken these matters to heart at all, showing no impact whatsoever. If he was really that angry, shouldn't he have felt some guilt or concern afterward? Could his cultivation really be that profound?"

With this observation, everyone began to feel something strange stirring in their hearts. Indeed, during this past month and a half, the family had been full of open and covert struggles, with tides rising and falling, everyone inside and out fighting for various things with all their might. Many had also fixed their gaze on this scholar, making him part of the struggle, trying constantly to show him contempt and displeasure to squeeze him out of the Su family, at least causing interference for Su Tan'er. But of this couple, one remained at the center of the whirlpool stubbornly doing things others couldn't quite understand, while the other... looking at it now, seemed to be living normally as if these matters weren't even on his mind. Everyone had always thought he was enduring, but to endure to this extent seemed rather excessive.

However, this was ultimately just casual speculation. A moment later, everyone shook their heads and laughed.

"That scholar couldn't be so capable..."

Su Chonghua probably understood Ning Yi the most and laughed most interestingly at this moment: "You're overthinking it. The Wu family just has some minor problems, and you're taking hearsay as truth. If Liheng were really so capable, he wouldn't be the Ning Liheng we know but rather Zhuge the Sleeping Dragon²—accepting a mandate in crisis, doing what needed to be done, then withdrawing when finished, with worldly matters not troubling his heart... Have any of you met such a person? Though he does have some literary talent and cultivation, and his temperament differs from others. In the past when everyone questioned his poetic talent, he couldn't be bothered to defend himself with even half a sentence. Now receiving some abuse and criticism, enduring it is still no problem..."

"Haha, Chonghua is right. You all are indeed overthinking..."

Amid their joking, everyone subsequently cast these matters from their minds. However, perhaps because they had chatted about these things in the afternoon, when Su Zhongkan encountered Ning Yi returning home that evening, he couldn't help but look at him a few more times. The young man in his blue robe held an old book he'd bought somewhere, walking while watching the sunset clouds in the western sky, lost in thought about something. Noticing his gaze, he turned back with a smile: "Second Uncle."

They exchanged greetings and passed each other. Su Zhongkan shook his head slightly. Indeed, he was too young, showing a composure belonging to youth, but not much scheming calculation. And such composure in a young person was mostly likely just an act. During this past month and more, he had received so much scorn and abuse—he was probably bottling it up inside, forced to put on this appearance...

He thought this, then turned his mind to how to persuade Ninth Uncle, the youngest of the uncles, tonight, no longer considering matters concerning Ning Liheng.

Small skirmishes were always matters for the younger generation; he needn't involve himself.

That same evening, in a room at a restaurant by the Qinhuai River, Wu Qilong and Xi Junyu met briefly. The two had "encountered each other by chance" today, each having their own matters to attend to. Xi Junyu, for instance, had recently been coming and going with many Su family members and First Branch shopkeepers, working hard to guide and lay groundwork for certain things, and it was already showing results. Tonight he had also arranged to dine nearby with several Su family disciples, leaving little time remaining.

"Brother Xi, how have things been recently?"

"Everything's fine. But your Wu family—did something happen these past two days?"

Wu Qilong looked at him for a moment, then took a sip of tea: "Nothing's wrong. I just wanted to ask you—regarding what we discussed before, how have your considerations been going? This past month and a half, you've been working to make the Su family blame the Imperial Merchant matter on Ning Yi, and I've had people help spread the word outside. Now the biggest problem with the Imperial Merchant business is that Ning Yi failed to guard the dye formula properly. But it seems the effect has been limited. At this point, what do you think?"

"Who says the effect is limited?" Xi Junyu smiled. "Until matters reach their final step, who knows what will happen? Given the Su family's current situation, both Su Tan'er and Ning Yi must be bottling up dissatisfaction. Su Tan'er is now too overwhelmed trying to grasp her last chance to deal with these emotions. Ning Liheng... he's been enduring all along, but one day he won't be able to endure anymore... Once the First Branch is lost under Su Tan'er's management, she'll remember everything that happened before. Then she'll recall how everyone was saying it was Ning Liheng's fault..."

"What if it doesn't work out that way?"

Xi Junyu shook his head: "That's not something I need to consider right now."

"Heh, that's truly your character..." Wu Qilong laughed as he leaned back in his chair. "Same as before—my Wu family's doors are always open to you, so when necessary, please give it some consideration."

Xi Junyu looked at him in slight silence, first nodding, then after some thought finally said: "You don't seem right—did something really go wrong on your end?"

"There are indeed problems—the workshop has had several accidents and Uncle Qin suddenly fell ill because things moved too fast and the pressure was too tight. We're now considering negotiating with the Weaving Bureau to delay delivery. While the problem isn't major, it's certainly not a good thing, and we can't let too many people know. It just makes me think how much better it would be if we had more capable people at our disposal..."

"Focus on your own matters." After Xi Junyu finished speaking, he turned and left.

Wu Qilong watched him leave before drinking his tea and sitting quietly in the room. As evening passed into night and the lights grew bright, a figure knocked and entered—if anyone from the Su family had been present, they would certainly have recognized this middle-aged man as Qi Guangzu, also a Su family manager. After closing the door and greeting Wu Qilong, he sat down in a nearby seat with a frown.

"Uncle Qi, how did it go?"

Qi Guangzu looked at Wu Qilong with concern: "Young Master, has the Wu family really encountered problems?"

Wu Qilong smiled as he lowered his head to sip his tea: "Uncle Qi, if my Wu family really has problems, it wouldn't benefit you either, would it?"

"I spoke with Shopkeeper Zhou yesterday." Qi Guangzu frowned. "Shopkeeper Zhou and Shopkeeper Bai are the most low-key and cautious among the Su family First Branch shopkeepers, which is why Second Miss had them responsible for developing that dye formula. After the Imperial Merchant matter, the Su family has been conducting internal investigations and these two have been extremely cold-shouldered during this time, but after receiving Young Master's message and drinking with Shopkeeper Zhou the night before last, it truly startled me... Young Master, what exactly happened?"

Wu Qilong's expression became serious: "What exactly happened—shouldn't I be asking you that? Uncle Qi, what exactly did Shopkeeper Zhou say?"

"He... he basically didn't say much." Since Wu Qilong wouldn't reveal anything, Manager Qi took a deep breath before continuing. "But throughout the entire drinking session, I couldn't see any worry in him—only yesterday did I realize he seemed not only unworried about the Su family's investigation but even unconcerned about the Su family's entire current situation, despite this clearly being something he and Shopkeeper Bai are responsible for. Young Master, there's only one thing I remember most clearly."

Qi Guangzu paused: "He was drunk at the time and said... in the entire Su family, the person he admired most, aside from the Old Master, was..."

"Heh, your Second Miss, I suppose..." Wu Qilong had almost already guessed what came next and raised his teacup with a cold laugh. Qi Guangzu looked at him somewhat awkwardly: "No... it's... Master Ning."

Wu Qilong froze, the teacup hovering near his lips. After a moment, his gaze shifted and he seemed unsure of where to set the cup down. Taking a deep breath, he exhaled long and slow before his eyes finally returned to Qi Guangzu.

"What did you say?"
