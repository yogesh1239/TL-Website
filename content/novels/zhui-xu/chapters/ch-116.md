It was afternoon, and the atmosphere in the Su family's main branch residence hung thick with complex tensions. The courtyard where Su Boyong lived had fallen eerily quiet, though people had gathered both inside and out. The door opened occasionally—someone carrying hot water in, someone else bringing bloodied water out. In the adjacent reception hall, Old Master Su Yu sat silently at the head of the hall, leaning on his cane, flanked by several elders from the side branches, with Su Zhongkan among them. Su Yunfang waited in the courtyard outside.

Su Tan'er sat near the door with her mother, the two concubines, and Ning Yi. The three older women wept softly, while behind them Xing'er, Juan'er, and Chan'er dabbed at their eyes. Though Su Tan'er had wept continuously when it first happened, she'd wiped away her tears and wasn't crying now. Her posture appeared no different from usual, but her hands gripped the chair's armrests so tightly her fingertips had turned white. With reddened eyes and a cold gaze, she waited for news—about her father, or about the captured assassin.

When they'd first returned, her hands had been covered in blood, her clothes spattered with crimson spots. If Ning Yi hadn't instructed Chan'er to bring water to wash her hands, they'd probably still be stained red. She still wore the bloodstained clothes, her hair having come slightly loose. She maintained her composure and clarity; there was little Ning Yi could do now except wait and watch how things developed.

Those in the courtyard were relatives close to the main three branches. Beyond this courtyard, those waiting for news were likely whispering among themselves, discussing possible outcomes, future developments, the structure of the Su family's three branches—vultures circling before the body had even cooled.

The assassin had been caught on the spot, though he wasn't at the Su residence now—the constables who'd arrived had taken him to the yamen¹. At this point, the Su family could only wait for preliminary news from the officials.

In the silent waiting, those entering and exiting the bedroom brought no good news—mostly saying the Master's injuries were too severe and he was still being treated. People occasionally entered the courtyard, asking quietly about the situation. At one point, more arrivals spoke in low voices at the reception hall entrance. Several pairs of eyes glanced into the hall, among them Su Wengui, who had just rushed over. His gaze darted around before he gritted his teeth and strode through the door.

"Ning Yi, you were right there—how could you fail to protect First Uncle?"

They had visited the pleasure quarters together just days ago, but now he'd turned hostile. The voice was low, sharp, and filled with indignation. Ning Yi raised an eyebrow as Su Wengui suddenly rushed over, angrily grabbing Ning Yi's clothes to pull him up. In the next moment, Ning Yi caught his wrist, twisted it with one hand, and pressed him against the pillar behind.

"Let go of me, you useless thing..." Su Wengui knew he couldn't make a scene now and hissed quietly. Ning Yi merely tilted his head slightly, watching him with indifferent eyes. From the front of the hall came a bang—the sound of a cane striking the floor. Old Master Su had risen from his seat. Though his hair and beard were white, he remained vigorous. The usually kind old man was clearly suppressing rage. The servant beside him hurried to support him but was pushed away. His steps were slow but steady as he approached.

Seeing the Old Master drawing near, a calculating gleam flashed in Su Wengui's eyes. "Let me go... Third Grandfather, Third Grandfather, look what he's—" He struggled a few times. Ning Yi watched for a moment, sighed inwardly, released his hand, and turned away. Outside, whispers arose as everyone watched the scene. Su Wengui stumbled a few steps. "Ha, Third Grandfather, look what he—" He'd barely turned when he met Su Yu's gaze. The old man's expression contained fury. Suddenly he raised his cane. Before Su Wengui could finish speaking, with a sickening thud, blood splattered across his face as the cane struck his head without mercy.

"At a time like this..." Ning Yi had just turned away, heading back to his seat while muttering under his breath. Su Wengui, struck by the blow, staggered backward out of the hall, tripping over the threshold and falling to the ground. As he struggled to turn around, the left side of his face was split open, blood and half a tooth spilling from his mouth. The Old Master's cane thudded against the ground as he approached step by step.

"At a time like this..." The old man shook his head slightly, his voice heavy with authority. "Put away your petty cleverness!"

In such a situation, Su Wengui's disruption—justified or not—would afterward have everyone saying how that live-in son-in-law had been present, how this and that. But such transparent manipulation couldn't fool Ning Yi or Old Master Su. With the three branches of the Su family competing, the Old Master wanted stability above all. What he absolutely didn't want to see was the brothers tearing each other apart. This incident's conclusion remained uncertain, but judging by the results, these two knife wounds could directly cripple the entire first branch. Who knew if the other two branches might be involved?

With matters still undecided, the most urgent priority in the Old Master's mind was likely preventing any form of internal strife within the Su family. Yet Su Wengui chose this moment for such petty tricks. The old man who'd shown a kind face for so many years finally erupted. He slowly walked past the threshold, swept his gaze across the whispering crowd outside, then sighed.

"Those with no business here, don't crowd the courtyard... Everyone go wait outside."

Su Yunfang in the crowd nodded and waved to those around him. Many people in the courtyard began filing out. The old man added, "Carry Wengui out too." Servants came to support Su Wengui.

Ning Yi found Su Wengui's extreme stupidity absurd from the start. The man had some cleverness—even called the "Many-Schemed Star" by his peers—but who knew where he'd applied it now. He stood watching for a moment before turning to sit down. As his hand touched the armrest, another hand covered his. Su Tan'er still sat there with pressed lips, but she'd placed her hand over his, gripping tightly, fingers trembling slightly as she glanced at him. Ning Yi nodded, covering her hand with his palm and patting it gently.

Someone emerged from the bedroom again. Su Yunfang was supporting the Old Master in the corridor. The person came to report—essentially the same as before. Ning Yi knew that with such injuries, the life-threatening period would last several days. If there were any definitive news now, it would likely be the worst news. But for now, they could only wait here.

The Old Master, supported by Su Yunfang, turned to walk back. Passing by Ning Yi and Su Tan'er, he paused briefly, reaching out to pat their joined hands. His expression complex, he finally just nodded and said, "You two must take care of each other." Then he turned toward his seat. After all, it wasn't yet time for final instructions...

After a while, one of the Su family stewards who'd gone to the yamen returned to report.

"...The assailant who stabbed the Master is named Chen Er, allegedly from Jiayu in Ezhou. According to him, three years ago when the Su family opened shops and acquired land in Ezhou, we hired thugs to drive his family from their home. His mother died because of it. He and his family were forced to move to low-lying areas. During this year's floods, his home was too low-lying to escape in time. His wife and children all died in the flood. So when he came to Jiangning and saw Su family members, he suddenly felt murderous. This person... has clear identification papers and travel permits, speaks with an authentic Ezhou dialect..."

The steward finished speaking, lowered his head, and paused slightly. "But when we tried to work our connections at the prefecture yamen, we couldn't get through. Steward Chen says the officials arrived rather quickly. By now, we can't make contact with that Chen Er no matter what... Someone has clearly greased all the wheels before us. Once the formal trial begins in a few days, even if Chen Er receives the death sentence, I'm afraid..."

Su Tan'er listened quietly, her expression unchanged, only her grip growing tighter. There was a bang as the Old Master's cane struck the floor. He spoke through gritted teeth, each word deliberate: "This isn't just targeting Boyong. Someone... wants to move against our Su family..."

Merchants valued reputation above all. Once such a reason for driving people to ruin came to trial, spread by those with ulterior motives, it would deal a heavy blow to the entire Su family. This person hadn't just stabbed someone, tilting the balance among the Su family's three branches—he was turning to bite the entire Su family. Su Yunfang's face darkened: "The Xue family?"

Su Zhongkan shook his head. "Hard to say."

The Old Master was silent for a moment. "Investigate further. Use every connection available. Check that Chen Er's background. Inside the city, outside the city, all the way to Ezhou. Have the manager in charge of Ezhou clarify whether this happened three years ago. Keep working the government connections too... Choosing to act after the city gates closed, murder followed by counter-accusation... This person's methods are vicious, their schemes deep—that much is clear. This concerns our Su family's very survival. You must stabilize the situation. I... must also prepare to visit some people..."

After saying this, the Old Master rose with his cane. "If Boyong's condition stabilizes, send word to me." He also offered some words of comfort to Su Tan'er's mother and the two concubines. At the threshold, he looked at the bloodstains in the corridor. After a long moment, he tapped with his cane: "No more indulging such foolishness."

With that, the old man departed, supported by servants and maids.

No matter how serious the injuries, treatment couldn't continue indefinitely. There comes a point when healing must pause. Near evening, the doctors had finally done all they could.

"The Master remains unconscious. These next few days will be dangerous—we don't know if he can cross this threshold, though there is hope. But... Madam, Young Miss, Young Master, you must prepare yourselves. The main issue is the knife wound to his back damaged his spine. Even if the Master pulls through, afterward, I fear he'll be paralyzed from the waist down... If it's only his legs, that would be the best case..."

As these words finished, Su Tan'er's mother suddenly swayed, then collapsed in a faint.

The sunset painted magnificent clouds across the sky. The entire Su residence had mobilized into action. As for this courtyard where some were shocked, some wept, and someone had fainted—in this vast, bustling estate, it had already become a small matter...

More things—more complex, more dangerous—perhaps already waited in the approaching veil of night...
