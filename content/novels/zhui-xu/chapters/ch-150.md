Wave after wave of discussion and confrontation spread outward from the assembly hall, converging into fierce and clamorous currents that gradually spilled into the small courtyard outside and the surrounding areas. Various voices of debate echoed throughout, each following their own reasoning and logic, sometimes sparking small arguments that, while not escalating further, represented an unprecedented situation in the Su family's history.

"Fifty thousand taels, ten thousand taels... and over there another twenty thousand. I've been saying for years that the First Branch has been making a mess of things..."

"I could see it from that red cloth business in Raozhou back then. They kept saying they had no spare funds, no spare funds. If it hadn't been for that..."

"This kind of thing is pure chaos. Just watch—after today, who knows how many more problems will surface..."

"I'd guess at least two hundred thousand taels in losses, maybe even more... Really don't know how they managed to hide it all this time..."

"Second Sister definitely won't be able to continue now..."

From the moment Su Tingguang first stepped forward with the accounts in his possession, followed by the second and third managers coming out, something that had been lurking beneath the surface exploded like a bomb. Similar accusations spread uncontrollably outside in a buzzing cacophony, while inside the assembly hall, members of the First, Second, and Third Branches¹ argued over the origins of these financial discrepancies.

In truth, with the family's business operations divided among three branches, such situations weren't uncommon. If one truly traced every piece of silver to its source, these funds might not represent genuine losses—when the year-end accounts were tallied, the First Branch's annual profits and development often matched those of the Second and Third Branches, which served as clear evidence. However, Su Tan'er had indeed sacrificed greater development possibilities to extract funds for the Imperial Merchant operations, and now, without the possibility of compensation, once exposed, these activities appeared as very unsightly entries in the Su family's ledgers.

Those outside the assembly hall like Su Wengui had no need to consider such complexities—even claiming Su Tan'er's losses reached a million taels carried no psychological burden for them. But for those within the assembly hall, once several managers from the First Branch had stepped forward to confess certain matters in their possession, the situation seemed to require no purely rational consideration. From Su Tingguang's initial appearance, various accusations erupted into chaotic arguments.

As the arguing continued, the leading figures from each branch gradually quieted down. Su Zhongkan returned to his seat to rest while sipping tea, Su Yunfang frowned as he discussed matters with Yu Daxian, and from the First Branch, Su Yunsong had begun to see the irreversible nature of certain developments. He had initially argued about those accounts for a while, but later realized that further argument might prove useless.

Certain things had begun to show their true nature amid the ongoing clamor—regardless of how these arguments concluded, what lay before the elders above was the fact that the First Branch was no longer favored and that people's hearts had begun to turn away. If it were someone else, there might still be opportunities, but as a woman, Su Tan'er's position could not withstand such a failure, regardless of right or wrong.

With all arrows pointing her way, Su Tan'er could only sit quietly beside her father, occasionally lifting her head to observe everything unfolding.

After finishing his tea, Su Zhongkan stood up and attempted to mediate between the two sides still arguing in the center, then returned to sit down. The quarreling appeared as intense as ever—members of the First Branch who knew they would inevitably lose power if this matter concluded continued to argue, while many from the Second and Third Branches accompanied them with equal fervor. Naturally, Su Zhongkan wasn't there to break up fights; the planned events had appeared as expected, and after a while longer, the arguments below would subside, leaving it time for the elders above, and ultimately their father as clan head, to draw the logical conclusion.

Since this meeting began, Father's mood had been low, with Seventh Uncle handling most of the speaking while he simply watched, only occasionally growing stern. Su Zhongkan understood the reasons—his second niece was capable, and Father had invested great effort in her, especially since the old man had hoped for family harmony these past years. The First Branch's sudden troubles and potential division would naturally disappoint and sadden him.

But no matter what, Father, when these matters are laid bare before us, there's ultimately no other choice. My actions with Yunfang haven't been harsh—we've merely gone with the flow. Tan'er has truly suffered too great a defeat this time, and Elder Brother has encountered such troubles... Surely you can understand this eventually...

After two months, events had finally developed to this point, reaching this stage where the situation was crystal clear. Father should be able to accept all of this now. Su Zhongkan sighed inwardly as he waited for the final quarter-hour or half-hour to pass, glancing at Su Yunfang, who smiled and silently shrugged in return. Moments later, Su Zhongkan noticed a small change among those above amid this chaotic arguing...

Su Chonghua felt somewhat bored, which perhaps made him the first to notice certain changes among the clan elders above.

From Su Tingguang's emergence onward, the arguments below had reached fever pitch, while the clan elders above refrained from interference, though they frowned and occasionally whispered among themselves. This was perfectly normal—those below argued continuously while those above summarized and concluded these matters. Beside Su Yu sat two elderly men who were the family's second and fourth sons respectively. Occasionally, the normally quiet Second Uncle would frown and exchange a few words with Su Yu, presumably worrying about the family's future. Su Yu might respond with a sentence or two, but his gaze remained fixed on the chaos below without much indication of preparing to take a stance.

This old man remained the center of the entire family—even in a coup, everyone needed to give him sufficient psychological preparation time. Everything displayed here today was actually designed to pressure these clan elders and ultimately force him to summarize and take a position.

Because he had remained so consistently calm, a small action amid this intense and chaotic scene was almost overlooked. At one moment, when Second Uncle leaned over to speak quietly, Su Yu also turned his head to respond briefly, then pulled several sheets of paper from his sleeve and handed them to the old man beside him.

This was perhaps the first time since the meeting began that Su Yu had made any clear, purposeful gesture. Of course, everyone below remained focused on arguing at that moment, unaware of these developments—they all knew that the more clearly they argued, the more it would help those above reach conclusions. Su Chonghua initially felt little curiosity about those few sheets of paper, but moments later, he noticed the expression changes on the old man's face as he read them above.

Su Yu's elder brother had frowned from the first page, glanced at Su Yu, said something after turning a page, then continued reading. The further he read, the more serious his expression became.

Perhaps... those were the drafts of Third Uncle's decision... Su Chonghua thought. But the subsequent situation proved slightly different.

The surrounding elders began noticing those few sheets of paper, with others moving closer and seeming to inquire with concern about something from Su Yu. Su Yu turned his head to answer briefly, and then one by one, two, three—these clan elders seemed to stop caring about the arguments below, gathering around those papers for discussion above.

When Su Zhongkan noticed, the entire situation had transformed into this scene—those few papers had captured the attention of the elders around Su Yu, while those seated nearby had also noticed this development, come over to look, and shown expressions of surprise.

Su Yu gazed downward, allowing his clan brothers beside him to discuss freely, while the arguments below also began to weaken amid slight bewilderment.

Soon after, the arguments below gradually ceased while the discussions above continued. One or two elders who had read those papers cast complex glances toward those below. Su Zhongkan looked toward Su Yunfang, not understanding the meaning of those suddenly appeared papers, then toward Su Tan'er's side, where the already physically weakened Su Boyong from his injuries remained silently bowed, while Su Tan'er continued sitting quietly, her thoughts unreadable. At this moment, the sound of a walking stick striking the floor finally rang out from above.

As clan head, Su Yu, who had observed everything from beginning to end, finally rose from his seat after sitting for so long. He appeared somewhat weary as his gaze swept across the entire assembly.

"Are you all... finished arguing? I've heard enough." The old man slowly walked over toward them. After the assembly hall quieted, the arguments outside also gradually subsided, with Su Wengui and others peering in from the entrance, awaiting the outcome.

"In these past four months, our Su family has encountered many troubles—external threats, and after those external threats, internal worries as well." He sighed, speaking sentence by sentence in measured tones. "I'm old now, and sometimes I feel somewhat powerless. From the moment Boyong was attacked, I sensed these things approaching."

"During the past four months, everyone clearly understands the Su family's problems. Today everyone has returned from various places to resolve these matters, and some have told me, 'Old brother, I know you're reluctant, but some decisions must ultimately be made.' I actually know this too..."

Noticing Father's tone, Su Zhongkan and Su Yunfang relaxed inwardly. Ah, it's almost over...

"A few years ago, I was already thinking about these matters. Our Su family's situation is somewhat strange—among the three branches, this group of children may be suitable for maintaining what we have, but insufficient for expansion. Perhaps our Su family's teaching methods were wrong. A few years ago, the one who seemed most promising and capable was a girl. I was quite hesitant then too, but when the day comes that I'm gone and Boyong and Zhongkan take charge of the family, there should at least be one capable person among them. Tan'er has also endured hardships, so I didn't mind letting her try then..."

The old man paused: "However, in business matters, girls ultimately can't take advantage—where others need to spend one portion of effort to achieve something, you must spend three. Because of this, it delayed Tan'er's marriage prospects at the time, and there was all sorts of gossip outside... Anyway, these matters have always worried me greatly. If one day Boyong steps down, could we really let a young woman manage so much business? Honestly, everyone lacked confidence..."

"Tan'er has high aspirations, and everyone has witnessed how the businesses under her management have fared these years. But she's ultimately still young, especially after Boyong's troubles, with everyone telling me various things that I've been contemplating. At this moment, can she continue managing these affairs? When Boyong steps down, will she have the capability and prestige to give everyone confidence? Today... I must make this decision..."

The old man closed his eyes as everyone inside and outside the assembly hall waited. When he opened them, he glanced toward the back: "Tan'er, you should prepare as well..." Su Tan'er nodded, leaning down to retrieve a small box from behind her father's wheelchair and rising to walk forward. The old man turned back toward his seat, his walking stick tapping the floor.

"From today forward, all matters originally managed under Boyong—the businesses and accounts from all states." He spoke thus: "Everything will be transferred to his eldest daughter, Su Tan'er, for management."

Su Yunfang stood up, Su Zhongkan hesitated briefly before also rising, while chaos erupted around them. From his seat, Su Yunsong stared wide-eyed, and in the Second Branch seating area, Su Chonghua sat stunned, yet something began surging from his heart—certain images repeatedly emerging: a little girl, rice paper, poetry.

"Uncle Shan, that's mine."

"Master traded it with Little Seven."

"Bamboo staff and straw sandals lighter than horses—what's to fear!²
A harsh spring wind sobered me from wine, slightly cold,
But the setting sun on the mountaintop still welcomes me...

...Calming the Waves³."

Ahead, Su Tan'er opened the small box on the table before the clan elders, removing items one by one—papers, banknotes⁴, contracts. She bowed to the elders in front, then turned back, her calm gaze surveying everyone present. Some in the assembly hall, inside and out, found themselves unconsciously awed by this figure's gaze, no longer expressing surprise or discussion, simply wanting to see what she intended to say.

"What everyone wanted to see is all here," she said. "This is only the first batch."

******************

"You... no, Tan'er... had already anticipated today's events?"

In the still-quiet courtyard where the sounds from the assembly hall could be heard in the distance, Ning Yi had finished the peanuts in the pavilion and felt somewhat bored. Su Danhong remained caught in complex and confused emotions—throughout this entire process, some of Ning Yi's words had seemed strange, as if he had anticipated and even arranged for the current situation, suggesting that today's events might hide some kind of turning point. However, since Ning Yi seemed unwilling to speak clearly, she could only run back and forth, occasionally checking on the arguments at the assembly hall, and when anxiety overcame her, returning again.

"This constant restlessness proves that subconsciously you don't believe Tan'er can turn things around, precisely because she's a woman. Those managers, like Uncle Tingguang and the others, think the same way—not necessarily with malicious intent, but..."

"Tan'er is indeed a daughter, and that's how others see her. Naturally, when I worry about her, I think the same way..."

"But there's no choice. You must make them stop thinking that way. There's no reasoning with this—even if she's a woman, once she controls this situation, she must make people abandon that notion and realize that even as a woman, she possesses abilities that absolutely don't lose to men. If you can't make people forget she's a woman, you must make them remember something rather profound... Listen, there's no sound from over there now."

Then tumultuous noise erupted again.

"Now they'll definitely remember this very deeply." Ning Yi smiled.

"What... what's happening..."

"Over forty-seven thousand taels⁵ in banknotes, more than twenty property deeds⁶, contracts for house and shop transfers, business contracts, formulas for about five types of cloth, and other things I'm not entirely sure about, but... these items should temporarily shut everyone's mouths..."

Ning Yi brushed the peanut shells from his clothes and stood up: "Come on, let's go watch the reversal."

"You, you, you—what exactly are you talking about? All this about... banknotes and property deeds... hey..."

******************

"This... is impossible!"

Su Zhongkan shook his head, examining the banknotes and contracts on the table. As for the textile formulas, Su Tan'er had tucked them into her sleeve, not allowing anyone to see them: "Where could you possibly have obtained these? No, this piece of land is... how could it be this piece?"

"Grandfather." Tan'er addressed the front, and Su Yu retrieved those initially produced papers from beside him, handing them to her. Su Tan'er placed the documents on the table: "Second Uncle, Third Uncle, and everyone else can see for yourselves. This contract was signed by Wu Chenghou⁷. Everything is listed above, and what he'll ultimately provide isn't limited to what's on the table."

Su Zhongkan and others examined the documents. Above, Seventh Uncle frowned and inquired: "The Wu family clearly... how could he possibly give these to you?"

"This way, how much would the Wu family have left!" someone in the crowd exclaimed.

"There's no way such a thing could happen..."

"But the Wu family's cloth faded."

"The Wu family clearly... ah..."

Everyone froze as Su Yunfang lifted his head to look at his niece: "What did you say?"

Su Tan'er smiled: "The Wu family's cloth faded. If he doesn't come begging to me, what other options does he have?"

"The Wu family's cloth..." Su Yunfang pondered, his gaze shifting. "The Imperial Merchant cloth? It faded?"

"Yes."

Silence fell as everyone contemplated this sudden revelation, looking at the woman before them who seemed almost innocent in her smile—she was, after all, only nineteen years old, and her smile at this moment appeared genuine and interesting.

"So four months ago, you had already..."

"The Wu family stole a fake formula?"

"The real formula is with you?"

"These months, you were all pretending? Waiting for the yellow cloth to fade?"

Amid tumultuous exclamations, Su Tan'er smiled ambiguously, and within moments, everyone inside and outside the assembly hall could piece together the entire picture. Above, Su Yu sighed.

"Now everyone need not question the Imperial Merchant results... Four months ago, when Boyong was attacked and Tan'er also fell ill, the First Branch established this plan, taking desperate risks. At the time... I also knew about it."

"This matter required strict secrecy, and success wouldn't come easily. Many people had already contributed their efforts while everyone remained in the dark... I also understand that everyone's concern for our Su family comes from genuine hearts. Actually, if our Su family's situation hadn't reached this point, this matter should have waited until everything was settled before being revealed..."

Su Yu stood up, explaining to everyone the process of these past four months, then describing the difficulty of succeeding in this matter, the intricate planning, the grasp and manipulation of human nature: "...After this affair, I finally know our Su family produced two traitors. The first: Qi Guangzu! The second: Han Qi, who currently manages the warehouse on Shengxing Street! They've already been detained and will be sent to the authorities for investigation tomorrow!"

Everyone remained lost in bewilderment from this reversal when the old man suddenly shouted out the two traitors' names, causing some to snap back to attention and look toward Su Tan'er. This affair had not only manipulated the Wu family this way and left the Second and Third Branches completely defeated, but had also identified the family's internal traitors in one stroke.

Nearby, Su Tingguang and others who had earlier been persuaded by Su Zhongkan and Su Yunfang to step forward still appeared panic-stricken, their gazes wandering without direction.

"This operation's difficulty and its substantial gains are clear to everyone. Outside, the Xue, Lu, Chen families and all the others who had been watching our Su family have completely failed. This success couldn't have happened without our Su family members working together wholeheartedly." This was formal rhetoric.

"Tan'er's control and manipulation of the overall situation." This was naturally true.

"And most importantly..." The old man paused. "Liheng's... strategic planning."

When this name finally emerged, Su Zhongkan looked up toward his father, thinking he had misspoken. Su Yunfang, Su Yunsong, and others had already widened their eyes, while Su Chonghua leaned back against his chair. At the table, Su Tan'er, who had been smiling slightly, also froze, that expression stiffening on her face as she turned her head to gaze somewhat bewildered toward her grandfather behind her. Su Yu smiled at her, his gaze unwavering.

"Tan'er, you have a good husband. Brother Zi'an... has a good grandson."

Grandfather...

********************

Su Danhong and Ning Yi took the winding path over, and as they approached the small courtyard, a certain atmosphere finally became palpable.

Ning Yi walked unhurriedly, observing groups of people as he moved—most of those gathered around the assembly hall entrance wore expressions that, while he couldn't hear what was being said inside, allowed him to guess their general nature. It truly seemed quite interesting indeed.

Just as he began approaching, tumultuous sounds started expanding, with people beginning to turn and look his way. Some discussed among themselves, some pointed, including Su Wengui and others who stared at him with expressions of seeing a ghost. More and more people looked over—all Su family relatives, but their gazes were definitely focused on him rather than Su Danhong beside him.

He stopped, his gaze shifting as he pressed his lips together.

These spectating expressions weren't exactly what he preferred seeing, as they felt somewhat excessive.

Su Danhong looked at everyone, then turned to him: "What... what's wrong?"

"Seems I shouldn't have walked with you—bad influence..." Ning Yi shook his head, turning to walk as diplomatically as possible toward a quiet corner.

Sigh, better hide for a bit.

Only Su Danhong remained standing there, looking at herself confusedly, then at others...

(End of Chapter)

FOOTNOTES:

¹ Three Branches (三房): Traditional Chinese wealthy families often organized business operations through multiple family branches, each descended from different sons, creating a complex governance structure that balanced family unity with individual branch interests.

² "Bamboo staff and straw sandals lighter than horses—what's to fear!": A line from the famous Song Dynasty poem "Calming the Waves" (定风波) by Su Shi, expressing fearless determination in the face of adversity.

³ Calming the Waves (定风波): A renowned poem by Song Dynasty poet Su Shi (Su Dongpo), metaphorically comparing life's storms to weather, emphasizing maintaining inner calm and determination despite external turmoil. The poem's title becomes symbolic of Su Tan'er's situation.

⁴ Banknotes (银票): Traditional Chinese financial instruments, early forms of paper money or promissory notes backed by silver, used for large transactions and long-distance trade.

⁵ Forty-seven thousand taels (四万七千两): A massive sum in traditional China. One tael of silver was roughly equivalent to 37-38 grams of silver, making this sum worth millions in modern currency.

⁶ Property deeds (地契): Official property ownership documents in traditional China, representing some of the most valuable and secure forms of wealth, as land ownership conferred both economic power and social status.

⁷ Wu Chenghou (乌承厚): Head of the rival Wu family merchant house competing for Imperial Merchant status.
