The New Year had just passed, with the Lantern Festival¹ still to come, and the streets along the Qinhuai River were filled with festive cheer and the occasional popping of firecrackers—some from shop openings or establishments welcoming guests for the new year, others scattered randomly as children ran through the streets laughing and setting off explosives wherever they pleased, while carriages and pedestrians moved through the thoroughfare.

The festive atmosphere couldn't dispel the winter cold. Throughout the market district, snow had been swept to the sides with no signs of melting, piled up like small mountains. In contrast, the private room at an establishment called Listening to Waves Pavilion was warm and comfortable. Well-positioned braziers burned vigorously, heating the room while maintaining air circulation. The luxurious space featured beaded curtains, through which incense smoke drifted gracefully, accompanied by the ethereal sound of a zither. The woman playing was elegant in form and lovely in appearance, but at this moment she served merely as accompaniment, speaking little.

Tea was poured into cups, the leaves unfurling in the rippling water as steam rose.

"I left Jiangning during the New Year and only returned yesterday. Has anything significant happened these past few days?"

"Same as Brother Tang, I also left the city to pay respects to my ancestors and visit family elders, so I have no news to share either."

The scene appeared peaceful—the two conversing were Puyang Yi and Tang Xu, both outstanding figures among Jiangning's younger generation of merchants, with Tang Xu being particularly refined and cultured, accomplished not only in business but also in literary talent and scholarship. The two were simultaneously rivals and friends who occasionally met for tea and conversation, and today's meeting happened to be a chance encounter after the New Year, with the courtesan Qilan accompanying them on the zither.

"...While I was away, I heard about the snowstorms in the north. Lin Shouzhou had a shipment coming through, encountered an avalanche, and lost everything—such a pity."

"Lin Shouzhou has plenty of tricks though. He always manages to get by through borrowing from here and there, haha. It's just that his luck's been poor these past couple years..."

"He certainly is formidable. With war approaching, I heard he invested heavily in the northern territories early on. Once fighting breaks out, he'll be positioned to profit. He's probably counting down the days now, seeking fortune amid the risks..."

"Those cloth merchants have been discussing things lately too. A few days ago when I drank with people from the Imperial Textile Bureau², they were all sighing."

"Hmm? Wasn't last year's affair enough trouble? What's happening now? Has the Xue family made a move?"

"It's still about the Su and Wu families."

Puyang Yi took a sip of tea, slightly surprised. "Didn't that finish when the Su family held their clan meeting back in late October? The Wu family was thoroughly outmaneuvered by that Ning Yi—their business handover should be mostly complete by now. Are they still refusing to accept it and planning more trouble?"

"The aftershocks haven't finished."

"There are still aftershocks?"

"I only figured it out today myself, though people in the cloth trade have keener senses and have probably already realized it—the Wu family proactively took on all the annual tribute cloth³ quotas for the Jiangning area, with officials at all levels making frequent visits."

"Taking on tribute cloth? Has he gone crazy?"

"More like he had no choice," Tang Xu said, his expression becoming complex. "I heard that recently the Wu family has been burning through money like water—their decline has been as dramatic as a mountain collapsing. After losing a third to the Su family's squeeze, they took on the tribute cloth contracts and had to grease palms up and down the hierarchy, which cost them nearly half of what remained. People only noticed these dealings after the New Year since their activities were so excessive, but now that it's settled, they've probably breathed a sigh of relief—though the Wu family's foundation is less than a third of what it was, and for the next several years they'll likely be working solely for the imperial commerce. I ran into Wu Qilong once—he's become much more restrained."

Puyang Yi's mouth fell open. "Why would this happen?"

"Take a guess. I only just realized it myself."

Puyang Yi brought his teacup to his lips, then blinked and set it down again. "The cloth fading... it can't be solved?" This wasn't really a question but rather a conclusion reached after careful thought, one that Tang Xu confirmed with a nod from across the table. "That's my guess too—I think quite a few people have already figured it out."

"This whole affair has had so many twists and turns, and it's still not finished even now..." Puyang Yi murmured, his expression complicated, but Tang Xu laughed. "Everyone was deceived, and even after everything was laid bare, they were still being deceived. From the assassination attempt on the Su family members, it's been one deception after another. When the imperial commerce position was decided at the end of August, everyone thought the Su family had been fooled and marveled at the Wu family. Then at the Su family's clan meeting in late October, everyone realized it was actually the Wu family that had been deceived. But who would have thought that even after the October revelation, everyone was still in the dark... Looking at how the Su family dealt with the Wu family at the time, almost everyone assumed they had reached some agreement, using the real Brilliant Gold Brocade to threaten the Wu family. But now it seems..." He took a sip of tea, his expression complex. "It was all sleight of hand—getting something for nothing⁴. That's truly admirable..."

Puyang Yi was silent for a moment before responding. "In other words, the Su family didn't actually use the real Brilliant Gold Brocade to extract a third from the Wu family, but instead exchanged merely a secret for that third, while the Wu family was then forced to waste away another third on their own. After everything was revealed in late October, no one even suspected this possibility. It really is... calculating ten steps ahead..."

"Looking back now, it makes sense." Tang Xu nodded. "The Wu family's foundation was solid—if they'd only lost a third, they probably could have maintained their position as one of the Three Major Textile Houses⁵ in the balance of power. After this incident, the Su and Wu families have formed a major rift. If I were Ning Yi, I wouldn't allow that situation to continue either. I'd have to completely crush the Wu family, making them unable to compete, before I could feel secure. It's just that at the time, such an appetite seemed too large, so no one considered it. Everyone thought the Su family had already gained the better part of the deal and should quit while ahead—that was the reasonable assumption... The Xue family and others missed their best opportunity. This Ning Yi appears gentle, but in reality... he's frightening."

Though they spoke in casual tones, the two men's family businesses were much larger than the Su family's and operated in different industries without direct competition, yet discussing the matter still left them shocked and amazed by the machinations behind it all. The outcome revealed at the clan meeting in late October had already been frightening enough—schemes within schemes, layers upon layers—but when people thought that was the end of it, they couldn't have imagined the affair would extend to the present day, reducing the Wu family to a mere third of their former strength, all orchestrated by a mere scholar.

"It's good he's not an enemy."

"Heh, such brilliant stratagems can't necessarily be deployed at will—there's probably some coincidence involved too. Brother Puyang surely isn't afraid of him, right? Besides, I heard you have a decent personal friendship with that Ning Liheng. What's he really like? Didn't you notice anything before all this?"

"We've gathered together a few times, though I wouldn't necessarily call it a close friendship. His nature is indifferent—he has little interest in gatherings or pleasure-seeking. I'd only thought his foundation in poetry and literature was impressive. I never imagined that when he stepped forward for his family this time, he could stir up such astonishing waves. An entire Wu family, just... thoroughly crushed... After October, I visited him as well. But I heard that once the imperial commerce matter was decided, he simply returned to his life teaching at Yushan Academy. Occasionally when I've run into him on the street, he seems completely unconcerned with the Su family's affairs. He lives far more carefree than either you or I."

"What a strange person." Tang Xu smiled and raised his teacup. "I was just thinking—from now on, anyone who wants to scheme against the Su family will have to carefully weigh the presence of this Ning Liheng standing behind them..."

Considering the Su family's current situation, any enemy facing them would find it truly unsettling to have someone called "ten steps ahead" like Ning Liheng always standing in the background. With one move, he'd erased a third of the Wu family—others would really need to think carefully before making any attempt.

Behind the beaded curtain, Qilan played her zither while gradually piecing together what the two men were discussing. Stories of talented scholars and beautiful women were always mainstream in the pleasure quarters, and as a top courtesan, she occasionally heard people mention Ning Yi and bring her into the conversation—which always stirred strange feelings in her heart. Ning Yi was truly one of Jiangning's most unusual talents—recognized as the foremost scholar yet distinctly distant from the pleasure quarters. What kind of talented man behaved this way? Yet she also felt Ning Yi deserved the recognition, that he was even more impressive than Cao Guan. The feeling was truly odd.

He kept his distance from the pleasure quarters with everyone, but if he were interested, he should get along well with her—Qilan occasionally thought this to herself, after all, she was different, and hadn't he rewarded her with several thousand taels at the last courtesan competition?

Of course, now that she had status, she was actually quite busy—entertaining guests for the Puyang family to repay their kindness, meeting other impressive scholars, various other things. She only had these thoughts during idle moments, and while she didn't think about Ning Yi often, whenever she did, she certainly didn't find it unpleasant. Sometimes she heard that Ning Yi had contact with Yuan Jin'er, the courtesan who had already left the profession, though she didn't know how true it was—the pleasure quarters were never short of rumors.

Today's gathering didn't require her to say much, so she was content to play casually while letting her mind wander. After chatting for a while longer, Puyang Yi and Tang Xu rose to take their leave, with Puyang Yi mentioning that in a few days he'd visit the Su family and invite Ning Yi to the Lantern Festival poetry gathering—Qilan wondered whether he would come.

Puyang Yi still had matters to attend to, so after seeing Tang Xu off at the entrance to Listening to Waves Pavilion, he and Qilan went their separate ways. In any case, Qilan had maids accompanying her and a driver to take her directly home. As it happened, while riding in the carriage that day, Qilan actually saw Ning Yi and Yuan Jin'er.

After the New Year, though there were quite a few people on the streets, not many were simply strolling leisurely—most were making New Year calls or had specific purposes. The carriage traveled along the Qinhuai River, and when they reached a certain intersection, she lifted the curtain and caught sight of Ning Yi and another person entering a riverside establishment. The person with Ning Yi looked somewhat like Yuan Jin'er, but also seemed different—not quite the same impression as before—so she called for the carriage to stop, mainly because Puyang Yi and Tang Xu had just been discussing Ning Yi, and curiosity compelled her to take a look. The riverside building was still under renovation, apparently halted for the New Year, but the design was rather unusual with some subtle stylistic changes. Though the modifications were small when examined closely, they managed to make this two-story building—presumably intended as a restaurant—stand out distinctly. Someone had clearly put considerable thought into it. Because of the cold, Ning Yi was bundled up quite heavily, and with a hat on looked somewhat rustic. The person beside him was a woman, also heavily dressed in brown clothing with a hat trimmed with white fur. Though her beauty still showed through, from a distance she resembled a little panda.

After looking more carefully, Qilan confirmed that the person walking with Ning Yi was indeed the legendary retired courtesan Yuan Jin'er.

As a courtesan, Qilan was still wearing an elegant, beautiful dress today that was both lovely and warm—considerable thought had gone into it. Yuan Jin'er had also been a courtesan, and in the past she certainly wouldn't have made such a casual appearance that seemed to deliberately hide her attractiveness. Ning Yi and Yuan Jin'er walked into the establishment, holding several papers and gesturing at the furnishings in the main hall. Qilan frowned, told her maid to wait, and went over herself.

The building was halfway through renovation, with some windows not properly closed. Ning Yi held a brush and was discussing something with Yuan Jin'er, occasionally leaning on a table to sketch and write, while Yuan Jin'er pushed things around in the cluttered hall. Their conversation drifted out:

"...What I want isn't a major renovation, but it has to emphasize cleanliness... Mm, actually I've been pretty busy lately. Two students—the little one keeps wanting to do dangerous experiments, and the older one... the older one's still a little girl too, thirteen this year, turning fourteen. I heard her family's picking out a husband for her, so she's been pretty annoyed lately..."

"Fourteen is already grown up! She can get married. At least she could get engaged."

"Deep issues about age, can't explain it to you... Mm, I've decided to add a few more stools... How old are you, anyway? Why haven't you gotten yourself married off?"

"My life is hard. I can only depend on Sister Yunzhu for survival... Don't get your hopes up! Oh right, I've been thinking, can we call this place 'Zhu's Kitchen—Jin'er Branch'? If you agree, I'll go order the sign."

"...Change the second branch to Jin'er Branch."

"Mm."

"Sure, no problem. Whatever you want."

"Why is your expression so weird..."

The two chatted about scattered things inside, and though she didn't understand some of what they said, the feeling was really warm—just like family, the feeling of husband and wife, or siblings.

(End of Chapter)

FOOTNOTES:

¹ *Lantern Festival (元夕) - Traditional festival on the 15th day of the first lunar month, marking the end of New Year celebrations with lantern displays and poetry gatherings*

² *Imperial Textile Bureau (织造局) - Government office managing textile production for the imperial court*

³ *Annual tribute cloth (岁布) - Yearly textile quota that must be supplied to the imperial court at reduced prices, a lucrative but burdensome obligation for designated merchants*

⁴ *Getting something for nothing (空手套白狼) - Chinese idiom literally meaning "catching a white wolf barehanded," describing clever schemes to obtain great value without investment*

⁵ *Three Major Textile Houses (织造三家) - The three dominant textile merchant families in Jiangning, maintaining a balance of power in the local cloth trade*
