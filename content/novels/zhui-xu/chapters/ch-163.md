The cloud layer in the sky remained thick, but the world had already brightened in this mid-November morning. The Eastern Capital had also received snow these past days. Today, after the first winter snow cleared, that expanse of white seemed to have settled onto the earth. The city was like a cushion outlined in a field of white snow, the streets slightly lighter white, drawing line after line through the city.

Coming down from a teahouse beside Imperial Avenue, Li Pin looked back at the majestic palace walls in the distance and exhaled a breath of hot air.

After two months of rushing around various places in the Eastern Capital, he'd finally obtained the official documents from the Ministry of Personnel's Bureau of Appointments two days ago. This meant the shadow of having offended Vice Minister of Personnel Fu Ying was now gone. He finally had his first real position and was formally entering official service, able to begin his great ambitions.

His appointment time was next February, when he would head north to Xingzhou to serve as magistrate of Nanhe County. Speaking of it, Nanhe was a good place, even bearing the reputation of "Capital Region's Granary." Its position in Xingzhou was pivotal—it would be very easy to produce achievements there. Getting this posting as a new entrant to officialdom was extremely difficult. It seemed the letter of recommendation Old Qin had written for him when he came had worked.

Thinking of Old Qin made him think of the trouble Ning Yi had encountered when he'd left Jiangning—he'd left right after the decision about imperial merchant status. The Su family had been put in such a difficult position by the Wu family—who knew how that crisis could be resolved. Liheng was already in the position of a live-in son-in-law, and after this matter, he must be finding it even harder to manage within the Su family. But winter travel was difficult, and he'd take up his post in February next year—there was no way to return to Jiangning in this kind of weather.

Thinking of these things, he felt he owed the other party a favor but now that the other party had troubles, he couldn't help. In his heart he actually felt somewhat guilty. Now he was a county magistrate after all, big or small he was an official. If he could go back to help, he could at least play some role. Though subconsciously he always felt there was something fishy about this matter and Ning Yi might not need much help, at the very least it was a matter of friendship.

Being able to become Nanhe's county magistrate—Qin Siyuan, whom Ning Yi had introduced him to, had played no small role. However, some of the connections involved made him feel very strange.

Qin Siyuan was a big figure. Though Ning Yi had been casual when making the introduction, he'd already understood at the time and remembered the name of this great scholar who'd once served as Minister of Personnel. After all, for many students, among the Three Departments and Six Ministries, only the Ministry of Personnel most directly concerned their personal interests. Among the six ministries, only the Ministry of Personnel's importance was hidden chief among the six. The old man he'd met had stood beneath only one person at court several years ago, with only a handful who could compare to him.

But his reasons for stepping down were quite complex. If not for Ning Yi's introduction, Li Pin wouldn't even have known such a great figure was living in seclusion in Jiangning. After the Black Water Alliance, Qin Siyuan had silently withdrawn from court. In the years since, that old man had even carried accusations like "traitor to the Han." When receiving that letter of recommendation, Li Pin had actually very much doubted whether this old man still had any influence, or whether, even if some people at court remembered old favors, because of the Black Water Alliance he might actually have more enemies. Coming here with Qin Siyuan's recommendation, he didn't know if it might have the opposite effect.

But the subsequent reactions were very intriguing.

It felt like many steps were giving him convenience, opening back doors, and though it had taken over two months, that seemed to be to arrange a good position like Nanhe for him. During these two months of activity in the capital, he always felt all the results weren't obtained through his own efforts. Those high officials' smiles were quite meaningful. He'd even vaguely heard that His Majesty had intended to summon him, then later dismissed the idea. That was somewhat frightening.

With only the jinshi degree from several years ago, not even in the top three ranks, and with no achievements, he'd rather believe it was false, yet at certain times he couldn't help connecting this information with some other things he'd recently heard.

The northern lands weren't peaceful anymore. Everyone was brewing war—he'd already sensed this in Jiangning. But in the Eastern Capital where officials gathered, similar feelings seemed to stretch his nerves even tighter. Beyond this, some people were digging up the matter of the Black Water Alliance to discuss, saying the court had deep intentions, that seeds had been planted six or seven years ago. In recent years the Jin-Liao disputes were certainly caused by Wanyan Aguda's heroic talents and unwillingness to remain subordinate, triggering conflicts between both sides. But at the same time, there was also the Wu Dynasty's manipulation from within, secretly trading various materials with the Jin people, arousing their greed—nearly an open scheme. These matters were told almost like storybook tales.

But... the atmosphere he'd felt in the Eastern Capital this recent period made him unable to help wanting to guess—these fantastical tales might actually be true. Capital officials' senses were much sharper than those in outlying areas. During this time, everywhere outside there were rumors of the Wu Dynasty conspiring with Jin people, and Liao people constantly sent envoys to the Wu Dynasty seeking aid. If this groundwork had truly been laid seven years ago when Qin Siyuan retired, then receiving such treatment now when he came to the capital with his recommendation letter—that really could be explained.

Actually last year in Jiangning people had been secretly spreading this matter. The Black Water Alliance appeared humiliating but was actually sowing discord, driving tigers to swallow wolves, using the clash of two powers to recover the Sixteen Prefectures of You and Yun. Of course at that time few people would believe such dreamlike talk... This matter was too significant after all. Even now Li Pin couldn't quite believe it. But between Jin and Liao, there would surely be a battle. If the Wu Dynasty joined in, Xingzhou lay on the route north. Nanhe was prosperous—when the time came it would certainly be a key transit point. If he went there and managed things well, achieving merit and establishing his career was a certainty now.

Thinking like this these past two days, his blood couldn't help boiling. And if those rumors were truly real, perhaps... Qin Siyuan, who'd been living in seclusion in Jiangning for seven years, would also wash away all accusations and return. If this capable and competent Minister of Personnel returned, a position as prime minister was probably certain—only whether left or right prime minister. When that time came, Liheng would probably also smoothly enter court. That would truly be the best possible outcome.

Thinking of this, he couldn't help smiling. On this rare sunny day in the eighth year of Jinghan's winter, Li Pin raised his head on Imperial Avenue to look at the sunlight, slightly narrowing his eyes.

Drive out the powerful enemy, recover Yan and Yun, restore the Han realm's rivers and mountains, wash away a century of humiliation. With the world's situation already in chaos, what came next might be a magnificent and turbulent era.

He always felt... he could achieve great things in this era...

On this day, the not-yet-appointed junior county magistrate thought such things in his heart...

Recently Ning Yi had also noticed that the guests at Old Qin's house seemed to have increased.

As the time approached the end of November, Ning Yi had been quite busy. Like last year, mainly accompanying Su Tan'er on visits everywhere—various kinds of merchants, new and old. Last year it had only counted as going through the motions. At that time his status was merely the Su family's live-in son-in-law. This year he already had a reputation like "Ten Steps, One Scheme"—whether praise or infamy. No one dared look down on him, and thus it became more troublesome instead. However, accompanying his "newly wed" wife in doing these things was only natural. After all, he'd already slept with her—there was nothing to complain about anymore.

They'd been sharing a bed for less than a month. Right now everyone was in the honeymoon period, just like all newlywed men and women. These days the place the two of them most loved being was probably the bed. Su Tan'er had her own reserve and upbringing, but with her personality—capable of burning down a building to advance their relationship—once certain relationships normalized, she actually wasn't too coy.

In the afternoons and evenings she'd handle commercial matters in the room, quite the feeling of a strong career woman. After finishing, she'd pull Ning Yi over to talk about more womanly little things, similar to their previous dates every few days. But now the conversations were more private, including things like the layout of where they'd live in the future, the names of babies they'd have—household matters and business matters both discussed. Talking and talking, talking their way to bed, she'd be stripped naked by Ning Yi. Winter after all—rolling in the sheets was beneficial to physical and mental health. What came next was self-evident.

She could already adapt to these things, and in front of Ning Yi she wasn't shy—she'd even come to enjoy it.

She'd make small initiatives, then afterward be terribly shy as if she'd done something tremendously bold. Ning Yi rather liked this feeling.

There were many interesting things in bedroom pleasures. Su Tan'er's embroidered bed had been used for quite some years. After the two of them had slept on it for about half a month, one night it suddenly started making small noises. The next day when Ning Yi came home, he discovered the bed had been completely disassembled. Several household servants were quietly carrying in a new bed that looked extremely sturdy and definitely wouldn't move, quietly assembling it. They were moving quietly because Su Tan'er sat right next to them at the desk, absorbed in handling business. She'd probably instructed these people to try not to disturb her, so they could only slow down their movements as much as possible.

Like this, though Su Tan'er had clearly ordered the bed replaced, she sat beside them pretending not to see anything at all. These servants could only suffer through assembling the bed. Ning Yi found it amusing. He moved a stool to sit beside and watch, then discovered he was somewhat in the way. Bang bang bang, he shifted over beside Su Tan'er, not saying anything. Su Tan'er's whole face turned red, but she still kept her head down handling official business. Thinking about it, their first time had also passed like this, pretending to be occupied.

Apart from getting along with Su Tan'er and visiting everywhere, he actually still had quite a bit of remaining time, during which he'd requested a group of craftsmen from Kang Xian to conduct research toward cement, mainly in preparation for building his own house.

He didn't put too much effort into this matter, just gave a general direction—mixing and firing lime with clay and such, using different raw materials to conduct more experiments. The rest was left to that group of craftsmen to slowly work on.

The difficulty of this matter wasn't small if you said it wasn't small, wasn't big if you said it wasn't big. Currently for building houses and city walls there were already batches of substitute solutions for cement. As long as the direction was determined, producing a batch of cement wouldn't be difficult. Just without very specialized production lines, the costs of research and production would certainly be quite high. But it didn't matter—just throw money at it. First build himself a small villa. This didn't need to benefit all people—first benefit himself a bit. Once he'd made a start, if Old Kang was interested, or if those craftsmen were interested, he'd leave it to them to develop.

During this period, Ning Yi went to Old Qin's place twice. Both times Old Qin's house had guests, seemingly officials and such who'd come to Jiangning from elsewhere, either passing through or returning to Jiangning to visit family, so they came to visit Old Qin. This matter was quite different from last year's situation, showing that now certain things were already beginning to undergo obvious changes.

The second time he went was on the twenty-first of November, still a heavy snow day, when he met Qin Hui.

By this time Jiangning had already lifted the alcohol ban. At Yunzhu's side, the small workshop had begun brewing the first batch of high-proof spirits and had achieved results. He came from Yunzhu's small building at this time, casually bringing a jar intending to give it to Old Qin. When he went, they were receiving guests inside. He handed the alcohol to Madam Qin, specially instructing a few sentences that this alcohol had high proof, then prepared to leave. But Madam Qin had long regarded him as a trustworthy nephew generation. At this time she kept him: "You just wait. I'll go get some things for you to take back."

This old lady understood Ning Yi's personality and didn't say to have Ning Yi meet Qin Siyuan. Then she quietly went over to inform Old Qin before dragging him inside to meet people. Old Qin had originally been a high official. The old lady actually knew something about court affairs. She knew having Ning Yi meet these officials always had benefits. With Old Qin there, Ning Yi wouldn't suffer any losses either. Using this method to bring him over was actually an extremely intimate gesture. Ning Yi could only accept the favor for the moment. Under Old Qin's introduction, he exchanged names with the two middle-aged men inside.

One of them was called Qin Hui, courtesy name Huizhi, currently serving as Vice Censor-in-Chief—Old Qin didn't say this, but Ning Yi roughly knew it was this person. The man was tall in stature, upright in appearance. His gaze seemed quite wise, his bearing and manner both appearing very composed, able to give people a good impression and feeling of reliability. Both were high officials. They probably thought Ning Yi was Old Qin's nephew generation. After exchanging a few sentences, they were quite warm. Then they used small cups to pour a few cups of alcohol to taste. They commented a few sentences on this high-proof alcohol and conversed quite happily.

The meeting was roughly like this. Ning Yi didn't have much to comment on.

On another front, when the academy was preparing for vacation, Zhou Pei brought up the matter of the apprenticeship ceremony with Ning Yi. Prince Kang's original plan was to make a big show of it—bringing a large group of people, using the prince's banner to visit the Su family, making the apprenticeship ceremony extremely grand, also giving the Su family and Ning Yi sufficient face. From then on the Su family would have a major backing in Jiangning. Ning Yi earnestly refused this.

People's relationship networks were sometimes quite interesting. When you were at a certain low level, high-level people wouldn't actively look your way. But if you suddenly displayed a very high level, people's gazes would become active. Just like the contrast between people's attitudes toward Ning Yi last year and this year. With this kind of initiative, favors and grudges would slowly arise. Though saying grudges were a matter of probability, since he had such high-level relationships, Ning Yi didn't want to actively take them out to show off. There was no meaning to it. After all, these things could be saved up as chips. If the Su family encountered some trouble now, he could use the prince's mansion's connections to sweep it away. But if he revealed the prince's mansion connections now, the problems he'd encounter afterward would only be at this level.

However, though he refused such a grand apprenticeship ceremony, at this year's end Ning Yi planned to bring his wife to visit the Imperial Son-in-law's Mansion and Old Qin's residence. Su Tan'er was extremely anxious about this and prepared for a long time, but actually the subsequent meeting was just ordinary chatting about family matters. A place like the Imperial Son-in-law's Mansion was very high-class for Su Tan'er. Later when she asked Ning Yi why he'd become friends with the Imperial Son-in-law, Ning Yi smiled and said: "Because we're both men who married in." Su Tan'er lightly punched him.

Though Ning Yi didn't mind, Su Tan'er didn't like him constantly mentioning his live-in son-in-law status.

The wind and snow drifted without seeming to stop. In the white expanse of accumulated snow, the firelight in the small courtyard's rooms was always warm and golden. The five people seemed to huddle here, passing through this winter. On one side of the city, in the small building by the bend of the Qinhuai River it was also always warm. Ning Yi would often go in the morning. The woman waiting on the steps wore a cloak, her face frozen red, rubbing her hands together, exhaling hot breath. He'd tell her to wait inside but she refused. Sometimes another woman full of vitality would be bouncing around on the steps. They'd pile up snowmen beside the small building. The woman full of vitality would busily provoke and quarrel with him when she saw Ning Yi.

The Qinhuai River froze over. Occasionally you could see that woman full of vitality sliding around on it. But this was after all a very cold winter. Most of the time, Yunzhu and Jin'er would stay in the room, huddling by the brazier, not knowing what they were chatting about, quite the feeling of depending on each other for survival.

If only that man wouldn't come, it would be even better... Jin'er, who wanted to monopolize Sister Yunzhu, would think this way...

December passed in such an atmosphere in the blink of an eye. New Year arrived. As firecrackers sounded to bid farewell to the old year, the light of the Wu Dynasty's eighth year of Jinghan finally passed, replaced by the Wu Dynasty's ninth year of Jinghan.

This year, prosperous places remained peaceful. Places where people couldn't make a living began to become even more desperate.

This year, the great trends under heaven stirred with wind and clouds. Natural disasters and man-made calamities also came frequently.

This year, uprisings arose in various places, then were promptly suppressed.

This year, war erupted.

North, north, north.

The sky was dark with wind and snow wailing, the driving wind and heavy snow submerging everything on the grassland. In the terrible weather where visibility was barely three meters, there were vaguely some scattered discordant sounds, like hallucinations.

Our vision patrolled forward, close to the ground, until corpses and fresh blood suddenly came into view. The people had died not long ago, but the blood had already gone cold, beginning to congeal in the wind and snow.

Not just one corpse. The corpses entering our vision extended outward in various miserable states—hands and feet hacked off, bodies pierced through, arrow shafts shot into foreheads. Fresh blood and shattered flesh gathered together. At the center of the battlefield were two large carts. The people around them had all died. One corpse had even been pierced through the chest by a long spear, nailed to the cart, both feet off the ground.

The view continued extending, revealing three people fleeing desperately forward in the wind and snow, wearing Liao Dynasty clothing, their footsteps kicking up waves of accumulated snow below though they could see nothing ahead. Suddenly an arrow flew out of the snow curtain. Thud—the person running on the side was pierced through the chest by the arrow shaft. His body flew up and slammed onto the ground with a bang.

In the hazy vision, massive outlines appeared in the wind and snow on the side, and though the two had already run past, they could no longer escape. They knew more people were still surrounding them.

The person running behind waved the blade in his hand, looking back. He looked like a Liao Dynasty officer: "Who are you! Which tribe! You dare to wantonly kill... imperial envoys."

With a roar, wind and snow rolled in. A warhorse neighed long. Behind him it was as if a thunderclap fell. At the same time came his companion's scream and the sound of a body being crushed to pulp. The instant he turned his head, in his peripheral vision ahead, a massive black warhorse reared up on its hind legs and thundered down, trampling his companion's entire body to pieces. His words hadn't finished when something pierced through his body!

He felt the wind and snow stop. But it wasn't so. The figure on the black warhorse was like a mountain peak, in an instant actually blocking this howling blizzard filling the sky. Yet he felt his body rising. His chest hurt terribly. A great spear had stabbed through his chest and emerged from his back. The person on the horse had lifted him up with one hand.

"Your Liao Dynasty is finished."

He heard the figure on the warhorse say this. More figures converged toward here, like this demon's followers.

"You, you are... what person..." He spat fresh blood from his mouth, wanting to use both hands to grasp the spear shaft. His mouth only subconsciously repeated: "Which... tribe, dare to wantonly kill... imperial envoys..." The demon on the warhorse gazed at him coldly.

"Borjigin..."¹ The blood was truly cold, and this was the last sound the Liao officer heard. Wind and snow howled up. His pupils were dilating. He didn't hear the final three syllables in the wind and snow.

"...TE! MU! JIN!"

Darkness descended.

Lüliang.

Heavy snow sealed the mountains, but the snow had stopped. Lu Hongti sat on the large rock beside the stronghold where she'd brushed away the accumulated snow, looking at the white expanse of undulating mountains near and far. Everywhere were mountains, and it truly looked too wild. She didn't know what winter in Jiangning would be like.

But some things existed in the mountains too, and she listened to the sounds of children playing and fighting coming from behind. A snowball flew over her head. Hey, it missed. Yesterday Er Hong and Liu Zi had gotten married. Today was still very lively—inside and outside the stronghold, you could feel it everywhere.

Recently she'd refused the court's amnesty offer and also refused the recruitment of "Hebei Tiger King" Tian Hu, who occupied the Mianshan area. The people in the stronghold didn't quite understand what she wanted. Refusing amnesty was understandable—amnesty didn't lead anywhere good. But refusing to join a rebellion, what for? Really unclear. Everyone was already doing rebellion anyway.

Those in positions of power had to maintain an air of mystery.

The setting sun cast its afterglow across this mountain range. Thinking of that scholar in Jiangning city, she should have kidnapped him and brought him up the mountain back then. She slightly narrowed her eyes.

Auspicious snow promised a good harvest, making this year a good year.

As long as there was no war, actually every year was a good year.

She hoped there would be no war...
