Inside a small painted boat that had grown dim from extinguished lanterns on the Qinhuai River, Xi Junyu glanced around his surroundings. Wu Qilong smiled and brought out a rice pot from the side, prompting Xi to serve himself before sitting down at a nearby table and pouring vegetables into his bowl. In the flickering lamplight, Wu Qilong tossed some greens into his mouth, crunching loudly as he observed, "It's always like this after festivities end—tables full of food in complete disarray, and you never know who actually got to eat their fill."

"At least nobody starves," Xi Junyu answered flatly.

"I always feel hungry myself... There was this one time I really envied that Ning Liheng. Not long ago when everyone was dining together, we'd invited him along with Manager Liao, Manager Luo..." Wu Qilong paused thoughtfully. "He just kept eating the whole time—*actually* eating."

"Outsiders naturally get their fill."

"True enough."

After this brief exchange, the two men settled into eating their meal. Though it appeared to be leftovers and cold dishes, everything had indeed been carefully prepared by renowned chefs, and even now the flavors remained quite excellent. The sounds of chewing filled the cabin as water lapped gently against the boat, and only after quite some time did Wu Qilong finally set down his chopsticks, drumming his fingers on the table.

"Tomorrow, the day after—by tomorrow night, everything will come to a head. At this point, no news is good news. Brother Xi, there shouldn't be any changes now, right?"

"None." Xi Junyu shook his head. "Chen Er confessed the assassination was ordered by someone else. The immediate crisis has passed, the path to the Imperial Merchant position is no longer blocked, and everyone's happy. Though they haven't lost their heads entirely, at least everyone can see clearly—take the Imperial Merchant position and all of First House's problems will resolve themselves. Going forward, there's no turning back; it's do or die². With no possibility of retreat, they can only push forward with the current momentum, so any real changes are impossible now."

"That's the good news then." Wu Qilong poured himself a cup of wine and downed it in one gulp with a smile. "Everything's ready on my end. Won't say much, but Father simply asked Lord Dong to arrange the order that evening. Heh, our Wu family's weaving has always surpassed the Su's—just need that first-mover advantage, and the rest we'll leave to the lords of the Weaving Bureau..."

After saying this, he smiled and waited until Xi Junyu finished eating and put down his chopsticks before shaking his head. "Two days from now, Su Tan'er basically has no chance of taking over the Su family. With the Su family's internal strife, those idiots will only squander the entire estate. There's no future there anymore. Really not coming to our Wu family?"

Xi Junyu glanced at him. "You know what I intend to do."

"We've known each other for years. When I invited you to join our Wu family years ago, you refused for Su Tan'er's sake—I understood. Now you're doing this for a woman who's already another man's wife. Tsk, you really are something..." Wu Qilong sighed deeply, then looked at Xi Junyu's expression and smiled again. "Fine, I know—they haven't consummated their marriage³. After the Imperial Merchant matter is decided, you can certainly manipulate some managers against Ning Liheng, ensure they never have the chance to consummate. Su Tan'er's years of effort going up in smoke, she'll definitely need someone to vent on. But in the end, this is really difficult, Xi Junyu. Once the Su family collapses, Su Tan'er's nature will compel her to grit her teeth and hold it all together, and while you help from behind the scenes, it could take years—even a decade—before she's indebted enough to you to be together. It's an incredibly difficult path."

Wu Qilong paused, and here Xi Junyu spoke calmly: "And by then, the Wu family will have become the Imperial Merchant, the timing will be right, you can move toward becoming the empire's premier cloth merchant. As for the Su family, decades of accumulation to reach this scale—once the Old Master dies and it collapses, they won't rise again for decades. We'll no longer be competitors, and naturally, I'll no longer be a threat to you."

"I've never cared about such threats, just find it a pity." Wu Qilong frowned. "In all of Jiangning, me, my younger brother, Xue Yan, you—none of us compare to Su Tan'er. Speaking honestly, in these few years, she grabs onto one thing and won't let go, pushing it this far—that's the true way of business. She really is formidable; we're all inferior to her. Speaking of business, among the younger generation besides Su Tan'er, only Puyang Yi of the Puyang family and Tang Xu of the Tang family make me feel inadequate. But she is a woman, after all, and while I admit I might not have accomplished as much in her position, she is ultimately limited by that—facing many complications that seem to arise from nowhere."

Wu Qilong took a breath. "Honestly, I've never specifically wanted to target the Su family. If not for this situation where both my family and the Xue family have set our sights on the Imperial Merchant position—if Su Tan'er was prepared, then what she should earn is hers to earn, nobody would fight her for it. Unfortunately for her, she decided to make her move just as everyone else was watching the same prize, and since we're all in the game, deception and intrigue are simply how it is. But I've never wanted to target anyone specifically—it's just business. Our Wu family is already Jiangning's premier cloth merchant. Brother Xi, Jiangning is just a pond. You could go out to the ocean—do you really want to stay in this pond?"

Xi Junyu smiled slightly. "It's just doing things. Not so many grand principles."

"True enough." Wu Qilong smiled and shook his head. "I know your thinking—in life we just do things, when there's something we want to do, we do it. But... she's just a woman. When you stand higher someday, maybe you'll find these matters very boring. Or in a few years you'll discover this woman is utterly ordinary, no longer having that initial feeling—you'll regret it. You know, I married at eighteen, and three years later she passed away. I swore I'd never touch another woman, but a year later one day, when I thought of her I'd forgotten what she looked like. I took two concubines... Women are all the same."

"People are all like this." Wu Qilong continued. "We men are meant for great things, while women—who can't accomplish anything—are all the same; if you let go of Su Tan'er, you'll find many others just like her. You know, many women like to preen and pose and put on airs. Whether they're pretending or genuine, just once is enough—the second time I absolutely won't waste my thoughts on them. These are small matters, but regarding them, let me give you a saying: 'Knowing well this longing serves no purpose—if you have no heart for me, then I shall cease!'⁴"

"Lot of nonsense today."

"Heh, I know you might not listen, but as long as there's a possibility, I must speak, because in three more days this matter will be resolved. Just because they haven't consummated, you plan to hover around her for ten or twenty years? Take one step forward and you'll see the ocean—just one step. The next ten or twenty years will be completely different for you. This Su family matter—success would be good, but even if the Imperial Merchant position went to the Su family, I wouldn't take it to heart. Our Wu family remains the Wu family. You and I together, our scope definitely won't be limited to just Jiangning."

Wu Qilong smiled. "How this turns out, you'll have to consider for yourself in the end."

The more capable someone was at accomplishing things, the more resolute their will. Xi Junyu wasn't someone who didn't think things through—convincing him would certainly be difficult, but when it was time to speak up, one still had to. After he finished saying all this, Xi Junyu's expression remained placid, and only after a long while did he finally speak.

"These last two or three days, don't let any complications arise. Su Tan'er isn't simple—she might have backup plans. For this New Year cloth matter, she's been pulling funds from various places, preparing for over two years already. With hundreds of thousands of taels of silver already invested, nobody can say what she might do when the Imperial Merchant is announced and all her hopes come to nothing."

"Heh heh, Brother Xi means dumping goods at reduced prices?" Wu Qilong laughed happily. "I'd actually welcome her doing that—breaking the rules, everyone would attack her together, the Su family would collapse even faster. Your family's old master wouldn't let her do that. Su Zhongkan and Su Yunfang wouldn't agree either. If she did that, she'd be dragging the entire Su family down into madness."

He shook his head, his voice rising somewhat from happiness and confidence. "What I'm guarding against now—Su Tan'er and Manager Liao have already done their utmost to build the Su family's momentum to this point. Su Yu is the most formidable person, back then single-handedly propping up the Su family and establishing Jiangning's three-legged cloth merchant structure. If he steps forward afterward, perhaps he could barely turn the tide, but he's old—the Su family won't last much longer. Of course that's a future matter. Now he's already let go, and the effect he can have is limited. As for the rest, who else is there? Could it be Brother Ning, receiving his mandate in crisis, drawing everyone's attention, turning the tide?"

Xi Junyu narrowed his eyes, his expression languid. Honestly, he didn't much like hearing that name. An incompetent person who somehow married Su Tan'er, and now the Su family had even pushed him forward to temporarily take charge. An incompetent person who had somehow taken away what could have been his. "Don't be arrogant. He is Jiangning's number one talent after all, overflowing with poetic genius. You're conducting shady business—be careful he denounces you with words and writing afterward."

"Haha, reasonable, reasonable." Wu Qilong slapped the table laughing, then his expression turned slightly serious. "This person isn't actually stupid though. Observing his bearing and manner compared to the Su family members, he's actually much more sensible. While his actions these days have been clumsy, they haven't been reckless, which shows he's genuinely trying to think and learn—though the Su family's dire situation is clearly making him anxious. In normal times with minor issues, letting him manage wouldn't lead to major mistakes. But right now... he's a scholar facing this situation, his opponents aren't even on the same level. What can a slightly clever beginner accomplish? This matter from start to finish isn't something he could participate in. Can only say... wrong timing."

"After this, I imagine he'll understand a lot." Xi Junyu thought about some of Ning Yi's actions these days and shook his head lightly, then turned to leave. "As long as there's nothing else, thanks for the hospitality."

"Great kindness needs no thanks—you should repay it like a gushing spring." Wu Qilong made a joke, then waved his hand. "Think about what I said. The ocean's right ahead—not worth it for a pond. The Wu family's doors are always open to you. Oh, and that line... 'Knowing well this longing serves no purpose...'"

As his silhouette dissolved into the darkness at the boat's rail, the departing Xi Junyu repeated the line: "'If you have no heart for me, then I shall cease,'" adding, "It would be best if we don't have meetings like this again."

"This matter is settled, there shouldn't be any more changes." Wu Qilong answered. When that hazy figure had departed with the small boat, he finally sighed, pushing aside the bowls and chopsticks before him, standing up and turning to leave: "What a pity..."

His words came out in a low murmur. No matter what, Xi Junyu was a talent he'd always wanted to recruit. When he took over the Wu family in the future, he'd need his own team. The Wu family getting the Imperial Merchant position now was certainly pleasing—some plans could be advanced, icing on the cake—but even without it, the Wu family would still be the Wu family. He was still young, with plenty of opportunities to expand in the future. Only such talents were rare and hard to find. What he truly valued was the future, not these immediate benefits.

Of course, since such benefits existed, there was nothing wrong with taking them in passing. He stood at the boat's rail, thinking of the Su family's panic over the past month, the fear hidden within their aggressive moves, the anxious running about of that Old Master Su who supposedly single-handedly brought the Su family to Jiangning's peak, and the gleefully gloating faces of the Xue family opposite. He couldn't help but smile and shake his head again.

Truly laughable.

The light on the river dimmed a bit more as the small painted boat sailed forward through the waves.

Dawn broke, then darkness fell again. This was August 24th. When dawn came again, it would be the morning of the 25th. Ning Yi slept in and thus missed the morning meeting. That evening would be the cloth merchants' annual general assembly held by the Weaving Bureau. Since autumn was harvest season, such general gatherings happened once a year in every trade.

**Footnotes:**

¹ **Discussing Over Wine (煮酒)**: The Chinese title literally means "cooking wine" but is a classical allusion to a famous scene in *Romance of the Three Kingdoms* where the warlord Cao Cao tests his rival Liu Bei's ambitions while discussing heroes over heated wine. It implies a strategic meeting between ambitious rivals sizing each other up.

² **Do or die (破釜沉舟)**: Literally "break the cauldrons and sink the boats." A classical idiom referring to burning one's bridges, leaving no possibility of retreat.

³ **Consummation of marriage**: In traditional Chinese society, a marriage wasn't considered fully binding until consummated. An unconsummated marriage could potentially be dissolved more easily.

⁴ **Classical poetry quotation**: "直道相思了无益，你既无心我便休" - A line expressing the futility of one-sided love, essentially meaning "Since longing is clearly pointless, if you have no feelings for me, I'll let go."
