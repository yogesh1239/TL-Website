By mid-February, the rains had begun.

There was still a short while before Qingming. The cold spring rain washed the world clean and bright, while sprouting grass and budding flowers gradually filled it out, little by little.

In these times, Qingming counted as a major occasion. Though there were still more than ten days to go, the Su family had already begun preparing for the ancestral rites. Just like last year, this was the season when Ning Yi actually became rather idle. A live-in son-in-law who did not change his surname could not enter the ancestral hall, so when the day came, he would not count as an especially important participant anyway. Some of the Su women still had to help with preparations for the rites, but Ning Yi naturally did not need to bother with any of that. Of course, he was usually fairly idle to begin with, so the difference did not make him stand out much more than usual.

Not entering the ancestral hall was not a big deal in itself, but it also meant not being taken seriously. By ordinary standards, that would make a man lose face. After all, people were always talking about what a "real man" ought to be. But in Ning Yi's case, things had passed that way last year, while this year it left the Su family in a difficult spot. According to Xiao Chan, several of the family elders had been discussing it with Su Boyong, trying to figure out whether there was some way to let Ning Yi take part in this year's ancestral rites so he would not feel slighted. They argued it back and forth and got nowhere, which left everyone miserable over the problem.

After all, Ning Yi's importance within the Su family had already become obvious. He might still hold the status of a live-in son-in-law, but in terms of actual achievement, no one could help but respect him. That status was something the Su family needed, so it could not be changed. Yet if they did not let him enter the ancestral hall, then in the future he would not receive the family's incense offerings from later generations either. People cared about that sort of thing these days, so instead they worried he might take offense. After everyone had discussed it, they even called Su Tan'er over to ask her thoughts. She came back with a headache and raised the matter by degrees, tapping around it indirectly. Ning Yi thought for a moment.

"Su Yi, Su Liheng... doesn't sound that good."

"This wife thinks so too."

"Then let's leave it alone... Don't think too much about all that nonsense. As for those old men, let them worry. If they want to trouble themselves over it, that's fine."

As for human nature, human feelings, rules, and formalities, Ning Yi only had to think a little to see the whole thing clearly. He understood where their distress came from, but no matter how little he cared about many things, there was no way he was changing his surname. They probably understood that too, which was exactly why they were so troubled.

Of course, suspicion and worry had been unavoidable from the night Old Master Su publicly revealed the truth about the imperial merchant appointment. These people were bound to worry about whether he harbored ambitions, whether he was too capable, whether he held some grievance or dissatisfaction. None of that mattered. If they wanted to bind him, suppress him, or limit him somehow, they were free to try. From the very beginning, Ning Yi had never had any interest at all in the Su family's wealth or power.

Although his relationship with his wife was very good now, when it came to matters like the ancestral rites, Su Tan'er still seemed a little hesitant whenever she mentioned them. That did not matter either. One day, she would roughly understand what kind of person he was. Even if she found it strange, given enough time, she would come to understand in the end.

It might be a long process, but they could simply go on finding their rhythm little by little.

Every day at the academy, among his group of students, Zhou Pei's recent emotional state struck Ning Yi as a little odd. He already knew the girl had been miserable over rumors that her family was choosing a commandery prince consort for her. That ordinarily tough little girl had even cried in secret. Lately, though, she had become stranger still. Two days ago he had seen her furtively plucking leaves, washing them clean, then hiding off to one side and chewing on them. Those leaves were bitter as hell. Just thinking about them made Ning Yi frown. He had no idea why that high and mighty little commandery princess had suddenly decided to torment herself.

As a teacher, he paid rather more attention to the few girl students in his class. That was only because they were rare. Once they had gone through his introductory teaching, they would have no further chance to continue their studies. The only things waiting for them afterward were marriage, serving their husbands, and raising children.

But his attention went no further than that. Trying to counsel them would be pointless. Even if they had psychological troubles, those troubles were societal ones. If society itself could not be changed, then the more they thought, the worse it would be for them. In the present world, the saying that "for women, lack of talent is a virtue" was, in a practical sense, still extremely true. So when it came to this little commandery princess who had suddenly grown peculiar, Ning Yi merely kept teaching her arithmetic and did not ask about anything else.

Outside the Su family, the assassination at Zhu's had, for the time being, come to an end now that several of the assassins had escaped. The affair had come abruptly and ended quickly as well, but the unusual implications faintly showing beneath it were enough to draw the attention of anyone who cared to look. The Liao, the Jin, the Wu Dynasty... certain complicated struggles had only just begun to show their outlines here.

For Ning Yi, the matter was still a little too large. And for Qin Siyuan, who stood inside it, life remained sunk in that heavy, complicated quiet, without much outward change. When Ning Yi visited now and then, they only chatted about books, played a game of go, or talked about household trifles. When Ning Yi mentioned the Su family's recent troubles, the other man laughed and said being too low-key could be troublesome for others too. But as for affairs of state, he would not utter a word.

During the assassination at Zhu's, Yunzhu had been injured, and Jin'er had subdued one of the attackers as well. That counted as saving Qin Siyuan's life. A long time ago, Ning Yi had considered having Qin Siyuan take Yunzhu in as an adopted daughter. Later, because of Yunzhu's performance at Smoke and Emerald Tower, the idea had been dropped, but Yunzhu and the Qin family had still formed ties, and she would occasionally come pay a visit to Grandpa Qin. After this incident, Qin Siyuan brought up the matter of taking her as a daughter once again.

The last time the idea had been raised, it was Ning Yi who brought it up, and the meaning behind that had already been extraordinary. If a former high official took a woman from the pleasure quarters as his adopted daughter, that would certainly damage Old Qin's reputation once word spread. But at the time, Ning Yi had thought it over because he understood the old man's feelings and had resolved to give Yunzhu a reliable backer. As for Old Qin's side, first, they knew each other reasonably well, and second, Ning Yi had already made up his mind to pay something for the matter himself. As long as the old man agreed, Ning Yi had the ability to make a few moves and ensure he did not come out the loser. Later, though, because Yunzhu once again stepped forward on his behalf, it would have been pushing things too far to ask for the old man to agree after that, so Ning Yi let the matter rest.

But this time, Old Qin himself was the one proposing it, which made the meaning far more unusual. Back then, Old Qin had only been a dismissed official. Now, with calls rising outside from every direction and with the Liao having even tried to assassinate him, once he returned to office he would immediately become a great minister beneath only the emperor and above all others. Yet he still brought up a matter like this. Even Ning Yi could not help being startled.

There was no way Ning Yi could agree on Yunzhu's behalf, and there was no need to say no either. Since important figures were constantly coming to Qin's residence these days, Yunzhu could not very well come over herself. So later, Ning Yi brought Yunzhu and Jin'er along on a visit, and the old man thanked them personally for stepping in.

That afternoon, tea was served in the courtyard while they chatted for a while. Qin Siyuan understood perfectly well what sort of relationship there was between Ning Yi and Yunzhu. "What exactly is going on between the two of you? I truly don't know how to describe it. I've never seen anything quite like it..."

There had long been a bond between the two that should have let them be together already. Yet because of Jin'er's obstruction, it still seemed they had never crossed that final line. Ning Yi had no way to bring her home, but at the same time, it felt perfectly natural for him to bring Yunzhu here on a visit, or to make decisions about major matters in her life. In fact, it felt as natural as a husband bringing his wife back to visit her father's home. When Qin Siyuan occasionally mentioned this sort of thing to Kang Xian, even he could only sigh helplessly over it.

Jin'er was already fairly familiar with Old Qin's household by now, so when she heard that, she immediately lit up and started enthusiastically tattling on Ning Yi. Ning Yi and Yunzhu could only listen, drinking tea and smiling bitterly now and then. In Jin'er's eyes, their whole attitude looked like the kind of shameless, thick-skinned retreat that feared nothing. She was deeply annoyed both by Sister Yunzhu's stubborn devotion and by the way she simply let things go. "Grandpa Qin, you really ought to scold them properly."

Qin Siyuan laughed. "You said it yourself, they're stubborn to the point of obsession. Scolding won't wake them up. In truth, if a person can really be stubborn about something once in life, that may not be a bad thing. Give them some time."

"Hmph." Jin'er gave a cold snort. "No."

That was what she said, but there was nothing she could really do about it. In truth, it was not as though she found no enjoyment in the whole situation either. After recovering from her illness, she still spent her days bickering with Ning Yi or talking about the affairs of Jin'er's shop, going on with her usual leisurely life. But over the past few days, the matter that had become more and more heated in Jiangning was Li Shishi's visit from the capital to call on friends. No one knew who was stirring things up, but the whole affair had been turned into a challenge from the Eastern Capital to Jiangning. Many of Jiangning's young literary talents had already gotten moving, egging Qilan, Luo Miaomiao, and the others into preparing a performance for those days and inviting Miss Li over for a contest. Li Shishi had not even arrived yet, and things on this side were already in full uproar.

"That Li Shishi is very famous. I heard lots of really impressive men of letters in the Eastern Capital have written poems for her. There's one called Zhou Bangyan who's supposed to be very famous too. I've read some of his lyrics. They're very good." Yuan Jin'er had come over to the little building that afternoon, and she too was paying attention to the Li Shishi business. "Lately a lot of Jiangning's talented scholars have been producing new works. So, Great Talent Ning, are you going to write a new lyric too and beat down all that arrogance from the Eastern Capital?"

"Write poetry? Sure. I've been feeling inspired lately." Ning Yi picked up his brush and started writing at once. Jin'er's mouth fell open, and she quickly leaned across the table to look. Yunzhu, who was passing by, also curiously leaned in.

"Germanium, plutonium, uranium, chlorine, thorium, arsenic, cesium, / Stealing all the charm in a little garden. / Potassium, sodium, calcium, magnesium, lithium, beryllium, francium, / Hidden fragrance drifting beneath the dusk moon... What kind of poem is this..."

Lately Ning Yi had been trying to recall the periodic table, though he could never quite get it all right. Holding the sheet of xuanzhi paper, he looked it over and shook his head in private amusement. "Fine poem, fine poem... Though there are still four lines left. Next comes lead, aluminum, tungsten, palladium, fluorine, carbon, itch... hmm, that last bit sounds kind of like 'not that itchy'..."

"Tch. If you don't want to write one, then don't. You're always like this..." Apparently "Little Plum Blossoms in a Mountain Garden" still had not made its way into Wu. Jin'er peered at those lines about stealing all the charm in a little garden and hidden fragrance drifting beneath the dusk moon. "What a waste of good lines. Great talents really are impressive..."

After thinking a moment, she smiled again. "Oh, right. A young gentleman came to visit Sister Yunzhu this morning. He was handsome, steady, refined, and an official too. He definitely writes better poetry than you. Sister Yunzhu had a very pleasant chat with him."

"Huh?" Ning Yi laughed. "I don't believe it. How could he possibly write something this good?"

"It was Old Qin's eldest son," Yunzhu said with a smile from behind him. "Because of Old Qin's assassination attempt, and with Qingming approaching, he hurried back from Jiangzhou. He came today specifically to offer thanks. He said he had visited Liheng's home yesterday, but it was a rushed visit and he didn't manage to see you. You didn't know?"

"Qin Shaohe... Someone did come by yesterday, but he didn't leave a name. He only said he'd come again..."

Ning Yi thought it over and understood. He had heard about Qin Siyuan's two sons many times before. Qin Shaohe and Qin Shaoqian, one civil and one military, were both highly valued in the government and the army because of Qin Siyuan's influence. Last year, thanks to his achievements in disaster relief, Qin Shaohe had already been promoted to Governor of Jiangzhou. Presumably because they were too busy, neither brother had returned to Jiangning at the end of the year. This time, they had likely heard that their father had been attacked and rushed back at once.

He asked Yunzhu a little about Qin Shaohe. As Qin Siyuan's eldest son, the man was in fact already close to forty. Jin'er's description of him as handsome, steady, and refined was not wrong. From what Ning Yi had heard before from Qin Siyuan, Qin Shaohe was modest and steady by nature, and very much his father's son. In terms of scholarship, he truly counted as the one who had inherited Qin Siyuan's mantle. But he was never flashy. Under Qin Siyuan's influence, he valued practical affairs and disliked empty display. His grounding in poetry and lyrics was quite solid as well, but because he wrote little, his literary fame was not especially prominent. In officialdom too, he had advanced one step at a time. In earlier years, he had ultimately been held back by his father's troubles, and only recently had his promotion begun.

The three of them talked for a while about the Qin brothers. Ning Yi also continued fooling around with "Little Plum Blossoms in a Mountain Garden" for a bit before finally writing out the real version and letting Yuan Jin'er take a look. After reading it once, Yuan Jin'er curled her lip. "It's only so-so." But she still seemed to like it.

Ning Yi told her not to show it off. Looking at it herself was fine, but if she really had to show it around, she was not to drag his name into it. On the way back that day, he stopped by the Qin residence to pay a visit and, sure enough, learned that the Qin family's eldest son had indeed returned. That afternoon, however, he had gone out to visit other people. When Ning Yi got home, he found visitors there as well, but it was not Qin Shaohe. It was Puyang Yi of the Puyang family. Since Ning Yi himself was not home yet, Su Tan'er and Su Boyong had been the ones receiving him.

"...Brother Ning must already have heard the rumors that Miss Shishi from the Eastern Capital will be coming soon. Right now the entire city of Jiangning is stirred up, and everyone is hoping Qilan can exchange pointers with Miss Shishi... Heh. Though we still do not know the other side's intentions, the possibility of a meeting remains. I feel that, since the reputation of Jiangning is involved, Brother Ning might make an exception this once and lend a hand. If you were to write Qilan a new lyric for the sake of the literary reputation of Jiangning, we would at least be prepared for anything..."

In all their previous dealings, Puyang Yi had always kept his posture very low and had never made any unreasonable request. This time, though, Ning Yi understood. The other man had been cultivating this for a long time and now meant to collect on the fruits of all that long-standing goodwill...
