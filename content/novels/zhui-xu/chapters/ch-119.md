In the early morning hours, past the hour of the Ox, Su Tan'er woke.

When she opened her eyes, a yellow lamplight was flickering weakly. Outside the window lay the stillness of night, yet she felt no peace. Her mind and body churned with a vague, suffering agitation. Why did she feel this way? Fragmented images drifted through the chaotic haze of her thoughts: a blocked path, pursuers at her back, things collapsing around her... and through it all, a familiar voice.

"Young Miss has awakened."

It was Xiao Chan's voice, instantly recognizable without thought or effort. Su Tan'er closed her eyes and struggled to remember for a moment. When she tried to force herself up, Xiao Chan pressed her back down while holding the blanket in place. The little maid hadn't used much strength—it was mainly that Tan'er had none left. Looking up, she saw red-rimmed eyes.

"What time is it?" she asked, her voice hoarse and rasping, sounding nothing like herself.

"The hour of the Ox is nearly over."

"Young Miss, don't get up..."

"I'll go heat the medicine..."

The voices surrounding her belonged to Chan'er, Juan'er, and Xing'er, but the one who'd answered about the time was Liheng. He'd stayed here too. Her mind still ached, but warmth touched her heart. She recalled what had happened before: "Manager Liao and the others..."

"Young Miss, please don't think about these things anymore, alright..." Juan'er beside the bed choked up with tears. Su Tan'er shook her head apologetically and whispered weakly: "I can't help it..."

"Manager Liao and the others have already gone back to rest." Ning Yi's voice came from nearby. He then spoke softly to Chan'er and Juan'er: "Let me talk with her. You two go help Xing'er first."

The two maids nodded and left for the adjacent room to prepare medicine. When the room quieted, Su Tan'er's vision and mind gradually came into focus. Her husband sat there in the same blue robe he'd worn during the day, having pulled over a stool to sit and watch her. His manner resembled their usual chats on the second floor—head tilted casually, carrying that scholarly air of calm composure. Though his young features didn't look particularly mature or dignified, this was indeed the image of the talented scholar she'd once envisioned.

His learning surpassed countless others, yet he never flaunted it, possessing a quiet, contained depth that held great power. Su Tan'er had never given it much thought. By all rights, the title of "top scholar" should have been incredibly impressive, but at home, none of them—not her, not Chan'er, Juan'er, or Xing'er—seemed to feel its weight. Their interactions had always been relaxed and natural. Others might feel a sense of pride when mentioning his title, or be startled upon learning of it, but those closest to him only ever saw the man himself. From a distance, people saw the halo of a top scholar; up close, they just saw him.

Yet somehow, seeing him now, that title of top scholar suddenly came to mind. He'd been here since evening without rest too. Though he was technically her husband, she'd still affected him after all. Her husband was ultimately a scholar who shouldn't be dragged into these business matters, but now... She smiled apologetically and was about to speak when Ning Yi held out a piece of pastry.

Just as she would during their chats on the second floor, Su Tan'er instinctively reached for it, but her hand had no strength. Ning Yi held the pastry out for a moment longer before popping it into his own mouth, chewing thoughtfully before taking a gulp of tea to wash it down. His expression remained calm.

"I can eat these things. You can't. You can only drink medicine."

She wanted to laugh, but the rising emotion brought a wave of dizziness and exhaustion. She felt somewhat helpless—why was this person trying to make her laugh?

Then she heard Ning Yi say: "Now then... there are some matters we need to discuss."

"Mm?" The weak woman in bed opened her tired eyes again.

******************

The charcoal fire burned. In the earthenware vessel, medicinal vapors began rising. The three maids kept watch nearby, occasionally glancing toward the wall, worry evident in their eyes.

Xiao Chan was slightly better off, while Juan'er and Xing'er were heavy with concern. Young Miss had finally awakened but still burned with fever. In this long night, things had really just begun. With Young Miss fallen ill, the First Branch's affairs would be difficult. They'd all grown up with Young Miss from childhood and knew the effort and sacrifice she'd put into this. She would never retreat. They wondered if Master could persuade Young Miss to rest. But even if he did, what would happen to the First Branch's affairs? Would they let Young Miss watch helplessly as all that hard work slipped away?

"...What Master asked us to do earlier, what was that for?"

"Can't understand it..."

"Master's experiment place has things like that too..."

"But what use could it be..."

"Don't know..."

While preparing the medicine, the three maids shared their confusion. Earlier, Ning Yi had asked them to stretch ink lines across rice paper in a grid pattern, then took account books from three years ago and began marking things on it. Some were place names or Su family shop names, but most were strange, crooked symbols—simple vertical lines, circles, half-circles and such, completely incomprehensible. Master recorded things quickly, only occasionally frowning in thought. Twice he'd called the three of them over to ask what caused certain income and expense issues, then noted it beside the symbols.

Master wanted to understand the Su family's situation, but what good would this do? No one could figure it out. Over the past year, Master had given them the impression of being very approachable and knowledgeable, but he'd never been involved in business matters after all. Now the entire Su family sensed the crisis. With so many managers and supervisors who'd been in business for decades all busy working, Master was ultimately a scholar. Even if he wanted to help, it was probably just a scholar's impulse to cram at the last minute—it wouldn't be much use. Each field had its specialists—surely it must be so.

"Master, he..." Xiao Chan, who was closest to Ning Yi, spoke with her head lowered, "Master is very capable..."

She'd always known Ning Yi was very capable. After becoming intimate with him, this feeling had naturally strengthened. But that too had limits. Previously when she'd wanted Master to help share Young Miss's burdens, it was mainly for the closeness between Master and Young Miss. Having someone help share the load, with such feelings present, would naturally make Young Miss happier. But in business matters, Master certainly couldn't replace Young Miss. Even in Xiao Chan's heart, Master was much more capable than ordinary people in these things—that was already very impressive. But to say Master was better than everyone at everything? How could that be possible? Even Xiao Chan couldn't think that way.

"We also know Master is very capable, very clever, but he can't be as amazing at everything as he is at writing poetry..." Juan'er said softly.

"Master promised he has a way. He wouldn't lie." At this point, Xiao Chan could only stubbornly believe this. Nearby, Xing'er gazed at the charcoal fire in long silence before finally wiping her eyes again.

"I know Young Miss's temperament, but if Master can just persuade Young Miss to rest properly this time, that would be enough." Perhaps because of the weight in her heart, Xing'er, usually the strongest-willed, had shed many tears this night. Her voice choked up: "As long as Young Miss is well, even if she doesn't become family head, Young Miss is still Young Miss, Master is still Master, we'll still be together... As long as it's like this, that's enough..."

Her emotions infected Juan'er and Chan'er nearby. As gray sorrow enveloped them again and Juan'er began to choke up, Chan'er said softly beside them: "Master will find a way..."

"Mm." Xing'er and Juan'er nodded beside her, though probably no one truly believed it.

As long as Young Miss could be alright, that would be best. As for the rest, they could only let others in the family work hard—Old Master, Manager Liao, Manager Xi, Second Master, Third Master's side... With such a large family, someone would surely be able to bear it...

*******************

While the maids went to prepare medicine, the oil lamp still flickered in the bedroom. Ning Yi sat by the bed, took a sip of tea, and slowly relayed Doctor Sun's diagnosis.

"It's not just a wind-cold¹—you know that yourself. I know you're suffering right now, but if your mind can't settle, you can't solve problems. If you can't find peace, you'll be even less able to solve problems. It's becoming a vicious cycle... I know my rambling annoys you too..."

He paused slightly. Su Tan'er shook her head faintly, then spoke, her voice as light as a wisp of smoke that might break in the wind at any moment: "Husband, I understand... but how can I let go..." Her expression held sorrow.

"Whether you let go or not is up to you." Ning Yi placed his palm on her forehead. "You're like this now, we can't discuss too much, so I'll keep it simple. I just finished looking through several of the First Branch's account books from three years ago."

"Hm?" Su Tan'er looked confused.

"I just finished looking through several of the First Branch's account books from three years ago." Ning Yi gazed at her calmly and repeated himself. "Father-in-law isn't out of danger yet, and neither are you. Grandfather might consider sending someone to take over, though you won't agree to that. But from now on, you can't leave this bed. Everything is laid out before us, so I'll help you from here on. Chan'er, Juan'er, and Xing'er are all here too, though for some matters, only I can represent you. This is the only way now..."

"I know you have questions, but we don't need to get into everything right now. We can talk slowly tomorrow, or the day after, when you have a clearer head. I have things to discuss with you, too. For now, just listen. We've known each other for over a year, so I want you to know something about me: when I say I can do something, I can do it. I am being completely serious. This business with the family—I will help you see it through."

Su Tan'er gripped his hand and shook her head with difficulty, showing a smile close to tears: "Husband, this matter... you don't know..."

Ning Yi stopped her from shaking her head and leaned in slightly to look at her: "No, I know the nature of these matters. I'm saying this after knowing. I want you to know this too. Whether you believe me depends on how you view my character and our friendship, but for now just remember that I've said this. This is most important..."

"So, for the next few days, we'll handle everything from this room. You can listen from your bed, watch, and think if you want. Before any decision is made, I'll explain my reasoning. If you nod, we'll move forward. I know you can't let this go—that it's impossible to leave you out of it—so I won't. I'll just handle the heavy lifting. All you need to do is decide if what I'm saying makes sense. Can you agree to that?"

Su Tan'er closed her eyes. After a long while, a tear slid from the corner of her eye. Ning Yi softened his tone.

"The Su family isn't at the point of crisis yet. That side hasn't made their move. You need to prepare to take your medicine now and think less. Just remember what I've said. I said I'll resolve this, and I definitely will. Then get a good sleep. At least for now, put your mind at ease. Nothing will happen to the family because I'm here. Alright?"

Su Tan'er kept her eyes closed and nodded.

"Good, we have an agreement."

Ning Yi sat back and took a sip of tea. A moment later, Su Tan'er opened her eyes from where she lay: "Husband, I feel much better..."

"You business people have no persuasive power at all." Ning Yi curled his lip and shook his head in disbelief.

Su Tan'er smiled faintly, then another wave of dizziness struck her mind.

Soon after, Xing'er, Juan'er, and Chan'er brought in the medicine bowl. Under everyone's concerned gazes, they helped her sit up and drink it all before she finally fell into deep sleep. Before sleeping, she said one thing: "Husband should rest too..."

"I know."

She woke once more during the night. The sky had brightened slightly. Xiao Chan was dozing in a chair beside the bed, while that familiar figure still sat at the table by the window, writing. Seeing him there, she closed her eyes and drifted back to sleep. And perhaps because of that silhouette, this time, her heart was a little more at peace.
